## 🔤 Vowels (5 Lessons)

### Lesson 1: Single Vowels (a, e, i, o, u)
**Duration**: 15 minutes  
**Objectives**: Recognize and produce 5 basic vowels

**Content**:
1. **Vowel /a/** (as in "pat")
   - Articulation: Open front vowel
   - Audio example: "pai" (go)
   - Practice words: pai, tapa, kapa, lapa, sapa
   - Exercises: Listening, repetition, minimal pairs

2. **Vowel /e/** (as in "pet")
   - Articulation: Mid front vowel
   - Audio example: "em" (mother)
   - Practice words: em, pen, ten, sen, lem
   - Exercises: Listening, repetition, minimal pairs

3. **Vowel /i/** (as in "pit")
   - Articulation: Close front vowel
   - Audio example: "in" (person)
   - Practice words: in, pin, tin, sin, kin
   - Exercises: Listening, repetition, minimal pairs

4. **Vowel /o/** (as in "pot")
   - Articulation: Mid back vowel
   - Audio example: "om" (uncle)
   - Practice words: om, pot, ton, son, lon
   - Exercises: Listening, repetition, minimal pairs

5. **Vowel /u/** (as in "put")
   - Articulation: Close back vowel
   - Audio example: "um" (grandmother)
   - Practice words: um, put, tun, sun, lun
   - Exercises: Listening, repetition, minimal pairs

**Exercises**:
- Listening identification (5 exercises)
- Repetition practice (5 exercises)
- Minimal pairs (5 exercises)
- Word recognition (5 exercises)
- Dictation (3 exercises)

**Assessment**: 80%+ accuracy on all exercises

### Lesson 3: Vowel Stress Patterns
**Duration**: 15 minutes  
**Objectives**: Recognize and produce stressed vs unstressed vowels

**Content**:
1. **Primary Stress**
   - Definition: Strongest emphasis in word
   - Audio examples: "PÁI" (go - stressed), "pái" (go - unstressed)
   - Practice: Stress placement in words

2. **Secondary Stress**
   - Definition: Weaker emphasis in longer words
   - Audio examples: Multi-syllable words
   - Practice: Identifying secondary stress

3. **Unstressed Vowels**
   - Definition: Reduced vowel sound (schwa)
   - Audio examples: Unstressed syllables
   - Practice: Recognizing reduced vowels

4. **Stress Patterns in Sentences**
   - Definition: Emphasis on key words
   - Audio examples: Sentence-level stress
   - Practice: Natural stress patterns

5. **Stress & Meaning**
   - Definition: How stress changes meaning
   - Audio examples: Minimal pairs with stress
   - Practice: Stress-based meaning changes

**Exercises**:
- Listening identification (5 exercises)

   - Audio examples: "bai", "dai", "gai"

   - Exercises: Listening, repetition, minimal pairs

3. **Nasals**: /m/, /n/, /ng/

### Lesson 5: Consonant Assimilation
**Duration**: 15 minutes  
**Objectives**: Understand consonant changes in connected speech

**Content**:
1. **Voicing Assimilation**
   - Voiceless consonants become voiced before voiced consonants
   - Audio examples: "pab" vs "pad"
   - Practice: Recognizing voicing changes

2. **Place Assimilation**
   - Consonants change place to match following consonant
   - Audio examples: "pam", "pan", "pang"
   - Practice: Recognizing place changes

3. **Manner Assimilation**
   - Consonants change manner to match following consonant
   - Audio examples: Various combinations
   - Practice: Recognizing manner changes

4. **Nasal Assimilation**
   - Nasals assimilate to place of following consonant
   - Audio examples: "pam", "pan", "pang"
   - Practice: Recognizing nasal assimilation

5. **Connected Speech Patterns**
   - How consonants change in phrases and sentences
   - Audio examples: Natural speech
   - Practice: Understanding natural pronunciation

**Exercises**:
- Listening identification (10 exercises)
- Assimilation recognition (10 exercises)
- Minimal pairs (10 exercises)

- Dictation (5 exercises)
