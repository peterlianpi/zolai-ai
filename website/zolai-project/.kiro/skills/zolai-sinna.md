# Zolai Sinna — Tedim Zolai Literacy Primer (ZAUS, 2010)

Source: https://archive.org/stream/siamsin/Zolai%20Sinna%20inside%20proved_djvu.txt
Full reference: `.kiro/resources/zolai-sinna.md`

## Orthography

**Consonants:** b, c, d, g, h, k, l, m, n, p, s, t, v, z
**Clusters:** kh, ng, ph, th
**NOT in Zolai:** f, j, q, r, x, y
**Final consonants:** k, l, m, n, ng, p, t
**Vowels:** a, e, i, o, u, aw
**Diphthongs:** ei, eu, ia, iu, oi, ou, ua, ui, awi
**Triphthongs:** uau, iai, uai, iau

## Phonological Rules (authoritative)

1. `o` = /oʊ/ always — `o pen ou bang in awsuak hi`
2. No `ti` clusters — `t le i kigawm ngeilo hi`
3. `c` never precedes a, e, o, aw — `C le a,e,o,aw te kigawm ngeilo hi`

## Grammar

**Prefixes:** `ki-` (reciprocal), `pi-` (causative)
**Key suffixes:** -bawl (do), -dan (manner), -huai (worthy), -kha (done), -khia (out), -pi (big), -pih (help), -sak (causative), -san (leave), -sat (decide)
**Combined:** ki-sak, ki-pih, ki-khia, ki-khial, ki-bawl

## Proverbs (Paunak)

1. Buipi leikei zong khua ngai — Even a big fish longs for home
2. Deklam tuktum zong galsuak — Even a short sword can win
3. Hakai pahtak lung heeng — A forgiving heart heals quickly
4. Khaikha a gui ah gah — Fruit falls in the tree's shadow (children reflect parents)
5. Sialtat nung a sialdai kai — Young bull follows old bull's tracks

## Similes (Tehpih Kam — end with "bang")

1. Vot si dawn bang — like a bat at dusk (restless)
2. Ai mitdeh bang — like a blind eye (unaware)
3. Baakvat bang — like a bat's wing (fragile)
4. Pasan sialnek bang — like a tiger eating deer (swift/powerful)
5. Sialpi vaikuan bang — like a bull in a pen (confined but powerful)

## Poetic Words (Lakam)

Reversed: sakhi→khisa, vakhu→khuva, vaphual→phualva
Compound: sam→besam, suang→lumsuang, tem→namtem
Unrelated: nu→tun, dawi→zinleng, inn→saumang

## Punctuation

. tawpna | , husanna | : ngakna | - thekna | ' tanglak | () kual | / git-awn

## Cultural Notes

- **Vaphual** (dove): sacred Zomi symbol, marital fidelity, adopted as national symbol 1980
- **February 20**: Zomi National Day (Zomi Namni)
- **Rev. J.H. Cope** (1882–1938): created Roman alphabet for Zolai, arrived Tedim 1910
- **France Gam Pai**: 1000 Zomi men sent to WWI France (1917); return sparked Christian growth
