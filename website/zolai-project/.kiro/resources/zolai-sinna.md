## About

Zolai Sinna ("Learn Zolai") is a 34-lesson Tedim Zolai literacy primer created by the
Zomi Association of USA (ZAUS) in 2010. It was originally developed for use in
Kalay Valley Baptist Association (KVBA) / UNICEF Child Rights projects in Myanmar,
and released online for free use by Zomi communities worldwide.

The primer covers the complete Roman-alphabet orthography of Tedim Zolai,
progressing from basic syllables through complex consonant clusters, diphthongs,
triphthongs, grammar, proverbs, poetry, and cultural texts.

---

## Orthography (from Sinna 1–21)

### Consonants (Laimung)
b, c, d, g, h, k, l, m, n, p, s, t, v, z

### Consonant Clusters (Nihkop Laimung)
kh, ng, ph, th

### Consonants NOT in Zolai
f, j, q, r, x, y

### Final Consonants (Laimung Dawn)
k, l, m, n, ng, p, t

### Vowels (Awphei)
a, e, i, o, u, aw

### Diphthongs (Awpheikop)
ei, eu, ia, iu, oi, ou, ua, ui, awi

### Triphthongs (Awphei-thumthuap)
uau, iai, uai, iau

## Phonological Rules (from Sinna 1)

1. **o = /oʊ/** — `o` is always pronounced like "ou" (diphthong), never pure /o/
   - Rule stated: `*o pen ou bang in awsuak hi`
2. **No ti clusters** — `t` and `i` never combine: `*t le i kigawm ngeilo hi`
3. **c + {a,e,o,aw} forbidden** — `c` never precedes a, e, o, aw: `*C le a,e,o,aw te kigawm ngeilo hi`

---

## Syllable Structure (Sinna 22)

When a word has more than one syllable, syllables are written together (not hyphenated):
- Sa+khi = Sakhi (not Sa-khi)
- To+pa = Topa
- Pa+sian = Pasian
- Van+gam = Vangam

---

## Hyphen Usage (Sinna 28)

Hyphens (thekna) are used for:
1. Long compound words: `sakhi-tuihup`, `pheituam-sungten`
2. Culturally compound terms: `annek-tuidawn`, `gilkial-dangtak`
3. Prefix/suffix separation where meaning could be confused

---

## Prefixes (Mathuap) — Sinna 25

- `ki-` (reflexive/reciprocal): kideih, kiho, kimawl, kium, ki-it, ki-at, ki-en
- `pi-` (causative/intensive): pibawl, picing, pimun, pingaihsut

## Proverbs (Paunak) — Sinna 30

1. Buipi leikei zong khua ngai — Even a big fish longs for home (home is always dear)
2. Deklam tuktum zong galsuak — Even a short sword can win a battle (size doesn't determine outcome)
3. Hakai pahtak lung heeng — A heart that forgives quickly heals
4. Khaikha a gui ah gah — The fruit falls in the shadow of the tree (children reflect parents)
5. Sialtat nung a sialdai kai — The young bull follows the old bull's tracks (youth follow elders)

---

## Similes (Tehpih Kam) — Sinna 31

Similes end with "bang" (like/as):
1. Vot si dawn bang — like a bat at dusk (restless, erratic)
2. Ai mitdeh bang — like a blind person's eye (unseeing, unaware)
3. Baakvat bang — like a bat's wing (thin, fragile)
4. Pasan sialnek bang — like a tiger eating a deer (swift, powerful)
5. Sialpi vaikuan bang — like a big bull in a pen (confined but powerful)

## Poetic Vocabulary (Lakam) — Sinna 32

Lakam are poetic/literary words used in songs, distinct from everyday speech.

### Reversed syllable lakam
sakhi → khisa, vakhu → khuva, vaphual → phualva

### Compound lakam
sam → besam, suang → lumsuang, tem → namtem

### Unrelated poetic words
nu → tun, dawi → zinleng, inn → saumang

## Name Writing Rules (Min Gelh Zia) — Sinna 34

1. **Minpi** (surname): capitalize each syllable
2. **Minno** (given name): capitalize each syllable
3. **Minsel** (nickname): capitalize — e.g. Lianpupa, Lamvungnu
4. Occupational titles: capitalize — Hausapa, Siavuanpa
5. Place names: capitalize — Tedim, Tualzang, Tungtuang, Lawibual
6. Animal names: capitalize — Zawhngeu, Sahang, Humpi-nelkai
7. Disease names: capitalize — mehthuk, tutphah, puanlaisan
8. Clan names: capitalize — Hatlang, Ngaihte, Hautual, Gualnam
9. Abstract nouns: capitalize — deihna, pilna, tupna, hauhna, cidamna

## Cultural Content

### Vaphual (Dove) — Cultural Symbol
The vaphual (dove) is a sacred bird in Zomi culture:
- Considered pure and faithful — mates for life; if one dies, the other dies too
- Used as a symbol of marital fidelity
- In 1980, adopted as the Zomi national symbol on Myanmar National Day
- Women traditionally wore vaphual feathers in their hair

### ABC Poem (Laimal Kim)
Each letter of the alphabet is paired with a Zolai cultural phrase:
- A: Akkhuangtathawhhunta
- F: February 20 ni Zomi Namni (February 20 = Zomi National Day)
- T: Tedim pau ei ma pau (Tedim language is our language)
- Z: Zomi te hanlung ciam khangto diam (May the Zomi people rise and prosper)

### Rev. Joseph Herbert Cope (1882–1938)
- American Baptist missionary who arrived in Tedim, November 1, 1910
- Created the Roman alphabet orthography for Zolai
- Published first Zolai literacy book "Tual Lai-sintawmna Bu" (1913)
- Published first Tedim Bible New Testament (1932)
- Published "Tedim Thukizakna Lai" journal (1919–1938)
- Produced 35+ books in Tedim, Falam, Haka, and Kanpetlet languages
- Received Kaiser-i-Hind medal (1927) and D.D. from Colgate University (1935)

### Zomi in World War I (France Gam Pai)
- 1000 Zomi men sent as laborers (Kuli) to France in 1917
- Departed from Gunkhawm, traveled via Kolkata, Mumbai, Suez Canal to Marseilles
- 24 died in France, buried in Marseilles war cemetery
- Upon return, brought knowledge of Western clothing, money economy
- Contributed to rapid growth of Christianity in Zogam (500 Christians in 1918 → 4,046 by 1924)
