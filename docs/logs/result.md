- **Sai le A Khihna (The Elephant and its tying rope)**
  - **Storyline:** A small rope ties a huge elephant. Though it can easily break free now, it was tied with the same rope when it was a baby. It couldn't break it then, so it doesn't even try now, believing it's impossible.
  - **Metaphorical Meaning:** People are held back by their past failures and self-limiting beliefs. Losing hope and lack of effort keep us tied to bad habits or false assumptions, preventing us from succeeding and gaining true freedom.

- **Muvanlai le Akno (The Eagle and the Chick)**
  - **Storyline:** An eagle chick is raised among normal chickens. It grows up acting and thinking exactly like a chicken. Even when it sees eagles flying gracefully in the sky and admires them, it never flies because it believes it's just a chicken.
  - **Metaphorical Meaning:** The environment and the people we surround ourselves with heavily influence our potential. If we don't realize our own true identity and capability, we will fail to reach our full potential, being limited by our peers.

- **Kau le A Bu (The Locust/Insect and its Nest)**
  - **Storyline:** A locust/insect struggles repeatedly to break out of its nest. Once a small hole is made, it can fly away. But if someone out of pity breaks the nest open to help it, the insect fails to fly. The struggle to break the nest is what strengthens its wings and gives it the power to fly.
  - **Metaphorical Meaning:** Our hardships and struggles are necessary to build our strength, courage, and resilience so we can reach greater heights.

- **Seng Tawh Tui Tawi (Carrying water with a basket)**
  - **Storyline:** A father asks his son to fetch water using a dirty bamboo basket. Every time the son fetches water, it leaks out completely. Despite repeated failed attempts to carry water, the father points out that the dirty basket is now clean.
  - **Metaphorical Meaning:** Continuous effort is never wasted. Even if we read the Bible or learn something and don't seem to retain or understand it fully, the process itself cleanses and purifies our mind and thoughts.

- **Singphuukpa le Hei (The Woodcutter and the Axe)**
  - **Storyline:** (Combines 13 & 14) A woodcutter cutting trees with a blunt axe tires out and chops fewer trees over time. Another woodcutter takes time to sharpen his axe first. Though it takes initial time to prepare, he ends up cutting trees much faster and more easily.
  - **Metaphorical Meaning:** Preparation and prioritizing important tasks are crucial. Taking time to prepare ("sharpening the axe") saves time in the long run and brings success. Wasting time on unimportant things instead of tackling the core issues causes failure.

- **Suangtang Vom le Kang (The Black and White Stones)**
  - **Storyline:** A man is heavily in debt to a rich man. The rich man says he will forgive the debt if he can marry the man's daughter, offering a game of chance: pick a white stone from a bag to forgive the debt and keep the daughter, or a black stone to forgive the debt and marry the daughter. The rich man cheats by putting two black stones in the bag. The father realizes this. If he exposes the cheat, he still might have to pay his debt or lose his daughter. Instead, he drops the drawn stone and says, "Look at the remaining stone in the bag to know what color I picked." Since the remaining one is black, the rich man has to admit the "drawn" one was white to hide his cheating.
  - **Metaphorical Meaning:** Having good critical thinking and maintaining composure in difficult situations can turn bad situations into an advantage. Instead of panicking over unfair problems, finding creative solutions brings victory.

- **Hehna le Siktukilh (Anger and the Nail)**
  - **Storyline:** A father tells his angry son to hammer a nail into a wooden board every time he gets angry. When the boy learns to control his anger, the father tells him to pull the nails out. Though the nails are gone, the holes remain in the wood forever.
  - **Metaphorical Meaning:** Words spoken in anger or frustration leave permanent scars on others' hearts, even after you apologize or regret them. It is critical to control our anger and be careful with our words.

- **Thangho le Liando (Famous orphan brothers tale)**
  - **Storyline:** Thangho and Liando are two brother orphans facing extreme poverty and hardship. When they barely had any food to eat, they would carefully divide a single grain of corn evenly between them to share.
  - **Metaphorical Meaning:** True love is about sharing and caring for one another even in times of extreme lack. You don't need to have excess wealth to help or give to others where true love exists.
