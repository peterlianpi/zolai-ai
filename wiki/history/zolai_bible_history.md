# Zolai Bible Translation History

The translation of the Bible into Zolai (Tedim) has a rich history, evolving from early colonial efforts to modern linguistic standards.

## Key Versions

| Year | Version Name | Description | Key Features |
|------|--------------|-------------|--------------|
| 1932 | Tedim 1932 | Early translation into the Tedim dialect. | Older orthography, foundational for the community. |
| 1977 | TDB77 / ZVS | Tedim Bible 1977. | Widely used in churches; bridges traditional and modern Zolai. |
| 2010 | Tedim 2010 | Modern revision reflecting current linguistic standards. | Updated vocabulary and grammar for the contemporary "Second Brain" architecture. |
| 2018 | Zokam Standard Version (ZVS) | The current gold standard for the Lung-Kha architecture. | Strict adherence to Verb Stem II shifts and theological mapping (Heart/Spirit). |

## Linguistic Evolution

The Zolai language has moved toward a more standardized "Zokam" form, which is reflected in the 2018 ZVS. This version is the primary source for the AI's core logic, while 1977 and 2010 provide historical and stylistic breadth for the corpus.

## Important Translators
- **J.H. Cope**: A foundational figure in Zomi literacy and early translation.
- **Local Language Committees**: Modern versions are the result of collective efforts by the Zokam/Tedim linguistic communities to preserve the "Lung-Kha" (Heart-Spirit) psycholinguistic depth of the language.
