# ZVS 2018 Linguistic Survey: Poetic & Wisdom Books (Batch 3)

## Overview
The Poetic Books (Job through Song of Solomon) represent the pinnacle of Zolai metaphorical expression and emotional depth. This batch is essential for teaching B2-C1 level Zolai, focusing on abstract reasoning, lyricism, and sophisticated parallelism.

## 1. Book-Specific Patterns

### Job (Job) - Dialectic & Suffering (C1 Level)
- **Complex Metaphors:** Intensive use of nature imagery to describe God's power (`Humpinelkai`, `Tuipi`, `Aksi`).
- **Legal/Disputational Tone:** Job's pleas for a trial (`Thukhenna`, `Tehci`).
- **Repetitive Questioning:** `na'ng in kua na hi hiam...` (Who are you that...).

### Psalms (Late) - Lyrical & Emotive (B2-C1 Level)
- **Parallelism:** The "echo" effect. `Topa aw, na heh laitak hong tai kei in la, lungso sa-in hong thuhilh kei in` (O Lord, rebuke me not in thine anger, neither chasten me in thy hot displeasure).
- **Nature as Sanctuary:** `Topa in ka suangpi, ka munkip...` (The Lord is my rock, my fortress).
- **Vocative Appeals:** Frequent use of `Topa aw` and `Ka Pasian aw` to initiate prayer units.

### Proverbs (Paunak) - Aphoristic Wisdom (B1-B2 Level)
- **Antithetic Parallelism:** Juxtaposing the wise (`Mipil`) and the fool (`Mihai`).
- **Ethical Imperatives:** `Zui in`, `Ngai in`, `Mangsak kei in`.
- **Cause-Effect Structures:** `Tua hi leh ...` (Then/So that ...).
- **Personification:** `Pilna` (Wisdom) personified as a woman calling in the streets.

### Ecclesiastes (Thuhilhna) - Existential & Cyclical (B2-C1 Level)
- **The "Vanity" Motto:** `A mawknapi, a mawknapi` (Vanity of vanities).
- **Cyclical Time Formulas:** `... hun om a ...` (There is a time for...). E.g., `Suah hun om a, sih hun om hi`.
- **Existential Cynicism:** `Leitungah a thak na khat beek om lo hi` (There is nothing new under the sun).

### Song of Solomon (Solomon La) - Descriptive & Romantic (B2 Level)
- **Physical Metaphors:** `Na mit vahui mit tawh kibang hi` (Your eyes are like doves' eyes).
- **Sensory Language:** High density of smells (`Namtui`), tastes (`Khum`), and textures (`Neel`).
- **Intimate Dialogue:** `Ngaihno aw`, `It luat aw`.

## 2. Grammatical Features for Curriculum

### Level B2: Metaphorical Competence
- **Similes:** Using `... bangin` and `... tawh kibang` for complex comparisons.
- **Passive/State Markers:** `... a kiciamteh hi` (is recorded), `... a kibawl hi` (is made).

### Level C1: Abstract Reasoning
- **Existential Conditionals:** `... a hih leh ...`, `... a hih kei leh ...`.
- **Dialectical Conjunctions:** `Ahih hangin`, `Tua ahih ciangin`, `Bang hang hiam cih leh`.

## 3. Key Vocabulary Domains
- **Nature/Cosmos:** `Suangpi`, `Gun`, `Mual`, `Aksi`, `Kha`, `Ni`.
- **Emotion:** `Nuam`, `Dah`, `Lungkham`, `Lungdam`, `Heh`.
- **Wisdom:** `Pilna`, `Theihtelna`, `Siamna`, `Haina`.

## 4. Pedagogical Application
Utilize the "Time for Everything" passage (Eccl 3) for B1 level verb/noun pairs. Use Psalms for B2-C1 level emotional expression and worship vocabulary.
