# Epistles & Revelation Linguistic Survey (Romans - Revelation)

This document details the propositional logic, corrective ethics, and apocalyptic imagery of the Epistles and the book of Revelation (Batch 7), representing the peak of linguistic complexity in the Zolai Bible.

## Overview
The Epistles (Romans through Jude) provide the **logical and theological framework** for the Christian faith, while Revelation provides a **highly symbolic, visionary conclusion**. These texts are ideal for **B2 (Intermediate-High) to C1 (Advanced)** learners.

---

## 1. Romans (Propositional Logic & Argumentation)
Romans is a masterpiece of logical Zolai, featuring dense argumentation and systematic proof.

### Key Linguistic Patterns
*   **Logical Conjunctions:** `Tua ahih ciangin` (Therefore), `Ahi zongin` (But/However), `Bang hang hiam cih leh` (For/Because).
*   **The "If... Then" Logic:** `...hong om leh...` (If... dwells/comes), `...i thuak nak leh...` (If we suffer...).
*   **Theological Definitions:** Mapping concepts like `Silsiangna` (Justification) and `Hehpihna` (Grace) into structured sentences.

### Pedagogical Utility
- **Level:** C1 (Advanced)
- **Focus:** Building complex paragraphs, logical sequencing, and formal debate.

---

## 2. 1 Corinthians (Practical Ethics & Analogies)
The Corinthian letters focus on social correction and use analogies to explain spiritual truths.

### Key Linguistic Patterns
*   **Analogy Frames:** `Naupang ka hih hun lai-in...` (When I was a child...).
*   **Ethical Priority:** Using `Itna` (Love) as the ultimate qualifier for actions.
    *   *Example:* `Itna ka neih kei leh...` (If I have not love...).
*   **Metaphysical Meta-talk:** `Dal limlang sunga et bangin...` (As through a mirror, darkly...).

### Pedagogical Utility
- **Level:** B2-C1
- **Focus:** Conditional ethics, social guidance, and using "as if"/analogical reasoning.

---

## 3. Hebrews & General Epistles (Comparative Logic)
Hebrews is characterized by its "Better Than" comparative structure, essential for mapping hierarchies.

### Key Linguistic Patterns
*   **The Superiority Formula:** `...sangin a hoihzaw hi.` (...is better/superior to...).
*   **Legacy Mapping:** Referencing OT history as proof (`Upna hangin Abraham in...`).

### Pedagogical Utility
- **Level:** B2
- **Focus:** Comparisons, historical referencing, and superlative structures.

---

## 4. Revelation (Apocalyptic Visual Logic)
Revelation uses high-level visionary vocabulary and "I saw" narrative frames.

### Key Linguistic Patterns
*   **Visionary Marker:** `...ka mu hi.` (I saw...), `Hih thuteng khit ciangin ka khuadak leh...` (After these things I looked...).
*   **Celestial Visuals:** `Tokhom` (Throne), `Lukhute` (Crowns), `Vantungmaingam` (Heavenly realm).
*   **Doxology:** `Nangmah ... na ngah tawntung dingin a kilawm hi.` (You are worthy to receive...).

### Pedagogical Utility
- **Level:** B2-C1 (Imagination/Symbolism)
- **Focus:** Descriptive visionary language, cosmic scale, and formal adoration.
