# Gospels & Acts Linguistic Survey (Matthew - Acts)

This document details the linguistic patterns, narrative structures, and pedagogical utility of the four Gospels and the book of Acts (Batch 6) for the Zolai AI curriculum.

## Overview
The Gospels and Acts represent a transition from the Old Testament's grand rhetoric to **direct narrative, theological discourse, and early institutional history**. They are vital for **A2 (Beginner-High) to C1 (Advanced)** levels.

---

## 1. Matthew (Pedagogy & Law)
Matthew focuses on Jesus as the teacher and the fulfillment of the Law. It is the bridge between OT and NT.

### Key Linguistic Patterns
*   **The Beatitudes Formula:** `Thupha ngah mi na hi-te hi...` (Blessed are you/ye...).
*   **Metaphorical Identity:** `Leitung ci` (Salt of the earth), `Leitung khuavak` (Light of the world).
*   **Synthesis of Old and New:** `Moses Thukham... ka phiat dingin a hong pai hiloin...` (I came not to destroy the law...).

### Pedagogical Utility
- **Level:** B1-B2
- **Focus:** Moral instruction, definitions of identity, and continuity.

---

## 2. Luke (Storytelling & Parables)
Luke contains the most famous parables, making it the primary source for narrative Zolai.

### Key Linguistic Patterns
*   **The Parable Intro:** `Jesuh in thugentehna khat gen leuleu a...` (Jesus told another parable...).
*   **Narrative Sequence:** Clear "Once upon a time" style frameworks (`Mi khat in tapa nih a nei hi` - *A certain man had two sons*).
*   **Social Compassion:** Vocabulary for mercy (`Hehpihna`), the poor (`Mizawng`), and outcasts.

### Pedagogical Utility
- **Level:** B1 (Storytelling/Narrative)
- **Focus:** Developing past-tense narrative skills and character descriptions.

---

## 3. John (High Theological Discourse)
John is linguistically distinct, focusing on philosophical concepts and the "I Am" statements.

### Key Linguistic Patterns
*   **The "I Am" Statements:** `Keimah pen ... ka hi hi` (I am the...).
    *   *Examples:* `Nuntakna an` (Bread of life), `Khuavak` (Light), `Lam` (Way).
*   **Deep Contrast:** `Khuamial` (Darkness) vs `Khuavak` (Light).
*   **Theological Definitions:** `A kipatcil-in Thu a om hi.` (In the beginning was the Word).

### Pedagogical Utility
- **Level:** B2-C1
- **Focus:** Identifying abstract concepts, metaphors for existence, and Christological grammar.

---

## 4. Acts (Institutional & Missionary Narrative)
Acts tracks the spread of the church, introducing administrative and travel vocabulary.

### Key Linguistic Patterns
*   **Institutional Terms:** `Pawlpi` (Church), `Sawltakte` (Apostles), `Upa` (Elder).
*   **Empowerment Logic:** `Kha Siangtho na ngah ding uha...` (You shall receive the Holy Spirit...).
*   **Travelogue and Mission:** Describing the spread across regions (`Judea`, `Samaria`, `leimong dong`).

### Pedagogical Utility
- **Level:** B2 (Administrative/Institutional)
- **Focus:** Discussing organizational growth, community reports, and history.
