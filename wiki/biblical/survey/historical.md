# ZVS 2018 Linguistic Survey: Historical Books (Batch 2)

## Overview
The Historical Books (Joshua through Esther) provide a transition from the legal/instructional prose of the Pentateuch to dynamic narrative, royal rhetoric, and administrative documentation. This batch is critical for teaching B1-B2 level Zolai, focusing on history, leadership, and institutional management.

## 1. Book-Specific Patterns

### Joshua (Joshua) - War & Territorial Conquest
- **Military Commands:** `Hang un la, namsau tawi un` (Be strong and take up the sword).
- **Territorial Logic:** Use of boundary markers `... gamgi ...` and distribution logic `... tan pia hi` (gave as a portion).
- **Covenantal Climax:** `na'ng leh na innkuante in kua na bia ding na hi-te hiam? Kei leh ka innkuante in ahih leh Topa ka bia ding-te hi` (Choose you this day whom ye will serve... but as for me and my house, we will serve the Lord).

### Judges (Thukhente) - Cyclical Narratives (B1 Level)
- **The Sin Formula:** `Israel mite Topa muhna-ah a sia gamta-te hi` (Israelites did evil in the sight of the Lord).
- **The Delivery Formula:** `Topa in honpa khat ... khangsak hi` (The Lord raised up a deliverer).
- **Chronological Transitions:** `... sih khit ciangin ...` (after the death of...).

### Ruth (Ruth) - Pastoral & Social Logic (A2-B1 Level)
- **Intimate Dialogue:** `na'ng na paina-ah ka pai ding a...` (Whither thou goest, I will go).
- **Social Obligation:** `Tanau nai ka hihna man hi` (Because I am a near kinsman).
- **Grace/Favor:** `Maipha ka muhna peuhpeuhpa’ nungah...` (In whose sight I shall find grace).

### 1 & 2 Samuel - Royal Transition & Kingship (B2 Level)
- **Institutional Requests:** `Kote hong uk dingin kumpi khat seh in` (Appoint for us a king to judge us).
- **Prophetic Calling:** `Samuel! Samuel! ... Hih lai-ah om ing!` (Samuel! Samuel! ... Here am I!).
- **Standard Address:** `Kumpipa nungta tawntung in` (Long live the king).

### Ezra & Nehemiah - Administrative & Restoration Prose (B2 level)
- **Official Inventories:** `Kham kuang sawmthum, ngun kuang tulkhat...` (30 gold basins, 1000 silver basins...).
- **Legal Authorization:** `Persia kumpipa ... lungsim ah Topa in suak sak hi` (... stirred the spirit of the King of Persia).
- **Architectural Terms:** `Kulh`, `Kongpi`, `Zawlte`, `Khawm`, `Phatkik` (Wall, Gate, Beams, Repair, Rebuild).
- **First-Person Leadership:** Nehemiah's personal prayers and reflections (`Ka Pasian aw, hih mite’ adingin ... hong phawk in` - Remember me, my God, for good).

## 2. Grammatical Features for Curriculum

### Level B1: Narrative Flow
- **Sequential Conjunctions:** `Tua ahih ciangin`, `Tua ciangin`, `Ahih hangin`.
- **Negation in Clauses:** `... a hih loh nadingin` (so that ... may not be).

### Level B2: Official & Formal Language
- **Honorifics/Titles:** `Ukpa`, `Menzipipa`, `Lai-atpa`, `Siampi Lian`.
- **Conditional Negation:** `... a hih kei leh ...` (If not ... / or else ...).
- **Passive-like Constructions:** `... a kiciamteh hi` (is recorded/registered).

## 3. Key Vocabulary Domains
- **War/Military:** `Gal`, `Galkap`, `Namsau`, `Teipi`, `LUM` (Shield).
- **Governance:** `Kumpipa`, `Ukna`, `Siah`, `Thukhen`.
- **Construction:** `Kulh`, `Suangseekpa`, `Khamseekpa`, `Sing`.

## 4. Pedagogical Application
Extract "Nehemiah's Leadership Quotes" for B2 level ethical discussion. Use "Ruth's Vows" for B1 level relationship/pastoral vocabulary.
