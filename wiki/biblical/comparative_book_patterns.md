# Comparative Biblical Sentence Patterns (A1-B2)

This document maps authoritative Zolai sentence structures from the Bible to English equivalents and proficiency levels.

---

## 🟢 Level A1-A2: Narrative Patterns
**Books:** Genesis, Exodus (History), Gospels (Matthew, Mark, Luke, John).
**Focus:** Telling what happened, who said what, and temporal sequences.

### 1. Temporal Sequential Markers
- **Pattern:** `Tua ciangin ...` / `Tua ahih ciangin ...`
- **English:** And then / And it came to pass.
- **Example:** `Tua ciangin Josef ... si hi.` (Gen 50:26)
- **Nuance:** `Tua ciangin` is for direct sequence; `Tua ahih ciangin` often marks a resultative transition.

### 2. Dialogue Tags
- **Pattern:** `[Subject] in ... ci hi / dong hi / dawng hi`
- **English:** [Subject] said / asked / replied.
- **Example:** `Pasian in ... "Khuavak om ta hen," ci hi.` (Gen 1:3)
- **Nuance:** Zolai dialogue follows the direct quote, with the verb usually at the very end.

### 3. Presence & Existentials
- **Pattern:** `... om hi` / `... ahi hi`
- **English:** There is / It is.
- **Example:** `Tui thukpi tungah khuamial om hi.` (Gen 1:2)
- **English:** There was darkness upon the deep.

## 🟡 Level B1: Instructional & Emotive Patterns
**Books:** Psalms, Parables of Jesus (Matthew, Luke).
**Focus:** Simple teaching, emotions, and moral qualities.

### 1. Parables (Teaching with Logic)
- **Pattern:** `... pen ... tawh kibang hi`
- **English:** ... is like ....
- **Example:** `Amah pen ... singkung tawh kibang hi.` (Psa 1:3)
- **English:** He shall be like a tree.

### 2. Emotive Invocations
- **Pattern:** `[Subject] aw ...`
- **English:** O [Subject] ...
- **Example:** `Topa aw, kong paunate hong ngai in.` (Psa 5:1)
- **Nuance:** The `aw` particle is essential for vocative/poetic address in prayer.

### 3. Resultative Finished State
- **Pattern:** `[Verb]sa ...`
- **English:** [Verb]ed (as an adjective).
- **Example:** `Nangma bawlsa, kha le aksite ...` (Psa 8:3)
- **English:** The moon and stars, which you have made.

## 🟠 Level B2-B2+: Logical & Theological Patterns
**Books:** Romans, Galatians, Hebrews.
**Focus:** Complex reasoning, causation, and formal negation.

### 1. Subordinate Reasoning
- **Pattern:** `... bang hang hiam cih leh ...`
- **English:** ... because ... / ... for ....
- **Example:** `... bang hang hiam cih leh Pasian in amah hong it hi.`
- **Nuance:** This is the standard high-register way to start an explanation in Romans.

### 2. Complex Conditionals & Contrast
- **Pattern:** `... ahih manin` (Therefore) / `... ahih hangin` (However).
- **English:** Therefore / But.
- **Example:** `Tua ahih manin midangte a mawhsak mi aw ...` (Rom 2:1)
- **Nuance:** Essential for following the "logical chain" of Pauline theology.

### 3. Subject-Dependent Negation ("Unless")
- **Pattern (2nd Person):** `... na hih kei leh`
- **Pattern (3rd Person):** `... a hih loh leh`
- **Example:** `Tua bang na hih kei leh amah in na'ng zong a hong satkhia ding hi.` (Rom 11:22)
- **English:** Otherwise [Unless you do so], he also will cut you off.

### 4. Passive & Reciprocal Action
- **Pattern:** `Prefix ki-` + `Verb`
- **English:** [Be] [Verb]ed / To each other.
- **Example:** `Upna tawh khat le khat tha i kipiaktuah ...` (Rom 1:12)
- **English:** Encouraged by each other's faith.

## 🔴 Genre-Specific Highlights

### Law & Commands (Exodus/Leviticus)
- **Strong Future Imperative:** `Na that ding uh hi.` (Thou shalt kill).
- **Prohibition:** `Nong hih ding hi lo hi.` (Thou shalt not).

### Apocalyptic Foreshadowing (Revelation)
- **Visionary Marker:** `Tua khit ciangin ka en a ...` (After this I looked).
- **Passive Result:** `... a kigelh sa` (that which was written).
