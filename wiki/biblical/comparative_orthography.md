# Comparative Orthography: Zolai Bible Versions

This document captures the stylistic and orthographic shifts between the Traditional (TDB77) and Modern (ZVS 2018) Tedim Zolai Bibles.

## 1. Theological Nomenclature

| Term | Traditional (TDB77) | Modern (ZVS 2018) | Note |
|------|---------------------|-------------------|------|
| **Jesus** | Zeisu | Jesuh | Shift from 'Z' to 'J' and added 'h' suffix. |
| **Christ** | Khazih | Khrih | Modern version uses the international 'Khrih'. |
| **God** | Pasian | Pasian | Stable across versions. |
| **Spirit** | Kha | Kha | Stable. |

## 2. Pronominal and Particle Shifts

- **Self-Reference:** TDB77 uses `ko` (we) more frequently in formal headers, while ZVS uses `kote` or `eite` for clarity.
- **Transitive Markers:** ZVS 2018 strictly enforces the ergative `in` (e.g., *Pasian in*), whereas TDB77 occasionally drops or merges them in rhythmic prose.

## 3. Stylistic Comparison (1 John 1:1)

### Traditional (TDB77)
> "A cil panin piang a, komau in za-in, ka mittang uh tawh mu-in, a tel takpi enin, ka khut uh tawh ka lawn’ khitsa uh nuntakna Thu tawh kipawl thute..."

- **Tone:** Rhythmic, heavy use of `-in` for coordination.
- **Archaic terms:** *A cil panin* (from the start/origin).

### Modern (ZVS 2018)
> "Leitung pian'cil lai peka kipanin a omsa ahi nuntakna a piathei Thu tawh kisai, note kiangah ka hong gelh uh hi."

- **Tone:** Descriptive, formal literary style.
- **Modern terms:** *Pian'cil lai peka* (from the very beginning of creation).

## 4. Orthographic Rules Identified
1. **'Z' vs 'J':** Older texts use 'Z' for names like *Zeisu* (Burmese influence), while 2018 Standard uses 'J' (*Jesuh*).
2. **'-h' Suffix:** Modern ZVS favors 'h' suffixes to indicate specific phonemes (e.g., *Jesuh*, *Khrih*).
3. **Punctuation:** Modern ZVS uses the apostrophe `'` more precisely for glottal stops or elisions (*pian'cil*).
