# Zolai History and Zomi Origins

## The Zomi People
The Zomi (also known as Chin, Kuki, Lushai, Paite, etc., depending on regional and colonial classifications) are a Tibeto-Burman ethnic group inhabiting the mountainous regions of northwestern Myanmar (Chin State), northeastern India (Manipur, Mizoram), and southeastern Bangladesh.

## Origins and Migration
*   **Ciimnuai:** A legendary ancestral settlement of the Zomi people, often regarded as a "cradle of civilization" for many clans.
*   **Yuan Dynasty Connections:** Historical research suggests the Zomi ancestors may have had connections to the regions controlled or influenced by the Yuan Dynasty during their southward migration.
*   **Migration Route:** Generally believed to have migrated from the Tibetan plateau through China into the Chindwin valley and eventually into the Chin Hills.

## Development of Zolai (Tedim)
*   **Oral Tradition:** For centuries, history, laws, and culture were preserved through oral poetry (*Pasaltit*) and songs.
*   **Christianity and Literacy:** The arrival of Christian missionaries (like Arthur Carson and J.H. Cope) in the late 19th and early 20th centuries led to the Romanization of the Zolai script.
*   **J.H. Cope:** Known as "Copepa," he was instrumental in developing the Tedim script and educational materials.

## The Bible as a Linguistic Standard
*   **Judson (1835):** Early translation efforts.
*   **Tedim 1932:** A landmark translation that stabilized many grammatical and spelling conventions.
*   **Zokam Standard Version (ZVS):** The modern standard used for formal and theological writing.

## Key Historical Concepts
*   **Galhiam:** Bravery and victory in battle.
*   **Beh le Phung:** Clan and lineage, the core of Zomi social structure.
*   **Innkuan:** The family unit, extending to the wider clan.
