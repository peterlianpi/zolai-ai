# Zomi Culture and Values

## Core Values

### 1. Itna (Love/Unity)
Zomi culture is deeply communal. `Itna` (Love) is often expressed through `Pumkhatna` (Unity) and `Kipumkhatna` (Staying together as one).

### 2. Galhiam (Bravery)
Historically, Zomi were known for their bravery in protecting their villages and clans. `Galhang` (Brave/Heroic) status was highly esteemed.

### 3. Hehpihna (Mercy/Grace)
A value deeply reinforced by Christianity, `Hehpihna` involves showing kindness to those in need.

## Social Structure

### 1. Beh (Clan)
Every Zomi belongs to a `Beh`. One's lineage defines their identity and social obligations. `Khanggui` (Genealogy) is the study and preservation of this lineage.

### 2. Upate (Elders)
The elders or village leaders (`Hausa` and `Upate`) traditionally made decisions for the community. Respect for elders is a non-negotiable cultural norm.

## Traditions and Festivals

### 1. Khuado
The most significant traditional festival, marking the end of the harvest and the beginning of a new year. It involves:
*   **Pawi:** Celebration/Festival.
*   **Late:** Traditional songs.
*   **Lamin:** Traditional dances.

### 2. Biakna (Worship)
Modern Zomi culture is inextricably linked with Christianity. The church (`Pawlpi`) is now the central hub for both spiritual and social life.

## Taboos and Etiquette
*   **Liansap:** Arrogance or showing off is culturally discouraged.
*   **Kituakna:** Harmony is prioritized; direct confrontation is often avoided in favor of communal resolution.
