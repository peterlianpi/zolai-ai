# Zolai Historical Lineage & Origins

## Concept / Rule
The Zomi people, residing primarily in the Chin State of Myanmar and surrounding areas, trace their history, migration, and the development of their written language (Zolai) through specific historical periods and milestones.

### Key Historical Milestones (AD 1899 - AD 2013)
The modern Zolai script, based on the Latin alphabet, was introduced by Christian missionaries. Prior to this, history and culture were passed down orally through *Zola* (traditional poetry and songs) and *Khanggui* (genealogy/lineage tracking).

1. **Pre-1899 (The Oral Tradition):** Knowledge was preserved exclusively through oral storytelling, memorized genealogies (`Khanggui`), and complex poetic forms. The "Ciimnuai" era represents a central point of origin and dispersal for many Zomi tribes.
2. **AD 1899 (The Arrival of the Script):** The introduction of the Latin-based alphabet by early missionaries (often associated with the arrival of Arthur Carson and Laura Hardin Carson in the Hakha region, and later J.H. Cope in the Tedim region). This marked the transition from an oral to a written culture.
3. **Early 20th Century (Bible Translation & Standardization):** The translation of Christian hymns and the Bible into local dialects (notably the 1932 Tedim Bible by J.H. Cope) heavily standardized the spelling, grammar, and vocabulary of Zolai. These texts became the *de facto* primers for literacy.
4. **Mid-to-Late 20th Century (Literary Expansion):** The formation of literature societies like ZCLS (Zomi Christian Literature Society) and ZOLLS (Zomi Language & Literature Society). These organizations produced the *Zolai Sinbu* (official readers) to standardize education across villages.
5. **AD 2013 & Beyond (Digital Era & the ZVS):** The push for a unified Zomi Standard Version (ZVS) Bible (published around 2018) and the digitization of Zolai dictionaries (like the Tongdot dictionary). This era focuses on resolving dialectal differences and establishing a strict, formal orthography for digital and academic use.

## Decision / Application
When the AI acts as a tutor or fields questions about Zolai history (`culture` domain), it must contextualize the language's development within this missionary-driven transition from oral to written tradition. It should acknowledge that much of "Standard Zolai" is deeply intertwined with biblical orthography.

## Reason
To provide accurate, culturally sensitive, and historically grounded answers, the AI must understand that Zolai is a relatively young written language with deep, ancient oral roots.

## Pattern / Code Snippet
When asked "How old is Zolai?", the AI should respond:
*   "The spoken language and oral traditions (Zola/Khanggui) are ancient, tracing back to Ciimnuai. However, the written script (Zolai) using the Latin alphabet was introduced around AD 1899 by Christian missionaries."

## Mistake / Anti-pattern
Do not assume Zolai has an ancient written script (like Burmese or Chinese). Always distinguish between the ancient oral history and the modern written history (post-1899). Do not ignore the massive influence of the 1932 Tedim Bible on modern spelling conventions.
