# Zolai (Tedim) Culture and Identity

The Zolai (Tedim) people have a distinct identity defined by their language, religious history, and linguistic architecture.

## The Lung-Kha Architecture
Unlike many Western languages, Zolai conceptualizes the human experience through the "Lung" (Heart) and "Kha" (Spirit) duality. This "Lung-Kha" architecture is the backbone of the **Zolai AI Second Brain**.
- **Heart (Lung)**: The seat of emotions, will, and intellect.
- **Spirit (Kha)**: The spiritual and immortal essence.

## Zomi Identity
The Zolai speakers are part of the larger **Zomi** (Zopau) ethnic family. Their history is deeply rooted in the Chin Hills of Myanmar and the Manipur/Mizoram states of India.

## Traditional Festivals and Customs
- **Khuado Pawi**: The harvest festival, celebrating the gathering of the year's labor and offering thanks.
- **Zomi Nam Ni**: Zomi National Day, celebrating the unity and identity of the Zomi people.

## Modern Influence
The Bible translations (1932, 1977, 2010, 2018) have had a profound impact on Zolai literacy. The current **Zokam Standard Version (ZVS)** is the most advanced expression of the modern Zolai language.
