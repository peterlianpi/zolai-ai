# Common AI Mistakes (Zolai Tedim)

Avoid these common mistakes when generating Zolai Tedim.

## 1. Mixing Dialects
- **Mistake:** Using "pasian" (Hakha) for God.
- **Correct:** Always use "Pasian".
- **Also avoid:** `gam` (use `gam`), `tapa` (use `tapa`), `topa` (use `topa`), `???` (use `kumpipa`), `tua`/`tuan` (use `tua`), `nunnak` (use `nuntakna`), `zalenna` (use `suahtakna`)

## 2. Incorrect Greeting Responses
- **Mistake:** Replying "Man ei" when asked "Na dam na?" (How are you?).
- **Correct:** Reply "Dam mah" or "Ka dam hi".

## 3. Invented Phrases
- **Mistake:** "Ka dammah, lungdamna thu" (mixing "I am fine" with "Gospel").
- **Correct:** If asked "Na dam na?", reply "Dam mah". For thanks, say "lungdam".

## 4. Incorrect Verb Stems
- **Mistake:** Using "Nunnak" (Hakha/Falam).
- **Correct:** Always use "Nuntakna".

## 5. "Man" vs "Dik"
- Both are correct for "True/Correct" in Tedim.
- "Man ei" and "Dik ei" are both acceptable for "That's correct/OK".

## 6. Incorrect "freedom"
- **Mistake:** Using "Zalenna".
- **Correct:** Use "Suahtakna".

## 7. Plurality violation `i` + `uh`
- **Mistake:** `I kiphawk mahmah-te ahihman in...`
- **Correct:** `I kiphawk mahmah ahihman in...` ‚Äî drop `uh` when using `i`.
- **Rule:** NEVER combine `uh` with `i`. (Khanggui lines 474‚Äì481)

## 8. Negative conditional `kei a leh` / missing `a` particle
- **Mistake A:** `Nong pai kei a leh...` ‚Äî uses `lo` instead of `kei` (Hakha/Falam form)
- **Mistake B:** `Nong pai kei leh...` ‚Äî missing the required `a` particle before `leh`
- **Correct:** `Nong pai kei a leh...`
- **Rule:** Negative conditionals require `kei a leh`. The `a` particle before `leh` is mandatory. NEVER use `kei a leh`.

## 9. Bad stem nominalizations (corpus-verified)
- `than'na` ‚Üí `than'na` | `kahna` ‚Üí `kahna` | `neina` ‚Üí `neihna`
- `sina` ‚Üí `sihna` | `hauna` ‚Üí `hauhna` | `hakna` ‚Üí `hahna`
- `thatna` ‚Üí `thahna` | `samna` ‚Üí `sapna`

## 10. Sentence-final particles (learned from 2.8M corpus)
- `maw` ‚Äî soft question/confirmation: `Hoih maw?`
- `uhi`/`uh hi` ‚Äî 3rd person plural declarative: `A pai-te hi.`
- `ing` ‚Äî 1st person exclusive future: `Ka pai ing.`
- `ahihi` ‚Äî contracted `ahi hi`
- `diam` ‚Äî soft question: `Na pai ding diam?`
- `cihi` ‚Äî contracted `ci hi` (quotative)
- `hia` ‚Äî informal question variant of `hiam`
- `hang` ‚Äî emphatic/exclamatory: `Hoih mahmah hang!`

## 11. `ciak` ‚Äî multiple valid meanings (NOT just eating)

`ciak` has several distinct uses in Tedim Zolai:

**1. Eating/consuming (food):**
- `An a ciak hi.` ‚Äî He eats food.

**2. Chattering/talking noisily (idle/excessive talk):**
- `A nih vua mawk ciakciak uh.` ‚Äî Those two are just chattering away.
- `Mawk ciakciak kei vo.` ‚Äî Don't just chatter/babble.
- Pattern: `mawk ciakciak` = idle chatter, babbling

**3. Making noise / sizzling / rattling sound:**
- `Na an huan a ciak hi.` ‚Äî Your food in the pot is sizzling.
- `Na phone ciakciak ei.` ‚Äî Your phone is buzzing/ringing repeatedly.
- `Hih van ciakciak veh aw.` ‚Äî This thing is making noise/rattling.

**Rule for reading:** Use `sim` not `ciak`:
- ‚úì `Laisiangtho a sim hi.` ‚Äî He reads the Bible.
- ‚úó `Laisiangtho a ciak hi.` ‚Äî Wrong (implies eating the Bible)

## 12. `van` ‚Äî thing/object/goods AND sky/heaven

**1. Thing / object / goods (most common):**
- `Hih van ciakciak veh aw.` ‚Äî This thing is making noise.
- `Tua van bang hi hiam?` ‚Äî What is that thing?
- Commerce roots: `van lei` (buy) | `van zuak` (sell) | `van man` (price) | `van guang` (freight)

**2. Sky / heaven:**
- `vantung` = heaven (van + tung = sky + above)
- `van awng thawl` = outer space
- `van dum mel` = sky-blue/azure

## 13. `kikhawm` vs `ki mu khawm`
- `kikhawm` = gather/assemble (general)
- `ki mu khawm` = meet together (people coming to meet each other specifically)
- For diplomatic meetings: use `ki mu khawm`
- Example: `US leh Iran te in Pakistan ah ki mu khawm-te hi.`

## 14. `thu a kigen` vs `thu a kikum`
- `thu a kigen uh` = they spoke/said words (general speech)
- `thu a kikum uh` = they deliberated/discussed (weighing matters together)
- For negotiations/consultations: use `thu a kikum`

## 15. `duh lo` vs `deih lo`
- **Correct:** `deih lo` = did not want / refused
- `deih` is the ZVS standard form for "want/desire"
- Example: `A deih lo hi.` (He did not want to.)

## 16. Failed negotiations phrasing
- `thu a kituah lo` = the matter did not happen (too vague)
- `a thu-te a kituak kei hi` = their matter did not come together/agree
- `thukim na a ngah kei-te hi` = they did not obtain agreement (most precise)

## 17. Degrees of leaving/departing
- `a ciah hi` = he left (neutral)
- `a ciah pah hi` = he left immediately/right away
- `a ciah vingveng hi` = he left abruptly/suddenly
- `a ciah kik hi` = he went back/returned

## 18. TDB77 ‚âÝ ZVS
- **TDB77** = Tedim Bible 1977 (traditional, widely used in churches)
- **ZVS** = Zokam Standard Version 2018 (current gold standard for this project)
- These are DIFFERENT versions. Never label TDB77 as ZVS.

## 19. `o` phonetic realization
- The letter `o` in Zolai is always realized as /o ä/ (like English "go")
- Never pronounce as /…í/ (British "hot") or /oÀê/ (pure long o)
- Example: `Hoi` = /ho ä…™/ not /h…î…™/

## 20. `siahuai` vs `lauhuai`
- `lauhuai` = dangerous / scary / fearful (from `lau` = fear)
- `siahuai` = fierce / bad / wicked / evil
- For something dangerous to release/handle: use `lauhuai`
- Example: `A lauhuai lua ahih manin pulaakkhia nuam lo-te hi.` (Too dangerous to release.)

## 21. `release` (product/model) = `pulaakkhia`
- `pulaakkhia` = release/publish/put out for public
- `suakta sak` = cause to come out (also valid)
- Example: `Model thak pulaakkhia-te hi.` (They released a new model.)

## 22. `hong ki beh lap hi` for product launches
- For many things being released/launched: `hong ki beh lap hi`
- Example: `April 2026 sung in AI lam ah model thak tampi hong ki beh lap hi.`
- More natural than `a suah hi` for product release context

## 23. `na sia mahmah hi` for fierce competition
- `na sia mahmah hi` = very fierce/intense
- Example: `Leitung AI lam kidona pen tu-in na sia mahmah hi.`
- More natural than `a that mahmah hi` for competition context

## 24. Ordinal day numbers ‚Äî two valid forms (from Genesis)

**TDB77 (1977) form** ‚Äî uses `-na` suffix:
- `ni khatna` (1st day), `ni nihna` (2nd), `ni thumna` (3rd), `ni lina` (4th), `ni nga-na` (5th), `ni gukna` (6th)

**Tedim Bible 2011 (TDB) form** ‚Äî repeats the number:
- `ni khat ni` (1st day), `ni nih ni` (2nd), `ni thum ni` (3rd), `ni li ni` (4th), `a ni nga ni` (5th), `a ni guk ni` (6th)

Both are correct. TDB77 = 1977 traditional register. Tedim = Tedim Bible 2011 (TDB).

## 25. 3rd person `a` marker ‚Äî Tedim is more consistent

Tedim Bible 2011 (TDB) adds `a` before verbs more consistently than TDB77:
- TDB77: `piang pah hi` | Tedim: `a piang pah hi`
- TDB77: `khen hi` | Tedim: `a khen hi`
- Rule: In Tedim Bible 2011 (TDB), 3rd person singular verb should have `a` prefix.

## 26. `om ta hen` vs `om hen` (benedictive)

- TDB77: `Khuavak om ta hen` (Let there be light ‚Äî with `ta` change-of-state marker)
- Tedim: `Khuavak om hen` (Let there be light ‚Äî without `ta`)
- Both valid. `ta` emphasizes the new state coming into existence.

## 27. Compound words ‚Äî Tedim ZVS prefers compounds

- TDB77: `lim leh meel nei loin` ‚Üí Tedim: `limlemeel neiloin`
- TDB77: `khua mial na` ‚Üí Tedim: `khua mial bikbek` (more descriptive: pitch dark)
- TDB77: `tuisung` ‚Üí Tedim: `tui sung` (separate)
- Tedim Bible 2011 (TDB) tends to compound related words more than TDB77.

## 28. Three Tedim Bible versions (bible.com)

| ID | Abbrev | Full Name | Year | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 368 | TDB | Tedim (Chin) Bible | 2011 | ‚úì In dataset |
| 3561 | TB77 | Tedim Bible | 1977 | ‚úì In dataset |
| 4189 | TBR17 | Tedim Bible Revision 2017 | 2017 | ‚úó Not yet in dataset |

TBR17 (2017) is a revision of TDB (2011) ‚Äî likely the most current standard.
Key differences seen in TBR17 vs TDB:
- Uses `le` instead of `leh` in some places (GEN 1:1: `vantung le leitung`)
- Uses ` º` (Unicode apostrophe) instead of `'`
- `khen khia` (separate) vs `khenkhia` (compound)
- `a bok vakte` (separate) vs `a bokvakte` (compound)

## 29. "Tedim 2010" is NOT a Bible version

**Wrong:** Listing "Tedim 2010" as a Bible translation alongside 1932, TDB77, and ZVS 2018.

**Correct:** There is no "Tedim 2010" Bible. The year 2010 was the **Cope Centennial Jubilee** ‚Äî 100 years of written Zolai (1910‚Äì2010). No new Bible translation was published that year.

The three Tedim Bible versions are:
1. **Tedim 1932** ‚Äî First complete Bible
2. **TDB77 (1977)** ‚Äî Major revision
3. **ZVS 2018** ‚Äî Current gold standard (also: TDB 2011 and TBR17 2017 on bible.com)

## 30. Judson 1835 is NOT a Tedim translation

**Wrong:** Listing "Judson (1835)" as an early Tedim Bible translation.

**Correct:** Adoniram Judson translated the **Burmese Bible** in 1835. It has no direct connection to Tedim/Zolai. In this project, the Judson 1835 corpus (`judson1835`, 26,589 entries) is used as **parallel Burmese data** for cross-linguistic training only ‚Äî it is not a Zolai source.

## 31. Missing Pronominal Agreement Markers
- **Mistake:** `Nang hong pai hi.` (You come ‚Äî missing agreement marker)
- **Correct:** `Nang na pai hi.` or `Nang hong pai na hi.`
- **Mistake:** `Amah an ne hi.` (He eats rice ‚Äî missing `a` marker)
- **Correct:** `Amah in an a ne hi.`
- **Rule:** 2nd person (Nang) requires `na`, 3rd person (Amah/Mite) requires `a` marker before the verb.

## 32. Proper Noun Capitalization (ZVS Standard)
- **Mistake:** `pasian`, `topa`, `laisiangtho`, `zolai`
- **Correct:** `Pasian` (God), `Topa` (Lord), `Laisiangtho` (Bible), `Zolai` (the language)
- **Rule:** Always capitalize religious, linguistic, and ethnic proper nouns.

## 33. Negation of `thei` (Ability)
- **Mistake:** `Ka pai thei lo hi.` (I cannot go ‚Äî uses `lo` which is more for 'not' than 'cannot')
- **Correct:** `Ka pai thei kei hi.` (Standard negation for ability/future)
- **Mistake:** `A thei lo hi.` (He does not know)
- **Correct:** `A thei kei hi.` (ZVS standard)

## 34. `mahkho` vs `mahkua` (Future/Front)
- **Mistake:** Using `mahkho` (Hakha influence).
- **Correct:** `mahkua` (Front/Future).
- Example: `Ih mailam mahkua...` (Our future/front...)

## 35. `Pasian' hong itna` ‚Äî NOT a mistake (previously mislabeled)

**Previously documented as wrong. This is CORRECT in formal/biblical register.**

`Pasian'` uses the genitive apostrophe ‚Äî it means **"God's"**, making `Pasian' hong itna` a **noun phrase** = "God's love [toward us]".

| Form | Type | Meaning | Register |
|---|---|---|---|
| `Pasian' hong itna` | Noun phrase (genitive) | **God's love** | Formal/Biblical ‚úÖ |
| `Pasian hong itna` | Verb phrase | **God loves** | TDB77 ‚úÖ |
| `Pasian in eite hong it hi` | Full sentence | **God loves us** | Standard ZVS ‚úÖ |

Bible evidence:
- `Pasian' hong itna` ‚Äî Tedim2010, Romans 5:8; Ephesians 2:4
- `Pasian hong itna ahi zongin` ‚Äî TDB77, 2 Corinthians 13:14
- `Pasian in eite hong it hi` ‚Äî 1 John 4:10

**This is the most common AI-generated error: correct ZVS words, wrong grammar.**

`Pasian in kei hong it hi` ‚Äî every word is valid ZVS, but the sentence is **grammatically wrong**.

**Why it's wrong:**
- `ka` = 1st person singular subject prefix ("I do the action")
- `Pasian in` = God (ergative subject ‚Äî God is doing the action)
- You cannot pair a 3rd-person subject (`Pasian`) with a 1st-person verb prefix (`ka`)
- Additionally, when a 3rd-person subject acts **toward the speaker**, the directional particle `hong` is required

**Correct forms:**

| English | ‚úÖ Correct | ‚ùå Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| God loves me | `Pasian in kei hong it hi.` | `Pasian in kei hong it hi.` |
| God loves us | `Pasian in eite hong it hi.` | `Pasian in i hong it hi.` |
| I love God | `Pasian ka it hi.` | `Pasian in kei hong it hi.` |
| My Father loves me | `Ka Pa in kei hong it hi.` | `Ka Pa in ka hong it hi.` |

**The rule:**
- 3rd-person subject + action toward speaker ‚Üí use `hong` + 3rd-person prefix `a` (or omit prefix)
- `ka` is ONLY used when the speaker (I) is the one performing the action
- When God/3rd-person is the agent and speaker is the patient: `[Subject] in [Object] hong [verb] hi`

**Bible evidence (TDB77):**
- `Ka Pa in kei hong it hi.` ‚Äî My Father loves me. (John 10:17)
- `Pasian in eite hong it hi.` ‚Äî God loves us. (1 John 4:10)
- `Pasian ka it hi.` ‚Äî I love God. (correct ‚Äî ka = I, Pasian = object, no `in` on object)

## 36. Wrong Directional Particle: `hong` vs `va` vs none

`hong` = action moving **toward** the speaker/base
`va` = action moving **away** from the speaker/base

| ‚ùå Wrong | ‚úÖ Correct | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| `Ka hong pai ding hi.` | `Ka va pai ding hi.` | I am going (away) ‚Äî use `va` |
| `Sia in lai va pia hi.` | `Sia in lai hong pia hi.` | Teacher gives book to me ‚Äî use `hong` |
| `Hong pai in.` (to someone leaving) | `Va pai in.` | Telling someone to go away ‚Äî use `va` |

**Rule:** If the speaker is the recipient of the action ‚Üí `hong`. If the action moves away from the speaker ‚Üí `va`. If no movement toward/away ‚Üí no directional particle.

## 37. Ergative `in` on Object (Not Subject)

`in` marks the **agent/doer** only. Never mark the object with `in`.

| ‚ùå Wrong | ‚úÖ Correct | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| `Ka vok in ka go hi.` | `Kei in vok ka go hi.` | `in` follows the subject (kei), not the object (vok) |
| `Tua lai in ka sim hi.` | `Tua lai ka sim hi.` | Object (book) never takes `in` |
| `Pasian in kei hong it hi.` | `Pasian ka it hi.` | When Pasian is object (I love God), drop `in` |

## 38. `pau` vs `gen` ‚Äî Intransitive vs Transitive Speech

- `pau` = speak/talk (intransitive ‚Äî no object): `Ama pau hi.` (He speaks.)
- `gen` = tell/say (transitive ‚Äî requires object or content): `Ama thu a gen hi.` (He said something.)
- `ci` = say/quote (quotative): `"Hoih hi," a ci hi.` (He said "Good.")

| ‚ùå Wrong | ‚úÖ Correct |
|---|---|
| `Ama thu a pau hi.` | `Ama thu a gen hi.` |
| `Kei kiangah gen in.` | `Kei kiangah pau in.` (speak to me) |

## 39. Honorific Register ‚Äî `Nang` is Rude to Elders

Using `nang` (you, 2nd person) directly to an elder, pastor, or superior is disrespectful.

| ‚ùå Wrong (to elder) | ‚úÖ Correct |
|---|---|
| `Nang na om hiam?` | `Sia na om hiam?` / `Note na om hiam?` |
| `Nang na deih hiam?` | `Sia na deih hiam?` |

**Rule:** Address elders/superiors by title (`Sia` = teacher/pastor, `Topa` = Lord) or use `Note` (honorific plural you).

---

## 40. Subject-Verb Agreement Prefix Must Match Subject Person

The verb prefix must always agree with the **actual subject**, not the topic.

| ‚ùå Wrong | ‚úÖ Correct | Error |
|---|---|---|
| `Na nek khit ciang ka pai ding hi.` | `Ka nek khit ciang ka pai ding hi.` | Subject is "I" ‚Äî use `ka`, not `na` |
| `Pasian in kei hong piangsak hi.` | `Pasian in leitung a piangsak hi.` | Pasian (3rd person) ‚Üí use `a` prefix |
| `I gam nuam hi.` (without pronoun) | `Ei i gam nuam hi.` | Inclusive "we" needs `ei` + `i` |
