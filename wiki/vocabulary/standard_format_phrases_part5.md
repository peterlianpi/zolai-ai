## Sentence Patterns

- `Pasian ading nong kihel nading kong zawn uhhi.` = They seek a way for you to turn to God.
  - e.g. `Pasian ading nong kihel nading kong zawn uhhi.`
- `Pasian adingin nong kikel nading kong zawn uhhi.` = They seek a way for you to turn to God (alternative structure).
  - e.g. `Pasian adingin nong kikel nading kong zawn uhhi.`
- `Khuapihte ading kong gensak hi.` = He tells them for the villages.
  - e.g. `Khuapihte ading kong gensak hi.`
- `Khuapih tading kong gensak hi.` = He tells it for the village.
  - e.g. `Khuapih tading kong gensak hi.`
- `Amau ading kong gensak hi.` = He tells it for them.
  - e.g. `Amau ading kong gensak hi.`
- `Kipawl nading kipawlna phuan zel ding maw?` = Should the organization for fellowship be established continuously?
  - e.g. `Kipawl nading kipawlna phuan zel ding maw?`
- `Tua ahih cianga, nong pai nadingin kong zawn phapha hi.` = Since that is the case, it is good that you seek (a way) for going.
  - e.g. `Tua ahih cianga, nong pai nadingin kong zawn phapha hi.`
- `Tua ahih cianga, nong pai nadingun kong zawn phapha hi.` = Since that is the case, it is good that you seek (a way) for going (respectful/plural).
  - e.g. `Tua ahih cianga, nong pai nadingun kong zawn phapha hi.`
- `Tua ahih cianga, nong pai nading kong zawn phapha hi.` = Since that is the case, you seek (a way) for going.
  - e.g. `Tua ahih cianga, nong pai nading kong zawn phapha hi.`
- `Tua ahih ciangin, nong pai nading kong zawn phapha hi.` = Since that is the case, you seek (a way) for going.
  - e.g. `Tua ahih ciangin, nong pai nading kong zawn phapha hi.`
- `Tua ahih cianga, nong pai ciangun hong zasak in.` = Since that is the case, let (them) know when you go (respectful/plural).
  - e.g. `Tua ahih cianga, nong pai ciangun hong zasak in.`
- `Pai inla va gen in.` = Go and then speak.
  - e.g. `Pai inla va gen in.`
- `Thu ngai khat ii tung ah direct in genna ah kizangh hi.` = It is used for direct speaking to one person.
  - e.g. `Thu ngai khat ii tung ah direct in genna ah kizangh hi.`
- `Pai unla va gen un.` = Go and then speak (plural/respectful).
  - e.g. `Pai unla va gen un.`
- `Thu ngaite tung ah direct in genna ah kizangh hi.` = It is used for direct speaking to many people.
  - e.g. `Thu ngaite tung ah direct in genna ah kizangh hi.`
- `Pai henla va gen hen.` = Go and then speak (singular).
  - e.g. `Pai henla va gen hen.`
- `Pa uhhenla va gen uhhen.` = Go and then speak (plural/respectful).
  - e.g. `Pa uhhenla va gen uhhen.`
- `Tuang in an ne hi.` = Tuang ate rice.
  - e.g. `Tuang in an ne hi.`
- `Tuang in an ne saisai hi.` = Tuang ate rice slowly.
  - e.g. `Tuang in an ne saisai hi.`
- `Tuang pen pasal hi.` = Tuang is a man.
  - e.g. `Tuang pen pasal hi.`
- `Tuang pen pasal hoih hi.` = Tuang is a good man.
  - e.g. `Tuang pen pasal hoih hi.`
- `Tuang in laibu sim hi.` = Tuang reads a book.
  - e.g. `Tuang in laibu sim hi.`
- `Tuang in laibu sim hi. Tua ama sim pen hoih mahmah hi.` = Tuang reads a book. He reads very well.
  - e.g. `Tuang in laibu sim hi. Tua ama sim pen hoih mahmah hi.`
- `Laibuteng le a simte koi lam tung uh ahia?` = Where do the books and their readers belong?
  - e.g. `Laibuteng le a simte koi lam tung uh ahia?`
- `I mawtaw tawh na pai un.` = Go with our car.
  - e.g. `I mawtaw tawh na pai un.`
- `Eiteng in bang i hih tam?` = What are we all doing?
  - e.g. `Eiteng in bang i hih tam?`
- `Tuang in laibu sim hi. Tua aa leh, cianga, aa, in, la, ahih ahih manin lai thei hi.` = Tuang reads a book. Because of that, and then, because, in, la, because of that, he knows reading.
  - e.g. `Tuang in laibu sim hi. Tua aa leh, cianga, aa, in, la, ahih ahih manin lai thei hi.`
