# Zolai Word of the Day: Deep Dives

This document explores key Zolai (Tedim) words, their etymology, and their multi-layered meanings.

## 1. Lung (The Seat of Everything)
*   **Literal Meaning:** Heart, Liver, or Center.
*   **Symbolic Meaning:** The location of emotion, logic, and will.
*   **Compound Examples:**
    *   `Lungdam`: (Heart + Good/Full) -> To be happy / Thank you.
    *   `Lung-hehna`: (Heart + Sad/Anger) -> Grief / Anger / Sadness.
    *   `Lungduai`: (Heart + Soft/Long) -> Patience / Long-suffering.
    *   `Lungkim`: (Heart + Complete) -> Satisfied / Agreed.
    *   `Lung-lut`: (Heart + Enter) -> Interested / Enthusiastic.
    *   `Lung-phawng`: (Heart + Awaken) -> To be moved / To be inspired.

## 2. Hehpihna (The Supreme Value)
*   **Meaning:** Grace, Mercy, Kindness.
*   **Theological Use:** Used to translate "Grace" in the New Testament.
*   **Cultural Use:** Acts of unmerited kindness to those who cannot repay.

## 3. Thuciamna (The Bond)
*   **Etymology:** `Thu` (Word/Matter) + `Ciam` (To promise/test) + `na` (Nominalizer).
*   **Meaning:** Covenant, Promise, Agreement.
*   **Biblical Note:** Used for the Old Testament (`Thuciam Lui`) and New Testament (`Thuciam Thak`).

## 4. Topa (The Highest Authority)
*   **Meaning:** Lord, Master, Boss, The One on Top.
*   **Etymology:** `To` (High/Seat) + `pa` (Male suffix).
*   **Modern Use:** Now primarily used for God, but originally referred to any high-ranking leader or master.

## 5. Siangtho (The Pure One)
*   **Meaning:** Holy, Clean, Pure, Undefiled.
*   **Compound:** `Kha Siangtho` (The Holy Spirit).
*   **Standard Note:** In 2018 Standard, `siangtho` is often used where older versions might use more archaic or regional terms.
