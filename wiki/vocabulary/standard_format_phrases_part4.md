## Sentence Patterns

- `X nek hoihlo hi` = X is not good to eat
  - e.g. `Golhguk nek hoihlo hi.`
- `Ka ci ka sa a golh hi` = My liver/gall is heavy/painful
  - e.g. `Ka ci ka sa a golh hi.`
- `X an kingolh hi` = X are being disciplined/punished
  - e.g. `Thu manglote an kingolh hi.`
- `Xate guza kipia hen` = Let X give their all/effort
  - e.g. `Gutate guza kipia hen.`
- `Khemna pen a gu khauh mahmah hi` = The trap/snare is very tight
  - e.g. `Khemna pen a gu khauh mahmah hi.`
- `Inn gol khat ka nei hi` = I have one house
  - e.g. `Inn gol khat ka nei hi.`
- `Ka guh ka taangteng nalua hi` = My back/waist hurts a lot
  - e.g. `Ka guh ka taangteng nalua hi.`
- `guk sat hi` = Killed by a wild animal/beast
  - e.g. `guk sat hi.`
- `Ka van hong kiguuksak laitak nai` = When my luck was about to turn good
  - e.g. `Ka van hong kiguuksak laitak nai`
- `Eite hong gum pen X hi` = The one who overcomes for us is X
  - e.g. `Eite hong gum pen Topa Zeisu hi.`
- `Nungak ngumngekte ka ngai hi` = I miss the shy young women
  - e.g. `Nungak ngumngekte ka ngai hi.`
- `Gun ah kimu ni ei` = Let us meet at the gun/fortress
  - e.g. `Gun ah kimu ni ei.`
- `Ngun le kham ka nei hi` = I have a gun and a bow
  - e.g. `Ngun le kham ka nei hi.`
- `Gup hauluate kimudah hi` = The proud ones are disgraced
  - e.g. `Gup hauluate kimudah hi.`
- `X pen Zopau Tedim awsuah siksanin lakna bek hi aa “Ng” aw a zangte aa zongh Zopau/Zolai mah bel hiveve hi` = These are just examples of Tedim pronunciation using the sound 'Ng', but they are truly Zopau/Zolai speakers
  - e.g. `Hihte pen Zopau Tedim awsuah siksanin lakna bek hi aa “Ng” aw a zangte aa zongh Zopau/Zolai mah bel hiveve hi.`
- `Pawlkhatte in Tedim awsuah ngah nuamlua liangin “Ng” awsuahna khempeuh ah “G” aw vive suaksak uh ahih manin, a khiatna tuampi khat ahihlam i phawkkhak ding deihna ahi hi` = Some people, enjoying the Tedim sound too much, change all 'Ng' sounds to 'G' sounds, so we want to point out that this is a major difference in pronunciation
  - e.g. `Pawlkhatte in Tedim awsuah ngah nuamlua liangin “Ng” awsuahna khempeuh ah “G” aw vive suaksak uh ahih manin, a khiatna tuampi khat ahihlam i phawkkhak ding deihna ahi hi.`
- `( ’ ) pen laimal tomlaakna ah kilim zat mahmah hi` = The apostrophe (') is very appropriate to use in word division
  - e.g. `( ’ ) pen laimal tomlaakna ah kilim zat mahmah hi.`
- `Paulai tawh i gelh ciang, pau awsuah bangin i gelh ciang kilim zat diak hi` = It is very appropriate to use when writing alongside words, when writing as it sounds
  - e.g. `Paulai tawh i gelh ciang, pau awsuah bangin i gelh ciang kilim zat diak hi.`
- `Laigil, lai thupite ah ahih leh laimal tomlaakna-in zatloh mah hoihzaw hi` = In official documents or important texts, it is better not to use it for word division
  - e.g. `Laigil, lai thupite ah ahih leh laimal tomlaakna-in zatloh mah hoihzaw hi.`
- `Kote in Sangpi ii mawtaw ka laploh manun noteng in nong nusia uhhi` = Because we did not pay the fare for the vehicle of Elder Pi, you all left us
  - e.g. `Kote in Sangpi ii mawtaw ka laploh manun noteng in nong nusia uhhi.`
- `Neihna lakna ah zongh “ii” cih mun tangin ( ’ ) kizangh thei hi` = In possession contexts, the apostrophe (') can be used instead of 'ii'
  - e.g. `Neihna lakna ah zongh “ii” cih mun tangin ( ’ ) kizangh thei hi.`
- `Sepna lakna ah zongh “in” cih mun tangin ( ’ ) kizangh thei hi` = In action contexts, the apostrophe (') can be used instead of 'in'
  - e.g. `Sepna lakna ah zongh “in” cih mun tangin ( ’ ) kizangh thei hi.`
- `Phonetic tuamtuam zat ding pen hoih khinlozaw hi` = Using various phonetics is not good
  - e.g. `Phonetic tuamtuam zat ding pen hoih khinlozaw hi.`
- `Banghang hiam cih leh Zolai pen theih baih, sin baih, ciapteh baih ahih ding kisam hi` = Because Zolai needs to be easily understood, easily read, and clearly defined
  - e.g. `Banghang hiam cih leh Zolai pen theih baih, sin baih, ciapteh baih ahih ding kisam hi.`
- `Tualoin Zolai a lunglut le a sin nuam kitawm semsem kha ding hi` = Otherwise, interest in Zolai and the desire to read it will gradually decrease
  - e.g. `Tualoin Zolai a lunglut le a sin nuam kitawm semsem kha ding hi.`
- `Phonetic tuamtuam a zangh minamdang tampi a om sam hang’ zangh hetlo aa a khangto veve, a lai uh a tangzai veve minam tampi mah na om kawikawi hi` = Many other ethnic groups who use various phonetics have not used them and have gradually disappeared, while many ethnic groups whose language and stories have been preserved still exist
  - e.g. `Phonetic tuamtuam a zangh minamdang tampi a om sam hang’ zangh hetlo aa a khangto veve, a lai uh a tangzai veve minam tampi mah na om kawikawi hi.`
- `“ah” cih laimal pen tang gelh ding mah hoihzaw hi` = It is better to write the word 'ah' as it is
  - e.g. `“ah” cih laimal pen tang gelh ding mah hoihzaw hi.`
- `Banghang hiam cih leh “a le h” kihel laimal kigawm tampitak Zolai ah om ahih manin “ah” tawh laimal matna in laisimna haksasak tuam thei hi` = Because there are many combined words of 'a' and 'h' in Zolai, using 'ah' to end a word can make reading difficult
  - e.g. `Banghang hiam cih leh “a le h” kihel laimal kigawm tampitak Zolai ah om ahih manin “ah” tawh laimal matna in laisimna haksasak tuam thei hi.`
- `Ahi uhhi. (Hih pen “h” nih kizom ci-in matloh ding hoih asa kiom thei aa, mat lehang sim dan nopzawin laisim hong manlangzawsak tuam hi.)` = They are. (It is sometimes considered better not to write two 'h's together, but if written, it makes reading faster and more pleasant.)
  - e.g. `Ahi uhhi. (Hih pen “h” nih kizom ci-in matloh ding hoih asa kiom thei aa, mat lehang sim dan nopzawin laisim hong manlangzawsak tuam hi.)`
- `“ahi hi” ah hal ding pen hoihzaw hi` = It is better to write 'ahi hi' as one word
  - e.g. `“ahi hi” ah hal ding pen hoihzaw hi.`
- `Banghang hiam cih leh, hih pen format khat hong suak hi` = Because this becomes one format
  - e.g. `Banghang hiam cih leh, hih pen format khat hong suak hi.`
- `Minpi pen Lawh khat ta ah hal ding hoih hi` = It is better to write the main name in one word at a time
  - e.g. `Minpi pen Lawh khat ta ah hal ding hoih hi.`
- `Minneu pen minpi sung aa teng lian bek pan a kilawh leh lawh khat ta ah hal ding hoih hi` = If the secondary name is only the first part of the main name, it is better to write it in one word at a time
  - e.g. `Minneu pen minpi sung aa teng lian bek pan a kilawh leh lawh khat ta ah hal ding hoih hi.`
- `Minneu pen minpi sung bek pan thamlo a meizom behlap a om leh a minpi sung aa tawh mat ding hoih hi` = If the secondary name is not just part of the main name but has an additional meaning, it is better to write it with the main name
  - e.g. `Minneu pen minpi sung bek pan thamlo a meizom Gtn. Piangpi, Khaitawng, Dimboih, Huaikhek behlap a om leh a minpi sung aa tawh mat ding hoih hi.`
- `Hih bang dan khiatna hau laimalgawm kibangte pen “double words” tawh khenluat ding hoihlozaw hi` = It is not good to separate these kinds of words with the same sound/form using "double words"
  - e.g. `Hih bang dan khiatna hau laimalgawm kibangte pen “double words” tawh khenluat ding hoihlozaw hi.`
- `Banghang hiam cih leh “double words” zatna ah zongh khiatna khat sangin a tamzaw om veve theilai hi` = Because in the use of "double words" there can often be more than one meaning
  - e.g. `Banghang hiam cih leh “double words” zatna ah zongh khiatna khat sangin a tamzaw om veve theilai hi.`
- `Tua ahih manin hih bang dan laimalte pen a laigual/a thu zui-in kikhen thei lai veve ahih manin khenloh mah hoihzaw hi` = Therefore, these kinds of words can still be separated according to their root/context, so it is better not to separate them
  - e.g. `Tua ahih manin hih bang dan laimalte pen a laigual/a thu zui-in kikhen thei lai veve ahih manin khenloh mah hoihzaw hi.`
- `English lai leh namdang tampite ii laite ah zongh hih bang dan mahin laimal kibang khiatna kibanglo, laimal kibanglo khiatna kibang cih dan tampi mah na om kawikawi hi` = In English texts and those of many other ethnic groups, there are also many cases where words with the same form have different meanings, or words with different forms have the same meaning
  - e.g. `English lai leh namdang tampite ii laite ah zongh hih bang dan mahin laimal kibang khiatna kibanglo, laimal kibanglo khiatna kibang cih dan tampi mah na om kawikawi hi.`
- `Zolai ah tua bang lianin khen theih hi khinlo hi` = In Zolai, it cannot be separated to that extent
  - e.g. `Zolai ah tua bang lianin khen theih hi khinlo hi.`
- `“i” in khat sang’ a tamzaw ahihna lakkhin ahih manin “uh” kisamthuah nawnlo hi` = Since 'i' already implies plurality, adding 'uh' is no longer necessary
  - e.g. `“i” in khat sang’ a tamzaw ahihna lakkhin ahih manin “uh” kisamthuah nawnlo hi.`
- `“i” zatna ah, “uh” cih tawh zatkhop kullo hi` = In the use of 'i', it is not necessary to use it together with 'uh'
  - e.g. `“i” zatna ah, “uh” cih tawh zatkhop kullo hi.`
- `“ihi hi”, “i kizasak hi” hileh khat sangin a tamzaw ahih lam kilangkhin hi` = If it is 'ihi hi' or 'i kizasak hi', it shows plurality more than one
  - e.g. `“ihi hi”, “i kizasak hi” hileh khat sangin a tamzaw ahih lam kilangkhin hi.`
- `“ihi uhhi”, “i kizasak uhhi” cih bang kulthuah nawnlo hi` = It is no longer necessary to add things like 'ihi uhhi' or 'i kizasak uhhi'
  - e.g. `“ihi uhhi”, “i kizasak uhhi” cih bang kulthuah nawnlo hi.`
- `Tua baan ah, “i” pen “noun” tawh matloh ding hi` = Furthermore, 'i' should not be used with nouns
  - e.g. `Tua baan ah, “i” pen “noun” tawh matloh ding hi.`
- `I khua ah i mawtaw tawh a pai ihi hi` = We are the ones who went to your village with our vehicle
  - e.g. `Gtn. I khua ah i mawtaw tawh a pai ihi hi.`
