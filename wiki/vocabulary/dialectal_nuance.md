> **Last updated: 2026-04-20**

## Linguistic Rules

- **Man / Dik:** Both are used for "correct" or "true" in Tedim.
- **Kha:** Always use *Kha* for spirit/soul in Tedim.
- **Nuntakna:** Always ensure the 't' is present. *Nunnak* is incorrect in Tedim.
- **Pasian:** Never use *pasian*.
- **Suahtakna:** Use for "freedom" or "liberty".
- **Lungdam vs Nuam:** Both are valid Tedim words. `Lungdam` = heart-joy / thankfulness (spiritual/emotional). `Nuam` = pleasant / enjoyable (sensory/general). Do NOT treat `Nuam` as a Hakha/Falam intrusion — it is native Tedim.
- **I vs Ko:** *I* is inclusive we (you and me), *Ko* is exclusive we (me and them, not you).
- **Uh with I:** In Tedim, do NOT use *uh* with *i* (we). `I pai hi` is correct; `I pai uh hi` is wrong.
- **In (ergative):** The ergative particle *in* is crucial for identifying the subject who acts (e.g., `Mipa in an ne hi` — The man eats rice).
- **Conditional negation:** Use `kei` not `lo` for conditionals: `nong pai kei a leh` (unless you come) — NEVER `kei a leh`.
