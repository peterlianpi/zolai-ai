# Modern Technology & Software

## Concept / Rule
Zolai is rapidly encountering the need for modern technological terminology (Internet, Software, AI, Data). As an oral-to-written language transitioning into the digital age, it relies on two primary methods for tech vocabulary:
1. **Loanwords (Phonetic Adaptation):** Adopting the English word phonetically (e.g., `Set` for Machine, `Kompita` for Computer).
2. **Compound Descriptive Nouns (Neologisms):** Combining existing Zolai words to describe the *function* of the technology.

### Core Tech Vocabulary Strategies
*   **Computer/Device:** `Set` (Machine/Device) or `Kompita`.
*   **Internet/Network:** `Leitungbup Kizopna` (Worldwide connection) or simply `Kizopna` (Connection). Often, the loanword `Internet` is preferred in casual speech.
*   **Artificial Intelligence (AI):** `Sik Set Pilna` (Machine Wisdom) or `A kibawltawm Pilna` (Made/Artificial Wisdom).
*   **Data/Information:** `Thu` (Word/Information) or `Ciamtehna` (Record/Mark).
*   **Software/App:** `Set Sung Om Pilna` (Wisdom inside the machine) or simply `App`.
*   **Smartphone:** `Khut Tawi Phone` (Hand-held phone) or `Phone Pil` (Smart phone).

## Decision / Application
The AI Tutor must generally prioritize **Compound Descriptive Nouns** when explaining a concept to a learner to ensure comprehension, but should acknowledge and accept **Loanwords** as they are universally used by native speakers (especially the youth).

## Reason
While descriptive nouns (`A kibawltawm Pilna`) preserve the linguistic integrity and logic of Zolai, rigid adherence to them can sound archaic or confusing to a modern speaker who just says "AI." The tutor must bridge the gap between formal comprehension and practical daily usage.

## Pattern / Code Snippet
When translating or tutoring tech terms, the AI should introduce the descriptive term, followed by the common loanword in parentheses:
```zolai
Tuni in a kibawltawm pilna (AI) thu i kikum ding hi. 
(Today we will discuss the concept of artificial intelligence.)
```

## Mistake / Anti-pattern
Do not force a literal translation if it obscures meaning. For example, translating "Cloud Computing" literally as `Mei-iik Set-tuatna` (Cloud calculating machine) is nonsensical in Zolai. It is better to use the English "Cloud" or describe it as `Vantung set kholhna` (Storing machines in the sky/cloud) accompanied by the loanword.
