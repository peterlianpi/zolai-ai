# Zolai Tedim Basics

Pure Tedim (Zolai) words for daily conversation.

## Greetings
| Tedim | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Na dam na? | How are you? |
| Dam maw? | How are you? |
| Dam mah | I'm fine |
| Ka dam hi | I am well |

## Common Phrases
| Tedim | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| lungdam | thank you |
| man ei / dik ei | that's correct |
| aw / e | yes |
| ai | no |
| tua ahi hiam? | that's it |

## Tenses (Temporal Markers)
| Tense | Marker | Example | Translation |
|-------|--------|---------|-------------|
| Present | **hi** | Ka pai **hi** | I go / I am going |
| Past | **khin** | Ka pai **khin** hi | I went / I have gone |
| Future | **ding** | Ka pai **ding** hi | I will go |

## Negation (No / Not)
- Use **kei** with future/intent and **lo** for general facts.
- **Ka pai ding** (I will go) -> **Ka pai kei ding hi** (I will not go)
- **Ka ne hi** (I eat) -> **Ka ne lo hi** (I do not eat)
- **Imperative:** **Pai kei in** (Do not go)

## Character & Diligence (Sinbu)
| Tedim | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| thanuam / hahkat | diligent / eager |
| thadah | lazy |
| kidem | compete / race |
| kihta | fear |
| damdam | slowly |
| hat | fast / strong |

## Examples
- Na dam na? - Dam mah. (How are you? - I'm fine.)
- Thanuamte in thadahte a zo tawntung uh hi. (The diligent always defeat the lazy.)
- Sumkuang sangin bilpi hatzaw hi. (Rabbit is faster than the snail.)
- Ken nang hong kihta keng. (I do not fear you.)
- An na ne na? - E, ka ne khin hi. (Have you eaten rice? - Yes, I have eaten.)
- Tui dawn o. (Drink water.)
- Nang hong pai o. (You come / Please come.)
- Hih bang hiam? - Hua inn hi. (What is this? - That is a house.)
- Lungdam mahmah. (Thank you very much.)
- Man ei. / Dik ei. (That's correct / OK.)
- Tua ahi hiam? / Ahi mah hiam? / Aman mah hiam? (That's it / Is that it?)
- Ka pai kei ding hi. (I will not go.)
- Ka pai khin hi. (I went.)
