## Sentence Patterns

- `sum hau ding [X] kullo hi.` = To be rich, [X] is not necessary.
  - e.g. `Lungnopna, lungkimna, nopsakna tampite pen sumhauh tektek kullo hi.`
- `Ahi zongin [A] tawh kibanglo aa [B] bekbek [C] hi.` = However, it was different from [A], and it was only [C] of [B].
  - e.g. `Ahi zongin vatawt dangte tawh kibanglo aa kha khat in tang khat ta khawng bek tui hi.`
- `Ahi zongin [A] in [B] piang sak hi.` = However, [A] causes [B] to happen.
  - e.g. `Thu theihlohna in hai gamtatna piang sak hi.`
- `A tung aa [X] a tuamtuamte zat theihloh, nek zawhloh, kimawl zawh nawnloh hun ciang [Y] sangin [Z] ding hi.` = When the time comes that one cannot use, eat, or enjoy the various [X]s mentioned above, rather than [Y], one should [Z].
  - e.g. `A tung aa nopsak theihna a tuamtuamte zat theihloh, nek zawhloh, kimawl zawh nawnloh hun ciang khualzit panpan sangin nopsak theih hun lai-in zinzawk, vakzawk, nekzawk, nopsak hun laakzawk ding hi.`
- `[X] pen sum tawh kileikik theilo hi.` = [X] cannot be bought back with money.
  - e.g. `Nop sak theih hun lai-in nopsakna pen sum tawh kileikik theilo hi.`
- `[X] bekbek na hikhin tuanlo aa [Y] zongh kisam hi.` = It is not only about [X], but [Y] is also necessary.
  - e.g. `Na khempeuh sum bekbek na hikhin tuanlo aa thahat laitak, sum tawh a kilei theilo khangno hun sung ah a nuam theithei in om ding hi.`
- `[X] simin kaplo, [Y], [Z] cihna hi khin pah tuanlo aa, [A] simin zongh [B]lo, [C]lo cihna hi pah tuanlo hi.` = It doesn't necessarily mean [C] just because of [X], nor does it necessarily mean [B] just because of [A].
  - e.g. `Sum huah simin kaplo, lungnuam, nopsa cihna hi khin pah tuanlo aa, sum hauhloh simin zongh lungnuamlo, nopsalo cihna hi pah tuanlo hi.`
- `Innkuan sung ah [X] ii kisap pen [Y] hikhinlo aa [Z] zongh kisam hi.` = In the family, what is needed is not only [X], but [Z] is also necessary.
  - e.g. `Innkuan sung ah ziletate ii kisap pen sum bekbek hikhinlo aa khat le khat hun kipia in kithuahkhawm aa kikholhkhop theih ding zongh kisam hi.`
- `Tate pen ki it thei mahmah aa, tate in [X] a phawk zah dingun [Y] khalo thei hi.` = Children love their parents very much, but children may not realize the extent of [X].
  - e.g. `Tate pen ki it thei mahmah aa, tate in nute’ pate’ itna a phawk zah dingun phawk khalo thei hi.`
- `[X] tawh na a piangpiang, [Y] a ki ngah den, [Z] kha pah cih bang hiloin, [A] mah in [B] ngah aa [C] zaw veve uhhi.` = It is not just about what happens through [X], always receiving [Y], or eventually achieving [Z], but those who actually do [A] receive [B] and are always [C].
  - e.g. `Phat zawh thu, gup zawh thu tawh na a piangpiang, maipha a ki ngah den, a kigual zo kha pah cih bang hiloin, a sem a bawlte mah in maipha ngah aa gualzozaw veve uhhi.`
- `[X] zongh [Y] ii phattuampih ding thupi mahmah hi.` = It is very important for children to [X] their parents.
  - e.g. `Tate zongh siamna, pilna, hauhna bangzah takin i nei zong nulepa ii phattuampih ding thupi mahmah hi.`
- `Khat veivei ciang i tung ah a [X] khat hong tung leh [Y] kisa kha thei aa, ahi zongin [Z] suak thei hi.` = Sometimes when a [X] comes upon us, we feel it is hard to bear, but it can result in a [Z] for us.
  - e.g. `Khat veivei ciang i tung ah a sia khat hong tung leh thuak hak kisa kha thei aa, ahi zongin ei ading a hoihzawkna suak thei hi.`
- `Pasian kiang zuat ding pen [X] masak, [Y] masak kullo hi.` = To approach God, it is not necessary to be first [X] or [Y].
  - e.g. `Pasian kiang zuat ding pen siangtho masak, hoih masak kullo hi.`
- `Pasian tung ah i deih i sapte ngen lehang, [X] mahmah hi.` = Even if we ask God for what we desire, the way for Him to give is [X].
  - e.g. `Pasian tung ah i deih i sapte ngen lehang, hong piak nading lampi hau mahmah hi.`
- `[X] sanglo, [Y], [Z] zongh a theilote tawh kithuah, kikholhna in [A] pan hong khaktan thei hi.` = Association with those who have no [X], no [Y], or even those who greatly desire but don't know, through fellowship, can hinder our [A].
  - e.g. `Ngimna sanglo, ut mel neilo, lunggulhluat zongh a theilote tawh kithuah, kikholhna in i khantoh nading pan hong khaktan thei hi.`
- `I nuntakna ah [X] a omloh hang’ [Y] ompeuh leh [Z] ki nei kha veve hi.` = Even if there is no [X] in our life, if only [Y] is present, we will eventually get [Z].
  - e.g. `I nuntakna ah sum a omloh hang’ Pasian ompeuh leh i deihte ki nei kha veve hi.`
- `[X] ciang bek [Y] zo ding, [Z] ciang bek [A] nei ding, [B] ciang bek [C] nei zo ding kisa kha thei hi.` = One might think that one can only [Y] when [X], only get [A] when [Z], and only get [C] when [B].
  - e.g. `Cidam ciang bek nasem zo ding, nasep zawh ciang bek sum nei ding, sum neih ciang bek deihte nei zo ding kisa kha thei hi.`
- `[X] kawmkal pan [Y] I neih theih nading [Z] lampi na theilua mahmah hi.` = God knows the way very well so that we can get [Y] even amidst [X].
  - e.g. `Cidamlo, nasep neilo, sum neilo kawmkal pan i deih khat I neih theih nading Pasian in lampi na theilua mahmah hi.`
- `Lai Siangtho sung ah [X] aa kipan mi tampite in [Y] tawh [Z] tampi na nei uhhi.` = In the Bible, many people who followed [X] gave many [Z] through [Y].
  - e.g. `Lai Siangtho sung ah Topa Zeisu aa kipan mi tampite in gentehna tawh thuhilhna, thugenna tampi na nei uhhi.`
- `[X] sangin [Y] hoihzaw.` = [Y] is better than [X].
  - e.g. `Thahatna sangin pilna hoihzaw`
- `[X] khat in a hoih tampi susia thei hi.` = One [X] can spoil much good.
  - e.g. `ahi zongin khialhna khat in a hoih tampi susia thei hi.`
