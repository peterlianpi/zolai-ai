## Sentence Patterns

- `Vantung kiukna pen bang tawh kibang hiam cihleh, [X] tawh kibang hi.` = To what shall I compare the kingdom of heaven? It is like [X].
  - e.g. `Vantung kiukna pen bang tawh kibang hiam cihleh, A lo ah taangcihoih a vawhpa tawh kibang hi.`
- `Pasian' ukna tawh kisai thusim, Pasian in note tungah hong gen khin zo hi. Ahi zongin a pualam aa om mite tungah na khempeuh, gentehna bek tawh kigen hi.` = The mystery of the kingdom of God has been given to you. But to those outside, everything is spoken in parables.
  - e.g. `Pasian' ukna tawh kisai thusim, Pasian in note tungah hong gen khin zo hi. Ahi zongin a pualam aa om mite tungah na khempeuh, gentehna bek tawh kigen hi.`
- `Hih bang gentehna zangin mite tungah thu hilh aa, amaute tel zawh zahciangciang thu a gen hi.` = He taught them in this way with many parables, and taught them as much as they could understand.
  - e.g. `Zeisu in hih bang gentehna zangin mite tungah thu hilh aa, amaute tel zawh zah ciangciang thu a gen hi.`
- `Gentehna-in, [X] khat le [Y] khat, note' kikhopna ah hong paikhawm leh, [Z] ci aa, ahi zongin [W] kiangah, [A] cih bangin kam na zat uh leh, [B] na hi uhhi.` = For example, if a rich man in fine clothes and a poor man in rags come into your meeting, and you pay special attention to the rich man and say to him, 'Sit here in a good place,' but say to the poor man, 'You stand there,' or 'Sit on the floor by my feet,' you are showing partiality.
  - e.g. `Gentehna-in, kham zungbuh bulhin puan manpha a silh mihau khat le puan siasa a silh mizawng khat, note' kikhopna ah hong paikhawm leh, limtakin a kizem mipa zahtakbawlzaw-in, “Hih lai ah tutna nuamah hong tu in,” ci aa, ahi zongin mizawngpa kiangah, “Tua lai ah na ding phot in,” a hihkei leh, “Sual tung, hih ka khe kiang khawngah hong tu in,” cih bangin kam na zat uh leh, mi a deidan na hi uhhi.`
