# Everyday Nature & Anatomy

## Concept / Rule
Educational materials, particularly the *Zolai Sinbu* (Zolai Readers for early grades), ground vocabulary in the immediate environment. The fundamental vocabulary for beginners consists of local flora, fauna, and human anatomy.

### Zolai Nature & Anatomy Vocabulary List

**Nature & Fauna**
- `mai gah` - pumpkin/gourd
- `hakai` - crab
- `akno` - chick/young chicken
- `ngapi` - fish paste
- `gua` - bamboo
- `bualtui` - pond/pool
- `uiphuk` - frog/toad
- `sumkuang` - tortoise/turtle
- `bilpi` - rabbit
- `kauphe` - butterfly/moth
- `miksi` - ant

**Human Anatomy**
- `lungtang` - heart (the physical organ, often used interchangeably with `lungsim` for the emotional seat)
- `sam` - hair
- `gil` - stomach/womb

## Decision / Application
The AI Tutor must introduce these terms when assessing a user as "Beginner" (Level 1). Sentences should be kept short (e.g., `Akno ka mu hi` - I see a chick).

## Reason
This vocabulary forms the foundation of Zolai literacy as mandated by the Zomi Language & Literature Society (ZOLLS). Mastering these concrete nouns before abstract concepts is crucial for a natural language acquisition curve.

## Pattern / Code Snippet
When creating practice sentences for beginners, prioritize SOV structures using this list:
```zolai
Kei in mai gah ka duh hi. (I want pumpkin.)
Amah in bilpi a kem hi. (He/she keeps a rabbit.)
Kauphe a hoih hi. (The butterfly is beautiful.)
```

## Mistake / Anti-pattern
Do not use complex theological terms (e.g., `Kha Siangtho`) or advanced abstract nouns when testing basic grammar structures like `Kei in [Noun] ka mu hi`. Use words from this list instead.
