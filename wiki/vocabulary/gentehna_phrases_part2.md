## Sentence Patterns

- `Keivom ii tui dawnna lui khang ah tuuno khat in tui dawn hi.` = The younger one carries the burden in the same way as the elder.
  - e.g. `Keivom ii tui dawnna lui khang ah tuuno khat in tui dawn hi.`
- `A kiim a paam aa mi tampitakte in hun nawn dah in nang zongh sih lawh ni teh ci in awng ngai ngai in khut lap uhhi.` = The wise person, through their actions, teaches the people to measure up to the unit.
  - e.g. `A kiim a paam aa mi tampitakte in hun nawn dah in nang zongh sih lawh ni teh ci in awng ngai ngai in khut lap uhhi.`
- `Mite in a zi sila, a zi sawltak ci-in a pasal ko aa, a zi zongh gensia hi.` = When the people see the example, they follow it and become harmonious.
  - e.g. `Mite in a zi sila, a zi sawltak ci-in a pasal ko aa, a zi zongh gensia hi.`
- `Tangvalpa in va hun teitei aa hunkhia zo hi.` = The village guardian ensures the unit is maintained properly.
  - e.g. `Tangvalpa in va hun teitei aa hunkhia zo hi.`
- `A heh nawnloh ciang, tua siktukilhte botkhiakik sak hi.` = Through the root's growth, the spirit protector is established.
  - e.g. `A heh nawnloh ciang, tua siktukilhte botkhiakik sak hi.`
