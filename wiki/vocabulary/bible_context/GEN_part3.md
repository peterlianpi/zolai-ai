# GEN - Part 3

## Vocabulary with Context

### ganhing = beast/animal
**Context:** Referring to the animals given green herbs for food.
**Example:** Leitung ganhing khempeuhte tung

### huih lak vasate = fowl of the air (birds)
**Context:** Referring to flying creatures.
**Example:** huih lak vasate khempeuh tung

### bokvakte = things that creep (creeping things)
**Context:** Referring to creatures that move along the ground.
**Example:** leitungah a bokvakte khempeuh tung

### nuntakna hu a neite = those who have the breath of life
**Context:** Referring to all living things.
**Example:** nuntakna hu a neite khempeuh tungah

### kung hing = green herb/plant
**Context:** What was given as food to the living creatures.
**Example:** kung hing khempeuh ka pia khin ta hi

### hoih mahmah = very good
**Context:** God's assessment of what he had made.
**Example:** en un, hoih mahmah hi.

### bei-in = when it ended/set
**Context:** Used to describe the transition from evening to morning.
**Example:** Nitak hong bei-in, zingsang hong tung a

### vantung = heavens
**Context:** Mentioned alongside 'leitung' (earth) as being finished.
**Example:** vantung leh leitung leh a sunga omte khempeuh kiman khin hi.

### kiman khin hi = were finished/completed
**Context:** Describing the completion of creation.
**Example:** khempeuh kiman khin hi.

### zo = finished/completed (verb form of 'kiman')
**Context:** God finishing his work.
**Example:** Pasian in a bawlsate khempeuh mu hi, en un, hoih mahmah hi.

### tawlnga = rested
**Context:** God resting on the seventh day.
**Example:** ama tawlnga hi.

### thupha pia-in = by blessing
**Context:** How God treated the seventh day.
**Example:** ni sagih ni thupha pia-in siangthosak hi.

### siangthosak hi = sanctified/made holy
**Context:** God's action towards the seventh day.
**Example:** thupha pia-in siangthosak hi.

### pian’sak nasepna = creative work/work of creation
**Context:** The reason God rested.
**Example:** pian’sak nasepna ama nasep khempeuh panin

### tangthu = generations/account/history
**Context:** The record of the heavens and earth.
**Example:** vantung leh leitung tangthu ahi hi.

### guah zusak loin = without causing rain to fall
**Context:** Describing the conditions before man existed.
**Example:** leitungah guah zusak loin

### gamsung kungnote = plant of the field/field plants
**Context:** Plants that had not yet grown.
**Example:** gamsung kungnote lei sungah om nailo a

### ankungte = herb/vegetation
**Context:** Plants that had not yet sprung up.
**Example:** lo sung ankungte a po khiat ma hun

### meiilum = mist/vapor
**Context:** What rose from the ground to water the earth.
**Example:** leitung panin meiilum kaaito-in

### leivui = dust/clay
**Context:** The material used by God to form man.
**Example:** leilak pan-a leivui tawh mihing bawl a

### nuntakna hu sangsuk = breathed the breath of life into (literally: poured/blew the breath of life)
**Context:** The act of giving life to the created man.
**Example:** a nak sungah nuntakna hu sangsuk hi

### huan = garden
**Context:** The place God planted for man.
**Example:** Eden-ah huan khat bawl hi

### koih hi = placed/put
**Context:** Where God positioned the man he made.
**Example:** tua lai munah ama' bawlsa mipa koih hi.

### singkung = tree
**Context:** Referring to various types of trees in the garden.
**Example:** a hoih singkung nam khempeuh

### nuntakna singkung = Tree of Life
**Context:** One of the specific trees in the center of the garden.
**Example:** huan laizangah zong nuntakna singkung

### a pha leh a sia theihna singkung = Tree of the knowledge of good and evil
**Context:** The other specific tree in the garden.
**Example:** leh a pha leh a sia theihna singkung

### kawtsak dingin = to water/irrigate
**Context:** The purpose of the river flowing out of Eden.
**Example:** Huan sung a kawtsak dingin

### gun = river
**Context:** The water source that divided into four heads.
**Example:** gun khat luang khia a

### kham = gold
**Context:** A valuable substance found in the land of Havilah.
**Example:** kham a omna, Havilah gambup kimkot-a

### bedolak = bdellium (a type of resin/gum)
**Context:** Mentioned alongside onyx stone as a treasure of the land.
**Example:** bedolak singnai leh onik suang om hi.

### onik suang = onyx stone
**Context:** A precious stone found in Havilah.
**Example:** bedolak singnai leh onik suang om hi.

### Kush = Cush/Ethiopia
**Context:** The region encompassed by the Gihon river.
**Example:** Kush gam kimkot-a luang pen ahi hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **leitung ganhing khempeuhte tung** = to every beast of the earth
  - Usage: Used to specify the recipients of the green herbs (all land animals).

- **nuntakna hu a neite khempeuh tungah** = to all those who have the breath of life
  - Usage: A comprehensive term for all living creatures.

- **nitak hong bei-in, zingsang hong tung a** = When the evening ended and the morning came
  - Usage: Describes the passage of a day (evening to morning).

- **a pha leh a sia theihna singkung** = the tree of the knowledge of good and evil
  - Usage: A specific, named tree in the Garden of Eden.

- **huan sung a kawtsak dingin** = in order to water the garden
  - Usage: Explaining the purpose of the river flowing from Eden.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X khempeuh'tung**
  - The suffix '-tung' (to/upon) combined with 'khempeuh' (all/every) is used repeatedly to list all categories of creation that received the green herbs (e.g., ganhing khempeuhte' tung, huih lak vasate khempeuh'tung).
  - Example: leitung ganhing khempeuhte' tung

- **A [Noun] [Verb]-in**
  - Used to describe sequential actions or states, often indicating time transition or manner. (e.g., Nitak hong bei-in - evening having ended/set).
  - Example: Nitak hong bei-in, zingsang hong tung a

- **X leh Y leh Z khempeuh**
  - A common structure for listing comprehensive sets, often using 'leh' (and) repeatedly before concluding with 'khempeuh' (all). (e.g., Heavens and earth and all things within them).
  - Example: vantung leh leitung leh a sunga omte khempeuh kiman khin hi.

- **X panin Y-in Z [Verb]**
  - Indicates the source or reason (panin) for an action (Verb) performed by the subject (Z). (e.g., From all his work, God rested).
  - Example: ama' hihsa nasep khempeuh panin ni sagih ni-in amah tawlnga hi.

- **A [Number]na [Noun] [Verb]**
  - Used for naming sequential items, specifically rivers here (First river, Second river).
  - Example: A masa pen gun a min in Pishon hi a
