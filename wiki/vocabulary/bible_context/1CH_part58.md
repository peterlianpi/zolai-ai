# 1CH - Part 58

## Vocabulary with Context

### sanggamte = brethren/colleagues
**Context:** Used to refer to the male relatives or associates of the main person mentioned (Hashabiah, Jerijah).
**Example:** Hashabiah leh a sanggamte

### siamna a nei mihing = men of valour/ability
**Context:** Describes men possessing great skill or bravery, often used for military leaders or important officials.
**Example:** siamna a nei mihing tulkhat leh zasagih

### tulkhat = one thousand
**Context:** A numerical term used in combination with 'za' (hundred) to denote large numbers of people.
**Example:** mihing tulkhat leh zasagih

### zasagih = seven hundred
**Context:** A numerical term.
**Example:** tulkhat leh zasagih

### nitumna lam = western side
**Context:** Refers to the geographical direction, specifically 'west' (nitum) side.
**Example:** Jordan nitumna lam Israel tungah

### uk uh hi = they ruled/were officers
**Context:** The verb 'uk' (to rule/govern) in the past tense plural form.
**Example:** Israel tungah Topa’ nasepna khempeuh ah uk uh hi.

### ukpi = chief/leader
**Context:** The primary leader or chief among a group.
**Example:** Jerijah in a ukpi ahi hi.

### kikantelna = census/reckoning
**Context:** The act of counting or registering people, often for military or administrative purposes.
**Example:** kikantelna bawl uh a

### kum sawmlina = the fortieth year
**Context:** A specific time marker, 'sawm' (ten) times 'li' (four).
**Example:** David’ uk a kum sawmlina kumin

### gamsung = territory/land of
**Context:** Used to denote the region or territory belonging to a place name (Gilead).
**Example:** Gilead gamsung Jazer-ah

### lutangte = chief fathers/heads of houses
**Context:** Refers to the heads of the ancestral lines or clans.
**Example:** a beh sungah lutang ahite, Pasian tawh a kisai na khempeuh ah

### vaite = affairs/matters
**Context:** Matters or business, often paired with religious matters ('Pasian tawh a kisai na khempeuh').
**Example:** kumpipa’ vaite ah

### langte = half/the other side
**Context:** Used here to mean 'half tribe' (Manasseh a langte).
**Example:** Manasseh a langte tungah

### seh hi = commanded/appointed
**Context:** The action of the king giving an order or assignment.
**Example:** a uk dingin a seh hi.

### tu-in = Now/At this time
**Context:** A temporal marker introducing the current arrangement or system.
**Example:** Tu-in Israel mite...

### khen khatah = in each course/division
**Context:** Refers to the specific contingent or division of soldiers/officers serving their rotation.
**Example:** leh khen khatah mi tul sawmnih leh tulli a om

### kha khat khit kha khat = month by month
**Context:** Describes the cyclical, recurring nature of their service rotation.
**Example:** kum tawntung kha khat khit kha khat hong tungin

### a kuankhia = who came in and went out
**Context:** Describes the rotation of service, coming and going.
**Example:** hong tungin a kuankhia a khente tawh kisai-in

### a kha masa sungin = for the first month
**Context:** Specifies the rotation period (month one).
**Example:** A kha masa sungin Zabdiel’ tapa Jashobeam a khen masa a uk hi a

### suanlehkhak = descendant/offspring
**Context:** Refers to lineage, similar to 'suan' (child/seed).
**Example:** Amah pen Perez' suanlehkhak hi a

### galkap a ukte = captains of the host/army
**Context:** Military commanders.
**Example:** galkap a ukte khempeuh a ukpi ahi hi.

### Ahoh-te beh = of the family/clan of Ahoh
**Context:** Identifying the lineage of the officer Dodai.
**Example:** Ahoh-te beh Dodai a khen a ukpi hi a

### sampi = chief priest
**Context:** A high-ranking priest (Jehoiada).
**Example:** sampi Jehoiada' tapa Benaiah

### thahat = mighty man/strength
**Context:** Describes Benaiah's exceptional strength, often used in military contexts.
**Example:** mihing sawmthumte sungah a thahat

### a liah dingin = after him/to succeed him
**Context:** Indicates succession in the leadership role.
**Example:** amah nungah amah a liah dingin a tapa Zebadiah om hi.

### Izrah-te beh = of the family of Izrah
**Context:** Identifying the lineage of Shamhuth.
**Example:** Izrah-te beh Shamhuth hi a

### Tekoa khuami = man of Tekoa (a place name)
**Context:** Identifying the origin of Ira.
**Example:** Tekoa khuami Ikkesh' tapa Ira hi a

### Efraim’ suante sung panin = from among the sons of Ephraim
**Context:** Identifying the tribal origin of Helez.
**Example:** Efraim’ suante sung panin Pelon khuami Helez hi a

### Jerah mi sung panin = from among the men of Jerah
**Context:** Identifying the specific lineage group of Sibbekai.
**Example:** Jerah mi sung panin Hushah khuami Sibbekai hi a

### Benjamin mi sung panin = from among the men of Benjamin
**Context:** Identifying the tribal origin of Abiezer.
**Example:** Benjamin mi sung panin Anathoth khuami Abiezer hi a

## Phrases & Usage

- **Topa’ nasepna khempeuh** = all the business of the Lord
  - Usage: Used to describe matters pertaining to God or religious duties.

- **kumpipa’ nasemna ah** = in the business/affairs of the king
  - Usage: Used to describe secular or royal administrative duties.

- **Pasian tawh a kisai na khempeuh** = every matter pertaining to God
  - Usage: Used to categorize religious responsibilities in official appointments.

- **a beh sungah lutang ahite** = the heads of their houses/clans
  - Usage: A formal way to refer to the patriarchal leaders of the tribes/families.

- **kha khat khit kha khat** = month by month
  - Usage: Indicates a regular, recurring monthly schedule.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X leh a sanggamte**
  - A common structure indicating the principal person (X) and their associates/brethren.
  - Example: Hashabiah leh a sanggamte

- **X a ukpi ahi hi**
  - Simple declarative sentence structure identifying X as the chief/leader. (X is the chief).
  - Example: Jerijah a ukpi ahi hi.

- **A kha [Number]na adingin a uk a [Number]-pa**
  - A standardized structure used to identify the officer responsible for a specific month's rotation (e.g., 'The [Number]th captain for the [Number]th month').
  - Example: A kha thumna adingin a uk a thumna-pa

- **X’ tapa Y**
  - Possessive structure indicating Y is the son of X (Y the son of X).
  - Example: Zabdiel’ tapa Jashobeam

- **X-te beh Y**
  - Structure used to identify lineage or clan affiliation (Y of the family/clan of X).
  - Example: Izrah-te beh Shamhuth
