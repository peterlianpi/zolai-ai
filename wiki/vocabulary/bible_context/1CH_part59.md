# 1CH - Part 59

## Vocabulary with Context

### sawmna = tenth
**Context:** Used to denote the tenth item in a sequence, specifically the tenth month's captain.
**Example:** A sawmna, a kha sawmna adingin...

### khen = course/division/group
**Context:** Refers to the division or contingent of men under a specific captain.
**Example:** ama khen sungah tul sawmnih leh tulli om hi.

### tul = thousand
**Context:** Used as a numerical unit (thousand) in counting the men in each division.
**Example:** tul sawmnih leh tulli om hi.

### sawm-le-khatna = eleventh
**Context:** Used to denote the eleventh item in a sequence (eleventh month's captain).
**Example:** A sawm-le-khatna, a kha sawm-le-khatna adingin...

### sawm-le-nihna = twelfth
**Context:** Used to denote the twelfth item in a sequence (twelfth month's captain).
**Example:** A sawm-le-nihna, a kha sawm-le-nihna adingin...

### minamte = tribes (plural)
**Context:** Refers to the tribes of Israel.
**Example:** Israel minamte tungah a ukpite...

### ukpite = rulers/princes
**Context:** Refers to the leaders appointed over the tribes of Israel.
**Example:** Israel minamte tungah a ukpite in...

### sanggamte = brethren/relatives
**Context:** Used here to describe Elihu's relationship to David (one of David's brethren).
**Example:** Judah’ adingin David, sanggamte’ sung panin Elihu ahi hi;

### langte = half (of a tribe)
**Context:** Used specifically to denote the 'half tribe' (e.g., half tribe of Manasseh).
**Example:** Manasseh minam a langte’ adingin...

### makaite = princes/leaders
**Context:** Refers to the overall leaders of the tribes of Israel.
**Example:** Hihte in Israel minamte’ makaite ahi-te hi.

### vantung aksi = stars of heaven
**Context:** Used metaphorically to describe a very large, uncountable number (like the stars).
**Example:** Israel mite vantung aksi zahin a tamin a bawl nading...

### thuciam khin = promised/decreed
**Context:** Refers to God's promise or decree regarding the number of descendants.
**Example:** Topa in thuciam khin ahih manin...

### nuaisiah = and under (age)
**Context:** Used in conjunction with age ('kum sawmnih' - twenty years) to specify the lower limit of the census.
**Example:** David in kum sawmnih nuaisiah a pha mite sim lo hi.

### kipan = began
**Context:** Indicates the start of an action (Joab starting the numbering).
**Example:** Joab in sim ding kipan a...

### henna = wrath/anger
**Context:** Refers to God's displeasure or wrath that fell upon Israel.
**Example:** Israel tungah Pasian’ henna hong tung a...

### kikum lo = was not recorded/put in the account
**Context:** Indicates that the count was not officially entered into the chronicles.
**Example:** tua a kisimte kikhum lo hi.

### vanmanpha = treasures/valuables
**Context:** Refers to the king's valuable possessions or stores.
**Example:** Kumpipa’ vanmanpha a ukte...

### koihnate = storehouses/depots
**Context:** Refers to places where goods (like treasures or wine) were stored.
**Example:** Kumpipa' vanmanpha koihnate-ah...

### khuaneute = captains/overseers (of smaller units)
**Context:** Used alongside cities and villages, likely referring to local overseers or garrisons.
**Example:** khuapi sungate, khuaneute leh tausangte, sunga omte...

### tausangte = castles/forts
**Context:** Refers to fortified places or strongholds.
**Example:** khuaneute leh tausangte, sunga omte...

### lo kho = field work/tillage
**Context:** Refers to the work related to cultivating the ground.
**Example:** tua ciangin lo kho dingin lo nasep a sem mite tungah...

### leenggui lote = vineyards (general)
**Context:** Refers to the general area or management of the vineyards.
**Example:** tua ciangin leenggui lote tungah Ramah khuami Shimei ahi hi;

### leenggahzu koihnate = wine cellars/wine stores
**Context:** Refers specifically to the storage places for the produce of the vineyards (wine).
**Example:** leenggui lo panin a piang leenggahzu koihnate’ adingin...

## Phrases & Usage

- **a kha sawmna sungin** = In the tenth month
  - Usage: Used to specify the time period corresponding to the tenth division/month.

- **mi sung panin** = from the family/lineage of
  - Usage: Indicates the ancestral origin of an individual, common in genealogical listings.

- **tul sawmnih leh tulli** = twenty and four thousand
  - Usage: A standard way to express the number 24,000 (20 thousand and 4 thousand).

- **Israel minamte’ makaite ahi-te hi** = These were the leaders/princes of the tribes of Israel
  - Usage: A concluding statement summarizing the status of the preceding list of names.

- **vantung aksi zahin a tamin a bawl nading** = as numerous as the stars of heaven
  - Usage: A hyperbolic expression used to emphasize a vast, uncountable quantity, referring to God's promise.

- **bang hang hiam cih leh hih thu hangin** = Because of some reason, and because of this matter
  - Usage: Introduces the reason why a certain event (the census not being completed) occurred, implying divine displeasure.

- **lo nasep a sem mite tungah** = Over those who did the work of the field
  - Usage: Designates responsibility over agricultural laborers.

## Grammar Patterns

- **A [ordinal number], a kha [ordinal number] adingin [Person] ahi hi**
  - This structure is used repeatedly to list the captains assigned to each month (e.g., 'The tenth [captain], for the tenth month, was [Person]'). It establishes a direct correlation between the sequential number and the month.
  - Example: A sawmna, a kha sawmna adingin Zerah mi sung panin Netofah khua mi Maharai ahi hi;

- **[Tribe/Group] mite’ adingin [Father]’ tapa [Son] ahi hi**
  - This is the standard pattern for identifying the appointed leader for a specific tribe, showing lineage via the possessive ('s) form of the father's name followed by 'tapa' (son of).
  - Example: Reuben mite’ adingin Zikri’ tapa Eliezer ahi hi;

- **[Possessor]' tapa [Name] ahi hi**
  - A consistent pattern for naming individuals by linking them to their father using the possessive marker and 'tapa' (son of).
  - Example: Adiel’ tapa Azmaveth ahi hi;

- **tua ciangin [Area/Group] tungah [Person] ahi hi**
  - Used to transition the list of officials to those responsible for specific domains (treasures, fields, vineyards), indicating oversight over that group.
  - Example: tua ciangin gam sunga van manpha koihnate... tungah Uzziah’ tapa Jonathan ahi hi;
