# 1CH - Part 26

## Vocabulary with Context

### tapa = son of
**Context:** Used repeatedly to denote lineage, similar to 'the son of' in English genealogies.
**Example:** leh Malkhijah’ tapa Pashhur

### ahi hi = they were / it is
**Context:** A concluding phrase indicating identity or state of being for the preceding list/subject.
**Example:** Maasai ahi hi

### banah = besides / in addition to
**Context:** Used to introduce additional groups or items following a primary list.
**Example:** Hihte banah amau' behte

### beh lutangte = heads of the clans/families
**Context:** Refers to the leaders of the respective family lines mentioned.
**Example:** a beh lutangte

### Pasian’ innah nasep nadingin = for the work of the service of the house of God
**Context:** Describes the purpose for which the men were qualified.
**Example:** Pasian’ innah nasep nadingin a siam mahmah mihing

### siam mahmah = very able / very skilled
**Context:** Intensifier describing the capability of the men.
**Example:** a siam mahmah mihing

### tulkhat leh zasagih leh sawmguk = one thousand and seven hundred and threescore (1760)
**Context:** A specific numerical count of the people.
**Example:** mihing tul khat leh za sagih leh sawmguk ahi hi.

### sung panin = from among / out of the group of
**Context:** Indicates selection from a larger tribal or familial group.
**Example:** Levi mite sung panin

### biakinn = place of worship / sanctuary
**Context:** Used here as a synonym for 'Pasian' inn' (House of God), referring to the Tabernacle.
**Example:** Pasian' biakinn-ah nasepna dingin

### khuaneute sungah a teeng = who dwelt in the villages of the...
**Context:** Describes the location where certain families resided.
**Example:** Netofah mite’ khuaneute sungah a teeng

### Kulhkongpi cingte = The gatekeepers
**Context:** The specific role assigned to this group (porters).
**Example:** Kulhkongpi cingte in: Shallum, Akkub...

### makaipa = chief / leader
**Context:** Indicates the person in charge of the gatekeepers.
**Example:** Shallum pen amau' makaipa ahi hi.

### nisuahna lam pangah = toward the east side
**Context:** Specifies a cardinal direction related to the location of the gates.
**Example:** Kumpipa' Kulhkongpi nisuahna lampangah a omte ahi-te hi.

### ahi ngei-te hi = they truly were / indeed they were
**Context:** Emphatic confirmation of their identity or role.
**Example:** Levi mite omna kongcingte ahi ngei-te hi.

### phual a ukte = those who were in charge of the sanctuary/altar area
**Context:** Refers to those who guarded the sacred area (Tabernacle/altar).
**Example:** Topa’ phual a ukte ahih-te mah bangin

### puanbuk kong = tent door / door of the tabernacle
**Context:** Refers specifically to the entrance of the portable sanctuary.
**Example:** puanbuk kong a cingte ahi-te hi.

### a beisa hun sungah = in time past / in the former time
**Context:** Indicates a historical period of leadership.
**Example:** Eleazar' tapa Finehas pen a beisa hun sungah amaute a ukpa hi a

### kikhopna puanbuk lutna = door of the tabernacle of the congregation
**Context:** Specific location of the entrance to the meeting tent.
**Example:** kikhopna puanbuk lutna-ah kongcingpa ahi hi.

### kiciamteh-te hi = they were reckoned / they were recorded
**Context:** Indicates that they were officially listed or accounted for.
**Example:** khangsimna-ah kiciamteh-te hi.

### thu-mupa = seer / prophet
**Context:** Title given to Samuel, indicating his prophetic role.
**Example:** David leh thumupa Samuel

### panmun = set office / appointed duty
**Context:** Refers to their established, regular assignment.
**Example:** amau' pan'mun-ah amaute phut hi.

### van manpha koihnate = precious things / treasures
**Context:** Refers to the valuable items kept within the sanctuary.
**Example:** Pasian' biakinn van manpha koihnate a uk ahi-te hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **leh ... a tapa ...** = and ... the son of ...
  - Usage: Standard genealogical connector used repeatedly to trace descent.

- **a khuaneute uh-a khangsimna ah** = in the genealogy of their clans
  - Usage: Used to describe the official record-keeping method based on family lineage.

- **a hun hunin ni sagih simin** = from time to time, every seven days
  - Usage: Describes the rotational schedule for duty.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X' tapa Y, a tapa Z**
  - A common structure for listing lineage, where 'tapa' (son of) connects individuals sequentially.
  - Example: Hashabiah, a tapa Azrikam, a tapa Hasshub' tapa Shemaiah

- **X ahi hi / X ahi-te hi**
  - The copula structure used at the end of sentences to state identity or existence, often translated as 'is/are'.
  - Example: Maasai ahi hi.

- **X in Y a uk**
  - Subject (X) performs the action (uk - guarded/oversaw) upon the object (Y).
  - Example: Shallum pen amau' makaipa ahi hi.
