# 1CH - Part 44

## Vocabulary with Context

### nasemte = servants/soldiers
**Context:** David took the shields of the servants of Hadadezer.
**Example:** David in Hadadezer’ nasemte in a puak kham kibawl lumte la-in Jerusalem ah ciahpih hi.

### puak kham kibawl lumte = shields of gold (literally: things made of gold/metal)
**Context:** The shields David took from Hadadezer's servants.
**Example:** David in Hadadezer’ nasemte in a puak kham kibawl lumte la-in Jerusalem ah ciahpih hi.

### sumngo = brass/bronze
**Context:** Material brought from Tibhath and Chun, used by Solomon for vessels.
**Example:** Solomon in tuate tawh sumngo tuikuangpi leh khuampite leh sumngo um-le-beelte a bawl hi.

### tuikuangpi = sea (specifically the 'brazen sea' in context)
**Context:** A large vessel Solomon made from the brass.
**Example:** Solomon in tuate tawh sumngo tuikuangpi leh khuampite leh sumngo um-le-beelte a bawl hi.

### galkap buppi = host/army
**Context:** Referring to Hadadezer's entire military force.
**Example:** David in Zobah kumpipa Hadadezer’ galkap buppi a zawhna thu...

### zawhna = defeat/conquest
**Context:** The act of David defeating Hadadezer's army.
**Example:** David in Zobah kumpipa Hadadezer’ galkap buppi a zawhna thu...

### hopih ding = to greet/inquire about welfare
**Context:** The purpose of sending Hadoram to David (to inquire about his well-being).
**Example:** Hadadezer simin a zawhna hangin a hopih ding leh a lungdampih dingin...

### lungdampih dingin = to congratulate/make happy
**Context:** The second purpose of sending Hadoram to David.
**Example:** Hadadezer simin a zawhna hangin a hopih ding leh a lungdampih dingin...

### kham ngun = silver and gold
**Context:** Precious metals taken as spoils of war.
**Example:** Amah in kham ngun, leh sumngo tawh kibawl na namkim khempeuh puak hi;

### ap khawm hi = dedicated/set apart
**Context:** David setting aside the spoils for the Lord.
**Example:** ...Topa’ tungah ap khawm hi.

### tul sawm leh tulgiat = eighteen thousand (literally: ten thousand and eight thousand)
**Context:** The number of Edomites slain by Abishai.
**Example:** Edom mite tulsawm leh tulgiat that hi.

### khuacingte koih hi = put garrisons
**Context:** David establishing military control over Edom.
**Example:** Tua ciangin amah in Edom ah khuacingte koih hi;

### thuman leh thutang = judgment and justice
**Context:** The manner in which David ruled Israel.
**Example:** amah in a mite khempeuh tungah thuman leh thutang zang hi.

### thuciamtehpa = recorder/chronicler
**Context:** The official title of Jehoshaphat in David's administration.
**Example:** Ahilud’ tapa Jehoshafat in thuciamtehpa ahi hi;

### lai-atpa = scribe
**Context:** The official title of Shavsha.
**Example:** Shavsha in lai-atpa ahi hi;

### maang liante = chiefs/principal men
**Context:** The role of David's sons in the king's service.
**Example:** David’ tapate in kumpipa’ nasepna-ah maang liante ahi uh hi.

### a tapa in ama tangin uk hi = his son reigned in his stead
**Context:** Describing the succession after Nahash died.
**Example:** Nahash si a, a tapa in ama tangin uk hi.

### thuman takin ka gamta ding hi = I will show kindness/deal kindly
**Context:** David's declaration of intent towards Hanun.
**Example:** Nahash’ tapa Hanun tungah thuman takin ka gamta ding hi,” a ci hi.

### huhau-in = to comfort/console
**Context:** The action David took to console Hanun.
**Example:** David in a pa' huhau-in amah a hehnem dingin kamtaite a sawl hi.

### ngaihsun na hi hiam = Do you think/Do you suppose
**Context:** The princes questioning Hanun's interpretation of David's messengers.
**Example:** David in nang kiangah hehnemte hong sawlna hangin na pa a pahtawi hi, ci-in ngaihsun na hi hiam?

### gamsung a pheklek ding = to search out/explore the land
**Context:** The princes suggesting the true, malicious intent of David's messengers.
**Example:** Gamsung a pheklek ding leh a lumlet ding leh gamsung a ensim dingin...

### khamul uh metsak a = shaved their beards
**Context:** The humiliating act Hanun performed on David's servants.
**Example:** Hanun in David' nasemte manin a khamul uh metsak a,

### kawngciang uhah a puante uh atkhiatsakna tawh = cutting off their garments by their buttocks
**Context:** The second part of the humiliation, exposing them.
**Example:** a kawng ciang uhah a puante uh atkhiatsakna tawh amaute daisakin a sawlkhia hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **a puak kham kibawl lumte la-in** = taking the shields made of gold/metal
  - Usage: Used to describe the act of seizing spoils of war (material objects).

- **sumngo tampi tak la hi** = took very much brass
  - Usage: Used to quantify the large amount of material acquired from a specific location.

- **a zawhna hangin** = because of his defeat/conquest
  - Usage: Used to introduce the reason for a subsequent action (sending a messenger).

- **a hehnem dingin** = in order to comfort him
  - Usage: Used to state the purpose of sending messengers (for consolation).

- **a paina peuhpeuh ah** = whithersoever he went
  - Usage: Used idiomatically to describe David's universal success in battle, implying divine favor wherever he went.

- **a tapa in ama tangin uk hi** = his son reigned in his place/stead
  - Usage: Standard phrase for succession upon a ruler's death.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X in Y la-in Z ah ciahpih hi.**
  - Structure indicating the subject (X) took object (Y) and brought/went (ciahpih hi) to location (Z). (Subject-Object-Action-Destination)
  - Example: David in Hadadezer’ nasemte in a puak kham kibawl lumte la-in Jerusalem ah ciahpih hi.

- **X in Y a zawhna thu Z in a zak ciangin,**
  - Subordinate clause structure indicating 'When Z heard the news of X's defeat of Y'. (Temporal clause)
  - Example: David in Zobah kumpipa Hadadezer’ galkap buppi a zawhna thu Hamath kumpipa To-u in a zak ciangin,

- **Amah in X, Y, leh Z tawh kibawl na namkim khempeuh puak hi.**
  - Structure listing various types of items (X, Y, Z) that were brought/taken, concluding with 'all kinds of things made of them'.
  - Example: Amah in kham ngun, leh sumngo tawh kibawl na namkim khempeuh puak hi;

- **X in Y tungah thuman leh thutang zang hi.**
  - Structure describing the manner of ruling: Subject (X) exercised/administered (zang hi) judgment (thuman) and justice (thutang) over the object (Y).
  - Example: amah in a mite khempeuh tungah thuman leh thutang zang hi.

- **X in Y kiangah Z a sawl hi.**
  - Structure indicating Subject (X) sent (sawl hi) messenger/person (Z) to recipient (Y).
  - Example: David in a pa' huhau-in amah a hehnem dingin kamtaite a sawl hi.
