# 1CH - Part 21

## Vocabulary with Context

### sanggamnu = sister
**Context:** Used to refer to a female sibling of a male figure (Gilead's sister).
**Example:** Tua ciangin Gilead’ sanggamnu Hammolekheth in...

### nei = had/begat (in this context)
**Context:** Used in the sense of 'bare' or 'had children' when referring to Hammoleketh having children.
**Example:** Hammolekheth in Ishhod, Abiezer, leh Mahlah nei hi.

### luppih = covered/concealed/went in to (wife)
**Context:** Used idiomatically to mean 'went in to his wife' (sexual intercourse leading to conception).
**Example:** Tua ciangin Efraim in a zi luppih a...

### gai-in = conceived/became pregnant
**Context:** Used immediately after the action of 'going in to the wife' to indicate conception.
**Example:** amah gai-in tapa khat nei hi;

### siatna = evil/badness/misfortune
**Context:** Refers to misfortune or evil happening within his house, leading to the naming of his son Beriah.
**Example:** ama inn sungah siatna tung ahih manin...

### lamnu = builder/maker/founder (female)
**Context:** Used to describe Sherah's role in building or establishing the towns (Bethhoron and Uzzen-sheerah).
**Example:** Bethhoron nuainung leh a tungnung leh Uzzenerah khua a lamnu ahi hi.

### kici = called/named
**Context:** Used to introduce a name given to a son (Refah).
**Example:** Tua ciangin Refah kici tapa khat nei a...

### khua = town/village/settlement
**Context:** Used frequently to denote the settlements or towns associated with larger geographical areas or cities (e.g., Beth-el's towns, Gath's towns).
**Example:** Beth-el leh a khuapite...

### nisuahna lamah = on the east side/towards the rising sun
**Context:** A directional phrase indicating location relative to a central point (Naaran was to the east).
**Example:** leh nisuahna lamah Na-aran...

### nitumna lamah = on the west side/towards the setting sun
**Context:** A directional phrase indicating location relative to a central point (Gezer was to the west).
**Example:** leh nitumna lamah Gezer leh a khuapite...

### uk-te hi = they ruled/they possessed
**Context:** Used to describe the extent of the territory controlled by Manasseh's sons.
**Example:** Dor leh a khuapite uk-te hi.

### teeng-te hi = they dwelt/they resided
**Context:** Used to state where the descendants of Joseph lived within the conquered territories.
**Example:** Hihte sungah Israel’ tapa Josef’ suante a teng-te hi.

### satpa = founder/establisher (male)
**Context:** Used to describe Malkhiel as the one who established or founded the town of Birzavith.
**Example:** Malkhiel pen Birzaith khuapi a satpa ahi hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **a tapa** = his son
  - Usage: Used repeatedly in genealogies to denote direct descent, often followed by the son's name.

- **a mah hehnem dingin** = to comfort him/to console him
  - Usage: Used when relatives come together to offer solace after a period of mourning.

- **a zi luppih a** = when he went in to his wife
  - Usage: A euphemistic or direct phrase indicating marital relations leading to conception.

- **a khuapite** = and its towns/and its villages
  - Usage: Used consistently after naming a major city to specify the surrounding dependent settlements included in the territory.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X’ tapa Y’ tapa Z’ suante ahi hi.**
  - Standard genealogical structure showing lineage: The descendants of X, son of Y, son of Z.
  - Example: Hihte in Manasseh’ tapa Makhir’ tapa Gilead’ suante ahi hi.

- **A tapa X, a tapa Y, a tapa Z ahi hi.**
  - Serial listing of descendants showing direct paternal line, emphasizing sequential generations.
  - Example: a tapa Shethelah, a tapa Bered, a tapa Tahath...

- **X in Y leh Z nei hi.**
  - Simple statement of possession or procreation: X had Y and Z (often used for daughters or multiple children).
  - Example: Hammolekheth in Ishhod, Abiezer, leh Mahlah nei hi.

- **X ahi-te hi.**
  - Plural copula structure used at the end of a list of sons/descendants, meaning 'they are/they were'.
  - Example: Ahian, Shekhem, Likhi, leh Aniam ahi-te hi.
