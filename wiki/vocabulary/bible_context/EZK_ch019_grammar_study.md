## Verses

### v1
**EN:** Moreover take thou up a lamentation for the princes of Israel,

**TDB77:** Israel uliante hih bangin tamlawh in hong ci hi.

**ZVS:** Israel uliante hih bangin tamlawh in hong ci hi:

**Grammar:** quotative: said/saying

### v2
**EN:** And say, What is thy mother? A lioness: she lay down among lions, she nourished her whelps among young lions.

**TDB77:** Na nu uh, humpinelkai tawh kibang! Humpinelkai dangte tawh kikhawlin, a note khoi hi.

**ZVS:** Na nu-te humpinelkai tawh kibang hi. Humpinelkai dangte tawh kikhawlin, a note a khoi hi.

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v3
**EN:** And she brought up one of her whelps: it became a young lion, and it learned to catch the prey; it devoured men.

**TDB77:** A note khat thuhilh a, hong khang cing hi. Sabet zong hong siam a, mi hong ne hi.

**ZVS:** A note khat thuhilh a, hong khangcing hi. Sabet zong hong siam a, mi hong ne hi.

### v4
**EN:** The nations also heard of him; he was taken in their pit, and they brought him with chains unto the land of Egypt.

**TDB77:** Mite in tua thu za-te a, gum sungah awksak-te hi. Bawm sungah khum-te a, Izipt gamah paipih-te hi.

**ZVS:** Mite in tua thu za-te a, gum sungah awksak-te hi. Bawm sungah khum-te a, Egypt gamah paipih-te hi.

**Grammar:** plural: uh marker

### v5
**EN:** Now when she saw that she had waited, and her hope was lost, then she took another of her whelps, and made him a young lion.

**TDB77:** A pi’ lametna teng bei ta hi, a no dang khat pantah leuleu a, a khangcing khat suah hi.

**ZVS:** A pi' lametnateng beita hi. A no dang khat pantah leuleu a, a khangcing khat a suah hi.

### v6
**EN:** And he went up and down among the lions, he became a young lion, and learned to catch the prey, and devoured men.

**TDB77:** Amah hong khangcing a, adangte tawh kikhawl hi. Sabet zong hong siam a, mi hong ne hi.

**ZVS:** Amah hong khangcing a, a dangte tawh kikhawl hi. Sabet zong hong siam a, mi hong ne hi.

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v7
**EN:** And he knew their desolate palaces, and he laid waste their cities; and the land was desolate, and the fulness thereof, by the noise of his roaring.

**TDB77:** Kulhpite sugawp a, khuapite siasak hi. Hawk zelzel a, a za mite lau mahmah hi.

**Grammar:** intensifier: very/greatly

### v8
**EN:** Then the nations set against him on every side from the provinces, and spread their net over him: he was taken in their pit.

**TDB77:** Amah hih dingin mite kikaikhawm a, mun khempeuh panin hong kuan-te hi. A ngenthang-te zak-te a, a thang-ah awksak-te hi.

**ZVS:** Amah a hih dingin mite kikaikhawm-te a, mun khempeuh panin hong kuan-te hi. A ngenthang-te zak-te a, thangah a awksak-te hi.

**Grammar:** purpose: in order to | reciprocal: ki- prefix | plural: uh marker

### v9
**EN:** And they put him in ward in chains, and brought him to the king of Babylon: they brought him into holds, that his voice should no more be heard upon the mountains of Israel.

**TDB77:** Bawm sungah khum-te a, Babilon kumpipa kiang tun-te hi. Israel mual tungte ah a hawkna a kizak nawn loh nadingin kikhumcip hi.

**ZVS:** Bawm sungah khum-te a, Babylon kumpipa kiang a tun'pih-te hi. Israel mualtung tengah ama hawkna a kizak nawnlohna dingin a khumcip-te hi.

**Grammar:** purpose: in order to | reciprocal: ki- prefix | plural: uh marker

### v10
**EN:** Thy mother is like a vine in thy blood, planted by the waters: she was fruitful and full of branches by reason of many waters.

**TDB77:** Na nu uh, lui tui gei-a a kisuan, leenggui tawh kibang hi. Tui tampi ngah a, a teh, a gah, pha hi.

**ZVS:** Na nu uh, luitui gei-ah a kisuan leenggui tawh kibang hi. Tui tampi ngah a, a teh, a gah, a pha hi.

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v11
**EN:** And she had strong rods for the sceptres of them that bare rule, and her stature was exalted among the thick branches, and she appeared in her height with the multitude of her branches.

**TDB77:** A gui hiangte hong lian a, kumpi ciangkhut hong suak hi. Leenggui khang a, van hong ban hi. A teh pha-in sang mahmah a, mi khempeuh in mu kim hi.

**ZVS:** A gui hiangte hong lian a, kumpi ciangkhut hong suak hi. Leenggui hong khang a, van hong ban hi. A teh pha-in sang mahmah a, mi khempeuh in mu kim hi.

**Grammar:** intensifier: very/greatly | serial verb: -in (and then) | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v12
**EN:** But she was plucked up in fury, she was cast down to the ground, and the east wind dried up her fruit: her strong rods were broken and withered; the fire consumed them.

**TDB77:** Ahi zongin hehna tawh leenggui kizung bawh a, leilak kiden’na hi. Nisuahna huihpi hong nung a, a gah teng keutumsak hi. A hiangte kikuai-tan a, keu-in kihaltum hi.

**ZVS:** Ahi zongin hehna tawh leenggui kizungbawh a, leilak kiden'na hi. Nisuahna huihpi hong nung a, a gahteng keutumsak hi. A hiangte kikuaitan a, keu-in kihaltum hi.

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then) | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v13
**EN:** And now she is planted in the wilderness, in a dry and thirsty ground.

**TDB77:** Tui om lohna lei keu gamah tu-in hih leenggui kituah hi.

**ZVS:** Tui omlohna lei keu gamah tu-in hih leenggui kituah hi.

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then) | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v14
**EN:** And fire is gone out of a rod of her branches, which hath devoured her fruit, so that she hath no strong rod to be a sceptre to rule. This is a lamentation, and shall be for a lamentation.

**TDB77:** Hih leenggui mei in kang a, a hiang a gah kangtum mang hi, a hiangte lian kik nawn lo ding a, kumpi ciangkhut suak kik ngei nawn lo ding hi. Hih a tunga pen tamlawhna hi a, a kisa zelzel mun khat ahi hi.

**ZVS:** Hih leenggui mei in kang a, a hiang a gah kangtummang hi. A hiangte liankik nawnlo ding a, kumpi ciangkhut suakkik ngei nawnlo ding hi. Hih a tunga pen tamlawhna hi a, a kisa zelzel ngei khat ahi hi.

**Grammar:** future: will | reciprocal: ki- prefix
