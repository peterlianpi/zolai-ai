# 1CH - Part 18

## Vocabulary with Context

### tapa = son
**Context:** Used to denote lineage, 'Zadok his son'.
**Example:** a tapa Zadok

### ahi = is/are (copula)
**Context:** Functions as the linking verb at the end of a clause.
**Example:** Ahimaaz ahi hi.

### gamgi = boundary/territory
**Context:** Refers to the extent or area of their dwelling.
**Example:** Amau’ gamgi huam sungah

### huam sungah = within the area/scope of
**Context:** Indicates location within a defined space or boundary.
**Example:** gamgi huam sungah

### ten'na = dwelling/residence
**Context:** Refers to their places of abode or castles.
**Example:** amau’ ten’na tawh kizui-in

### kizui-in = following/according to
**Context:** Indicates that the locations correspond to their residences.
**Example:** ten’na tawh kizui-in

### omna munte = places where they were/residences
**Context:** The specific locations of their settlements.
**Example:** amau’ omna munte in

### beh = clan/family/tribe
**Context:** Used to denote tribal or family groupings (e.g., Kohath-te beh).
**Example:** Kohathhte beh sung pan

### suante = descendants/offspring
**Context:** Refers to the descendants of Aaron.
**Example:** Aaron’ suante tungah

### kipia = given/allotted
**Context:** Past tense/passive form of giving something (the lot/cities).
**Example:** tungah a kipia

### tanh = lot/share/portion
**Context:** Refers to the portion assigned by lot.
**Example:** amau’ adingin a tanh-te om a

### kiim lononate = surrounding areas/suburbs
**Context:** Areas adjacent to the main city.
**Example:** Hebron leh a kiim lononate

### pia-te a = they gave (and)
**Context:** Action of giving, connected to the next clause.
**Example:** amaute tungah pia-te a

### ahih hangin = however/but
**Context:** Contrastive conjunction.
**Example:** ahih hangin khuapi lote leh

### lonona gamte = areas/territories of residence
**Context:** Used repeatedly to specify the territory associated with a city.
**Example:** Shekhem a lonona gamte tawh

### innkuan = household/family unit
**Context:** Refers to the specific family groupings within the larger tribe/clan.
**Example:** Amau’ innkuan khempeuh’ khuapite

### khempeuh = all/entirety
**Context:** Used to emphasize completeness.
**Example:** Amau’ innkuan khempeuh’

### sawm-le-thum = thirteen (ten and three)
**Context:** Compound number.
**Example:** sawm-le-thum ahi hi.

### aisana = remainder/residue
**Context:** Refers to the remaining portion of the Kohathites.
**Example:** aisaana tawh Manasseh minam a langte’

### langte = half/side
**Context:** Used here to mean 'half tribe'.
**Example:** Manasseh minam a langte’ gam sung panin

### sawmlenih = twelve (ten and two)
**Context:** Compound number.
**Example:** khuapi sawm-le-nih kipia hi.

### huan bangin = in this manner/as follows
**Context:** Introduces the description of how the cities were given.
**Example:** hih bangin Levi mite tungah

### zong = which/that (relative pronoun)
**Context:** Introduces a relative clause describing the cities.
**Example:** panin zong a min-a kilo hih khuapite

### min-a kilo hih = called by name
**Context:** Describes the cities that were specifically named.
**Example:** zong a min-a kilo hih khuapite

### pawl khat = one group/a portion
**Context:** Refers to a specific contingent or group within the Kohathites.
**Example:** Kohathte beh pawl khat in

### mual tung gam = mountain region/hill country
**Context:** Describes the geographical location of Shechem.
**Example:** Efraim mual tung gam sungah

## Phrases & Usage

- **a tapa** = his son
  - Usage: Standard possessive/lineage marker, preceding the name of the son.

- **a lonona gamte tawh** = with the territories of their residence
  - Usage: Used repeatedly to list cities along with their surrounding lands/suburbs.

- **innkuan-a kizui-in** = following their families/according to their households
  - Usage: Indicates that the distribution of cities was organized based on family units.

- **tua ciangin** = And then/After that
  - Usage: Transitional phrase marking the sequence of events or distribution.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X-te beh sung pan**
  - Indicates origin or source: 'from within the clan/family of X'.
  - Example: Kohathhte beh sung pan

- **A [noun] [location] a lonona gamte tawh**
  - Structure used to list cities and their associated territories (suburbs). 'A' acts as a definite article or marker for the noun.
  - Example: Shekhem a lonona gamte tawh

- **X-te’ gam sung panin**
  - Indicates the source territory from which cities were taken: 'out of the land of X'.
  - Example: Benjamin minamte’ gam sung panin

- **X tungah Y kipia hi**
  - Passive construction showing assignment: 'Y was given to X'.
  - Example: khuapi sawm kipia hi.
