# GEN - Part 1

## Vocabulary with Context

### kipat cil = beginning (at the start)
**Context:** Used to denote the very start of creation.
**Example:** A kipat cil-in Pasian in...

### piangsak = caused to be/created
**Context:** The verb form of 'piang' (to be/happen), used here for God's act of creation.
**Example:** Pasian in vantung leh leitung a piangsak hi.

### vantung = heaven/sky
**Context:** Refers to the expanse above the earth, created by God.
**Example:** Pasian in vantung leh leitung a piangsak hi.

### leitung = earth/world
**Context:** The physical world created by God.
**Example:** Pasian in vantung leh leitung a piangsak hi.

### lim leh meel = form and shape (without form)
**Context:** Describing the initial state of the earth (without form).
**Example:** Leitung in lim leh meel nei loin...

### awngthawlpi = void/empty/formless expanse
**Context:** Describes the state of the earth before structure was imposed.
**Example:** Leitung in lim leh meel nei loin a awngthawlpi ahi hi.

### thuk tuipi = deep waters/the deep
**Context:** Refers to the primordial waters covering the deep.
**Example:** A thuk tuipi tung tengah khuamialna om a...

### khuamialna = darkness
**Context:** The state of darkness covering the deep.
**Example:** A thuk tuipi tung tengah khuamialna om a...

### Pasian' Kha = Spirit of God
**Context:** The active agent of God moving over the waters.
**Example:** tua tuitung teng ah Pasian' Kha a gamtang hi.

### gamtang = moved/hovered/brooded
**Context:** Used to describe the action of the Spirit of God over the waters (similar to KJV 'moved').
**Example:** Pasian' Kha a gamtang hi.

### ci = said/called
**Context:** Used when God speaks a command or names something.
**Example:** Pasian in, “Khuavak om ta hen,” ci hi;

### om pah hi = it came to be immediately/it was so
**Context:** Indicates immediate fulfillment of God's command.
**Example:** tua ciangin khuavak om pah hi.

### hoih = good/right
**Context:** Used to describe God's assessment of the creation (e.g., light).
**Example:** Pasian in khuavak hoih hi ci-in mu hi;

### khenkhia = separated/divided
**Context:** The action of separating light from darkness.
**Example:** Pasian in khuamial panin khuavak khenkhia hi.

### Sun = Day
**Context:** The name given to the light.
**Example:** Pasian in khuavak pen Sun ci a...

### Zan = Night
**Context:** The name given to the darkness.
**Example:** ...khuamial pen Zan ci hi.

### nitak hong bei-in, zingsang hong tung a = When evening passed and morning came
**Context:** The standard phrase used to mark the end of a creation day.
**Example:** Nitak hong bei-in, zingsang hong tung a...

### laizang = midst/middle
**Context:** Location where the firmament is to be placed (in the midst of the waters).
**Example:** Tuite’ laizang-ah van kuumpi om hen la...

### van kuumpi = firmament/expanse
**Context:** The structure created to separate the waters.
**Example:** Pasian in van kuumpi bawl a...

### kikhensak = separate (reflexive/reciprocal)
**Context:** The function of the firmament: to divide the waters.
**Example:** tua van kuumpi in tui leh tui kikhensak hen,

### nuai-a = underneath
**Context:** Used spatially to describe the waters below the firmament.
**Example:** van kuumpi nuai-a om tuite...

### Vantung = Heaven (as a proper noun)
**Context:** The name given to the firmament.
**Example:** Pasian in van kuumpi pen Vantung ci hi.

### kikhawm = gather together
**Context:** The command for the waters to collect in one place.
**Example:** Tuite mun khatah kikhawm hen la...

### lei keu = dry land
**Context:** The land that appears after the waters gather.
**Example:** lei keu kidawk hen,

### kidawk = appear/show itself
**Context:** Used for the dry land appearing.
**Example:** lei keu kidawk hen,

### Tuipi = Seas
**Context:** The name given to the gathered waters.
**Example:** a kikaikhawm tuite pen Tuipite ci hi.

### kung tuamtuamte = various kinds of plants/grasses
**Context:** The general category of vegetation commanded to grow.
**Example:** Leitung in kung tuamtuamte piangsak hen la...

### tang a nei kungnote = herb yielding seed
**Context:** Specific type of plant mentioned in the creation command.
**Example:** tang a nei kungnote,

### a gah sungah tang a om singgah kungte = fruit tree yielding fruit whose seed is in itself
**Context:** Describing the fruit tree according to its reproductive nature.
**Example:** a gah sungah tang a om singgah kungte,

### amau’ nam ciatin = according to their kind
**Context:** Emphasizing reproduction true to species.
**Example:** amau' nam ciatin leitungah piangsak hen,

### piangsak hi = it brought forth/it produced
**Context:** The earth's action in producing vegetation.
**Example:** Leitung in kung tuamtuamte piangkhiasak a...

### ni thumna = the third day
**Context:** Marking the end of the third day of creation.
**Example:** ni thumna ahi hi.

### khuavakte = lights (celestial bodies)
**Context:** Referring to the sun, moon, and stars.
**Example:** vantungah khuavakte om hen;

### pawi hun leh khuahun = festivals and seasons
**Context:** One of the purposes of the celestial lights.
**Example:** tuate pen pawi hun leh khuahun,

## Phrases & Usage

- **A kipat cil-in** = In the beginning
  - Usage: Used to start a narrative about the absolute commencement of time or action.

- **ci-in mu hi** = saw that it was
  - Usage: Used after God observes a creation to confirm its quality (usually 'good').

- **Nitak hong bei-in, zingsang hong tung a** = And the evening and the morning were
  - Usage: The standard formula for concluding a day in the creation narrative.

- **Tua mah bangin piang pah hi** = And it was so immediately
  - Usage: Used to confirm the immediate fulfillment of a divine command.

## Grammar Patterns

- **Pasian in, [Command/Statement] ci hi; tua ciangin [Result] om pah hi.**
  - This structure shows the direct cause-and-effect of God's spoken word: God speaks (ci hi), and the result immediately occurs (om pah hi).
  - Example: Pasian in, “Khuavak om ta hen,” ci hi; tua ciangin khuavak om pah hi.

- **[Noun] pen [Name] ci a, [Noun] pen [Name] ci hi.**
  - This is the standard pattern for naming things created by God, using the verb 'ci' (to call/name).
  - Example: Pasian in khuavak pen Sun ci a, khuamial pen Zan ci hi.

- **A [Noun] tungah [Condition] om a, tua [Noun] teng ah [Action] hi.**
  - Describes the initial state of the earth, showing condition over a location (e.g., darkness over the deep waters).
  - Example: A thuk tuipi tung tengah khuamialna om a, tua tuitung teng ah Pasian' Kha a gamtang hi.
