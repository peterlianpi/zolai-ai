# 1CH - Part 52

## Vocabulary with Context

### sih = died
**Context:** Referring to the death of Nadab and Abihu.
**Example:** Ahih hangin a pa-te sih ma-in Nadab leh Abihu si-in...

### ta nei lo-te = had no children
**Context:** Describing the state of Nadab and Abihu after their death.
**Example:** Nadab leh Abihu si-in ta neilo-te ahih manin...

### siampi suak-te hi = became priests
**Context:** The result of Eleazar and Ithamar succeeding their deceased brothers.
**Example:** Eleazar leh Ithamar-te siampi suak-te hi.

### suan = descendant/son of
**Context:** Used to trace lineage (e.g., Zadok is the descendant of Eleazar).
**Example:** Eleazar’ suan Zadok, leh Ithamar’ suan Ahimelek'...

### huhna tawh = with the help of
**Context:** Indicates assistance provided by Ahimelek.
**Example:** leh Ithamar' suan Ahimelek' huhna tawh...

### kiseh bangin = as they were distributed/appointed
**Context:** Describes how David assigned them to their duties.
**Example:** David in amau’ nasepna ah a kiseh bangin...

### koih hi = placed/appointed
**Context:** The action David took in assigning the descendants.
**Example:** Aaron' suante a koih hi.

### ukpi = chief men/leaders
**Context:** Referring to the number of principal men found in each lineage.
**Example:** Eleazar' suante lakah ukpi tam om zaw ahih manin...

### pawl = group/division
**Context:** Used with numbers to denote the size of the divisions.
**Example:** Eleazar' suante pawl sawm-le-guk suahin, Ithamar' suante pawl giat suah hi.

### sawm-le-guk = sixteen (10 + 6)
**Context:** The specific number of chief men from Eleazar's line.
**Example:** Eleazar' suante pawl sawm-le-guk suahin...

### giat = eight
**Context:** The specific number of chief men from Ithamar's line.
**Example:** Ithamar' suante pawl giat suah hi.

### biakinn uliante = governors of the sanctuary
**Context:** One category of officials among the descendants.
**Example:** biakinn uliante leh Pasian’ uliante om tuak-te ahih manin...

### Pasian’ uliante = governors of the house of God
**Context:** The second category of officials.
**Example:** biakinn uliante leh Pasian’ uliante om tuak-te ahih manin...

### om tuak-te ahih manin = since they were found together/alike
**Context:** The reason for their equal appointment method.
**Example:** ...uliante om tuak-te ahih manin...

### kibangin = equally/alike
**Context:** Describing the manner of their appointment.
**Example:** amaute in a kibangin aisanna tawh amaute koih-te hi.

### aisanna = by lot/casting lots
**Context:** The method used for division.
**Example:** a kibangin aisanna tawh amaute koih-te hi.

### lai-atpa = scribe
**Context:** Describing Shemaiah's role.
**Example:** Levi mi Nethanel’ tapa ahi lai-atpa Shemaiah in...

### behlutangte = heads of families/chiefs
**Context:** Referring to the leaders present during the selection process.
**Example:** leh Levi mi beh lutangte’ omna ah...

### ciamteh hi = wrote down/recorded
**Context:** The action performed by Shemaiah.
**Example:** amaute ciamteh hi.

### kiteel = chosen/allotted
**Context:** Used to describe the selection for each lineage.
**Example:** Eleazar’ suante’ adingin khat kiteelin, tua ciangin Ithamar’ suante adingin khat kiteel a...

### kilaih sak niloh-te hi = they were made pleasing/acceptable (or perhaps 'they were confirmed')
**Context:** The final status of the selection process.
**Example:** kilaih sak niloh-te hi.

### tung = fell to/was assigned to
**Context:** Used in sequence to indicate which lot fell to which name.
**Example:** Aisaanna a masa pen Jehoiarib tung...

## Phrases & Usage

- **a pa-te a sih ma-in** = before their father died
  - Usage: Used to establish a time frame relative to a death.

- **si-in ta neilo-te ahih manin** = because they died and had no children
  - Usage: A causal clause explaining the necessity for succession.

- **lak sangin** = than (in comparison to)
  - Usage: Used for comparative statements, often preceding the larger quantity.

- **om tuak-te ahih manin** = since they were found together/alike
  - Usage: A causal phrase indicating similarity leading to a specific action.

- **a kibangin aisanna tawh** = by lot equally
  - Usage: Describes the method of fair distribution.

- **a sawm-le-khatna** = the eleventh
  - Usage: Ordinal numbering structure (sawm = ten, khat = one, -na = suffix for ordinal).

## Grammar Patterns

- **X' suan Y**
  - Possessive structure indicating lineage: 'Y, the descendant/son of X'. The apostrophe indicates the possessive marker on the preceding noun (Eleazar').
  - Example: Eleazar’ suan Zadok

- **X leh Y-te**
  - Pluralization using the suffix '-te' attached to the conjunction 'leh' (and). This structure groups X and Y together as a plural subject or object.
  - Example: Nadab leh Abihu-te

- **sawm-le-Xna**
  - Compound ordinal numbering structure for numbers 11 through 19, combining 'sawm' (ten), 'le' (and), and the ordinal suffix '-na' on the second number (X).
  - Example: a sawm-le-khatna (eleventh)

- **X ahih manin**
  - A common causal structure meaning 'because X is so' or 'since X happened'.
  - Example: ukpi tam omzaw ahih manin
