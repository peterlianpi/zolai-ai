## Vocabulary with Context

### pa = father
**Context:** Used to denote fatherhood in a lineage (Moza in Binea’ pa ahi hi - Moza was the father of Binea).
**Example:** Moza in Binea’ pa ahi hi

### tapa = son
**Context:** Used repeatedly to trace descent (Binea’ tapa Rafah - Binea's son Rapha).
**Example:** Binea’ tapa Rafah

### ahi hi = is/are/was/were
**Context:** The main copula verb, indicating existence or identity.
**Example:** Azel ahi hi

### guk = six
**Context:** Used as a cardinal number describing the quantity of sons.
**Example:** tapa guk nei

### min = name
**Context:** Referring to the names of the sons.
**Example:** hihte in amau’ min ahi hi

### leh = and
**Context:** A conjunction used to list items, often preceding the final item in a series.
**Example:** Obadiah, leh Hanan ahi-te hi

### khempeuh = all/entirety
**Context:** Used to emphasize that the preceding group constitutes the whole set.
**Example:** Hihte khempeuh in Azel’ tapate ahi-te hi

### sanggampa = brother (of the same father/lineage)
**Context:** Used to denote Eshek as Azel's brother.
**Example:** Azel’ sanggampa Eshek’

### tacil = firstborn
**Context:** Used to specify the first son in a sequence (Ulam, the firstborn).
**Example:** a tacil Ulam

### nihna = second
**Context:** Used to specify the second son in a sequence.
**Example:** a nihna Jeush

### thumna = third
**Context:** Used to specify the third son in a sequence.
**Example:** a thumna Elifelet

### thahat = mighty/strong
**Context:** Adjective describing the sons of Ulam as mighty men.
**Example:** a thahat galhangte

### galhang = archer/warrior (implied by context 'mighty men of valour, archers')
**Context:** Describing the capability of Ulam's sons.
**Example:** a thahat galhangte

### thal tawite = bowmen/those who handle bows
**Context:** Further description of Ulam's sons, likely archers.
**Example:** thal tawite

### tupa = grandson/descendant
**Context:** Used alongside 'tapa' (son) to indicate multiple generations of descendants.
**Example:** tapa tampi leh tupa tampi a neite hi

### pha = reach/amount to
**Context:** Used to state the total count reached (an hundred and fifty).
**Example:** mi za leh sawmnga pha-te hi

### minam = tribe/clan
**Context:** Referring to the tribal affiliation (Benjamin minamte).
**Example:** Benjamin minamte ahi hi

### ciangin = when/after/then
**Context:** Temporal marker indicating sequence of events (Tua ahih ciangin - After that/Then).
**Example:** Tua ahih ciangin Israel khempeuh khangsimna ah kiciamteh hi

### khangsimna = generation/lineage record
**Context:** Referring to the genealogical records.
**Example:** Israel khempeuh khangsimna ah kiciamteh hi

### kiciamteh = recorded/reckoned
**Context:** Passive verb indicating that the people were registered in the records.
**Example:** Israel khempeuh khangsimna ah kiciamteh hi

### kigelh = written/recorded
**Context:** Passive verb indicating where the records were kept (in the book).
**Example:** hihte kigelh hi

### thumanlohna = disobedience/unfaithfulness
**Context:** The reason given for the deportation (for their transgression).
**Example:** Amau’ thumanlohna hangin

### gimpiakna-in = by force/compulsion (implied: punishment)
**Context:** The manner in which they were taken captive (KJV: for their transgression).
**Example:** gimpiakna-in Babylon-ah salin

### salin = as captives/exiles
**Context:** The state in which the Judahites were taken to Babylon.
**Example:** Babylon-ah salin Judah mite kipaipih hi

### kipaipih = were led away/carried away
**Context:** The action taken against the Judahites.
**Example:** Judah mite kipaipih hi

### Tu-in = Now/At this time
**Context:** Transitional phrase marking the shift to describing the inhabitants who returned.
**Example:** Tu-in amau’ neihsa amau’ khuapite’ sungah a teeng kik masate

### khuapi = city/town
**Context:** The location where the first inhabitants settled.
**Example:** amau’ khuapite’ sungah

### teeng kik masate = the first inhabitants that dwelt again
**Context:** Refers to the initial settlers upon returning.
**Example:** a teeng kik masate pen

### nautangte = Israelites (lay people)
**Context:** Listing the groups of returning people, distinguishing them from priests/Levites.
**Example:** Israel nautangte, siampite, Levi mite

### siampite = priests
**Context:** One of the specific groups listed.
**Example:** siampite, Levi mite

### biakinn nasemte = temple servants (Nethinims)
**Context:** The group responsible for service in the sanctuary.
**Example:** leh biakinn nasemte ahi-te hi

### pawl khat = a portion/some
**Context:** Indicates that only a segment of these tribes settled in Jerusalem.
**Example:** Efraim, leh Manasseh mi pawl khat Jerusalem ah teeng-te ### makai = leader/chief
**Context:** Designating the head of a specific family line.
**Example:** a makai ahi hi

### Innkuan lutang = Head of the family/Chief of the household
**Context:** A title for the leader of a specific lineage (Asaiah).
**Example:** Innkuan lutang Asaiah

### sung panin = from among the line of
**Context:** Specifies the origin of the leader from a larger tribal division.
**Example:** Judah’ tapa Shelah mite sung panin

### innkuan = family/household
**Context:** Used to count the number of established families within a tribe.
**Example:** innkuan za guk leh sawmnga-le-guk ahi hi

### behcatah = according to their lineage/by their clan
**Context:** Indicates organization based on ancestral lines.
**Example:** amau’ beh ciatah beh lutangte

### beh lutangte = chiefs of the fathers/heads of the clans
**Context:** The leaders corresponding to the recorded lineages.
**Example:** beh ciatah beh lutangte ahi-te hi

### ukpipa = ruler/governor (chief)
**Context:** Used to describe Ahitub's role in the house of God.
**Example:** Pasian’ inn sung a ukpipa Ahitub

## Phrases & Usage

- **a tapa guk nei** = had six sons
  - Usage: Standard construction for stating the number of male offspring.

- **mi za leh sawmnga pha-te hi** = amounted to one hundred and fifty men
  - Usage: Used for stating a total count, often at the end of a descriptive list.

- **Amau’ thumanlohna hangin** = Because of their disobedience
  - Usage: Causal phrase explaining the reason for a subsequent action (exile).

- **a teeng kik masate pen** = the first ones who settled again
  - Usage: Identifies the initial returning population group.

- **innkuan za guk leh sawmnga-le-guk** = six hundred and fifty-six families
  - Usage: Specific numerical quantification of family units.

- **Pasian’ inn sung a ukpipa** = the ruler of the house of God
  - Usage: A formal title describing a high-ranking religious official (Priest).

## Grammar Patterns

- **X in Y pa ahi hi**
  - Possessive/Relational structure: X is the father of Y. (e.g., Moza in Binea’ pa ahi hi).
  - Example: Moza in Binea’ pa ahi hi

- **X, a tapa Y, a tapa Z ahi hi**
  - Linear descent structure: X, whose son is Y, whose son is Z. This is the standard way to list successive generations.
  - Example: Binea’ tapa Rafah, a tapa Eleasah, a tapa Azel ahi hi.

- **X’ tapate in: A, B, leh C ahi hi**
  - Listing the sons of X: A, B, and C were the sons.
  - Example: Azel’ sanggampa Eshek’ tapate in: a tacil Ulam, a nihna Jeush, leh a thumna Elifelet ahi hi.

- **X khempeuh in Y ahi-te hi**
  - Concluding statement of identity/membership: All of X are Y.
  - Example: Hihte khempeuh in Azel’ tapate ahi-te hi.

- **X sung panin Y a makai ahi hi**
  - Identification of a leader from a larger group: From among the line of X, Y was the leader.
  - Example: Judah’ tapa Perez' tapate sung panin Bani' tapa Imri... a makai ahi hi.
