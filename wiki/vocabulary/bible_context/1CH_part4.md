# 1CH - Part 4

## Vocabulary with Context

### sih ciangin = when (he/she) died
**Context:** Used to mark the transition of power after a ruler's death.
**Example:** Jobab a sih ciangin ama tangin Teman mite gam mi Husham in uk hi.

### tangin = in his/her place/stead
**Context:** Indicates succession to a position of rule.
**Example:** Jobab a sih ciangin ama tangin Teman mite gam mi Husham in uk hi.

### gam mi = person of the land / inhabitant of the region
**Context:** Identifies the origin or affiliation of the ruler.
**Example:** Teman mite gam mi Husham in uk hi.

### uk hi = ruled
**Context:** The main verb indicating the act of reigning.
**Example:** Husham in uk hi.

### gamsungah = in the land of
**Context:** Specifies the geographical area where an action (smote/defeated) took place.
**Example:** Moab gamsungah Midian a zopa

### zopa = one who smote/defeated
**Context:** Describes Hadad's past military achievement related to Midian.
**Example:** Midian a zopa, Bedad’ tapa Hadad in ama tangin uk hi

### khuapi min = name of his city
**Context:** Identifies the capital or residence of the ruler.
**Example:** ama khuapi min in Avith ahi hi.

### khuami = resident of the town/city
**Context:** Identifies the origin of Samlah.
**Example:** Masrekah khuami Samlah in uk hi.

### gun gei-a om = situated by the bank/side of the river
**Context:** Geographical description of Rehoboth relative to the Euphrates river.
**Example:** Eufrates gun gei-a om Rehoboth khuami Shawl in uk hi.

### tapa = son of
**Context:** Standard patronymic marker.
**Example:** Akbor’ tapa Baalhanan in uk hi.

### zi = wife
**Context:** Refers to the wife of the ruler Hadad.
**Example:** ama zi’ min in Mehetabel hi a

### tanu = daughter of
**Context:** Indicates lineage, specifically Matred's daughter.
**Example:** Matred’ tanu ahi hi.

### si hi = died
**Context:** Simple statement of death.
**Example:** Tua ciangin Hadad si hi.

### ukpite = dukes/chieftains
**Context:** Plural term for the rulers/heads of Edom.
**Example:** Edom ukpite in: ukpi Timna...

### leh = and
**Context:** Conjunction used to list names.
**Example:** Magdiel, leh Iram ahi uh hi.

### tapate in = the sons of
**Context:** Introduction to the list of Jacob's sons (Israel's sons).
**Example:** Israel’ tapate in: Reuben, Simeon...

### ta cil = firstborn son
**Context:** Specifically identifies Er as the firstborn of Judah.
**Example:** Tu-in Judah’ tacil Er in Topa’ muhna ah gilo a

### gilo = did evil/was wicked
**Context:** Describes Er's actions in the sight of the Lord.
**Example:** Topa’ muhna ah gilo a

### that hi = killed (him)
**Context:** The Lord's action against Er due to his wickedness.
**Example:** tua ciangin Topa in amah that hi.

### zong = in-law (daughter-in-law)
**Context:** Refers to Tamar, who married Er and Onan.
**Example:** A tapa’ zi Tamar in zong amah tawh Perez leh Zerah nei hi.

### vekpi-in = altogether / in total
**Context:** Indicates the total count of Judah's sons.
**Example:** Judah in a vekpi-in tapa nga nei hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **ama tangin uk hi** = reigned in his stead
  - Usage: Used repeatedly to denote the succession of kings/rulers.

- **Topa’ muhna ah gilo a** = was evil in the sight of the Lord
  - Usage: A standard biblical phrase describing moral failing or sin.

- **gun gei-a om** = situated by the river bank
  - Usage: Used for precise geographical location description.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X a sih ciangin Y in ama tangin Z in uk hi.**
  - This is the primary historical narrative structure for succession: 'When X died, Y reigned in his stead as Z.' (X = predecessor, Y = successor, Z = identifier/origin).
  - Example: Jobab a sih ciangin ama tangin Teman mite gam mi Husham in uk hi.

- **ama khuapi min in [Name] ahi hi.**
  - Structure used to state the name of the ruler's capital city.
  - Example: ama khuapi min in Avith ahi hi.

- **ama zi’ min in [Name] hi a, [Relation] ahi hi.**
  - Structure used to detail the wife's name and her lineage.
  - Example: ama zi’ min in Mehetabel hi a, Mezahab’ tanu ahi Matred’ tanu ahi hi.

- **X tapate in: Y, Z, ... ahi uh hi.**
  - Standard structure for listing the sons of a patriarch (e.g., Judah's sons, Israel's sons).
  - Example: Judah’ tapate in: Er, Onan, leh Shelah ahi uh hi.
