# 1CH - Part 7

## Vocabulary with Context

### pa = father (of)
**Context:** Used to denote paternity, 'father of X'.
**Example:** Attai in Nathan’ pa hi a

### ahi = is/was (a state of being)
**Context:** Used as a copula, often appearing at the end of clauses describing identity or state.
**Example:** Nathan in Zabab’ pa ahi hi.

### sanggampa = brother (of the same father/lineage)
**Context:** Refers to a brother within a lineage context, specifically Caleb being the brother of Jerahmeel.
**Example:** Jerahme-el’ sanggampa Kaleb’ tapate in

### tapa = son (of)
**Context:** Used to denote sonship, 'son of X'.
**Example:** Kaleb’ tapate in

### tacil = firstborn
**Context:** Used to specify the firstborn son/descendant.
**Example:** a tacil Mareshah ahi hi

### leh = and
**Context:** Conjunction used to connect items in a list.
**Example:** Korah, Tappuah, Rekem, leh Shema ahi-te hi.

### zineu = concubine
**Context:** Specifically refers to Caleb's secondary wife/concubine.
**Example:** Kaleb’ zineu Efah in

### nei = bore/had (children)
**Context:** Verb used for giving birth or having offspring.
**Example:** Efah in Haran, Moza, leh Gazez nei hi

### kici = called/named
**Context:** Used to introduce the name of someone or something.
**Example:** Haran in Gazez kici tapa khat nei hi.

### suan leh khak = descendants and posterity (offspring)
**Context:** A compound term referring to descendants or lineage.
**Example:** Hihte in Kaleb’ suan leh khakte ahi-te hi.

### zi = wife
**Context:** Refers to Caleb's wife, Efrath.
**Example:** Kaleb’ zi Efrath’ tacil Hur’ tapate in

## Phrases & Usage

- **X’ pa hi a** = X is the father of
  - Usage: Standard construction for establishing direct paternal lineage in a sequence.

- **a tacil Y ahi hi** = was the firstborn Y
  - Usage: Used to identify the firstborn son in a list of descendants.

- **X’ tapa Y ahi hi** = The son of X is Y
  - Usage: Used to state the identity of a son belonging to a specific father.

- **X khuapi a sat Y** = Y who founded/established the city of X
  - Usage: Used when a descendant is associated with founding or being the namesake of a city.

## Grammar Patterns

- **A in B’ pa hi a, B in C’ pa ahi hi.**
  - A sequential chain of descent (A begat B, and B begat C). The structure uses 'pa' (father of) repeatedly.
  - Example: Attai in Nathan’ pa hi a, Nathan in Zabab’ pa ahi hi.

- **X’ tapate in: Y, Z, leh W ahi-te hi.**
  - Listing the sons of X. 'tapate' (sons) is followed by a list connected by 'leh' (and), concluding with the plural marker 'ahi-te hi' (they are).
  - Example: Hebron’ tapate: Korah, Tappuah, Rekem, leh Shema ahi-te hi.

- **Kaleb’ zineu X in Y leh Z nei hi.**
  - Describing the offspring of a concubine (zineu). 'nei' functions as the verb 'bore/had'.
  - Example: Kaleb’ zineu Efah in Haran, Moza, leh Gazez nei hi.
