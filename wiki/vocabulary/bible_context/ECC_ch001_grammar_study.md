## Verses

### v1
**EN:** The words of the Preacher, the son of David, king in Jerusalem.

**TDB77:** Jerusalem khua sungah kumpi a sem, David’ tapa Thugen Siampa’ thugente;

**ZVS:** Jerusalem khua sungah kumpi a sem, David' tapa, Thugen Siampa' thugente:

### v2
**EN:** Vanity of vanities, saith the Preacher, vanity of vanities; all is vanity.

**TDB77:** Thugen Siampa in hih bangin ci hi: A mawknapi, a mawknapi, na khempeuh a mawknapi,

**ZVS:** Thugen Siampa in hih bangin ci hi: A mawknapi, a mawknapi, na khempeuh a mawknapi.

**Grammar:** quotative: said/saying

### v3
**EN:** What profit hath a man of all his labour which he taketh under the sun?

**TDB77:** Gimpi-a nisa thuak-a nasepna-ah mihing in bang noptuamna ngah ahi hiam?

**ZVS:** Gimpi-a nisa thuakin nasepna-ah mihing in bang noptuam ngah ahi hiam?

### v4
**EN:** One generation passeth away, and another generation cometh: but the earth abideth for ever.

**TDB77:** Khang khat bei-in khang khat hong khang a, ahi zongin leitung kibang bang niloh hi.

**ZVS:** Khang khat bei-in khang khat hong tung a, ahi zongin leitung kibangbang niloh hi.

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then) | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v5
**EN:** The sun also ariseth, and the sun goeth down, and hasteth to his place where he arose.

**TDB77:** Ni hong suakin ni tum a, a suakkik dingin tumzel hi.

**ZVS:** Ni hong suakin ni tum a, a suakkik dingin tum zel hi.

**Grammar:** purpose: in order to

### v6
**EN:** The wind goeth toward the south, and turneth about unto the north; it whirleth about continually, and the wind returneth again according to his circuits.

**TDB77:** Leitaw lam-ah huih nungin, leilu lam-ah nung leuleu a, a zuihna ngei teng mah zui-in, nungnung niloh hi.

**ZVS:** Leitaw lamah huih nungin leilu lamah nung leuleu a, a zuih ngeina teng mah zui-in nungnung niloh hi.

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then)

### v7
**EN:** All the rivers run into the sea; yet the sea is not full; unto the place from whence the rivers come, thither they return again.

**TDB77:** Lui khempeuh tuipi-in luangsuk a, ahi zongin tuipi dim nai lo hi; tuinak-ah tuite tung kik zelin, a nak panin hong luangsuk kik zel hi.

**ZVS:** Lui khempeuh tuipi-ah luangsuk a, ahi zongin tuipi dim nailo hi. Tuipi tuite a khu-in lengin tuinakah guahin zu a, a nak panin hong luangsuk kik zel hi.

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then) | negation: lo | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v8
**EN:** All things are full of labour; man cannot utter it: the eye is not satisfied with seeing, nor the ear filled with hearing.

**TDB77:** Na khempeuh lunggim huai a, kuama gen zawh ding hi lo hi; Mit in a muh cim thei lo a, bil in a zak cim thei lo hi.

**ZVS:** Na khempeuh lunggimhuai a, kuama genzawh ding hilo hi. Mit in a muh cim theilo a, bil in a zak cim theilo hi.

**Grammar:** future: will | negation: lo

### v9
**EN:** The thing that hath been, it is that which shall be; and that which is done is that which shall be done: and there is no new thing under the sun.

**TDB77:** A omsa mah bangin hong piang kik ding a, a kisemsa bangin hong kisem kik ding hi. Leitungah a thak na khat beek om lo hi.

**ZVS:** A omsa mah bangin hong piang kik ding a, a kisemsa bangin hong kisem kik ding hi. Leitungah a thak na khat beek omlo hi.

**Grammar:** future: will | negation: lo | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v10
**EN:** Is there any thing whereof it may be said, See, this is new? it hath been already of old time, which was before us.

**TDB77:** Hih in a thak ahi hi, a kici thei ding khat beek om lo hi. Eite’ khan’ ma-in, a omsa vive ahi hi.

**ZVS:** Hih pen a thak ahi hi, a kici thei ding khat beek omlo hi. Eite' khan' ma-in a omsa vive ahi hi.

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then) | negation: lo | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v11
**EN:** There is no remembrance of former things; neither shall there be any remembrance of things that are to come with those that shall come after.

**TDB77:** A beisa nate kiphawk kha nawn lo a, hong tung lai ding thute zong a khang kikte in phawk nawn lo ding hi.

**ZVS:** A beisa nate kiphawkkha nawnlo a, hong tung lai ding thute zong a khangkikte in phawk nawnlo ding hi.

### v12

**TDB77:** Thugen siampa keimah in Jerusalem khua-ah Israel kumpi ka hi ngei hi.

**ZVS:** Thugen Siampa kei pen Jerusalem khua-ah Israel kumpipa ka hi ngei hi.

### v13
**EN:** And I gave my heart to seek and search out by wisdom concerning all things that are done under heaven: this sore travail hath God given to the sons of man to be exercised therewith.

**TDB77:** Hih leitungah a kisem na khempeuh en telin a thu kan ding ka ngaihsun hi. Pasian in tua bang nasep ei tungah hong guat pen a nuam het lo nasep khat ahi hi.

**ZVS:** Hih leitungah a kisem na khempeuh entelin a thu kan ding ka ngaihsun hi. Pasian in tua bang nasep ei tungah hong guat pen a nuam hetlo nasep khat ahi hi.

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v14
**EN:** I have seen all the works that are done under the sun; and, behold, all is vanity and vexation of spirit.

**TDB77:** Leitungah a kisem khempeuh ka mu khin a, tuate khempeuh a mawknapi na hi a, huihpi nungdelh tawh kibang hi.

**ZVS:** Leitungah a kisem khempeuh ka mu khin a, tuate khempeuh a mawknapi na hi a, huihpi nung tawh kibang lel hi.

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v15
**EN:** That which is crooked cannot be made straight: and that which is wanting cannot be numbered.

**TDB77:** A kawisate kimamsak thei lo a, a om lopi a omin kiseh thei lo hi.

**ZVS:** A kawisate kimamsak theilo a, a omlopi a omin kiseh theilo hi.

**Grammar:** negation: lo | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v16
**EN:** I communed with mine own heart, saying, Lo, I am come to great estate, and have gotten more wisdom than all they that have been before me in Jerusalem: yea, my heart had great experience of wisdom and knowledge.

**TDB77:** “Pilna sangpi ka nei a, keima mai-ah Jerusalem a uk midangte khempeuh sangin ka pilzaw hi; pilna leh theihna a kici khempeuh ka theih loh om lo hi,” ka kici hi.

**ZVS:** “Pilna sangpi ka nei a, keima mai-ah Jerusalem a uk dangte khempeuh sangin ka pilzaw hi. Pilna leh theihna a kici khempeuh ka theiloh omlo hi,” ka ci hi.

**Grammar:** negation: lo | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v17
**EN:** And I gave my heart to know wisdom, and to know madness and folly: I perceived that this also is vexation of spirit.

**TDB77:** Pilna leh haina, theihna leh mawkna, a kilamdan’na ka zong hi. Ahi zongin tua bang ka hahkatna zong a mawknapi ahih lam ka phawk hi.

**ZVS:** Pilna leh haina, theihna leh mawlna, a kilamdan'na ka zong hi. Ahi zongin tua bang ka hahkatna zong a mawknapi ahihlam ka phawk hi.

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

**EN:** For in much wisdom is much grief: and he that increaseth knowledge increaseth sorrow.
