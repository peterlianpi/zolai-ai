## Phrases & Usage

- **ka min dingin** = for my name's sake
  - Usage: Used when an action is performed to honor or establish the reputation of God (or a king).

- **hong ci hi** = he said
  - Usage: Standard narrative marker for direct speech.

- **Topa na Pasian’ inn na lamna-ah** = in the building of the house of the Lord your God
  - Usage: Refers specifically to the construction of the Temple.

- **Thakhauh in la, hang in** = Be strong and courageous
  - Usage: A formal encouragement, often preceding a difficult task.

- **Topa in nang hong ompih ta hen** = May the Lord be with you
  - Usage: A blessing or farewell statement invoking divine presence.

- **na kiim khempeuh uhah** = in all your borders/regions
  - Usage: Referring to the extent of the territory under control.

## Grammar Patterns

- **Nangmah in [Action] na hih manin**
  - Causal structure: 'Because you did [Action]...'. Here, it links the past violent actions to the present consequence (not building the Temple).
  - Example: nangmah in keima mai leitungah sisan tua zahta luangsak na hih manin

- **Amah in [Noun] hi-in, kei [Noun] ka hi ding a**
  - Establishes a relationship between the son (Amah) and the father (Kei/I) regarding their roles (son/father) and future status (king/father of the king).
  - Example: Amah ka tapa hi-in, kei, ama pa ka hi ding a

- **[Condition] ding hi/ding hi.**
  - Future tense/obligation marker using '-ding' (will/shall), often used in prophecy or instruction.
  - Example: Amah in keima min dingin biakinn khat lam ding hi.

- **Topa in [Object] [Action] ta hen.**
  - Optative/Blessing structure using '-ta hen' (May [subject] do [action] to [object]).
  - Example: Topa in nang hong ompih ta hen.

- **Tu-in [Subject] [Verb] a, [Subject] [Verb] hi.**
  - Narrative summary structure, often used to conclude a period of time or an action.
  - Example: Tu-in David teekin kum tampi pha a, amah in Israel tungah kumpi-in a tapa Solomon a koih hi.
