## Vocabulary with Context

### pa = father
**Context:** Used to denote the father of a person, e.g., 'Ahihub’ pa Heglam' (Ahihub's father Heglam).
**Example:** Ahihub’ pa Heglam a kici Gera ahi-te hi.

### kici = called/named
**Context:** Used to state the name or title of someone, often following the person's description.
**Example:** Ahihub’ pa Heglam a kici Gera ahi-te hi.

### zite = wives (plural of zi)
**Context:** Refers to the wives of Shaharaim.
**Example:** Shaharaim in a zite ahi Hushim leh Baara a mak khit ciangin...

### mak = to send away/dismiss
**Context:** Used in the context of Shaharaim sending away his wives.
**Example:** Shaharaim in a zite Hushim leh Baara a mak khit ciangin...

### tapate = sons (plural of tapa)
**Context:** Used generally for male offspring.
**Example:** Moab gam sungah tapate nei hi.

### nei = to have/to take (a wife)/to beget
**Context:** Used here in the sense of 'taking wives' or 'having children' (in the context of the KJV translation, it implies begetting children through marriage).
**Example:** Moab gam sungah tapate nei hi.

### zikik = wife (formal/specific)
**Context:** Used specifically for Hodesh, indicating she was his wife.
**Example:** Amah in a zikik Hodesh tawh a neih a tapate in:

### tawh = with
**Context:** Indicates accompaniment or the person through whom something is done (here, marriage/begetting).
**Example:** Amah in a zikik Hodesh tawh a neih a tapate in:

### beih = clan/family/tribe
**Context:** Refers to the lineage or family group ('beih sungah' - within the clan).
**Example:** Hihte in amau’ beh sungah innkuan lutangte ahi-te hi.

### innkuan lutangte = heads of the household/family leaders
**Context:** Describes the status of the listed individuals within their clan.
**Example:** Hihte in amau’ beh sungah innkuan lutangte ahi-te hi.

### tawh zong = also with
**Context:** Indicates an additional wife/union.
**Example:** Amah in Hushim tawh zong a neih a tapate in:

### khuapi = town/city
**Context:** Refers to the settlements built by Shemed (Ono and Lod).
**Example:** leh Ono leh Lod a khuapite tawh a sat Shemed,

### sat = built/made
**Context:** Past tense verb indicating construction.
**Example:** leh Ono leh Lod a khuapite tawh a sat Shemed,

### taisak = drove away/expelled
**Context:** Describes the action Beriah and Shema took against the inhabitants of Gath.
**Example:** Gath a, teengte a taisak Aijalon a, a teeng beh lutangte

### suante = descendants/offspring (plural)
**Context:** Used interchangeably with 'tapate' (sons) to denote lineage.
**Example:** Zebadiah, Meshullum, Hizki, Heber, Ishmerai, Izliah, leh Jobab-te pen Elpaal' suante ahi-te hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **a kici** = who is called
  - Usage: Used to introduce the name of a person following a description or title.

- **a mak khit ciangin** = after he sent them away
  - Usage: Temporal clause indicating an action that preceded the main event (begetting children).

- **beh sungah** = in the clan/family
  - Usage: Specifies the social unit or lineage to which someone belongs.

- **a khuapite tawh a sat** = who built the towns of
  - Usage: Describes the builders and the cities they established.

- **teengte a taisak** = who drove away the inhabitants
  - Usage: A relative clause describing the historical action of a group against another group.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X in Y tawh a neih a Z**
  - Structure indicating 'X, through/with his wife Y, begat Z (sons)'. Demonstrates possession/relationship leading to offspring.
  - Example: Amah in a zikik Hodesh tawh a neih a tapate in: Jobab, Zibia, Mesha, Malkam,

- **A, B, leh C ahi-te hi.**
  - Simple declarative sentence structure listing individuals and concluding with 'they are' (ahi-te hi).
  - Example: Jeuz, Sakhia, leh Mirmah ahi-te hi.

- **X' tapate in: Y, Z, leh W**
  - Possessive structure showing lineage: 'The sons of X are Y, Z, and W'.
  - Example: Elpa-al’ tapate in: Eber, Misham, leh Ono leh Lod a khuapite tawh a sat Shemed,

- **Hihte pen amau beh sungah innkuan lutangte ahi-te hi.**
  - Identification sentence: 'These (Hihte) are the leaders of the household within their clan.' Uses 'pen' (topic marker) for emphasis.
  - Example: Hihte pen amau beh sungah innkuan lutangte ahi-te hi.
