# 1CH - Part 40

## Vocabulary with Context

### tungah = to/upon
**Context:** Indicates the recipient or object of an action or statement, often used with covenants or laws.
**Example:** Jakob tungah thu ciamin

### kipsak = to confirm/establish/secure
**Context:** Used in the context of confirming a covenant or law.
**Example:** amah in a kipsak

### tawntung = everlasting/eternal
**Context:** Describes the nature of the covenant.
**Example:** tawntung thuciamna-in

### thuciamna = covenant/promise
**Context:** Refers to a solemn agreement or promise.
**Example:** tawntung thuciamna-in

### hong pia-in = to give (as a gift/bestowal)
**Context:** Used when bestowing the land of Canaan.
**Example:** Kanaan gam hong pia-in

### gamhluah = inheritance/allotment of land
**Context:** Refers to the portion of land designated as an inheritance.
**Example:** gamhluahin na tanh dingin

### tanh = to measure out/allot
**Context:** Used in connection with measuring out the land inheritance.
**Example:** gamhluahin na tanh dingin

### phawk = to remember/recall
**Context:** Used when recalling the words spoken previously.
**Example:** a cihte phawk tawntung hi

### tawmin = to be few/small in number
**Context:** Describes the small number of people.
**Example:** Amaute a mi-in tawmin

### gentaak loin = without being spoken of/without being mentioned (or perhaps 'without being provoked' in some contexts, but here likely 'without being mentioned/reproved')
**Context:** Used to describe how they were treated when few in number.
**Example:** gentaak loin peemta hi uh a

### peemta = to wander/migrate/move about
**Context:** Describes the movement of the people from place to place.
**Example:** gentaak loin peemta hi uh a

### kawikawi = to wander/roam/move around
**Context:** Describes the movement between nations.
**Example:** minam khat pan nam khatah vak kawikawi-in

### nengniam sak = to oppress/do wrong to/treat unjustly
**Context:** Describes the action God prevented others from doing to His people.
**Example:** kuamah nengniam sak lo hi

### hangin = because of/for the sake of
**Context:** Indicates the reason for God's action (reproving kings).
**Example:** Amaute' hangin amah in kumpite taigawp a

### taigawp = to rebuke/reprove/scold (often strongly)
**Context:** Used for God rebuking kings.
**Example:** kumpite taigawp a

### sathau nilhte = anointed ones (literally: 'my oiled ones')
**Context:** A direct reference to God's chosen people or prophets (anointed ones).
**Example:** Ka sathau nilhte lawng kei un

### lawng = to touch (negatively)
**Context:** Used in the negative command not to touch the anointed ones.
**Example:** Ka sathau nilhte lawng kei un

### kamsangte = prophets (literally: 'my mouth-speakers')
**Context:** Refers to God's messengers.
**Example:** ka kamsangte tungah siatna hih kei un

### hotkhiatna = salvation/deliverance
**Context:** Refers to the act of saving or delivering.
**Example:** ama hotkhiatna thu gengen un

### minthan'na = glory/fame
**Context:** Used when declaring God's glory among the nations.
**Example:** ama minthan'na genkhia un la

### lamdang = marvelous/wonderful
**Context:** Describes God's works.
**Example:** a lamdang ama septe

### nakpi takin = greatly/mightily
**Context:** Adverbial phrase indicating the manner in which God should be praised.
**Example:** nakpi takin phat ding ahi hi

### lamdang sakzawk = more marvelous/more wonderful
**Context:** Comparative usage, stating God is more wonderful than all other gods.
**Example:** pasian khempeuh sangin lamdang sakzawk ding ahi hi

### milim = idol
**Context:** Used to describe the gods of other nations.
**Example:** pasian-te milim hi lel a

### umcih = to surround/be present around
**Context:** Describes the presence of glory and honor around God.
**Example:** Pahtawina leh minliatna in amah umcih a

### kimkot = to encompass/surround
**Context:** Describes strength and gladness surrounding God's place/presence.
**Example:** thahatna leh lungdamna in amah kimkot hi

### theitel = to know/acknowledge (often used for recognizing God's greatness)
**Context:** A call to acknowledge or know the Lord.
**Example:** Topa theitel un la

### kilawm = worthy/deserving
**Context:** Used to describe the amount of glory due to God.
**Example:** Amah in a ngah ding a kilawm minthan'na

### Biakna puan silhin = wearing garments of worship/in garments of holiness
**Context:** Refers to the proper attire or state for worshipping God.
**Example:** Biakna puan silhin Topa bia un

### ling = to tremble/quake/fear (in reverence)
**Context:** An instruction to fear or tremble before God.
**Example:** ama mai-ah ling un

### kilok = to move/shake/be moved
**Context:** Used in the context of the stability of the world.
**Example:** kilok ngeilo ding hi

### maang = to reign/to rule
**Context:** Used in the declaration that the Lord reigns.
**Example:** Topa maang hi!

## Phrases & Usage

- **tawntung thuciamna-in a kipsak** = confirmed by an everlasting covenant
  - Usage: Used to describe the permanence and binding nature of God's promise to Israel.

- **gamhluahin na tanh dingin** = to measure out as your inheritance
  - Usage: Used when describing the granting of the land of Canaan as an inheritance.

- **minam khat pan nam khatah** = from one nation to another nation
  - Usage: Describes the nomadic movement between different peoples or tribes.

- **ka sathau nilhte lawng kei un** = Touch not mine anointed ones
  - Usage: A direct command/warning against harming God's chosen people.

- **a ni ni-in** = day by day
  - Usage: Indicates a continuous, daily action (singing of salvation).

- **pasian khempeuh sangin** = than all the gods
  - Usage: Used for comparison to emphasize the supremacy of the Lord over all other deities.

- **Biakna puan silhin** = in garments of holiness/worship
  - Usage: Describes the required state or attire for approaching God in worship.

- **leitung kip mahmah a** = the whole world/the entire earth
  - Usage: Used to emphasize the scope of God's stability and dominion.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X tungah Y-in a kipsak**
  - Structure showing the recipient (X) and the means/instrument (Y) by which something is confirmed or established. (e.g., Jakob tungah tawntung thuciamna-in a kipsak - He confirmed it to Jacob by an everlasting covenant.)
  - Example: Jakob tungah tawntung thuciamna-in a kipsak

- **A cihte phawk tawntung hi**
  - Verb phrase indicating continuous remembrance or recollection of past statements. (phawk = remember; tawntung = continuously).
  - Example: a cihte phawk tawntung hi

- **X-in Y-sak lo hi**
  - Negative structure indicating that X did not cause Y to happen to someone/something. (e.g., amah in amaute kuamah nengniam sak lo hi - He did not allow anyone to oppress them.)
  - Example: amah in amaute kuamah nengniam sak lo hi

- **X khempeuh sangin Y ahi hi**
  - Comparative structure: 'Y is [adjective] than all X.' (e.g., pasian khempeuh sangin amah lamdang sakzawk ding ahi hi - He is more marvelous than all the gods.)
  - Example: pasian khempeuh sangin lamdang sakzawk ding ahi hi

- **X aw, Y un!**
  - Vocative address followed by an imperative command, often used in calls to action directed at a group (e.g., Leitung mi khempeuh aw, Topa theitel un la - O people of the earth, know the Lord!)
  - Example: Leitung mi khempeuh aw, { Topa } theitel un la
