# PSA - Part 3

## Vocabulary with Context

### lau-in = stand in awe/fear
**Context:** Command to stand in awe or fear (of God).
**Example:** Lau-in liing un la, mawhna pel un;

### liing = tremble/shake (often used with 'lau-in' for awe)
**Context:** Used with 'lau-in' to mean stand in awe/fear.
**Example:** Lau-in liing un la, mawhna pel un;

### mawhna = sin/wickedness
**Context:** Command to avoid sin.
**Example:** mawhna pel un

### pel = avoid/turn away from
**Context:** Used in a negative command to avoid sin.
**Example:** mawhna pel un

### lupna = bed/resting place
**Context:** Referring to where one lies down to rest or meditate.
**Example:** Na lupna tung uh-ah lungngai phapha un la,

### lungngai = meditate/consider deeply
**Context:** Command to meditate upon one's heart/thoughts.
**Example:** lungngai phapha un la,

### phapha = thoroughly/well (intensifier)
**Context:** Intensifying the action of meditating.
**Example:** lungngai phapha un la,

### om khikhe = be still/be silent
**Context:** Command to cease activity and be quiet.
**Example:** om khikhe lel un.

### biakna piaknate = sacrifices (offerings)
**Context:** Referring to religious offerings or acts of worship.
**Example:** A maan biakna piaknate lui un la,

### lui = offer/present
**Context:** Used with sacrifices, meaning to offer them up.
**Example:** A maan biakna piaknate lui un la,

### muang = trust/rely on
**Context:** Command to place trust in the Lord.
**Example:** Topa muang un.

### Thupha = blessing/good things
**Context:** What people desire to receive from God.
**Example:** Thupha ngah thei leeng ut si hang.

### maitang vang = favor/countenance (lit. face light)
**Context:** A request for God's favorable look or presence.
**Example:** Ko tungah na maitang vang hong taangsak in,

### taangsak = show/cause to appear
**Context:** To cause one's favor to shine upon someone.
**Example:** na maitang vang hong taangsak in,

### an leh leenggahtui = corn and wine (provisions/wealth)
**Context:** Metaphor for material abundance or prosperity.
**Example:** Amaute in an leh leenggahtui a hauh cianga

### hauh = increase/become abundant
**Context:** Describing the increase of provisions.
**Example:** a hauh cianga

### lianzaw = greater/more abundant
**Context:** Used for comparison of joy.
**Example:** nuamna lianzaw ka lung sungah nong guan hi.

### guan = put/place
**Context:** God placing gladness in the heart.
**Example:** ka lung sungah nong guan hi.

### lungmuan’na mun = place of peace/rest
**Context:** A state or place characterized by tranquility provided by God.
**Example:** lungmuan’na munah nong om sak hi.

### thudon = listen to/pay attention to (in prayer)
**Context:** A request for God to hear prayers.
**Example:** kong thumnate hong thudon in.

### kikona = cry/calling out (in prayer)
**Context:** The sound of one praying earnestly.
**Example:** ka kikona aw hong ngai in.

### zingsang simin = every morning/in the morning
**Context:** Indicating a regular, daily action of prayer.
**Example:** zingsang simin ka aw nong za a,

### gitlohna = wickedness/evil deeds
**Context:** Referring to the nature of things God does not desire.
**Example:** gitlohna a deih Pasian na hi kei a,

### tawlkhat zong = even for a short time
**Context:** Emphasizing that evil cannot coexist with God even briefly.
**Example:** tawlkhat zong nang tawh a omkhawm thei kei hi.

### kisathei = foolish/stupid ones
**Context:** Referring to those who act foolishly, contrasted with God's sight.
**Example:** A kisatheite na mit tawh kituak lo hi;

### mudah = hate/detest
**Context:** God's reaction to workers of iniquity.
**Example:** siatna a bawlte khempeuh nangmah in na mudah hi.

### zuauthu = leasing/falsehood/lies
**Context:** Referring to those who speak lies.
**Example:** Zuauthu a gen mite na susia hi;

### susia = destroy/ruin
**Context:** God's action against those who speak falsehoods.
**Example:** Zuauthu a gen mite na susia hi;

### kicing = multitude/abundance
**Context:** Referring to the abundance of God's mercy.
**Example:** a kicing na itna kip suangin

### zahtakna = fear/reverence
**Context:** The proper attitude when worshipping God's holy place.
**Example:** nangmah zahtakna tawh kunin

### kunin = bowing down/worshipping (in reverence)
**Context:** The manner of worship driven by reverence.
**Example:** nangmah zahtakna tawh kunin

### makaih = lead/guide
**Context:** A plea for God to lead the speaker.
**Example:** hong makaih in la,

### thuman = faithfulness/truth
**Context:** Describing the lack of faithfulness in the enemies' mouths.
**Example:** amau sungah thuman om lo hi;

### gawlgui = throat/gullet
**Context:** Describing the enemies' throat as an open pit.
**Example:** amau' gawlgui pen a kihongsa hankhuk tawh kibang hi.

### hankhuk = sepulchre/grave
**Context:** Used metaphorically for the throat of the wicked.
**Example:** a kihongsa hankhuk tawh kibang hi.

### mawk phat = flatter/praise falsely
**Context:** Describing the deceitful speech of the wicked.
**Example:** A leii-te tawh mawk phat-te hi.

### puksak = cast down/overthrow
**Context:** A request for God to cause the wicked to fall by their own words.
**Example:** amau thu mah tawh amau puksak in.

### lehdo = rebel/oppose
**Context:** Describing the action of the wicked against God.
**Example:** Amaute in nangmah hong lehdo-te ahih manin

### paaikhia = cast out/drive away
**Context:** A request for God to expel the wicked due to their transgressions.
**Example:** amau mawhna tampite hangin amau paaikhia in.

## Phrases & Usage

- **Lau-in liing un la, mawhna pel un** = Stand in awe and sin not
  - Usage: An imperative command urging reverence and moral uprightness.

- **lungngai phapha un la** = Meditate thoroughly
  - Usage: Used to instruct deep, careful introspection, usually upon one's own heart/thoughts.

- **om khikhe lel un** = Be completely still
  - Usage: A strong command for silence and cessation of movement/speech.

- **A maan biakna piaknate lui un la** = Offer the sacrifices of righteousness
  - Usage: A call to offer proper, righteous acts of worship.

- **na maitang vang hong taangsak in** = Cause your countenance to shine upon me
  - Usage: A common petition for God's favor and blessing.

- **nuamna lianzaw ka lung sungah nong guan hi** = You have put greater joy in my heart
  - Usage: Expressing that God's provision brings superior joy compared to material wealth.

- **nangmah bek in lungmuan’na munah nong om sak hi** = You alone make me dwell in a place of peace
  - Usage: Attributing personal peace and security solely to God.

- **zingsang simin ka aw nong za a** = You hear my voice every morning
  - Usage: Affirming God's consistent attention to the speaker's daily prayers.

- **nang tawh a omkhawm thei kei hi** = Cannot dwell with you
  - Usage: Stating the incompatibility of evil with God's nature.

- **a kicing na itna kip suangin** = In the multitude of your mercy
  - Usage: Stating the basis upon which the speaker approaches God (His abundant mercy).

- **nangmah zahtakna tawh kunin** = Worshipping in your fear/reverence
  - Usage: Describing the manner of worship characterized by deep respect.

- **amau' gawlgui pen a kihongsa hankhuk tawh kibang hi** = Their throat is like an open sepulchre
  - Usage: A strong metaphor describing the deadly, consuming nature of the wicked's speech/intentions.

## Grammar Patterns

- **Imperative + la + Imperative**
  - A common structure for issuing sequential commands, often used in psalms for instruction (e.g., 'Do X, and do Y').
  - Example: Lau-in liing un la, mawhna pel un;

- **Noun + leh + Noun + a + Verb**
  - Used to list related items, often representing general prosperity or provisions.
  - Example: Amaute in an leh leenggahtui a hauh cianga

- **Comparative structure: X sangin Y lianzaw**
  - Used to state that Y is greater than X (X-than Y greater).
  - Example: a nuam-te sangin nuamna lianzaw ka lung sungah nong guan hi.

- **Bang hang hiam cih leh [Statement of Fact]**
  - A rhetorical structure meaning 'Why is this? Because [reason follows].' Often used to explain God's nature or actions.
  - Example: Bang hang hiam cih leh gitlohna a deih Pasian na hi kei a,

- **Noun + tawh kituak lo hi**
  - Used to express incompatibility or rejection (e.g., the foolish do not 'match' God's sight).
  - Example: A kisatheite na mit tawh kituak lo hi;

- **Ahih leh [Contrast/Shift]**
  - Used to introduce a contrast, often shifting from describing others to describing the speaker's own action (But as for me...).
  - Example: Kei ahih leh, a kicing na itna kip suangin...

- **Noun + hangin [Result/Cause]**
  - Indicates the reason or basis for an action (because of/on account of).
  - Example: amau mawhna tampite hangin amau paaikhia in.
