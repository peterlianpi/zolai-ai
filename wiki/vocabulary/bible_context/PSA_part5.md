# PSA - Part 5

## Vocabulary with Context

### omlopi-in = without cause/reason
**Context:** Used to describe an action done without justification (rewarding evil to a peaceful person).
**Example:** bangmah thu omlopi-in ka lawmte khemin

### khemin = to reward/repay (in a negative sense here, 'to repay evil')
**Context:** Paired with 'omlopi-in' (without cause) to mean repaying evil.
**Example:** ka lawmte khemin

### huh = to repay/requite (often used for evil/vengeance)
**Context:** Used for repaying enemies ('galte ka huh tak leh').
**Example:** ka galte ka huh tak leh

### delhin = to persecute
**Context:** Used in the context of enemies pursuing the soul.
**Example:** galte in kei hong delhin hong pha-te hen la

### pha = to chase/pursue
**Context:** Used alongside 'delhin' (persecute).
**Example:** hong delhin hong pha-te hen la

### tuancilsak = to tread down/crush
**Context:** Describing the enemy's action against the life ('kei hong tuancilsak in').
**Example:** leilakah kei hong tuancilsak in la

### sialpai = to lay low/cast down (honor)
**Context:** Used metaphorically for laying honor in the dust ('hong sialpai ta-te hen').
**Example:** leilakah kei hong sialpai ta-te hen

### hehsa-in = with anger/fiercely
**Context:** Describing how God should arise ('Tho in, Topa, hehsa-in thasaan in la').
**Example:** a hehsa-in thasaan in la

### thasaan = to rise up/stand up (with force/anger)
**Context:** Used when calling upon God to rise up in anger.
**Example:** a hehsa-in thasaan in la

### dal = to save/deliver/protect from
**Context:** Asking God to deliver from the anger of enemies ('ka galte' hehna panin kei hong dal in').
**Example:** ka galte' hehna panin kei hong dal in

### thukhenna ding hun = time for judgment
**Context:** Referring to a set time for God's judgment.
**Example:** thukhenna ding hun khat na seh khinzo hi

### kikhawmsak = to gather together/assemble
**Context:** Asking people to assemble around God's dwelling place.
**Example:** Nangma omna kiimah mite kikhawmsak in la

### thuman = righteous/just/true
**Context:** Used to describe God who knows the heart, and also used for the just/righteous people.
**Example:** Lungsim leh ngaihsutna a en a thuman Pasian aw

### kipsak = to establish/make firm
**Context:** Asking God to establish the righteous people.
**Example:** mi thumante kipsak in

### hupa = defense/refuge
**Context:** Stating that God is the defense of the speaker.
**Example:** Pasian, kei hong hupa hi a

### thalkhau = sword
**Context:** Referring to God preparing His sword.
**Example:** Pasian in a thalkhau suii zo a

### thaltang = arrow/dart
**Context:** Referring to the instruments of death prepared by God.
**Example:** Amah in mei thaltang bawl a

### galthiam = persecutors/enemies
**Context:** Referring to those against whom God ordains His arrows.
**Example:** sih theihna ama galhiamte a baihsa-in a koihzo hi

### zuauthu = falsehood/deceit
**Context:** What the wicked person brings forth after conceiving mischief.
**Example:** zuauthu a hingkhia hi

### kia = to fall (into a trap/pit)
**Context:** Describing the wicked person falling into the pit they dug.
**Example:** amah a kia hi

### lunggulh = desire/intent (negative)
**Context:** Referring to the wicked person's evil desire returning upon their head.
**Example:** Ama lunggulh hoihlo pen ama lu tungah tukik a

### lian = great/mighty/magnified
**Context:** Used to describe the greatness of God's name throughout the earth.
**Example:** leitung khempeuhah na min lian mahmah hi

## Phrases & Usage

- **bangmah thu omlopi-in** = without any cause/reason
  - Usage: Used to emphasize that an action (like repaying evil) was completely unprovoked.

- **leilakah kei hong tuancilsak in la** = let him tread down my life upon the earth
  - Usage: A plea for severe affliction or defeat by the enemy.

- **lungsim leh ngaihsutna a en a thuman Pasian aw** = O God, who is true, who examines the heart and mind
  - Usage: An invocation addressing God's omniscience regarding inner thoughts.

- **a Sangpen Topa min, phatna la ka sak ding hi** = I will sing a song of praise to the name of the Most High Lord
  - Usage: A declaration of future worship based on God's righteousness.

- **a hong kiphatna awgingte in vantung ban a** = Your praises reach the heavens
  - Usage: Describing the extent and glory of God's praise.

## Grammar Patterns

- **Verb + -in (Adverbial form)**
  - Used to form adverbs describing the manner of action, e.g., 'hehsa-in' (angrily), 'omlopi-in' (without cause).
  - Example: a hehsa-in thasaan in la

- **Verb + -in la (Imperative/Hortative)**
  - A common structure for making requests or commands, often sequential, e.g., 'Arise, and deliver me' (Tho in la, ... hong dal in la).
  - Example: Tho in la, ... hong dal in;

- **Noun' + -tungah (Locative)**
  - Indicates location 'upon' or 'on top of' something, often used metaphorically for consequences returning to the head.
  - Example: ama lu tungah tukik a

- **Noun + a + Noun (Possessive/Genitive)**
  - Used to show possession or relationship, often seen in 'ka galte' hehna' (the anger of my enemies).
  - Example: ka galte' hehna panin
