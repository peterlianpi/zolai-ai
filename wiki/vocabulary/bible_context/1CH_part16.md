## Vocabulary with Context

### tapa = son (of)
**Context:** Used repeatedly to denote lineage, equivalent to 'his son' in the KJV.
**Example:** a tapa Elkanah

### leh = and
**Context:** A conjunction used to connect items in a list.
**Example:** Uzziah, leh a tapa Shaul ahi hi.

### ahi hi = it is/they are
**Context:** A common copula phrase indicating existence or identity, often appearing at the end of a descriptive clause.
**Example:** Shaul ahi hi.

### tapate = sons (plural)
**Context:** The plural form of 'tapa', referring to multiple sons.
**Example:** Elkanah, tapate in:

### suante = descendants/offspring (plural)
**Context:** Used here to refer to the children/descendants of a person (Ahimoth, Merari). It seems broader than just 'sons'.
**Example:** Ahimoth’ suante in:

### uh = they/them (plural marker)
**Context:** A plural marker often attached to verbs or used as a pronoun suffix, seen here in 'ahi uh hi'.
**Example:** ahi uh hi.

### tacil = firstborn
**Context:** Used to specify the eldest son in a list of sons.
**Example:** A tacil Joel

### nihna = second
**Context:** Used to specify the second son in a list.
**Example:** a nihna Abijah

### Topa’ inn sungah = in the house of the Lord
**Context:** A compound phrase indicating location within God's dwelling place.
**Example:** Topa’ inn sungah thuciamna singkuang a tawlngak khit ciangin

### thuciamna singkuang = Ark of the Covenant
**Context:** The specific religious artifact mentioned.
**Example:** thuciamna singkuang a tawlngak khit ciangin

### tawlngak = to be placed/set down/rest
**Context:** Used in relation to the Ark resting or being placed somewhere.
**Example:** a tawlngak khit ciangin

### lasak nasep = service of song/singing work
**Context:** Refers to the musical duties performed.
**Example:** tua sungah lasak nasep a ukin

### ukin = to begin/to start
**Context:** Indicates the commencement of the singing service.
**Example:** lasak nasep a ukin

### koih mite = those whom [he] appointed/set
**Context:** Refers to the people David assigned to the task.
**Example:** David in a koih mite in hihte ahi uh hi.

### Biakinn = Temple/House of God
**Context:** Used interchangeably with 'Topa' inn' (House of the Lord) to refer to Solomon's structure.
**Example:** Solomon in Jerusalem-ah { Topa' } Biakinn a lam ciangdong

### lam ciangdong = until [it was] built
**Context:** Indicates duration up to the completion of the building.
**Example:** Biakinn a lam ciangdong

### kikhopna puanbuk = tent of the congregation
**Context:** Refers to the Tabernacle structure.
**Example:** kikhopna puanbuk ahi biakbuk mai-ah

### hunhun = their respective times/seasons
**Context:** Indicates that the service was performed according to a schedule or rotation.
**Example:** a nasep uh a hunhun a banbanin

### banbanin = in turn/alternately/according to division
**Context:** Describes the manner in which the service was carried out (rotationally).
**Example:** a hunhun a banbanin sem uh hi.

### khangsimna = genealogy/lineage/succession
**Context:** Refers to the recorded line of descent.
**Example:** Hih nasep a semte' khangsimna

### beh = clan/family/tribe
**Context:** Used to categorize the descendants (e.g., Kohath mite beh).
**Example:** Kohath mite beh sung panin:

### la makaipa = leader of song/chief musician
**Context:** Heman's title.
**Example:** Heman la makaipa hi

### pupi = ancestor/forefather
**Context:** Refers to a distant male ancestor.
**Example:** a pupi Israel lam manawhin

### manawhin = to turn back/to return
**Context:** Used in the context of tracing lineage back to an ancestor.
**Example:** a pupi Israel lam manawhin

### kileh simto = to trace back/to ascend (in lineage)
**Context:** Describes the process of tracing the genealogy upwards.
**Example:** kileh simto hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **a tapa X, a tapa Y** = the son of X, the son of Y
  - Usage: Standard formula for listing successive male descendants.

- **ahi uh hi** = they are
  - Usage: Used at the end of a list or description to confirm identity or existence of the group mentioned.

- **a hunhun a banbanin** = in their respective turns/alternately
  - Usage: Used to describe scheduled or rotational duties.

- **a pupi Israel lam manawhin kileh simto** = traced back ascending to the ancestor Israel
  - Usage: A specific phrase describing the upward tracing of a genealogy.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X, tapa Y, tapa Z...**
  - Repetitive structure using 'tapa' (son) to list generations sequentially.
  - Example: a tapa Elkanah, a tapa Ebiasaf, a tapa Assir,

- **Noun' + suante in:**
  - Possessive marker (') followed by 'suante' (offspring/descendants) and the particle 'in' (topic marker), introducing the list of descendants.
  - Example: Ahimoth’ suante in:

- **X ahi hi / X ahi uh hi**
  - Copula structure indicating 'X is/are'. 'uh' marks the plural subject.
  - Example: Shaul ahi hi. / ahi uh hi.

- **Topa’ inn sungah [Event] ciangin**
  - Locative phrase ('in the house of the Lord') followed by 'ciangin' (when/after), setting the temporal context for the subsequent action.
  - Example: Topa’ inn sungah thuciamna singkuang a tawlngak khit ciangin
