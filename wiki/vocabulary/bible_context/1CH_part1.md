# 1CH - Part 1

## Vocabulary with Context

### ta = to
**Context:** used to indicate the relationship between a father and a son
**Example:** Adam' ta Seth

### tapa = son
**Context:** used to refer to a male child
**Example:** Noah in tapa thum nei a

### tuate = children
**Context:** used to refer to multiple children
**Example:** tuate in Shem, Ham, leh Jafeth ahi-te hi

### suante = sons
**Context:** used to refer to multiple male children
**Example:** Gomer' suante in: Ashkenaz mite, Rifath mite, leh Togarmah mite ahi hi

### mite = descendants
**Context:** used to refer to the descendants of a person
**Example:** Ashkenaz mite, Rifath mite, leh Togarmah mite ahi hi

### amau = they
**Context:** used as a pronoun to refer to a group of people
**Example:** amau' min pua-in minam piang ciat hi

### minam = name
**Context:** used to refer to a person's name
**Example:** amau' min pua-in minam piang ciat hi

### ciat = call
**Context:** used to refer to the act of calling or naming someone
**Example:** amau' min ciat pua-in minam tuamtuam a piang hi

### pian = born
**Context:** used to refer to the act of being born
**Example:** Filistia mite' hong pian'na

### lehung = and
**Context:** used as a conjunction to connect words or phrases
**Example:** Shem, Ham, leh Jafeth ahi-te hi

### pen = is
**Context:** used as a verb to indicate a state of being
**Example:** Noah in tapa thum nei a, tuate pen Shem, Ham, leh Jafeth ahi-te hi

### hi = is
**Context:** used as a verb to indicate a state of being
**Example:** Gomer' suante in: Ashkenaz mite, Rifath mite, leh Togarmah mite ahi hi

### nei = has
**Context:** used as a verb to indicate possession
**Example:** Noah in tapa thum nei a

### kici = begat
**Context:** used to refer to the act of fathering a child
**Example:** Kush in Nimrod kici tapa khat nei hi

### vanglian = mighty
**Context:** used to describe someone who is powerful or strong
**Example:** amah in leitungah a vanglian ding khatin hong kipan hi

## Phrases & Usage

- **ta** = to
  - Usage: used to indicate the relationship between a father and a son, e.g. 'Adam' ta Seth'

- **tapa thum** = firstborn son
  - Usage: used to refer to the firstborn son of a person, e.g. 'Noah in tapa thum nei a'

- **suante in** = the sons of
  - Usage: used to refer to the male children of a person, e.g. 'Gomer' suante in: Ashkenaz mite, Rifath mite, leh Togarmah mite ahi hi'

- **amau' min** = their name
  - Usage: used to refer to the name of a group of people, e.g. 'amau' min pua-in minam piang ciat hi'

- **ciat pua-in** = to call by name
  - Usage: used to refer to the act of calling or naming someone, e.g. 'amau' min ciat pua-in minam tuamtuam a piang hi'

- **hong pian** = was born
  - Usage: used to refer to the act of being born, e.g. 'Filistia mite' hong pian'na

## Grammar Patterns

- **Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) word order**
  - The typical word order in Zolai is Subject-Verb-Object, e.g. 'Kush in Nimrod kici tapa khat nei hi'
  - Example: Kush in Nimrod kici tapa khat nei hi

- **Use of possessive pronouns**
  - Possessive pronouns are used to indicate ownership or possession, e.g. 'Noah in tapa thum nei a'
  - Example: Noah in tapa thum nei a

- **Use of relative clauses**
  - Relative clauses are used to provide additional information about a noun, e.g. 'amau' min pua-in minam piang ciat hi'
  - Example: amau' min pua-in minam piang ciat hi

- **Use of conjunctions**
  - Conjunctions are used to connect words or phrases, e.g. 'Shem, Ham, leh Jafeth ahi-te hi'
  - Example: Shem, Ham, leh Jafeth ahi-te hi
