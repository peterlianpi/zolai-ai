# PSA - Part 2

## Vocabulary with Context

### siktawn = rod/staff (of iron)
**Context:** Used metaphorically for a tool of severe judgment or breaking.
**Example:** Siktawn khat tawh amaute na satkham ding a...

### satkham = to break/smash (with force)
**Context:** Used in the context of breaking something with an iron rod, implying severe destruction.
**Example:** Siktawn khat tawh amaute na satkham ding a...

### beelden’khap = potter's vessel/earthenware
**Context:** Compared to the object that is to be dashed in pieces.
**Example:** beelden’khap bangin amaute lei-ah na dengzan ding hi

### dengzan = to dash/smash into pieces
**Context:** Used for the action of breaking the objects (amaute) like a potter's vessel.
**Example:** amaute lei-ah na dengzan ding hi

### pil = wise (verb form/imperative)
**Context:** An exhortation to the kings to be wise.
**Example:** tu-in pil ta un

### thu phawk = to understand/be instructed/take heed
**Context:** An instruction to the judges of the earth to take heed or be instructed.
**Example:** leitung gam a ukte aw, tu-in thu phawk ta un.

### lin = to fear/revere (often used with 'launa' - fear)
**Context:** Used in the context of serving God with reverence/fear.
**Example:** lin'na tawh a khe nam un

### sinso = wrath/anger (divine)
**Context:** Referring to God's anger if the command is not followed.
**Example:** amah sinso-in lamkalah na siamang ding-te hi

### heh pakpak = kindled but a little/slightly burning
**Context:** Describing the slightness of God's anger that can still cause destruction.
**Example:** bang hang hiam cih leh amah heh pakpak hi.

### mi nuamsate = blessed ones/those who are happy/prosperous
**Context:** Describing those who trust in Him (the Son).
**Example:** Amah a bel khempeuh mi nuamsate ahi hi.

### langpan = to rise up against/oppose
**Context:** Describing the enemies rising up against the speaker.
**Example:** kei hong langpan’nainai hi.

### kidalna lum = shield/protector (lit. 'defense light/fire')
**Context:** Describing God as the source of defense and glory.
**Example:** nang pen ka kidalna lum

### hong phong = to lift up/exalt (the head)
**Context:** God is the one who lifts up the speaker's head (honor/victory).
**Example:** ka lu hong phongpa na hi hi.

### galsap = to cry out/call upon (with distress)
**Context:** The speaker crying out to the Lord in distress.
**Example:** Nakpi takin { Topa } ka galsap a

### kem = to keep/preserve/sustain
**Context:** God kept the speaker while sleeping.
**Example:** { Topa } in hong kem ahih manin ka thokik hi.

### thokik = to awake/wake up
**Context:** The result of God keeping the speaker.
**Example:** ka thokik hi.

### umcih = to surround/encircle
**Context:** Describing the many people who have set themselves against the speaker.
**Example:** hong umcih mi tul sawm tampite

### paang satgawp = to break the jaw/smash the mouth
**Context:** God breaking the power/pride of the enemies.
**Example:** ka galte khempeuh' paang nangmah in na beenggawp a

### sutan = to crush/grind to dust
**Context:** God crushing the teeth of the wicked.
**Example:** migilote' ha na sutan hi.

### gualzawhna = salvation/victory
**Context:** Attributing salvation solely to the Lord.
**Example:** Gualzawhna pen { Topa } nangma hang ahi hi.

### a thuman = righteousness/truth (in this context, often righteousness)
**Context:** Addressing God as the God of the speaker's righteousness.
**Example:** A thuman ka Pasian aw

### nopsakna = joy/gladness/relief
**Context:** God granting joy when the speaker was in distress.
**Example:** Gimna ka thuak ciangin nopsakna nong pia hi.

### thumawkna = falsehood/vanity/lies
**Context:** The people loving vanity and seeking after lies.
**Example:** Bang tanvei thumawkna deihin

### tuamkoihlam = a special place/set apart (for Himself)
**Context:** The Lord sets apart the godly for Himself.
**Example:** { Topa } in ama-a' dingin miphate a tuamkoihlam thei ta un.

## Phrases & Usage

- **beelden’khap bangin** = like a potter's vessel
  - Usage: Used for comparison when something is to be utterly destroyed or dashed to pieces.

- **tu-in pil ta un** = Be wise now
  - Usage: An imperative command urging immediate wisdom or understanding (often used in prophetic/admonitory speech).

- **a mah heh pakpak hi** = when his wrath is kindled but a little
  - Usage: Describes a situation where even a small measure of anger leads to severe consequences.

- **ka kidalna lum, ka minthan’na leh ka lu hong phongpa** = my shield, my glory, and the lifter up of my head
  - Usage: A poetic description of God as the source of defense, honor, and exaltation.

- **a mual siangtho panin** = from his holy hill
  - Usage: Specifies the location from which God answers or acts (heaven/holy dwelling place).

- **ka galte khempeuh’ paang na satgawp a** = you have broken the jaw of all my enemies
  - Usage: A strong declaration of God's decisive victory over all adversaries.

- **nopsakna nong pia hi** = you grant gladness/joy
  - Usage: Used to express that God provides inner relief or joy during times of trouble.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X tawh Y ding a, Z bangin W ding hi**
  - A structure indicating means/instrument (tawh) followed by a comparison (bangin) describing the resulting action (ding hi). (e.g., With X, you will do Y, and like Z, you will do W.)
  - Example: Siktawn khat tawh amaute na satkham ding a, beelden’khap bangin amaute lei-ah na dengzan ding hi

- **X aw, tu-in Y ta un**
  - Vocative address (X aw) followed by an imperative urging immediate action (tu-in... ta un). The particle 'ta' emphasizes the completion or immediacy of the command.
  - Example: kumpite aw, tu-in pil ta un

- **Bang hang hiam cih leh...**
  - A rhetorical structure meaning 'Why is it that...' or 'For what reason is it that...' introducing a justification or question about a state of affairs.
  - Example: Bang hang hiam cih leh amah heh pakpak hi.

- **Nangmah in ka X na hi hi**
  - A declarative statement identifying the subject (Nangmah - You) as the source or embodiment of the quality/role (X).
  - Example: nang pen ka kidalna lum... na hi hi.

- **X ka sap a, Y in hong dawng hi**
  - A sequence of actions: the speaker performed action X (cried/called), and as a result, Y (God) performed action Z (answered).
  - Example: Nakpi takin { Topa } ka galsap a, amah in... kei a hong dawng hi.
