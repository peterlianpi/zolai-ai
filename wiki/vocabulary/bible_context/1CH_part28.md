# 1CH - Part 28

## Vocabulary with Context

### pa = father
**Context:** Used to denote lineage/fatherhood in genealogies ('X's father is Y').
**Example:** Jarah’ pa hi a

### leh = and
**Context:** Conjunction connecting nouns in a list.
**Example:** Alemeth, Azmaveth, leh Zimri’ pa ahi hi

### tapa = son
**Context:** Used to denote direct male offspring.
**Example:** a tapa Refaiah

### nei = have/possess (literally 'hold')
**Context:** Used in the sense of having children ('had sons').
**Example:** Azel in tapa guk nei a

### minte = names (plural)
**Context:** Refers to the list of names.
**Example:** amau’ minte in

### uh hi = they are
**Context:** Plural copula/marker indicating the preceding list constitutes the subject.
**Example:** Hanan ahi-te hi

### Tu-in = Now/At this time
**Context:** Transitional phrase introducing a new section or time frame (Chapter 10).
**Example:** Tu-in Filistiate in Israel mite sim-te hi

### sim = fight/war against
**Context:** Used transitively to describe military action against a group.
**Example:** Filistiate in Israel mite sim-te hi

### tai = flee/run away
**Context:** Used to describe retreat from battle.
**Example:** Israel mite tai-te a

### tuk = fall (down)
**Context:** Used specifically for falling down slain in battle.
**Example:** Gilboa Mual tungah tuk zeizai-te hi

### pha = reach/overtake/pursue hard
**Context:** Used to describe the intensity of the pursuit by the Philistines.
**Example:** Filistia mite in Saul leh a tapate pha-te hi

### that = kill
**Context:** Direct verb for slaying the individuals.
**Example:** Jonathan leh Abinadab leh Malkhishua that-te hi

### gal kidona = battle/warfare
**Context:** Refers to the state or progress of the fighting.
**Example:** Gal kidona in Saul neh mahmah a

### neh = hard/fierce/severe
**Context:** Adverbial use modifying the severity of the battle ('went sore against').
**Example:** Saul neh mahmah a

### thaltawite = archers (bowmen)
**Context:** Specific military unit.
**Example:** thaltawite in amah pha hi

### liamsak = wound/injure severely
**Context:** Causing a serious injury, often by shooting.
**Example:** thaltawite in amah liamsak hi

### galvan puapa = armourbearer
**Context:** Saul's attendant responsible for his armor.
**Example:** a galvan puapa kiangah

### dok = draw/pull out
**Context:** Commanding the armourbearer to draw the sword.
**Example:** Na namsau dok in la

### sun let = thrust through/stab
**Context:** The action Saul requested to avoid capture.
**Example:** tua tawh kei hong sun let in

### dawi bia mite = uncircumcised ones (derogatory term for enemies)
**Context:** Referring to the Philistines, used contemptuously.
**Example:** tua hi kei leh hih dawi bia mite hong pai-in

### khel bawl = abuse/treat shamefully
**Context:** The fate Saul feared if captured.
**Example:** kei hong khel bawl kha ding-te hi

### lau = fear/be afraid
**Context:** Describing the armourbearer's reaction.
**Example:** amah lau mahmah hi

### puk = fall upon/throw oneself onto
**Context:** Used for Saul committing suicide by falling on his sword.
**Example:** tua tungah puk hi

### sikhawm = die together
**Context:** Indicates simultaneous death.
**Example:** si khawm-te hi

### kuam sung = valley (literally 'hollow place')

### khuapi = fortified place/city/stronghold
**Context:** The towns/cities held by the Israelites.

### kithatte = slain ones/dead bodies

## Phrases & Usage

- **a tapa Refaiah, a tapa Eleash, a tapa Azel ahi hi** = Rephaiah his son, Eleasah his son, Azel his son
  - Usage: Standard genealogical construction showing direct descent using repetition of 'a tapa' (his son).

- **Filistiate mai-ah** = from before the Philistines
  - Usage: Indicates direction of flight or retreat, emphasizing the enemy's presence/pressure.

- **tua tawh kei hong sun let in** = thrust me through therewith
  - Usage: A direct command/request using the instrumental marker 'tawh' (with/by means of).

- **hizsa thu khempeuh** = all that was done
  - Usage: A comprehensive phrase referring to all the actions taken by the Philistines.

- **a thahat mi khempeuh kithawi-in** = All the valiant men arose/gathered themselves
  - Usage: Describes the unified action of the brave men.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X in Y' pa ahi hi**
  - Standard Zolai structure for establishing paternity/lineage: X is Y's father. The possessive marker ('s) is often represented by the apostrophe in transliteration (' pa).
  - Example: Jarah in Alemeth... Zimri’ pa ahi hi

- **Azel in tapa guk nei a, ... ahi-te hi**
  - Structure for listing descendants: Subject (Azel) + verb 'nei' (has) + object (six sons), followed by a concluding statement identifying them as the sons of the subject ('these were the sons of Azel').
  - Example: Azel in tapa guk nei a, ... ahi-te hi

- **X leh Y that-te hi**
  - Simple past tense structure for killing multiple people: X and Y they killed. 'uh' marks the plural subject/action.
  - Example: Jonathan leh Abinadab leh Malkhishua that-te hi

- **Tua ciangin X in Y a Z ci hi**
  - Narrative sequence marker: 'Then/After that, X did Z to Y.' Used frequently for sequential actions in narrative.
  - Example: Tua ciangin Saul in a galvan puapa kiangah... a ci hi

- **Ahih hangin X lo hi**
  - Contrastive structure: 'But/However, X did not do [action].' Used to introduce a refusal or counter-action.
  - Example: Ahih hangin a galvan puapa in hih ngam lo hi
