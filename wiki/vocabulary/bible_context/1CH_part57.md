# 1CH - Part 57

## Vocabulary with Context

### leitaw = southward
**Context:** Directional term indicating the south side.
**Example:** Obededom adingin leitaw lamah hong suak a...

### vankoihna in = house of custody/storehouse
**Context:** Refers to a specific building or chamber designated for keeping things, likely sacred items or treasures.
**Example:** a tapa tungah vankoihna inn kipia hi.

### kipia = was given/assigned
**Context:** Past tense verb indicating assignment or allocation.
**Example:** a tapa tungah vankoihna inn kipia hi.

### kongpi san = causeway/raised path
**Context:** A raised road or path, likely associated with a gate or entrance.
**Example:** Shallekheth kongpi san, nitumna lamah kia hi.

### nitumna lam = westward
**Context:** Directional term indicating the west side.
**Example:** nitumna lamah kia hi.

### galvil = ward/division (of guards/officials)
**Context:** Used here to denote a section or division, likely of gatekeepers or officials, facing each other.
**Example:** Galvil leh galvil kimaituah hi.

### kimaituah = opposite each other/facing
**Context:** Describes the arrangement of the 'galvil' (wards) being set against one another.
**Example:** Galvil leh galvil kimaituah hi.

### nisuahna lam = eastward
**Context:** Directional term indicating the east side.
**Example:** nisuahna lamah guk omin...

### guk = six
**Context:** The number six, used here for counting Levites assigned to a direction.
**Example:** nisuahna lamah guk omin...

### li = four
**Context:** The number four, used for counting Levites assigned to a direction.
**Example:** leitaw lamah li om hi;

### kiseh khenna = divisions/allotments
**Context:** The act of dividing or the resulting divisions among the gatekeepers.
**Example:** kongcingte a kiseh khenna ahi hi.

### kongcing = gatekeeper/porter
**Context:** Refers to the individuals whose duty was guarding the gates.
**Example:** kongcingte a kiseh khenna ahi hi.

### Pasian’ inn van manpha = treasures of the house of God
**Context:** Refers to the valuable dedicated items belonging to the sanctuary.
**Example:** Pasian’ inn van manpha koihleh a ki-ap letsong koihnate uk hi.

### ki-ap letsong = dedicated things
**Context:** Items consecrated or set apart for religious use.
**Example:** a ki-ap letsong koihnate uk hi.

### uk = to rule over/be in charge of
**Context:** Verb indicating authority or oversight.
**Example:** Pasian’ inn van manpha koihleh a ki-ap letsong koihnate uk hi.

### beh = clan/family line
**Context:** Used to denote a lineage or family group within the Levites.
**Example:** Gershom-te beh sung panin...

### innkuan tampi a pian’khiatna mi khat = chief father/head of many households
**Context:** A leader who was the progenitor or head of numerous family branches.
**Example:** innkuan tampi a pian'khiatna mi khat hi a...

### sanggam = brother (in the sense of collateral relative, cousin, or fellow member of the same group)
**Context:** Used here for Joel being Zetham's brother within the same lineage group.
**Example:** a sanggampa Joelte in...

### suan = descendant/offspring
**Context:** Indicates direct lineage.
**Example:** Eliezer' suan Shelomith tawh zong...

### beh kinai = related by clan/family connection
**Context:** Indicates a close familial relationship between two lines.
**Example:** Shelomith tawh zong beh kinai ahi hi.

### kumpi = king
**Context:** Title for the reigning monarch (David).
**Example:** kumpi David, leh beh lutangte...

### beh lutang = chief fathers/heads of clans
**Context:** Leaders of the major family groups.
**Example:** leh beh lutangte...

### tultul leh zaza = thousands and hundreds
**Context:** Used idiomatically to mean large numbers or military units (captains over thousands and hundreds).
**Example:** leh a tultul leh a zaza-a uk uliante...

### galkap a ukte = captains of the host/army leaders
**Context:** Military commanders.
**Example:** leh galkap a ukte in...

### ngah uh van kinusiate = spoils won in battles
**Context:** War booty dedicated to the temple maintenance.
**Example:** Gal sungah a ngah uh van kinusiate sung panin...

### puahna = maintenance/repair/building up
**Context:** The act of sustaining or restoring the temple.
**Example:** { Topa' } biakinn puahna dingin...

### Thumupa = seer
**Context:** Title for Samuel, indicating his prophetic role.
**Example:** Thumupa Samuel...

### pualam nasepna = outward business/external affairs
**Context:** Matters concerning the public or external administration, as opposed to internal temple duties.
**Example:** a pualam nasepna-ah kiseh hi.

### thukhente = judges
**Context:** Those responsible for legal rulings.
**Example:** leh thukhente-in a pualam nasepna-ah kiseh hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **leitaw lamah** = to the south side
  - Usage: Used to specify direction, often in relation to assignments or movements (e.g., Obededom's assignment).

- **nitumna lamah** = to the west side
  - Usage: Used to specify direction, often in relation to gate assignments.

- **nisuahna lamah** = to the east side
  - Usage: Used to specify direction, often in relation to gate assignments.

- **a tapa tungah** = to his sons
  - Usage: Indicates assignment or transfer of responsibility to the next generation.

- **van manpha koihnate uk** = ruled over the treasures
  - Usage: Describes the administrative role of overseeing sacred valuables.

- **beh sung panin** = from within the clan of
  - Usage: Used to trace lineage or identify the origin of an individual among the Levites.

- **a ki-ap letsong khempeuh** = all the dedicated things
  - Usage: A comprehensive term referring to all consecrated items.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X adingin Y lamah hong suak a**
  - Structure indicating assignment ('adingin') to a specific direction ('Y lamah') and the action of coming out/being assigned ('hong suak a').
  - Example: Obededom adingin leitaw lamah hong suak a...

- **X leh Y adingin Z kia hi**
  - Structure used for assigning lots or portions ('kia hi') to specific individuals (X leh Y) concerning a location or direction (Z).
  - Example: Shuppim leh Hosah-a' dingin lampi a paitohna... nitumna lamah kia hi.

- **X mah bangin Y omin, Z omin**
  - Comparative structure showing parallel distribution or assignment: 'Just as X [was assigned/present], Y was assigned [number] and Z was assigned [number]'.
  - Example: Vankoihna inn kongah nih nih-in a om mah bangin nisimin nisuahna lamah guk omin, ni simin leilu lamah li omin...

- **X mite sung panin Y in Z uk hi**
  - Structure identifying an individual (Y) who holds authority ('uk hi') originating from a specific lineage (X mite sung panin) over a specific object (Z).
  - Example: Tua ciangin Levi mite sung panin Ahijah in Pasian’ inn van manpha koihnate uk hi.

- **X leh a sanggamte in Y khempeuh uk uh hi**
  - Structure indicating collective authority over a comprehensive set of items (Y khempeuh) held by an individual (X) and their associates (a sanggamte).
  - Example: Hih Shelomith leh a sanggamte in... a ki-ap letsongte a kikoihna khempeuh uk uh hi.
