## Phrases & Usage

- **a thahat a vanglian** = mighty of valour/great in strength
  - Usage: Used to describe men of exceptional military or personal strength (e.g., Zadok, Ephraim men).

- **beh sung panin** = from the house/lineage of
  - Usage: Indicates origin from a specific family or clan.

- **hun pai zia a thei** = one who knows the way of the times
  - Usage: Used to describe those with wisdom regarding timing and appropriate action (Issachar).

- **lungsim a kikhat** = of one heart/united
  - Usage: Describes complete agreement and unity of purpose.

- **ngaihsutna takpi tawh** = with a perfect intention/sincerely
  - Usage: Emphasizes the sincerity and lack of hidden agenda in an action.

- **an ne tui dawnin omkhawm-te hi** = they remained eating and drinking
  - Usage: Describes a period of feasting and fellowship.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X sung panin Y ahi hi.**
  - Structure used to list the number of people belonging to a specific group (tribe/lineage). 'Sung panin' means 'from within' or 'out of'.
  - Example: Efraim mite sung panin... mi tul sawmnih leh zagiat ahi hi.

- **A [quality] a [quality] [person], leh ama beh sung panin [role] [number] ahi hi.**
  - A common structure for introducing a specific leader and detailing the number of subordinates from his lineage.
  - Example: A thahat a vanglian a khangto Zadok, leh ama beh sung panin galkap a ukte mi sawmnih leh nih ahi hi.

- **X tawh a kithawi mihing Y ahi hi.**
  - Describes the number of people equipped with specific items (usually weapons) for a purpose.
  - Example: galhiam khempeuh tawh a kithawi mihing tul zakhat leh tul sawmnih ahi hi.

- **Tua mah bangin X khempeuh zong Y ahi hi.**
  - Used to show that the sentiment or state of one group is mirrored by another group ('In the same way, all of X were also Y').
  - Example: Tua mah bangin Israel a omlaite khempeuh zong David kumpi-in a bawl ding-te a ngaihsutna-te kikhat hi.
