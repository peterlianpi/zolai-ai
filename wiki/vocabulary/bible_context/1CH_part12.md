# 1CH - Part 12

## Vocabulary with Context

### khuaneute = villages/hamlets
**Context:** Referring to the smaller settlements associated with the main cities.
**Example:** Tua ciangin amau’ khuaneute in Etam, Ain, Rimmon...

### kiim-a = around about/surrounding
**Context:** Describing the location of the villages relative to the main cities.
**Example:** Baal ciangdong hih khuapite kiima om a khuaneute khempeuh tawh ahi hi.

### ten’na = habitation/dwelling place
**Context:** Used to denote where a group of people lived.
**Example:** Hihte pen amau' ten'na hi a...

### khangsimna = genealogy/lineage
**Context:** Used in connection with 'ciamtehna' (sign/record) to mean genealogical record.
**Example:** ...amaute in khangsimna ciamtehna nei-te hi.

### tapa = son of
**Context:** Standard patronymic marker, showing descent.
**Example:** Amaziah’ tapa Joshah

### kilo hih mite = those mentioned by name
**Context:** Referring back to the list of individuals named previously.
**Example:** a minin a kilo hih mite pen amau' beh ciatah ulian hi-te a...

### beh ciatah = in their families/by their clan
**Context:** Used to specify the organizational structure of the people mentioned.
**Example:** ...amau' beh ciatah ulian hi-te a...

### nakpi takin = greatly/very much
**Context:** An adverbial phrase intensifying the verb 'khang' (increased).
**Example:** ...amau' beh ciat nakpi takin a khang hi.

### lonona = pasture/grazing land
**Context:** The object they were seeking for their flocks.
**Example:** A tuuhonte uh-a' dingin lonona a zong dingin...

### kuam nisuahna lam pang = towards the rising of the sun/east side
**Context:** Directional phrase indicating the path they took.
**Example:** ...kuam nisuahna lampang, Gedor lutna lamah khualzin-te a...

### leihoih = fat/rich (land)
**Context:** Describing the quality of the pasture found.
**Example:** And they found fat pasture and good...

### gamdai-in = peaceably/quietly
**Context:** Describing the condition of the land they found.
**Example:** ...leh gam zai-in, gam dai-in, a nopna mun mu-te hi;

### teeng masate = the former inhabitants/those who dwelt there of old
**Context:** Referring to the original inhabitants of the land (Hamites).
**Example:** ...tua lai-ah a teng masate pen Ham mite ahi-te hi.

### kumpipa = king
**Context:** Title for Hezekiah.
**Example:** Judah kumpipa Hezekiah' hun sungin hong pai-te a...

### puanbukte = tents/booths
**Context:** Referring to the temporary dwellings of the Meunim people that were destroyed.
**Example:** ...tua lai-ah a om Meunim mite leh amau' puanbukte susia-in...

### susia-in = having destroyed/smote
**Context:** Action taken against the former inhabitants and their dwellings.
**Example:** ...amau' puanbukte susia-in tuni dongin amaute beisak-te a...

### makai-in = having as leader/captains
**Context:** Indicating who led the group of Simeonites.
**Example:** ...Ishi' tapate Pelatiah, Neariah, Refaiah, leh Uzziel makai-in a nei-te hi.

### suakta a om laite = the rest/those who escaped
**Context:** Referring to the remaining Amalekites after the initial conflict.
**Example:** Amaute in Amalek mi a suakta a omlaite susia-te a...

### tacil = firstborn/eldest
**Context:** Used to describe Reuben's status among Israel's sons.
**Example:** Israel' tate sung panin Reuben a tacil ahi hi.

### luppih ahih manin = because he defiled/since he violated
**Context:** The reason Reuben lost his birthright privilege.
**Example:** (Amah in a pa' zineute khat luppih ahih manin tacil hi napi-in...

### innluahza = birthright/inheritance portion
**Context:** The special status or portion due to the firstborn.
**Example:** ...amah innluahza pen Josef' tungah kipia hi.

### kizui-in = according to/following
**Context:** Used to show that the lineage followed the inheritance, not the birth order.
**Example:** Tua ahih ciangin innluahza tawh kizui-in khangsimna-ah amah kiciamteh lo hi.

### thahat suakin = prevailed/was mighty
**Context:** Describing Judah's superior position among his brothers.
**Example:** A sanggamte lakah Judah a thahat suakin...

## Phrases & Usage

- **khangsimna ciamtehna** = record of genealogy
  - Usage: Used to refer to the official documentation of lineage.

- **a min-a a kilo hih mite** = those mentioned by their names
  - Usage: A formal way to refer back to a list of individuals previously enumerated.

- **lei hoih, lonona, leh gam zai-in, gamdai-in** = fat pasture and good, and the land was wide, and peaceable
  - Usage: A descriptive cluster used to praise the quality and safety of a location.

- **tua ni dongin** = unto this day
  - Usage: Indicates a state or action continuing up to the present time of the narrative.

- **a pa’ zineute khat luppih ahih manin** = forasmuch as he defiled his father's bed
  - Usage: A formal clause explaining the reason for a loss of privilege (often used in legal/historical contexts).

## Grammar Patterns

- **X' tapa Y**
  - Possessive marker ('s) followed by 'son of' (tapa) indicating direct descent (e.g., Amaziah' tapa Joshah = Joshah, son of Amaziah).
  - Example: Amaziah’ tapa Joshah

- **X leh Y, Z, khuapi nga**
  - Listing of items (cities/villages) followed by a summary number, using 'leh' (and) for conjunctions.
  - Example: Etam, Ain, Rimmon, Tokhen, leh Ashan, khuapi nga

- **X ahih hangin Y ahi hi**
  - Contrastive structure: 'Although X, he is Y' or 'Because X, he is Y' (often used to state a fact despite a preceding condition).
  - Example: (Amah in a pa' zineute khat luppih ahih manin tacil hi napi-in...

- **X susia-in Y**
  - Verbal participle structure: 'Having destroyed X, [they did] Y' (indicating sequential or causal action).
  - Example: ...amau' puanbukte susia-in tuni dongin amaute beisak-te a...
