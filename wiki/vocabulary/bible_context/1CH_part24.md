# 1CH - Part 24

## Vocabulary with Context

### suante = sons/descendants
**Context:** Used to denote lineage, 'the sons of X'.
**Example:** Shashak' suante ahi-te hi

### ahi-te hi = they are
**Context:** A concluding copula phrase indicating identity or state for a plural subject.
**Example:** Shashak' suante ahi-te hi

### leh = and
**Context:** Conjunction used to link items in a list, similar to 'and' in English.
**Example:** Ifdeiah, leh Penuel-te

### pen = the (marker for specific noun/subject)
**Context:** Used to specify or emphasize the subject, often preceding the verb or predicate.
**Example:** Jeiel pen Gibeon-ah teng a

### khang tawh kizui-in = according to their generations/lineage
**Context:** Describes how the listed individuals were organized or identified.
**Example:** amau’ khang tawh kizui-in

### beh lutangte = heads of families/clans
**Context:** Refers to the leaders or patriarchs of the groups.
**Example:** beh lutangte, a ukpite ahi-te hi

### ukpite = chief men/rulers
**Context:** Synonymous or closely related to 'beh lutangte', meaning leaders.
**Example:** behlutangte, a ukpite ahi-te hi

### teeng-te hi = they dwelt/resided
**Context:** The verb phrase indicating residence in a place (Jerusalem).
**Example:** Hihte Jerusalem ah teeng-te hi

### khuapi = city/town
**Context:** Used to describe Gibeon.
**Example:** Gibeon khuapi a satpa

### satpa = father/founder (of a place)
**Context:** Used here to mean the founder or progenitor associated with the city Gibeon.
**Example:** Gibeon khuapi a satpa Jeiel

### tua ciangin = and then/subsequently
**Context:** Indicates sequence in the listing of descendants.
**Example:** Abdon, tua ciangin Zur

### upa = firstborn/eldest
**Context:** Used to specify the first son.
**Example:** Ama tapa upa pen in

### amah = he/she/it (referring to a male or object)
**Context:** Pronoun used to refer back to Mikloth.
**Example:** amah in Shimeah’ pa

### suan leh khakte = sons and brothers (descendants and relatives)
**Context:** Refers to the wider family group or descendants.
**Example:** amau' suanlekhakte zong

### gal khatah = over against/opposite
**Context:** Describes the spatial relationship of where they dwelt relative to their brethren.
**Example:** amau' behte’ gal khatah

### pa = father of
**Context:** A common suffix/modifier indicating paternity, e.g., Kish' pa = father of Kish (Ner).
**Example:** Kish' pa, Kish pen Saul' pa ahi hi

## Phrases & Usage

- **ahi-te hi** = they are
  - Usage: Used at the end of a sentence to state the identity of a plural subject (e.g., 'they are the sons').

- **khang tawh kizui-in** = according to their generations
  - Usage: Used to describe organization by lineage or time period.

- **behte’ gal khatah** = over against their brethren
  - Usage: Used to describe a location relative to another group, implying proximity or opposition.

- **tapa upa pen** = his firstborn son
  - Usage: Used specifically to identify the eldest male offspring.

## Grammar Patterns

- **Noun' + suante ahi-te hi**
  - Pattern for identifying a group as the descendants of a specific person. (e.g., X' suante ahi-te hi = They are the sons of X).
  - Example: Shashak' suante ahi-te hi

- **Noun + leh + Noun + ...**
  - Simple coordination using 'leh' (and) to list names or items.
  - Example: Ifdeiah, leh Penuel-te

- **Noun + pen + Predicate + ahi hi**
  - Structure used to define the subject (Noun + pen) with a predicate, often used for identification or description.
  - Example: Jeiel pen Gibeon-ah teng a

- **Possessive Marker ('s) + Noun + pa**
  - Indicates paternity or origin, where the possessive form of the father's name is followed by 'pa' (father).
  - Example: Kish in Saul' pa ahi hi
