# JHN - Part 5

## Vocabulary with Context

### ciap = tasted/sampled
**Context:** Used in the context of the ruler of the feast tasting the water that had become wine.
**Example:** Pawi ukpa in tui a ciap ciangin...

### leenggahtui = wedding wine (lit. feast wine)
**Context:** Refers to the wine served at the wedding feast.
**Example:** leenggahtui suak a...

### ngahna = source/origin
**Context:** Used to describe knowing where the wine came from.
**Example:** tua leenggahtui ngahna a theih uh hangin...

### zinei tangvalpa = bridegroom
**Context:** The person who is getting married, called by the governor of the feast.
**Example:** ci-in theilo ahih manin zinei tangvalpa samin,

### khumte = best/finest (things)
**Context:** Used to describe the good wine that is usually served first.
**Example:** leenggahzu khumte hawm masakin nei uh a,

### khamgawp = drunk well/satisfied (with drinking)
**Context:** When people have drunk enough of the initial wine.
**Example:** mite in a khamgawp khit uh ciangin...

### sitlai = kept/reserved
**Context:** Used to describe keeping the best wine until the end.
**Example:** Ahi zongin nangmah in a khum pen teng tudong na sitlai hi,” a ci hi.

### kilangsak = manifested/shown forth
**Context:** Referring to Jesus showing forth his divine nature/glory.
**Example:** Pasian' pianzia a neihna kilangsak a,

### tawmkhat = a short time/a few days
**Context:** Describing the duration of their stay in Capernaum.
**Example:** tua lai-ah ni tawmkhat a taam uh hi.

### Paisan Pawi = Passover Feast
**Context:** The Jewish festival mentioned in the text.
**Example:** Jew mite' Paisan Pawi hun hong naita ahih manin...

### huangsung = courtyard/area within the enclosure
**Context:** The area inside the temple where animals were sold.
**Example:** Biakinn huangsung a lut ciangin...

### sabuai = table/counter
**Context:** The tables used by the money changers.
**Example:** sumkhekte' sabuai-ah a tu mu hi.

### hawlkhia = drove out/expelled
**Context:** Jesus driving out the animals from the temple area.
**Example:** ganhing khempeuh hawlkhia a,

### sawnpaih = overthrew/tossed over
**Context:** Describing Jesus overturning the money changers' tables.
**Example:** sumkhekte' sabuai zong sawnpaih sakin...

### paimangpih = take away/remove
**Context:** Jesus telling those selling doves to take them away.
**Example:** Na kawl vakhute uh hih lai panin paikhiatpih un.

### phualpi = place of business/market
**Context:** Jesus telling them not to make his Father's house a marketplace.
**Example:** Ka Pa' inn pen sumzuakna inn phualpi-in nei kei un,” a ci hi.

### uklah sak = consumed/eaten up (figurative)
**Context:** The disciples realizing Jesus' zeal for God's house was consuming him.
**Example:** Nangma inn ka deihluatna in ka lungsim hong uklah sak hi,”...

### kigelhna thu = that which is written (Scripture)
**Context:** The disciples remembering the written prophecy.
**Example:** Lai Siangtho sungah a kigelhna thu, a nungzuite in a phawk uh hi.

### lim = sign/proof/authority
**Context:** The Jews asking Jesus for a sign to prove his authority.
**Example:** Hih bangin na gamtat theihna ding thu na neihna bang lim tawh hong lakthei ding na hi hiam?”

### susia = destroy/tear down
**Context:** Jesus referring to the destruction of the temple.
**Example:** Hih biakinn susia le uh cin ni thum sungin ka dingsak kik ding hi,”

### tawntung kilam = has been built/constructed over a long time
**Context:** The Jews stating how long the temple took to build.
**Example:** Hih biakinn kum sawmli leh kum guk sung a kilam ahi hi.

### dingsak kik = raise up again/rebuild
**Context:** Jesus claiming he could rebuild the temple in three days.
**Example:** Ni thum sungin dingsak kikzo ding na hi hiam?”

### cihnopna = meaning/intention
**Context:** Explaining that Jesus was referring to his own body.
**Example:** Ahi zongin Jesuh in biakinn a cih ciangin ama pumpi a cihna ahi hi.

### kithawhkik sak = was raised from the dead
**Context:** Referring to Jesus' resurrection.
**Example:** sihna panin amah a kithawhkik sak ciangin...

## Phrases & Usage

- **leenggahtui hoihte hawm masa-in** = serve the good wine first
  - Usage: Used to describe the custom of serving the best wine at the beginning of a feast.

- **a kham khit uh ciangin** = after they have drunk well/become satisfied
  - Usage: Indicates the point when guests are no longer sober or discerning.

- **a hoih leenggahtui tudongin na koihcing hi** = you have kept the good wine until now
  - Usage: A statement of surprise or commendation regarding the quality of the wine served last.

- **ama minthan’na kilangsakin** = manifesting his glory
  - Usage: Describes the result or purpose of Jesus' first miracle.

- **sumzuakna inn na suaksak kei un** = do not make it a house of business/commerce
  - Usage: A command against profaning a sacred place for commercial gain.

- **Nangma inn ka deih luatna in kei hong netum ding hi** = The zeal for your house will consume me
  - Usage: A direct quote reflecting intense devotion to God's house (Psalm 69:9).

- **ni thum sungin ka lamkik ding hi** = I will raise it up in three days
  - Usage: Jesus' cryptic statement about rebuilding the temple, referring to his body.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X in Y a ciap ciangin, Z suak a...**
  - Temporal clause structure: 'When X did Y, Z happened...'. Shows sequence of events.
  - Example: Pawi ukpa in tui a ciap ciangin, leenggahtui suak a...

- **Ahi zongin [Subject] in [Object] tudong na [Verb] hi.**
  - Contrastive statement using 'Ahi zongin' (However/But) to show an exception to the norm.
  - Example: Ahi zongin nangmah in a khum pen teng tudong na sitlai hi,”

- **X in Y a muh ciangin; Z a bawl hi.**
  - Structure showing action taken immediately upon observation: 'When X saw Y, he did Z.'
  - Example: bawngte, tuute, vakhute a zuakte leh sumkheekte tu-a om uh a muh ciangin; a sat nading khautep a bawl khit ciangin...

- **Hih nate na paimangpih un. Ka Pa’ inn sumzuakna inn na suaksak kei un.**
  - Imperative commands using the suffix '-un' (let/do it) and negative imperative '-kei un' (do not do it).
  - Example: Hih nate na paimangpih un. Ka Pa’ inn sumzuakna inn na suaksak kei un,”

- **Tua ahih ciangin, [Subject] in [Question] a ci uh hi.**
  - Structure indicating a reaction or response following a preceding event: 'Because of that, [Subject] asked [Question].'
  - Example: Tua ahih ciangin Judah mite in, “Nang in hih bang na hihna tawh bang nalamdang hong lak na hi hiam?” a ci uh hi.
