# 1CH - Part 17

## Vocabulary with Context

### ahi hi = they are/it is
**Context:** Used at the end of a list of names to signify they are the ones being referred to (the sons of Israel).
**Example:** a pa Levi, a pa Israel, ahi hi.

### lasa pawl = choir/singing group
**Context:** Refers to the organized groups of singers/musicians in the temple service.
**Example:** Asaf in lasa pawl a nihna a makaipa hi a

### makaipa = leader/chief
**Context:** Indicates the person in charge of a specific group (in this case, the second choir).
**Example:** lasa pawl a nihna a makaipa hi a

### khangsimna = lineage/generation/descendants
**Context:** Refers to the line of descent from a specific ancestor.
**Example:** ama khangsimna a pupi Levi lam manawhin kileh simto hi.

### pupi = ancestor/forefather
**Context:** Used here to mean 'ancestor' in the context of tracing lineage back to Levi.
**Example:** a pupi Levi lam manawhin kileh simto hi.

### manawhin = through/via (the line of)
**Context:** Indicates the path or line through which the descent is traced.
**Example:** a pupi Levi lam manawhin kileh simto hi.

### kileh simto = traced back/followed down
**Context:** Describes the action of tracing the genealogy back to the ancestor.
**Example:** manawhin kileh simto hi.

### beh sung = clan/family division
**Context:** Refers to the specific sub-division within the larger tribe (Merari-te beh sung = the clan of Merari).
**Example:** Merari-te beh sung panin Ethan in lasa pawl a thumna a ukpa hi a

### ukpa = leader/governor/one who rules
**Context:** Used here to mean the leader of the third choir group.
**Example:** lase pawl a thumna a ukpa hi a

### sanggamte = brethren/relatives
**Context:** Refers to the other members of the tribe or group (the other Levites).
**Example:** Levi mi a sanggamte uh

### kisehte = appointed/assigned
**Context:** Indicates they were designated for a specific task.
**Example:** nasep dingin a kisehte ahi hi.

### biakbuk nasep dang khempeuh = all other service of the tabernacle
**Context:** Refers to all the miscellaneous duties related to the tabernacle service, excluding the priestly duties.
**Example:** Pasian’ biakbuk nasep dang khempeuh a sem dingin

### siangtho pen mun = most holy place
**Context:** Refers to the inner sanctuary of the tabernacle.
**Example:** siangtho pen mun nasepnate khempeuh

### kilemna a bawl dingin = to make atonement
**Context:** The purpose of their actions on the altars.
**Example:** Israel mite-a' dingin kilemna a bawl dingin

### meihal biakna tau = altar of burnt offering
**Context:** The specific altar used for burnt sacrifices.
**Example:** meihal biakna tau tung leh paknamtui biakna tau tungah piakna bawl uh hi.

### paknamtui biakna tau = altar of incense
**Context:** The specific altar used for burning incense.
**Example:** paknamtui biakna tau tungah piakna a bawl uh hi.

### suanlehkhakte = descendants/offspring
**Context:** A formal way to refer to the sons and grandsons (descendants) of Aaron.
**Example:** Aaron' suanlekhakte:

## Phrases & Usage

- **a pa [Name]** = the son of [Name]
  - Usage: Used repeatedly in genealogical lists to denote direct descent.

- **a pupi Levi lam manawhin kileh simto hi** = traced back through the line of their ancestor Levi
  - Usage: Used when describing how a lineage connects back to a major tribal ancestor.

- **Pasian’ nasem Moses’ thupiaksate khempeuh bangin** = according to all that Moses the servant of God commanded
  - Usage: Used to emphasize that actions were performed strictly according to divine instruction given through Moses.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X, a pa Y, a pa Z, ... ahi hi/ahi uh hi.**
  - Standard Zolai construction for listing genealogies, where 'a pa' functions as 'the son of' connecting each successive generation.
  - Example: a pa Izhar, a pa Kohath, a pa Levi, a pa Israel, ahi hi.

- **Noun-te beh sung panin [Person] pen [Role] a [Number] a [Title] hi a, ...**
  - Structure used to identify individuals belonging to a specific clan (beh sung) and their role/rank within a group (e.g., choir leader).
  - Example: Merari-te beh sung panin Ethan pen lasa pawl thumna a ukpa hi a

- **Noun leh Noun-te in [Action] a bawl uh hi.**
  - Structure indicating that two or more groups performed a specific action together (using 'leh' for 'and').
  - Example: Aaron leh a tapate in ... piakna a bawl uh hi.
