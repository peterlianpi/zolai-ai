# 1CH - Part 10

## Vocabulary with Context

### tapa = son
**Context:** Used to denote lineage, 'son of'.
**Example:** Shobal’ tapa Reajah in Jahath’ pa hi a

### pa = father
**Context:** Used to denote ancestry, 'father of'.
**Example:** Jahath’ pa hi a

### ahi hi = is/was
**Context:** A common copula structure indicating identity or state.
**Example:** Jahath’ pa hi a

### teeng = to dwell/live
**Context:** Referring to the place where a group resides.
**Example:** Zorah khua a teeng minamte

### minam = clan/tribe/family group
**Context:** Referring to the lineage groups residing in a place.
**Example:** Zorah khua a teeng minamte

### pu leh pate = ancestors and forefathers
**Context:** A compound term emphasizing deep ancestry.
**Example:** minamte pu leh pate ahi-te hi

### zi = wife
**Context:** Used to denote a man's spouse.
**Example:** a pa Kaleb' zi Efrath'

### ta upa = firstborn
**Context:** Indicates the firstborn among sons or descendants.
**Example:** Kaleb' zi Efrath' ta upa pen hi a

### suanlekhakte = descendants
**Context:** Refers to the offspring or progeny of someone.
**Example:** ama suanlekhakte in Bethlehem khuapi sat-te hi

### khuapi = city/town
**Context:** Geographical location, often used when referring to founding a settlement.
**Example:** Bethlehem khuapi sat-te hi

### tapa thumte = three sons
**Context:** Specifying the number of sons.
**Example:** Hur' tapa thumte: Etam, Penuel, leh Ezer ahi-te hi

### tanu khat = one daughter
**Context:** Specifying a single daughter.
**Example:** Tanu khat zong nei a

### satpa = founder/settler
**Context:** One who established or founded a city.
**Example:** Penuel pen Gedor khuapi a satpa hi a

### tawih = to bear/give birth (implied in context)
**Context:** Used in the context of Jabez' mother's sorrow.
**Example:** Gimpi-in amah tawh Ahuzzam... nei hi

### pahtawihuai zaw = more honorable
**Context:** Comparative adjective phrase describing Jabez.
**Example:** Jabez a kici mi khat in a sanggamte sangin pahtawihuai zaw hi

### Gimpi = sorrow/pain
**Context:** The reason the mother named her son Jabez.
**Example:** “Gimpi-in amah nei ka hih manin,” ci-in a min Jabez phuak hi.

### thu ngen = to pray/make a request
**Context:** The action Jabez performed towards God.
**Example:** Jabez in Israel Pasian tungah thu ngen a

### gamgi hong keeksak in = enlarge my border/territory
**Context:** A specific petition in Jabez' prayer.
**Example:** ka gamgi hong keeksak in

### hong ompih in = be with me
**Context:** A request for divine presence.
**Example:** Kei hong ompih in la

### gimsak loh nadingin = so that it may not grieve/harm me
**Context:** A request for protection from harm.
**Example:** kei hong gimsak loh nadingin siatna panin hong kem in!

### ngetna = request/petition
**Context:** What God granted Jabez.
**Example:** Pasian in ama ngetna pia hi.

### sanggampa = brother
**Context:** Used to denote a male sibling relationship.
**Example:** Shuhah’ sanggampa Khelub

### suante pupi = the families of the descendants
**Context:** Referring to the lineage groups originating from Aharhel.
**Example:** Harum’ tapa Aharhel’ suante' pupi ahi hi.

### khutsiamte vive = all craftsmen
**Context:** Describing the skill set of the people of Geharashim.
**Example:** tua khua-a teng mite khutsiamte vive ahih manin

## Phrases & Usage

- **pu leh pate** = ancestors and forefathers
  - Usage: Used to emphasize the deep, foundational lineage of a group.

- **ta upa pen hi a** = was the firstborn
  - Usage: Used to identify the eldest son in a generation.

- **a mah tawh... nei hi** = he/she bore him/her with...
  - Usage: Used when listing children born to a specific parent, often implying the mother (when 'amah' refers to the mother).

- **thupha hong pia-in** = bless me indeed
  - Usage: A formal request for divine blessing.

- **siatna panin hong kem in** = keep me from evil
  - Usage: A plea for protection against wickedness or harm.

- **tua min a kiphuak ahi hi** = that name was given/derived
  - Usage: Explaining the origin or reason behind a place name.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X' tapa Y in Z pa hi a**
  - Possessive structure showing lineage: 'The son of X, Y, was the father of Z.' (X's son Y was Z's father).
  - Example: Shobal’ tapa Reajah in Jahath’ pa hi a

- **Hihte in [Place] a teeng minamte pu leh pate ahi-te hi.**
  - Identification of a group as the foundational ancestors of the clans dwelling in a specific location.
  - Example: Hihte in Zorah khua a teeng minamte pu leh pate ahi-te hi.

- **Amah in [List of sons] nei hi.**
  - Simple statement of progeny: 'He/She had [list of sons/daughters].'
  - Example: Naarah in amah tawh Ahuzzam, Hefer, Temeni, leh Haahashtari nei hi.

- **X in Y tungah thu ngen a, [Quotation]**
  - Structure for reporting prayer: 'X prayed to Y, saying, [Quotation]'.
  - Example: Jabez in Israel Pasian tungah thu ngen a, “Kei thupha hong pia-in...
