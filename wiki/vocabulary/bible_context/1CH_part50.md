# 1CH - Part 50

## Vocabulary with Context

### tulli = twenty-four thousand (or four thousand, contextually)
**Context:** Used to denote specific numbers of people assigned to tasks (24,000 for work, 4,000 for porters/praise). The KJV suggests 4,000 for the latter parts.
**Example:** Hihte tul sawmnih leh tulli in Topa’ inn sungah nasep uk ding a

### nasep uk ding = to be set forward/to manage the work
**Context:** Refers to the responsibility of overseeing the work in the Lord's house.
**Example:** tul sawmnih leh tulli in Topa’ inn sungah nasep uk ding a

### mangte = porters/gatekeepers (from context, likely related to guarding/keeping)
**Context:** One of the groups assigned duties, alongside overseers and judges.
**Example:** tulguk in mangte leh thukhente hi ding a

### thukhente = judges/those who decide
**Context:** A group assigned a specific role alongside porters/overseers.
**Example:** tulguk in mangte leh thukhente hi ding a

### kongcingte = gatekeepers
**Context:** A specific group of Levites assigned the duty of guarding the gates.
**Example:** tulli in kongcingte, leh tulli in phatna ding a

### phatna = praise/worship
**Context:** The act of giving praise to the Lord, often associated with music/singing.
**Example:** leh tulli in phatna ding a

### lasaknate = songs/psalms
**Context:** The musical compositions used for praising God.
**Example:** ka bawlsa lasaknate tawh Topa’ tungah phatna pia ding-te hi

### beh behin = by their families/clans (literally 'group by group')
**Context:** Describes how David organized the Levites.
**Example:** David in Levi mite a beh behin pawl thum suah a

### tapate = sons/descendants
**Context:** Used consistently to refer to the male descendants of the patriarchs (Gershon, Ladan, etc.).
**Example:** Gershon tapate in Ladan leh Shimei ahi-te hi.

### ukpi = chief/head
**Context:** Used to denote the primary leader or head of a specific lineage or group.
**Example:** A ukpi Jehiel, leh Zetham, leh Joel, thum pha-te hi.

### beh lutangte = heads of the families/chiefs of the clans
**Context:** Refers to the principal members of the Ladan clan.
**Example:** Hihte in Ladan’ beh lutangte ahi-te hi.

### innkuan khat kisuak sakin = counted as one household/family unit
**Context:** Explaining why two smaller branches were grouped together for counting.
**Example:** innkuan khat kisuak sakin sim khatin kisim khawm hi.

### paknamtui = incense (literally 'fragrant powder/substance')
**Context:** The material Aaron and his sons were to burn before the Lord.
**Example:** paknamtui a hal tawntung ding

### siangtho pen nate = the most holy things
**Context:** The most sacred duties assigned to Aaron, specifically related to the altar/tabernacle service.
**Example:** a siangtho pen nate a ap dingin Aaron kituam koih hi.

### kituam koih = was set apart/consecrated
**Context:** Describes the setting aside of Aaron for priestly duties.
**Example:** Aaron kituam koih hi.

### Pasian’ mipa = Man of God
**Context:** A title used specifically for Moses.
**Example:** Pasian’ mipa Moses’ tapate ahih leh

## Phrases & Usage

- **Topa’ tungah phatna pia ding-te hi** = they shall give praise to the Lord
  - Usage: Used to describe the function or duty assigned to a group (here, those with musical compositions).

- **a beh behin pawl thum suah a** = he divided them into three groups by their clans
  - Usage: Describes the organizational action taken by a leader (David) over a large group (Levites).

- **tapa tampi nei lo-te ahih manin** = because they did not have many sons
  - Usage: Provides the reason for an administrative grouping or consolidation.

- **Topa’ mai-ah paknamtui a hal tawntung ding** = to burn incense continually before the Lord
  - Usage: Describes a perpetual priestly duty.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X leh Y ahi-te hi.**
  - A simple declarative structure used to list items or people belonging to a category (e.g., 'X and Y they were').
  - Example: Gershon tapate in Ladan leh Shimei ahi-te hi.

- **X’ tapate in: A, B, leh C, [number] pha-te hi.**
  - A pattern used to list the sons/descendants of a patriarch (X), followed by the total count.
  - Example: Ladan’ tapate: A ukpi Jehiel, leh Zetham, leh Joel, thum pha-te hi.

- **Ahih hangin X ahih leh Y lakah kisim hi.**
  - A structure indicating exception or specific placement: 'However, concerning X, they were counted among Y.'
  - Example: Ahih hangin Pasian’ mipa Moses’ tapate ahih leh Levi minamte lakah kisim hi.

- **X in tapa dang nei lo a, ahih hangin Y tam mahmah hi.**
  - A contrastive structure showing that while the father (X) had few sons, his descendants (Y) were numerous.
  - Example: Eliezer in tapa dang neilo a, ahih hangin Rehabiah’ tapate tam mahmah hi.
