# 1CH - Part 13

## Vocabulary with Context

### tapate = sons of
**Context:** Used to introduce the lineage or descendants of Joel.
**Example:** Joel’ tapate in: A tapa Shemaiah...

### suante = sons of
**Context:** Used similarly to 'tapate' to denote descendants, often in a more formal genealogical context.
**Example:** Joel' suante: A tapa Shemaiah...

### kumpipa = king
**Context:** Refers to Tilgath-pilneser, the king of Assyria.
**Example:** Assiria kumpipa Tilgath-pilneser in...

### amau’ = their
**Context:** Possessive pronoun referring to the people being discussed (Reubenites/descendants).
**Example:** amau’ minam makai...

### minam = nation/people/tribe
**Context:** Used to refer to a specific group or tribe.
**Example:** amau’ minam makai...

### makai = chief/leader
**Context:** Designates a person holding a position of leadership within the tribe.
**Example:** amau’ minam makai Be-erah...

### sal-in a puakkhiatpa = one who carried away captive
**Context:** Describes the action taken by the Assyrian king against Be-erah.
**Example:** Tilgath-pilneser in amau’ minam makai Be-erah manin sal-in a puakkhiatpa ahi hi

### ukpipa = prince/ruler
**Context:** Describes Be-erah's status among the Reubenites.
**Example:** amah in Reuben mite a ukpipa ahi hi.

### khangsimna = genealogy/generation record
**Context:** Refers to the official recording of their lineage.
**Example:** amau’ khangsimna a kiciamteh ciangin...

### kiciamteh = reckoned/recorded
**Context:** Used in the context of official documentation of genealogies.
**Example:** khangsimna a kiciamteh ciangin...

### innkuan kuanin = by their families/household by household
**Context:** Indicates the manner in which the leaders were identified (family by family).
**Example:** a innkuan kuanin ama behte in...

### beh = clan/house/family unit
**Context:** A smaller division within the tribe.
**Example:** Joel mite beh sung panin...

### sung = within/part of
**Context:** Indicates membership within a larger group (clan or house).
**Example:** beh sung panin...

### a teeng-te hi = they dwelt/resided
**Context:** Describes where a specific group settled.
**Example:** Nebo leh Baalmeon ciang dong Aroer-ah a teeng-te hi.

### bawngte = cattle/livestock
**Context:** Refers to their wealth or possessions, which necessitated expansion.
**Example:** amau’ bawngte khangin tampi pha ahih manin...

### tampi pha = multiplied greatly
**Context:** Indicates a large increase in their livestock.
**Example:** bawngte khangin tampi pha ahih manin...

### nisuahna lam = eastward direction/side
**Context:** Geographical direction.
**Example:** nisuahna lam pang sehnel gam lutna ciang dong...

### do-in zo-te hi = fought and conquered/defeated
**Context:** Describes military victory over the Hagarites.
**Example:** Hagri mite do-in zo-te hi;

### puanbukte = tents/encampments
**Context:** Refers to their temporary or mobile dwellings.
**Example:** amau’ puanbukte luahin a sungah teeng-te hi.

### luahin = taking possession of/occupying
**Context:** Indicates taking over the area where the tents were.
**Example:** puanbukte luahin a sungah teeng-te hi.

### gal khatah = over against them/opposite them
**Context:** Describes the location of the Gadites relative to the Reubenites' territory.
**Example:** Reubente’ gal khatah a teeng Gad minamte in:

### beh makai = clan leaders
**Context:** Leaders of the specific clans within the Gad tribe.
**Example:** Bashan-ah beh makai ahi-te hi.

### a zom = the next/the second in rank
**Context:** Used to denote the next person listed after the chief (Joel).
**Example:** Ukpi Joel, a zom Shafam...

### sagih pha-te hi = there were seven in total
**Context:** A concluding statement indicating the total count of the listed brethren.
**Example:** leh Eber, sagih pha-te hi.

### mi thahat = valiant men/mighty men
**Context:** Describes the quality of the soldiers listed.
**Example:** mi thahat, lum leh namsau kuahin...

### lum = shield/buckler
**Context:** Refers to military equipment.
**Example:** lum leh namsau kuahin thal a tawi...

### namsau = sword
**Context:** Refers to military equipment.
**Example:** lum leh namsau kuahin...

### thal a tawi = who bore/carried the bow
**Context:** Describes those skilled with the bow (archers).
**Example:** lum leh namsau kuahin thal a tawi...

### gal a siam = skilful in war
**Context:** Describes their military proficiency.
**Example:** gal a siam, nasem dingin a baihsa-in a om...

### a baihsa-in a om = ready/prepared
**Context:** Indicates readiness for service or duty.
**Example:** nasem dingin a baihsa-in a om mi...

### tul sawmli leh tulli leh zasagih leh sawmguk = forty-four thousand seven hundred and sixty
**Context:** A large numerical count of the fighting men.
**Example:** mi tul sawmli leh tulli leh zasagih leh sawmguk nei hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **a tapa** = his son
  - Usage: Standard genealogical marker used repeatedly to trace descent (e.g., A tapa Shemaiah, a tapa Gog).

- **manin sal-in a puakkhiatpa ahi hi** = he is the one who was carried away captive by
  - Usage: A passive construction describing someone taken into exile by a foreign power.

- **a innkuan kuanin ama behte in** = by their families, household by household
  - Usage: Describes the method of enumeration or organization by family unit.

- **ciang dong** = up to the boundary/as far as
  - Usage: Used to define the extent of territory or settlement (e.g., Aroer-ah a teeng-te hi).

- **nisuahna lam pang** = on the side of the east/eastward
  - Usage: Used for directional description of territory.

- **do-in zo-te hi** = they fought and conquered
  - Usage: A concise way to state a military victory.

- **a khuapite sung** = in their suburbs/outskirts
  - Usage: Describes the area surrounding a main town or settlement.

- **khangsimna-ah kiciamteh hi** = were reckoned in the genealogy
  - Usage: Formal statement indicating inclusion in the official records during a specific reign.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X’ tapa Y, a tapa Z, a tapa W...**
  - Repetitive structure used for listing direct male descendants in a lineage (X's son is Y, Y's son is Z, Z's son is W).
  - Example: Joel’ tapate in: A tapa Shemaiah, a tapa Gog, a tapa Shimei,

- **Amah pen [Tribe] mite a [Status] ahi hi.**
  - Sentence structure identifying a person's role or status within a specific tribal group. (Amah pen = He is/was).
  - Example: amah in Reuben mite a ukpipa ahi hi.

- **[Location]-ah a teeng-te hi.**
  - Simple statement of residence or settlement, using the locative suffix '-ah'.
  - Example: Aroer-ah a teeng-te hi.

- **Hihte khempeuh [Time Period]-in khangsimna-ah kiciamteh hi.**
  - A summary statement indicating that all the preceding individuals/groups were officially recorded during specific historical reigns.
  - Example: Hihte khempeuh Judah kumpi Jotham’ hun sung...ciangin khangsimna-ah kiciamteh hi.

- **[Noun] kuahin [Noun] a tawi**
  - Describes the equipment carried by the soldiers, using 'kuahin' (with/by means of).
  - Example: lum leh namsau kuahin thal a tawi
