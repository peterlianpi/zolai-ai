# 1CH - Part 51

## Vocabulary with Context

### tapa = son(s)
**Context:** Used to denote lineage, 'sons of [father's name]'.
**Example:** Kohath’ tapa thumna Hebron’ tapate lakah

### tapate = sons (plural)
**Context:** Plural form of 'tapa', referring to the male descendants.
**Example:** Kohath’ tapa thumna Hebron’ tapate lakah

### ukpi = first (chief/head)
**Context:** Used here to mean 'the first one' in a sequence of sons, likely implying the firstborn or chief among them.
**Example:** Jeriah a ukpi

### nihna = second
**Context:** Used to denote the second position in a sequence.
**Example:** Amariah a nihna

### thumna = third
**Context:** Used to denote the third position in a sequence.
**Example:** Jahaziel a thumna

### lina = fourth
**Context:** Used to denote the fourth position in a sequence.
**Example:** leh Jekameam a lina ahi hi.

### nei loin = without having (not having)
**Context:** Indicates the absence of sons before death.
**Example:** Eleazar in tapa neiloin si a

### tanu = daughter(s)
**Context:** Refers to female descendants.
**Example:** tanu bek nei hi

### bek = only
**Context:** Used restrictively, meaning 'only' or 'just'.
**Example:** tanu bek nei hi

### tanau = brethren/relatives (male)
**Context:** Refers to the male relatives, specifically the sons of Kish in this context.
**Example:** Amau' tanau, Kish tapate in

### ten'pih uh hi = took them (as wives)
**Context:** In the context of inheritance/marriage when a man dies without sons, his relatives marry the daughters.
**Example:** Kish tapate in amaute ten'pih uh hi.

### pha uh hi = were three (in number)
**Context:** Indicates the total count of the listed individuals.
**Example:** thum pha uh hi.

### nasepna = work/service
**Context:** Refers to the duties or service required, specifically for the house of the Lord.
**Example:** Topa’ inn nasepna ah na a sem ding

### tungsiah = and above/upwards of
**Context:** Used with age to indicate the minimum age requirement for service.
**Example:** kum sawmnih panin a tungsiah

### mi phazah = number of people/head count
**Context:** Refers to the census or counting of the Levites.
**Example:** a mi phazah zui-in

### kiciamtehna = designation/marking
**Context:** Refers to how they were officially recorded or identified.
**Example:** a kiciamtehna bangin

### beh lutangte = heads of clans/chiefs of families
**Context:** Refers to the leaders of the family groups within Levi.
**Example:** beh lutangte

### lungnopna = peace/contentment/rest
**Context:** Used here in the sense of establishing peace or rest for the people.
**Example:** mite tungah lungnopna piazo a

### tawntung = forever/continually
**Context:** Indicates permanence.
**Example:** Jerusalem-ah amah om tawntung ta hi.

### puanbuk = tabernacle
**Context:** The portable sanctuary structure.
**Example:** Levi mite in puanbuk hita leh

### kisam nawn lo hi = will no longer be required/needed
**Context:** Indicates that a previous duty is now abolished.
**Example:** a puak ding uh kisam nawnlo hi

### thupiakna = command/ordinance
**Context:** Refers to David's final instructions or decrees.
**Example:** David' thupiakna a nunung pen tawh

### huhin = assisting/helping
**Context:** Used to describe the supportive role the Levites played for Aaron's sons.
**Example:** Aaron' suante huhin

### inntualte = courtyards (of the sanctuary)
**Context:** Refers to the outer areas surrounding the holy place/tent.
**Example:** biakinn tualte leh

### inndeite = tents/tents of the sanctuary (Holy Place)
**Context:** Likely referring to the inner, more sacred areas or the structure itself.
**Example:** biakinn deite kemin

### siangtho sak ding = to sanctify/make holy
**Context:** The duty of keeping things clean and consecrated for worship.
**Example:** biakna na khempeuh a siangtho sak ding

### Ompihna Anlum = Shewbread/Bread of the Presence
**Context:** Specific offering bread.
**Example:** Ompihna Anlum

### annel = meat offering/grain offering
**Context:** Refers to the flour/grain component of sacrifices.
**Example:** an tawh biakna dingin annel

### silngo sawh loh anlum moh = unleavened cakes
**Context:** Refers to bread made without leaven.
**Example:** silngo sawhloh anlum moh

### ki-em piakna = that which is baked (in a pan/griddle)
**Context:** Referring to a specific preparation method for offerings.
**Example:** a ki-em piakna

### sathau tawh a kihel piakna = that which is fried with oil
**Context:** Referring to offerings cooked by frying.
**Example:** sathau tawh a kihel piakna

### lungdamna koin = with songs of thanksgiving
**Context:** Describing the manner of praising the Lord in the morning.
**Example:** lungdamna ko-in { Topa } phatin

### meihal biakna = burnt offering
**Context:** Refers to sacrifices completely consumed by fire.
**Example:** Topa tungah meihal biakna a kipiak peuhpeuh ciangin

### kikhopna puanbuk = tabernacle of the congregation
**Context:** The formal name for the portable sanctuary.
**Example:** kikhopna puanbuk leh biakinn kemin

### kisehkhennate = divisions/arrangements
**Context:** Refers to the organized classes or orders of the priests.
**Example:** Aaron' suante a kisehkhennate in hihte ahi hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **a ukpi, a nihna, a thumna, leh a lina** = the first, the second, the third, and the fourth
  - Usage: Used sequentially to list individuals in order of rank or birth among a group of brothers.

- **kum sawmnih panin a tungsiah** = from twenty years old and above
  - Usage: Specifies the minimum age for inclusion in the service count or duty.

- **a tawntungin Topa mai-ah om ding uh hi** = they shall continually stand before the Lord
  - Usage: Describes the perpetual nature of their priestly duties.

- **a beh behin Levi suante ahi hi** = the descendants of Levi, according to their families
  - Usage: Used to categorize the Levites based on their lineage and family structure.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X' tapa Y**
  - Possessive structure indicating 'Son(s) of X' (Y). The apostrophe denotes possession/relation.
  - Example: Kohath’ tapa thumna

- **A [noun] a [ordinal number] ahi hi.**
  - Structure used for listing individuals in order: 'He/She is the [ordinal number] of [noun group]'.
  - Example: Jeriah a ukpi, Amariah a nihna, Jahaziel a thumna leh Jekameam a lina ahi hi.

- **X in Y nei loin si a, Z bek nei hi.**
  - Describes a sequence of events regarding descendants: 'X died without having Y, and only had Z.'
  - Example: Eleazar in tapa neiloin si a, tanu bek nei hi.

- **X dingin Y a sem ding uh ahi hi.**
  - Purpose clause structure: 'They shall do Y for the purpose of X.'
  - Example: biakna na khempeuh a siangtho sak ding, leh Pasian' inn nasepna-ah hih ding khat peuhpeuh a sem ding uh ahi hi.
