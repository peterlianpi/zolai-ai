# 1CH - Part 48

## Vocabulary with Context

### ciangin = then/after that
**Context:** Used to mark the sequence of events.
**Example:** Tua ciangin Ornan in David kiangah...

### hoih = good/pleasing
**Context:** Used to describe what the king finds acceptable or pleasing.
**Example:** La in, ka to kumpipa in hoih na sak bangin hih in;

### meihal biaknate = burnt offerings
**Context:** Refers to sacrifices consumed by fire.
**Example:** thei in, meihal biaknate a, zat dingin bawngtalte...

### ancilna singte = threshing instruments/sticks
**Context:** Items used for threshing, offered as wood for the offering.
**Example:** a sing dingin ancilna singte...

### mangbuhte = wheat/grain
**Context:** The grain offered along with the animals/wood for the meat offering.
**Example:** leh an tawh biak nadingin a mangbuhte kong pia hi.

### vekpi-in = entirely/wholly
**Context:** Indicates giving everything offered.
**Example:** A vekpi-in kong pia hi,” a ci hi.

### Hilo hi = It is not so/Nay
**Context:** A strong negation or refusal.
**Example:** “Hilo hi, a man a cing tawh kong lei ding hi.

### cing tawh = with full price/value
**Context:** Indicates paying the full cost.
**Example:** a man a cing tawh kong lei ding hi.

### bei loin = without loss/without taking for free
**Context:** Emphasizes that David will pay, not take freely.
**Example:** keima sum bei loin meihal biaknate zong ka pia kei ding hi,”

### kham shekel zaguk a gik = six hundred shekels of gold by weight
**Context:** The specific amount and form of payment.
**Example:** kham shekel zaguk a gik a pia hi.

### biakna tau = altar
**Context:** A structure built for sacrifice/worship.
**Example:** Topa’ adingin biakna tau khat lamin...

### thu-nuama biaknate = peace offerings/offerings of well-being
**Context:** A type of offering distinct from burnt offerings.
**Example:** meihal biaknate leh thu-nuama biaknate bawlin...

### meikuang = fire/flame
**Context:** Fire descending from heaven, indicating acceptance of the offering.
**Example:** vantung panin meikuang pia suk hi.

### namsau = sword
**Context:** Refers to the angel's weapon, symbolizing judgment or divine action.
**Example:** vantungmi in a namsau a pai sungah siat kik hi.

### siet kik hi = was sheathed/put away
**Context:** The angel's sword being returned, indicating the plague/judgment stopped.
**Example:** a namsau a pai sungah siat kik hi.

### biakbuk = tabernacle
**Context:** The portable sanctuary used during the wilderness wandering.
**Example:** Moses in gamlakah a bawl Topa’ biakbuk...

### pai ngam lo hi = could not go/was not able to go
**Context:** Expresses inability due to fear or prohibition.
**Example:** David pai ngam lo hi.

### kikaihkhopna dingun = for gathering together
**Context:** Purpose of commanding the strangers (foreigners).
**Example:** gamdang mite a kikaihkhopna dingun David in thu a pia hi.

### suangseekte = stones/quarried stones
**Context:** Materials prepared for building the temple.
**Example:** suangseeksate a bawl dingin suangseekte a koih hi.

### siktukkilh = nails/spikes
**Context:** Metalwork prepared for securing doors and joints.
**Example:** kongpi kongkhak-a zat dingin siktukkilh leh gakna dingin...

### sumngo = brass/bronze
**Context:** Metal prepared in large quantity.
**Example:** teh zawhlohin sumngo tampi tak zong koih a,

### taamtaak singte = cedar wood
**Context:** Specific type of wood brought for construction.
**Example:** taamtak singte simzawh hilo hi;

### khangno = young/youth
**Context:** Describing Solomon's youthfulness.
**Example:** Ka tapa Solomon khangno-in pumpi tawh sinna om nai lo a,

### kiging khol ding hi = I will prepare/gather in advance
**Context:** David's intention to stockpile materials before Solomon builds.
**Example:** tua biakinn dingin keimah ka kigingkhol ding hi,”

### sam a = called/summoned
**Context:** David summoning Solomon.
**Example:** Tua ciangin amah in a tapa Solomon sam a,

### lungsimah om hi = was in my heart/mind
**Context:** Expressing David's long-held desire to build the temple.
**Example:** lam ding ka lungsimah om hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **La in** = Take it
  - Usage: An imperative command to accept something offered.

- **a man a cing tawh kong lei ding hi** = I will verily buy it for the full price
  - Usage: A formal declaration of intent to purchase something at its full value, refusing a gift.

- **vantung panin meikuang pia suk hi** = fire descended from heaven
  - Usage: Describes a divine sign or acceptance of an offering.

- **minthan’na leh pahtawina ah alian mahmah ding ahi hi** = it will be very great in fame and glory
  - Usage: Prophesying the future greatness and renown of the structure being built.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X in Y kiangah, "..." a ci hi.**
  - Standard structure for reporting speech: X said to Y, '...'.
  - Example: Ornan in David kiangah, “La in, ...” a ci hi.

- **Ahih hangin X, Y ahih manin Z lo hi.**
  - Structure showing contrast or reason: 'However, because X is Y, Z could not happen.'
  - Example: ahih hangin amah in { Topa' } vantung mi' namsau kihta ahih manin Pasian tungah thu a dong dingin David pai ngamlo hi.

- **X dingin Y a bawl dingin Z koih hi.**
  - Structure indicating purpose: X was done for the purpose of Y, Z was prepared.
  - Example: Pasian' biakinn lamna dingin suangseeksate a bawl dingin suangseekte a koih hi.

- **X khangno-in pumpi tawh sinna om nai lo a, ...**
  - Describing someone's youthfulness and lack of experience ('has not yet experienced adulthood/full strength').
  - Example: Ka tapa Solomon khangno-in pumpi tawh sinna om nai lo a,
