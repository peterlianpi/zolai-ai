## Vocabulary with Context

### kuana = ninth
**Context:** Used to denote the ordinal number for the ninth position in a list.
**Example:** a kuana Mattaniah

### tapate = his sons
**Context:** Refers to the male offspring of the person named.
**Example:** a tapate leh a sanggamte

### sanggamte = his brethren/relatives
**Context:** Refers to male relatives, likely brothers or close kin, associated with the main person.
**Example:** a tapate leh a sanggamte

### sawm-le-nih = twelve
**Context:** The number twelve, used here to state the count of people in each group.
**Example:** sawm-le-nih tung tu hi

### tung tu hi = were/amounted to
**Context:** A concluding phrase indicating the total number or state of being.
**Example:** sawm-le-nih tung tu hi

## Phrases & Usage

- **a kuana [Name]** = The ninth to [Name]
  - Usage: Used to introduce the ninth entry or person in a sequential list.

- **leh a tapate leh a sanggamte** = he, his sons, and his brethren
  - Usage: A standard construction used repeatedly to group the principal person with his immediate male relatives when counting.

- **sawm-le-nih tung tu hi** = were twelve
  - Usage: A concluding statement confirming the count of twelve for the preceding group.

## Grammar Patterns

- **Ordinal Number + [Name]**
  - The structure for listing entries sequentially, e.g., 'a kuana Mattaniah' (The ninth Mattaniah). The prefix 'a' marks the ordinal.
  - Example: a kuana Mattaniah

- **sawm-le-[Number]na**
  - Compound formation for numbers 11 through 19, combining 'sawm' (ten) with the subsequent number using '-le-' (and) and ending with '-na' (ordinal marker). E.g., sawm-le-khatna (10+1+ordinal = 11th).
  - Example: sawm-le-khatna (Eleventh)

- **sawmnih-le-[Number]na**
  - Compound formation for numbers 21 through 23, combining 'sawmnih' (twenty) with the subsequent number using '-le-' and ending with '-na'.
  - Example: sawmnih-le-khatna (Twenty-first)
