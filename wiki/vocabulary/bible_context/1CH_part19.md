# 1CH - Part 19

## Vocabulary with Context

### lonona = suburbs/surrounding areas
**Context:** Used repeatedly with city names (e.g., Jokmean a lonona gamte) to denote the associated suburbs or surrounding territories.
**Example:** Jokmean a lonona gamte tawh

### gamte = places/territories/towns (plural)
**Context:** Used in conjunction with 'lonona' (suburbs) to mean 'all the suburbs' or 'the territories of the suburbs'.
**Example:** Jokmean a lonona gamte tawh

### tawh = and/with
**Context:** Functions as a conjunction, linking listed items (cities and their suburbs).
**Example:** Jokmean a lonona gamte tawh, Bethhoron a lonona gamte tawh

### leh = and
**Context:** A standard conjunction used to connect items in a list, often appearing before the final item or when continuing a list.
**Example:** leh Manasseh minam a langte’ gam sung panin

### minam = tribe/clan
**Context:** Refers to the tribal divisions (e.g., Manasseh minam).
**Example:** Manasseh minam a langte’ gamsung panin

### langte’ = half (of)
**Context:** Used with 'minam' to mean 'half tribe' (e.g., Manasseh minam a langte’ = half of the tribe of Manasseh).
**Example:** Manasseh minam a langte’ gamsung panin

### gamsung = territory/land/region
**Context:** Refers to the geographical area belonging to a tribe or group.
**Example:** Manasseh minam a langte’ gamsung panin

### panin = from/out of
**Context:** Indicates the source or origin from which something is given or taken (e.g., from the territory of a tribe).
**Example:** Manasseh minam a langte’ gamsung panin

### beh = family/division/household
**Context:** Used to denote smaller kinship groups within a tribe (e.g., Kohath-te beh, Gershom-te beh).
**Example:** hihte pen Kohath-te beh a omlaite-a' ding ahi hi.

### omlaite = the remaining ones/the remnant
**Context:** Used with 'beh' (family) to specify the remaining families or remnant of a lineage (e.g., Kohath-te beh a omlaite = the remnant of the families of Kohath).
**Example:** Kohath-te beh a omlaite-a' ding ahi hi.

### ading = for/intended for
**Context:** Indicates purpose or allocation.
**Example:** Kohath-te beh a omlaite-a' ding ahi hi.

### tungah = to/unto/upon
**Context:** Indicates the recipient of the allocation.
**Example:** Gershom-te beh tungah a kipiate

### kipiate = were given/allotted
**Context:** The verb form indicating the act of distribution or assignment.
**Example:** Gershom-te beh tungah a kipiate

### tua ciangin = Then/After that
**Context:** A transitional phrase marking the next item or section in the list.
**Example:** Tua ciangin Issakhar-te' gamsung panin

### tapate = sons (plural)
**Context:** Refers to the male descendants of a patriarch (Issakhar' tapate = sons of Issachar).
**Example:** Issakhar' tapate: Tola, Puah, Jashub, leh Shimron

### li pha = four in number
**Context:** A concise way of stating the quantity of the preceding list (four sons).
**Example:** li pha uh hi.

### nisuahna lam = east side/direction
**Context:** Specifies a geographical direction relative to the Jordan River (Jordan nisuahna lam = east side of the Jordan).
**Example:** Jordan nisuahna lam

### kiang = near/beside
**Context:** Indicates proximity to a landmark (Jericho kiang = near Jericho).
**Example:** Jerikho kiang Jordan galkhat sung pan

### galkhat = one side/one bank
**Context:** Used with 'sung pan' to specify one side of the Jordan River.
**Example:** Jordan galkhat sung pan

### pawn-a om = situated in the wilderness/out in the open
**Context:** Describes the location of Bezer (Bezer a lonona gamte tawh, pawn-a om = Bezer with its suburbs, situated in the wilderness).
**Example:** Pawn-a om Bezer a lonona gamte tawh

## Phrases & Usage

- **a lonona gamte tawh** = with its suburbs
  - Usage: Standard formula used repeatedly to list cities granted to the tribes, indicating the city and its surrounding territory.

- **minam a langte’ gamsung panin** = out of the territory of the half tribe
  - Usage: Used to specify the source of allocation when dealing with the half-tribes (Manasseh).

- **beh tungah a kipiate** = were given unto the family/division
  - Usage: Used to denote the specific family unit receiving the allotment.

- **Jordan nisuahna lam, Jerikho kiang Jordan galkhat sung pan** = On the east side of the Jordan, on one side of the Jordan near Jericho
  - Usage: A detailed geographical locator for the allotments given to Reuben.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X a lonona gamte tawh, Y a lonona gamte tawh, leh Z a lonona gamte tawh**
  - A repetitive listing structure using 'tawh' (and/with) to connect multiple named locations, each followed by 'a lonona gamte tawh' (with its suburbs). This is the primary pattern for listing the allocated cities.
  - Example: Jokmean a lonona gamte tawh, Bethhoron a lonona gamte tawh, leh...

- **[Tribe Name]-te' gamsung panin: [City A]... leh [City B] ahi hi.**
  - Pattern indicating allocation: 'From the territory of [Tribe Name], [City A]... and [City B] were given.' Shows the source (gamsung panin) and the result (ahi hi - it is/they are).
  - Example: Asher-te' gamsung panin: Mashal a lonona gamte tawh, Abdon a lonona gamte tawh,

- **[Noun]-te beh a om laite-a' ding ahi hi.**
  - Defines the recipient group: 'It is for the remaining ones of the family of [Noun].' Uses the possessive/genitive marker '-te' and the purpose marker '-ading'.
  - Example: hihte pen Kohath-te beh a omlaite-a' ding ahi hi.

- **[Noun]’ tapate in: [Name 1], [Name 2], [Name 3], leh [Name 4], li pha uh hi.**
  - A structure used to list descendants (sons) and conclude with a numerical summary: 'The sons of [Noun] were: [List], four in number.'
  - Example: Issakhar' tapate: Tola, Puah, Jashub, leh Shimron, li pha uh hi.
