# 1CH - Part 33

## Vocabulary with Context

### giatna = eighth
**Context:** Used as an ordinal number following a name (Johanan).
**Example:** a giatna Johanan

### kuana = ninth
**Context:** Used as an ordinal number following a name (Elzabad).
**Example:** a kuana Elzabad

### sawmna = tenth
**Context:** Used as an ordinal number following a name (Jeremiah).
**Example:** a sawmna Jeremiah

### sawm-le-khatna = eleventh
**Context:** A compound ordinal number (sawm 'ten' + le 'and' + khat 'one').
**Example:** a sawm-le-khatna Makbannai

### ahi-te hi = they were
**Context:** A common copula structure indicating past tense plural identity.
**Example:** ahi-te hi.

### Hih = These
**Context:** Demonstrative pronoun referring to the preceding group.
**Example:** Hih Gad mite

### galkap uliante = captains of the host/army
**Context:** A collocation describing military leaders.
**Example:** galkap uliante hi a

### neuzawte = the least/youngest ones
**Context:** Used in comparison, referring to the lesser in rank or stature.
**Example:** a neuzawte in zakhat val

### golzawte = the greatest ones
**Context:** Used in comparison, referring to the greater in rank or stature.
**Example:** a golzawte in tulkhat val

### val = over/exceeding (used with numbers)
**Context:** Used after a number to mean 'over' or 'more than' that quantity.
**Example:** zakhat val

### uk-te hi = they ruled/commanded
**Context:** Verb indicating command or rule over the specified number.
**Example:** tulkhat val uk-te hi.

### piau khempeuh = all the banks/edges (of the river)
**Context:** Describing the extent of the overflowing river.
**Example:** Jordan a piau khempeuh a dimlet laitak

### dimlet laitak = when it had overflowed
**Context:** A temporal clause describing the condition of the river.
**Example:** a dimlet laitak kha masa sungin

### kantanin = at the edge/bank
**Context:** Locative phrase related to the river bank.
**Example:** Jordan kantanin

### nisuahna lam leh nitumna lamah = toward the east and toward the west
**Context:** Directional phrase.
**Example:** nisuahna lam leh nitumna lamah

### taisak mite = those who put to flight
**Context:** Causative verb form indicating they caused others to flee.
**Example:** a taisak mite ahi-te hi.

### pawlkhat = some (of)
**Context:** Indicates a portion of a larger group.
**Example:** Judah mi pawlkhat

### mun muanhuai = hold/stronghold (place of safety)
**Context:** The location where David was staying.
**Example:** David kiang mun muanhuai ah

### dawntuah dingin = to meet/to receive them
**Context:** Purpose clause for David's action.
**Example:** Amaute dawntuah dingin David pai khia-in

### kilawmtatna tawh = peaceably/with goodwill
**Context:** Adverbial phrase describing the manner of their coming.
**Example:** Kei kiangah kilawmtatna tawh

### lungsim kihual khawm ding hi = my heart shall be knit together (be united)
**Context:** Figurative expression for deep agreement or unity.
**Example:** note tawh ka lungsim kihual khawm ding hi

### khialhna = wrong/fault/guilt
**Context:** Used in the negative to assert innocence.
**Example:** bangmah khialhna nei lo napi-in

### lehhek dingin = to betray/to turn against
**Context:** Used in the context of treachery against David.
**Example:** ka langte tungah kei a lehhek dingin

### i pu i pate’ Pasian = the God of our fathers
**Context:** A formal invocation of their ancestral deity.
**Example:** i pu i pate’ Pasian in thei hen la

### tai hen = let them depart/turn back
**Context:** Imperative/hortative form used as a curse or dismissal.
**Example:** note hong tai hen,” a ci hi.

### Pasian Kha Siangtho = Holy Spirit of God
**Context:** The divine agent that empowered Amasai.
**Example:** Pasian Kha Siangtho hong tung a

### nangma’ aa hi ung = we are yours
**Context:** Declaration of allegiance.
**Example:** Kote in nangma’ aa hi ung, David aw

### nopna = peace/blessing
**Context:** Used repeatedly to wish well-being.
**Example:** Nopna, nopna nang tungah om hen la

### hong huhte tungah = to those who help you
**Context:** Referring to David's supporters.
**Example:** nang hong huhte tungah nopna om ta hen!

### sang a = received/accepted
**Context:** David's action towards the newly allied soldiers.
**Example:** David in amaute sang a

### galkap uliante-in bawl hi = made them captains of the host
**Context:** David's action of commissioning them.
**Example:** ama galkap uliante-in bawl hi.

### do dingin = to fight against
**Context:** Purpose clause for the battle.
**Example:** Saul a do dingin Filistiate tawh

### tai-in = defecting/fleeing over
**Context:** Action of soldiers leaving Saul for David.
**Example:** Manasseh mi pawlkhat tai-in David beel hi.

### hu thei lo hi = could not help
**Context:** Describing the inability of the defectors to assist David against Saul.
**Example:** David in Filistiate huh thei lo hi

### thu kikupna = counsel/agreement/consultation
**Context:** The decision-making process of the Philistine lords.
**Example:** thu kikupna khat nei-te a

### siat nadingin = for the destruction/harm of
**Context:** The perceived negative outcome if David were allowed to join them.
**Example:** a topa Saul kiangah David tai ding hi,” ci-in amah sawl khia-te hi.

### a beelte = those who defected to him
**Context:** Referring to the soldiers who joined David.
**Example:** amah a beelte

### mi a tul-in a ukte = captains of the thousands
**Context:** A specific military rank designation (tul = thousand).
**Example:** Manasseh sungah mi a tul-in a ukte ahi-te hi.

### mi’ na a sut thei mite = mighty men of valour (literally: those who could cut/strike men)
**Context:** Describing soldiers skilled in combat.
**Example:** Amaute in mi’ na a sut thei mite panin David huh-te hi

### thahat a vanglian mi = mighty men of strength/valor
**Context:** Another description emphasizing their military prowess.
**Example:** amaute a thahat a vanglian mi hi a

### galkap hon bangin = like the host of God
**Context:** A simile used to describe the growing size of David's army.
**Example:** Pasian’ galkap hon bangin

### a ni ni-in = day by day
**Context:** Temporal adverbial phrase indicating continuous action.
**Example:** a ni ni-in David a huh dingin

### a hei dingin = to turn/restore to him
**Context:** Purpose clause related to transferring the kingdom.
**Example:** Saul kumpipa' gam David tungah a hei dingin

### khen phazah = the number of bands/divisions
**Context:** Referring to the count of military units.
**Example:** a pai galkap a khen phazah ahi-te hi:

### lum leh tei a pua = that bare shield and spear
**Context:** Describing the equipment of the Judah warriors.
**Example:** Judah mi: Lum leh tei a pua galkap

### tulguk leh zagiat = six thousand and eight hundred
**Context:** Specific numerical count.
**Example:** galkap tul guk leh za giat ahi hi.

### galdo dingin = for the war
**Context:** Purpose clause specifying the use of their strength.
**Example:** Simeon mi: Galdo dingin

### tulsagih leh zakhat = seven thousand and one hundred
**Context:** Specific numerical count for Simeonites.
**Example:** mihging tul sagih leh za khat ahi hi.

### tulli leh zaguk = four thousand and six hundred
**Context:** Specific numerical count for Levites.
**Example:** Levi mi: Tul li leh za guk ahi hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **a neuzawte in zakhat val** = one of the least over an hundred
  - Usage: Used to express the minimum capability or rank within a group (e.g., military unit).

- **a golzawte in tulkhat val** = the greatest over a thousand
  - Usage: Used to express the maximum capability or rank within a group.

- **Jordan a piau khempeuh a dimlet laitak** = when Jordan had overflowed all its banks
  - Usage: A specific temporal marker indicating a time of high water/difficulty.

- **bangmah khialhna nei lo napi-in** = seeing there is no wrong in mine hands
  - Usage: A formal declaration of innocence before an appeal to God.

- **Pasian’ galkap hon bangin** = like the host of God
  - Usage: A simile used for strong emphasis on the size or divine favor of an army.

## Grammar Patterns

- **Ordinal Number + Noun (e.g., a giatna Johanan)**
  - Zolai uses the structure 'a [ordinal number] [Name]' to list individuals sequentially, similar to English 'Name the [ordinal number]'.
  - Example: a giatna Johanan

- **Noun + in + Number + val**
  - This structure is used to quantify the command structure: 'The [group] commanded over [number]'. 'val' functions as 'over' or 'exceeding'.
  - Example: a neuzawte in zakhat val uk-te hi.

- **X lam leh Y lamah**
  - Used for directional specification: 'toward X direction and toward Y direction'.
  - Example: nisuahna lam leh nitumna lamah

- **X tawh Y pai/hong pai**
  - Indicates movement towards a person or place, often with a purpose implied or stated separately. 'tawh' means 'with' or 'towards' in this context.
  - Example: David kiang mun muanhuai ah hong pai-te hi.

- **X in Y a dawntuah dingin Z pai khia-in**
  - Complex structure showing action (Z went out) for the purpose (dingin) of meeting (dawntuah) X (the object).
  - Example: Amaute dawntuah dingin David pai khia-in

- **X tawh/tawhin Y a kuan laitak**
  - Temporal clause indicating an action happening concurrently with another event: 'while David went/fought with X'.
  - Example: Saul a do dingin Filistiate tawh David a kuan lai takin

- **X sung panin Y tai-in**
  - Indicates defection: 'From within X, they defected/fled, joining Y'.
  - Example: Manasseh sung panin tai-in David a beel hi.
