# 1CH - Part 63

## Vocabulary with Context

### Pasian = God
**Context:** Used as the name/title for God, specifically 'Israel Pasian' (God of Israel).
**Example:** Israel Pasian, Topa aw

### ngimnate = desires/intentions (plural)
**Context:** Refers to the inner thoughts or desires God should instill in people's hearts.
**Example:** tua bang ngimnate leh ngaihsutnate omsak tawntung in la

### ngaihsutnate = thoughts/considerations (plural)
**Context:** Paired with 'ngimnate' to mean desires and thoughts.
**Example:** tua bang ngimnate leh ngaihsutnate omsak tawntung in la

### omsak = to cause to be/to place/to make exist
**Context:** Used transitively with 'ngimnate leh ngaihsutnate' to mean 'cause desires and thoughts to be present'.
**Example:** tua bang ngimnate leh ngaihsutnate omsak tawntung in la

### tawntung = always/continually
**Context:** Adverb modifying 'om-sak' (to cause to be), meaning to cause something to exist continually.
**Example:** om sak tawntung in la

### kai in = draw/pull towards
**Context:** Imperative command to draw hearts towards God ('nangma lamah' - towards you).
**Example:** amaute’ lungsim nangma lamah kai in.

### khempeuh = all/entirety
**Context:** Used to emphasize completeness, as in 'a lungsim khempeuh tawh' (with all his heart).
**Example:** Amah in a lungsim khempeuh tawh na thupiaknate

### tecipannate = testimonies/ordinances
**Context:** Referring to God's established decrees or testimonies.
**Example:** na thupiaknate, na tecipannate, leh na thukhamte kemin

### thukhamte = commandments/statutes
**Context:** Referring to God's laws.
**Example:** na thukhamte kemin a vekpi-in sem a

### kemin = keeping/observing
**Context:** Verbal noun form of 'kem' (to keep/guard), used adverbially meaning 'by keeping'.
**Example:** na thukhamte kemin a vekpi-in sem a

### vekpi-in = completely/entirely
**Context:** Adverbial phrase meaning 'in its entirety' or 'completely'.
**Example:** kemin a vekpi-in sem a

### vaihawmsa = prepared/made ready
**Context:** Describes the temple/house ('inn lianpi') that David had prepared.
**Example:** keimah in ka vaihawmsa inn lianpi

### utna lungsim = willing heart/desire
**Context:** A heart that is willing or inclined to do something (in this case, to build the temple).
**Example:** Solomon tungah utna lungsim guan in

### guan in = place/put/bestow (imperative)
**Context:** Command to place or grant the willing heart to Solomon.
**Example:** utna lungsim guan in,” a ci hi.

### kikhawmte = assembly/congregation
**Context:** The gathered people addressed by David.
**Example:** David in a kikhawmte khempeuh tungah

### phat un = praise (imperative plural)
**Context:** Command to praise God.
**Example:** Topa na Pasian uh phat un,” a ci hi.

### phatin = praising (adverbial/participial)
**Context:** Describes the manner in which the people bowed their heads—while praising God.
**Example:** Pasian Topa phatin a lu uh kun uh a

### kun uh = they bowed down
**Context:** Past tense action of bowing the head in reverence.
**Example:** a lu uh kun uh a

### biakpiakna = offering/sacrifice
**Context:** Refers to the religious offerings made to the Lord.
**Example:** Tua ciangin amaute in Topa tungah biakpiakna bawl uh a

### meihal biakna = burnt offering
**Context:** Specific type of sacrifice, usually involving fire.
**Example:** Topa tungah meihal biakna bawngtal tul khat

### tulkhat = one head/one unit (of cattle)
**Context:** A counter word used for sacrificial animals (bulls, rams, etc.).
**Example:** meihal biakna bawngtal tul khat

### leenggahzu = wine (literally 'flowing liquid' or 'drink')
**Context:** Used in the context of drink offerings accompanying the sacrifices.
**Example:** a leenggahzu tawh a biakna teng tawh

### lungdamna = joy/gladness
**Context:** Describes the great joy with which they celebrated.
**Example:** lungdamna lianpi tawh Topa mai-ah pawi khamin om uh hi.

### pawi khamin = feasting/celebrating
**Context:** Describes the act of holding a feast or celebration.
**Example:** Topa mai-ah pawi khamin om uh hi.

### sathau nilhin = anoint with oil
**Context:** The act of consecrating Solomon and Zadok as king and priest, respectively.
**Example:** amah kumpi-in sathau nilhin, siampi dingin Zadok sathau nilh uh hi.

### tokhom = throne
**Context:** The seat of royal authority.
**Example:** Topa’ kumpi tokhom tungah kumpi-in Solomon tu hi

### tu hi = he sat/was seated
**Context:** Past tense of 'to sit' or 'to be enthroned'.
**Example:** kumpi-in Solomon tu hi

### dau pai = went out/proceeded
**Context:** Refers to Solomon's procession or appearance after being crowned.
**Example:** Amah a dau pai a

### thu mang uh hi = they obeyed his word
**Context:** Indicates the submission and obedience of the people to the new king.
**Example:** Israel mi khempeuh in ama thu a mang uh hi.

### minthan’na = glory/great renown
**Context:** The great honor and fame God bestowed upon Solomon.
**Example:** Solomon tungah minthan'na lianpi pia a

### kumpi min liatna = honor of kingship
**Context:** The unique dignity and respect associated with his reign, which no previous king had achieved.
**Example:** kumpi minliatna a guan' hi.

### gamtatnate = acts/deeds
**Context:** Referring to the historical record of King David's actions.
**Example:** Kumpi David’ gamtatna a patna pan a tawpna dongin

### thu-mupa = seer/prophet (one who sees)
**Context:** Title for Samuel, indicating his prophetic role.
**Example:** thumupa Samuel' Khang Tangthu sung

### kamsang = prophet (one who speaks forth)
**Context:** Title for Nathan, emphasizing his role as a speaker for God.
**Example:** kamsang Nathan' Khang Tangthu sung

### Khang Tangthu = History/Chronicles
**Context:** Refers to the historical records or books written by the prophets.
**Example:** Samuel' Khang Tangthu sung

### vangliatnate = might/power/great deeds
**Context:** Referring to David's strength and accomplishments.
**Example:** ama ukna leh ama vangliatnate khempeuh

### kiciamtehna = records/memorials/things remembered
**Context:** The documentation of events that occurred during his reign.
**Example:** a tung thute a kiciamtehna tawh kigelh khawm hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **lungsim nangma lamah kai in** = draw [their] hearts toward you
  - Usage: A prayer request asking God to redirect the people's affections towards Himself.

- **a lungsim khempeuh tawh** = with all [his] heart
  - Usage: Emphasizes complete devotion or sincerity in performing an action (like keeping commandments).

- **a vekpi-in sem a** = to do completely/to execute fully
  - Usage: Indicates the thorough execution of God's laws.

- **Topa’ mai-ah pawi khamin om uh hi** = they remained feasting before the Lord
  - Usage: Describes a communal celebration of worship and joy in God's presence.

- **a dau pai a** = he proceeded out/went forth
  - Usage: Used to describe the formal appearance or procession of the newly crowned king.

- **a patna pan a tawpna dongin** = from his beginning to his end
  - Usage: Used to denote the entirety of a period or life, often in reference to historical records.

## Grammar Patterns

- **Verb + tawntung in**
  - Adverbial construction where the verb stem + 'tawntung' (always) + 'in' creates an adverb meaning 'continually doing X'.
  - Example: om sak tawntung in la

- **Noun + khempeuh**
  - The suffix '-khempeuh' functions as an intensive pluralizer, meaning 'all of the Noun' or 'the entire Noun'.
  - Example: a lungsim khempeuh

- **Noun + -ah + Noun + -ah + Noun + -ah**
  - Repetitive locative case marking ('-ah') used to list multiple locations or entities where an action applies or where things are recorded.
  - Example: ama tung leh Israel tung, leh gamsung kumpi gamte khempeuh tungah

- **Noun + -in (Instrumental/Agentive)**
  - The suffix '-in' is used to mark the agent or instrument, often turning a noun into an adverbial modifier describing *how* an action is done.
  - Example: Pasian Topa phatin (by praising God)

- **Verb (Root) + in (Imperative)**
  - The suffix '-in' attached to a verb root often forms a strong imperative command.
  - Example: guan in (Place/Grant!)
