## Vocabulary with Context

### ciangin = then, afterwards
**Context:** Used to mark the sequence of events, similar to 'then' or 'after that'.
**Example:** Tua ciangin David in Pasian kiangah...

### Pasian = God
**Context:** The supreme being, God.
**Example:** Tua ciangin David in Pasian kiangah...

### nakpi takin = greatly, heavily
**Context:** Adverbial phrase modifying 'khial' (sinned), meaning sinned greatly.
**Example:** Hih na ka sepna ah nakpi takin ka khial hi.

### khial hi = I have sinned/erred
**Context:** The verb 'khial' (to err, sin) in the perfect tense.
**Example:** Hih na ka sepna ah nakpi takin ka khial hi.

### kong thuum hi = I beseech/pray to you
**Context:** A formal plea or prayer directed towards God ('nang tungah' - to you).
**Example:** Ahih hangin tu-in nang tungah kong thuum hi.

### mawhna = iniquity, fault, sin
**Context:** The noun form of wrongdoing, used here for the servant's sin.
**Example:** na nasempa mawhna hong maisak in;

### maisak in = forgive (literally: cause to become clean/clear)
**Context:** Imperative form of 'maisak' (to forgive, pardon).
**Example:** na nasempa mawhna hong maisak in;

### haihuai = foolish
**Context:** Adjective describing David's action.
**Example:** a haihuai mahmahin ka gamta khin hi

### gamta khin hi = I have acted/done
**Context:** Past action, 'gamta' (to act/do) + 'khin' (completed aspect).
**Example:** a haihuai mahmahin ka gamta khin hi

### Topa = Lord
**Context:** Title used for God, often interchangeably with Pasian.
**Example:** Tua ciangin Topa in David' thu-mupa Gad tungah thu gen a,

### thuumupa = messenger, one who is sent to speak/pray
**Context:** Derived from 'thuum' (to pray/beseech), referring to the prophet Gad.
**Example:** David' thu-mupa Gad tungah thu gen a,

### thu gen = spoke a word/message
**Context:** Verb phrase meaning 'to speak' or 'to deliver a message'.
**Example:** Gad tungah thu gen a,

### kong pia hi = I give you
**Context:** The Lord is giving David a choice/command.
**Example:** Nangma tungah thu thum kong pia hi;

### teel in = choose (imperative)
**Context:** Command to select one option from the choices given.
**Example:** tuate khat teel in,

### kialpi = famine (literally: hunger-time)
**Context:** Referring to the plague of famine.
**Example:** Kialpi kum thum sung;

### simgawp = to destroy, wipe out
**Context:** Used in the context of enemies overtaking David.
**Example:** na galte in nang hong simgawp laitakin

### galtaina = war, battle, affliction by the sword
**Context:** Refers to the affliction by the sword of the enemies.
**Example:** kha thum sung galtaina;

### pulnatna = plague, pestilence
**Context:** One of the three possible punishments.
**Example:** gamsungah pulnatna

### vantung mi = angel (heavenly being)
**Context:** The messenger of God.
**Example:** Topa’ vantung mi in Israel sung khempeuh ah suksiatna

### suksiatna = destruction, devastation
**Context:** The action carried out by the angel.
**Example:** suksiatna ni thum sung a piangsak

### namsau = sword
**Context:** The instrument of destruction/warfare.
**Example:** Topa’ namsau

### lungkhamna lianpi = great strait, great distress
**Context:** David expressing his difficult situation.
**Example:** Keimah in lungkhamna lianpi khat ka tuak hi.

### hehpihna = mercy, compassion
**Context:** David appealing to God's mercy.
**Example:** Ama hehpihna lian mahmah ahih manin

### khut sungah hong puksak in = let me fall into the hand of
**Context:** A plea to be delivered into God's hand rather than man's.
**Example:** Topa’ khut sungah hong puksak in la,

### tungsak hi = caused to come upon
**Context:** God inflicting the plague upon Israel.
**Example:** Topa in Israel tungah pulnatna khat tungsak hi;

### tul sawmsagih = seventy thousand (70,000)
**Context:** The number of people who died in the plague.
**Example:** Israel mi tul sawmsagih puk hi.

### susia dingin = in order to destroy
**Context:** Purpose clause indicating the angel's mission.
**Example:** Pasian in a susia dingin Jerusalem ah vantungmi sawl hi;

### kisik hi = He repented/relented
**Context:** God relenting from the destruction.
**Example:** tua thusia ah amah kisik hi;

### Pha zo hi = It is enough/Stop now
**Context:** The command from God to the angel to cease destruction.
**Example:** Pha zo hi; tu-in na khut khawlsak in,”

### khawlsak in = stop, hold back (imperative)
**Context:** Command to cease the action.
**Example:** tu-in na khut khawlsak in,”

### ancilna = threshing floor
**Context:** The location where Ornan was working and where the angel appeared.
**Example:** Jebus mi Ornan’ ancilna gei-ah

### khuadak to a = looked up
**Context:** David looking up to see the vision.
**Example:** Tua ciangin David khuadak to a,

### kidoksa namsau = drawn sword
**Context:** Describing the angel's weapon.
**Example:** a khutsungah a kidoksa namsau khat omin

### puanhampi silhin = having spread sackcloth
**Context:** An act of deep mourning, repentance, or reverence.
**Example:** David leh a upate puanhampi silhin mai puk-te hi.

### maipuk-te hi = they bowed down (prostrated)
**Context:** An act of deep reverence or submission.
**Example:** puanhampi silhin mai puk-te hi.

### gitloh gamtat mahmahpa = the one who has done very wickedly
**Context:** David accepting full responsibility for the sin.
**Example:** Khialin a gitloh gamtat mahmahpa in keimah ka hi hi.

### innkuan = household, family
**Context:** David praying for his family along with himself.
**Example:** keimah leh ka innkuan gim hong pia in.

### biakna tau = altar for sacrifice/worship
**Context:** The structure David intended to build.
**Example:** biakna tau khat a lam theih nadingin

### lam theih nadingin = in order to build
**Context:** Purpose clause for building the altar.
**Example:** biakna tau khat a lam theih nadingin

### a man a cingin piak dingin = to give it for its full price
**Context:** Ornan offering the land freely, but David insisting on paying.
**Example:** A man a cingin piak dingin hong pia in,”

## Phrases & Usage

- **bang hang hiam cih leh** = for whatever reason (or: if it is so)
  - Usage: Used to introduce a justification or consequence, often rhetorical.

- **tu-in nang tungah kong thuum hi** = now I beseech you
  - Usage: Formal expression of prayer or earnest request directed to God.

- **a haihuai mahmahin** = very foolishly
  - Usage: Adverbial phrase emphasizing the degree of foolishness.

- **Topa’ khut sungah hong puksak in** = let me fall into the hand of the Lord
  - Usage: A plea for divine judgment/mercy rather than human judgment.

- **a zan mu hi** = he saw (it) in the night
  - Usage: Describing a vision seen during the night.

- **a mai lei lam ngatin** = with his face toward the ground
  - Usage: Describing the posture of deep respect or submission (prostration).

## Grammar Patterns

- **X in Y kiangah, Z a ci hi.**
  - Standard narrative structure for reported speech: X (subject) to Y (object), said Z (speech).
  - Example: Tua ciangin David in Gad kiangah, “...” a ci hi.

- **Ahih kei leh X; ahih kei leh Y; ahih kei leh Z.**
  - Structure used to present three distinct, mutually exclusive options (Either X, or Y, or Z).
  - Example: Kialpi kum thum sung; ahih kei leh na langte...; ahih kei leh gam sungah...

- **V-dingin V-theih nadingin**
  - Double use of the purpose marker '-dingin' (in order to/so that one can) to express the goal of an action.
  - Example: biakna tau khat a lam theih nadingin na ancilna mun hong pia in

- **V-a, V-in**
  - Sequential action using the conjunctive participle (-a or -in) to link two actions performed by the same subject.
  - Example: Ornan khuadak a, David mu-in hong paikhia a,
