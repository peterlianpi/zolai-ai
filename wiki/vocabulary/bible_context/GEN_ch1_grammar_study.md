## Verse-by-Verse Analysis

### GEN.1:1 — Creation formula
```
EN:  In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth.
TDB: A kipat cil-in Pasian in vantung leh leitung a piangsak hi.
ZVS: A kipat cilin Pasian in vantung leh leitung a piangsak hi.
```
**Grammar:**
- `A kipat cil-in` = In the beginning (lit: at the very first start)
- `Pasian in` = God + ergative marker `in` (transitive subject)
- `vantung leh leitung` = heaven and earth (`leh` = and between nouns)
- `a piangsak hi` = created (`a` = 3rd person prefix, `piangsak` = cause to exist, `hi` = declarative)
- **TDB77 vs ZVS:** `cil-in` → `cilin` (hyphen removed in ZVS)

**Vocabulary:**
- `kipat` = beginning/start
- `vantung` = heaven/sky
- `leitung` = earth/world
- `piangsak` = create (causative: `piang`=exist + `sak`=cause)

### GEN.1:2 — Formless and void
```
EN:  And the earth was without form, and void; and darkness was upon the face of the deep.
TDB: Leitung in lim leh meel nei loin a awngthawlpi ahi hi. A thuk tuipi tung tengah khuamialna om a, tua tuitung teng ah Pasian' Kha a gamtang hi.
```
**Grammar:**
- `lim leh meel nei loin` = without form and void (`nei lo` = not having, `-in` = serial verb)
- `a awngthawlpi ahi hi` = was completely empty/desolate
- `khuamialna om a` = darkness existed (and...)
- `Pasian' Kha` = Spirit of God (apostrophe = possession)
- `a gamtang hi` = was moving/hovering

**Vocabulary:**
- `lim leh meel` = formless and void
- `khuamial` / `khuamialna` = darkness
- `tuipi` = deep waters / sea
- `Kha` = Spirit/breath

---

### GEN.1:3 — Let there be light
```
EN:  And God said, Let there be light: and there was light.
TDB: Pasian in, "Khuavak om ta hen," ci hi; tua ciangin khuavak om pah hi.
ZVS: Pasian in, "Khuavak om hen," ci hi; tua ciangin khuavak om pah hi.
```
**Grammar:**
- `"Khuavak om ta hen,"` = "Let there be light" (optative: `om` + `ta` + `hen`)
- `ci hi` = said (quotative — marks end of direct speech)
- `tua ciangin` = then/after that (sequence)
- `khuavak om pah hi` = light appeared immediately (`pah` = punctual/sudden)
- **TDB77 vs ZVS:** `om ta hen` → `om hen` (ZVS drops `ta`)

**Key pattern:** `[thing] om hen` = Let there be [thing] (optative creation formula)

---

### GEN.1:4 — God saw it was good
```
EN:  And God saw the light, that it was good: and God divided the light from the darkness.
TDB: Pasian in khuavak hoih hi ci-in mu hi; Pasian in khuamial panin khuavak khenkhia hi.
```
**Grammar:**
- `hoih hi ci-in mu hi` = saw that it was good (lit: "good it is, saying, saw")
  - `hoih hi` = it is good
  - `ci-in` = saying (serial verb: `ci` + `-in`)
  - `mu hi` = saw
- `khuamial panin khuavak khenkhia hi` = divided light from darkness
  - `panin` = from (source particle)
  - `khenkhia` = separate out (`khen`=divide + `khia`=out)

**Recurring formula:** `[adj] hi ci-in mu hi` = saw that it was [adj]

### GEN.1:5 — Naming Day and Night
```
EN:  And God called the light Day, and the darkness he called Night.
TDB: Pasian in khuavak pen Sun ci a, khuamial pen Zan ci hi.
```
**Grammar:**
- `[thing] pen [name] ci a/hi` = called [thing] [name]
- `pen` = topic marker (as for / the)
- `ci a` = said (and...) — serial verb with `-a` (not final)
- `ci hi` = said (final)

**Day formula:** `Nitak hong bei-in, zingsang hong tung a, ni [ordinal] ahi hi.`
= Evening came, morning came, it was the [ordinal] day.
- `nitak` = evening | `zingsang` = morning
- `hong bei` = came to an end (toward speaker)
- `hong tung` = arrived (toward speaker)

### GEN.1:6-8 — Firmament
```
TDB: "Tuite' laizang-ah van kuumpi om hen la, tua van kuumpi in tui leh tui kikhensak hen," ci hi.
```
**Grammar:**
- `laizang-ah` = in the midst of (`laizang` = middle + `ah` = locative)
- `om hen la` = let there be... and (optative + `la` = sequential)
- `kikhensak` = cause to separate (`ki-`=reciprocal + `khen`=divide + `sak`=causative)

**Vocabulary:**
- `van kuumpi` = firmament/expanse
- `laizangah` = in the midst of

### GEN.1:26 — Let us make man
```
EN:  And God said, Let us make man in our image, after our likeness.
TDB: "Eima' lim, eimah hong suunin mihing bawl ni..."
ZVS: "Eima lim, eimah hong sunin mihing bawl ni."
```
**Grammar:**
- `Eima'` = our (1pl possessive — God speaking as plural)
- `eimah` = us/ourselves
- `hong sunin` = resembling (toward us)
- `mihing bawl ni` = let us make man (`ni` = hortative "let us")
- **Key:** `ni` = hortative (let us do) — different from `hen` (let it be)

**Vocabulary:**
- `mihing` = human being / mankind
- `lim` = image/likeness
- `eima` = our (1pl possessive)

---

### GEN.1:27 — Male and female
```
TDB: Pasian in ama' lim bangin mihing piangsakin, Pasian' lim bangin amah piangsak hi; amah in pasal leh numei piangsak hi.
```
**Vocabulary:**
- `pasal` = male/man/husband
- `numei` = female/woman/wife
- `lim bangin` = in the image of

### GEN.1:31 — Very good
```
EN:  And God saw every thing that he had made, and, behold, it was very good.
TDB: Pasian in a bawlsate khempeuh mu hi, en un, hoih mahmah hi.
ZVS: Pasian in a bawlsate khempeuh en a, hoih sa mahmah hi.
```
**Grammar:**
- `a bawlsate khempeuh` = everything he had made (`bawlsa` = made + `te` = plural + `khempeuh` = all)
- `en un` = behold! (imperative plural — TDB77)
- `hoih mahmah hi` = very good (`mahmah` = very/greatly)
- **TDB77 vs ZVS:** `en un, hoih mahmah` → `hoih sa mahmah` (ZVS uses `sa` = already/indeed)

## Recurring Formulas in Genesis 1

1. **Creation command:** `"[thing] om hen," ci hi` = "Let there be [thing]," said
2. **Fulfillment:** `tua mah bangin piang pah hi` = and it was so (immediately)
3. **Approval:** `hoih hi ci-in mu hi` = saw that it was good
4. **Day formula:** `Nitak hong bei-in, zingsang hong tung a, ni [N] ahi hi` = evening and morning, day N
5. **Naming:** `[thing] pen [name] ci hi` = called [thing] [name]
