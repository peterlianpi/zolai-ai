# 1CH - Part 46

## Vocabulary with Context

### kumpite = kings
**Context:** Referring to the kings who go out to battle.
**Example:** Kumpite in khuakhal sungin galsimin kuankhia zel uh hi.

### khuakhal = year (expired/time)
**Context:** Used to denote the time when kings go out to battle, implying the end of a cycle or year.
**Example:** Tua hun hoih khuakhal hong tun’ ciangin Joab in galkapte pai khiatpihin...

### galsimin = for war/battle
**Context:** Describing the purpose of the kings going out.
**Example:** Kumpite in khuakhal sungin galsimin kuankhia zel uh hi.

### kuankhia = go out/march out
**Context:** The action of the kings departing for battle.
**Example:** Kumpite in khuakhal sungin galsimin kuankhia zel uh hi.

### sugawp = waste/lay waste
**Context:** Joab's action against the land of the Ammonites.
**Example:** Ammon mite gam sugawp a, hong pai-in Rabbah khua umcih hi.

### umcih = besiege/settle down against
**Context:** Joab's action of besieging the city of Rabbah.
**Example:** hong pai-in Rabbah khua umcih hi.

### lumlet = smote/destroyed
**Context:** Joab's final action against Rabbah.
**Example:** Tua ciangin Joab in Rabbah khua vatin lumlet hi.

### kumpi lukhu = king's crown
**Context:** The crown taken from the king of the Ammonites.
**Example:** David in amau’ kumpipa’ kumpi lukhu ama tung panin lak khiatsak hi;

### suangmanpha = precious stone
**Context:** Describing what was found inside the crown.
**Example:** tua lukhu kham talen khat gikin a sungah suangmanpha khat a omna amah in thei hi;

### kinusiate = spoil/booty
**Context:** The great amount of plunder David brought from the city.
**Example:** tua khuapi panin van kinusiate tampi tak pai khiatpih hi.

### sing-atnate = saws
**Context:** Tools David used to deal with the captured people.
**Example:** sing-atnate leh tuzumte leh heite tawh na a sem dingin koih hi;

### tuzumte = harrows (of iron)
**Context:** Tools David used for punishment/labor.
**Example:** sing-atnate leh tuzumte leh heite tawh na a sem dingin koih hi;

### heite = axes
**Context:** Tools David used for punishment/labor.
**Example:** sing-atnate leh tuzumte leh heite tawh na a sem dingin koih hi;

### kidona = war/battle
**Context:** The conflict that arose with the Philistines.
**Example:** Gezer ah Filistiate tawh kidona hong om hi;

### mithagolte’ suanlehkhakte = offspring/descendants of the giants
**Context:** Referring to the lineage of the slain giant (Sippai).
**Example:** mithagolte’ suan leh khakte khat ahi Sippi that hi;

### kinuaisiah = subdued/humbled
**Context:** The result of the battle against the Philistines.
**Example:** tua ciangin Fillistiate kinuaisiah hi.

### sanggampa = brother (of a notable person)
**Context:** Lahmi is identified as the brother of Goliath.
**Example:** Gath khuami Goliath’ sanggampa Lahmi that hi.

### mi thalian = a giant/a man of great stature
**Context:** Describing the large man encountered in Gath.
**Example:** mi thalian khat a omna Gath ah gal om kik hi;

### khutme = fingers
**Context:** Describing the unusual number of fingers on the giant.
**Example:** a khut lang tuakah khutme guk a nei,

### kheme = toes
**Context:** Describing the unusual number of toes on the giant.
**Example:** a khe langtuakah zong kheme guk mah a nei,

### hanthawn = provoke/incite
**Context:** Satan's action against David regarding the census.
**Example:** Israel mi phazah a sim dingin David hanthawn hi.

### galkap a ukte = rulers of the people/captains of the host
**Context:** The military leaders David addressed.
**Example:** David in Joab leh galkap a ukte kiangah,

### phazah = number/count
**Context:** The object of the census David commanded.
**Example:** Israel mi phazah va sim un la,

### thuzawh = prevail/obey (the command)
**Context:** The king's command overriding Joab's objection.
**Example:** Ahih hangin kumpipa in Joab thu-zawh hi.

### kihemkhia = departed/set out
**Context:** Joab's action after the king commanded him.
**Example:** Tua ciangin Joab kihem khia-in Israel sung khempeuh ah pai a,

### awn = thousand (as a unit)
**Context:** Used in counting the military strength (one thousand thousand = one million).
**Example:** mihing awn khat leh sang khat om a,

### thupiakna = command/ordinance
**Context:** Referring to the king's order for the census.
**Example:** Ahih hangin Joab in kumpipa’ thupiakna hoih sa het lo ahih manin

### hoihsa het lo = did not like/abominable
**Context:** Joab's strong disapproval of the king's command.
**Example:** kumpipa’ thupiakna hoih sa het lo ahih manin

### hel = include/count
**Context:** Joab's decision not to include Levi and Benjamin in the count.
**Example:** a simna ah Levi minam leh Benjamin minamte hel lo hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **kumpite in khuakhal sungin galsimin kuankhia zel uh hi** = It was the custom for kings to go out to battle during the year (at the appointed time).
  - Usage: Describes a recurring annual military practice.

- **Rabbah khua vatin lumlet hi** = He smote/destroyed the city of Rabbah.
  - Usage: Indicates a decisive military victory and destruction of a city.

- **a khut lang tuakah khutme guk a nei** = He had six fingers on his hand (literally: on his hand side, six fingers he had)
  - Usage: A descriptive phrase used to detail the physical abnormality of the giant.

- **Israel mi phazah a sim dingin David hanthawn hi** = Satan provoked David to number Israel.
  - Usage: Explains the motivation behind David's action (being incited by Satan).

- **Bang hangin mawhna tun’ ding na hi hiam?** = Why would you bring sin upon Israel?
  - Usage: A rhetorical question expressing strong moral objection.

- **kumpipa’ thupiakna hoih sa het lo** = He did not like the king's command at all.
  - Usage: Expresses strong disapproval or finding something detestable.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X in Y tawh kidona hong om hi**
  - Structure indicating that a conflict/war arose with X against Y.
  - Example: Gezer ah Filistiate tawh kidona hong om hi;

- **Ahih hangin X in Y thu-zawh hi**
  - Shows that X's command/will prevailed over Y's objection or hesitation.
  - Example: Ahih hangin kumpipa in Joab thu-zawh hi.

- **X in Y kiangah, '...' a ci hi**
  - Standard structure for reporting direct speech or command given to a specific person/group.
  - Example: David in Joab leh galkap a ukte kiangah, '...' a ci hi.

- **X in Y sung pana hong piang ahi uh hi**
  - Indicates the origin or lineage of a group of people (born of/descended from).
  - Example: Hih mi thumte pen Gath-a mithagolte' sung pana hong piang ahi uh hi.
