## Vocabulary with Context

### Pasian’ = God's
**Context:** Possessive form of Pasian (God), used here to refer to the house of God.
**Example:** Pasian’ biakinn kiimah...

### biakinn = house of worship/temple
**Context:** Refers to the sanctuary or temple building.
**Example:** Pasian’ biakinn kiimah...

### giak = lodge/stay
**Context:** Verb meaning to stay or lodge, used in the context of where they stayed overnight.
**Example:** amaute giak-te hi

### vilna = guarding/watching
**Context:** Noun derived from 'vil' (to guard/watch), referring to the duty of watching over something.
**Example:** amaute tungah vilna nasep

### nasep = work/duty
**Context:** Refers to a specific task or responsibility assigned to them.
**Example:** vilna nasep

### kiguan = assigned/appointed
**Context:** Passive verb form meaning something was entrusted or appointed to them.
**Example:** nasep kiguan hi

### kizang = set up/arranged
**Context:** Used in the context of vessels or items being set up inside the temple.
**Example:** biakinn sunga kizang um-le-beelte

### um-le-beel = vessels/utensils
**Context:** Refers to the sacred vessels or instruments used in the temple service.
**Example:** kizang um-le-beelte uk-te hi

### uk = to rule/to be in charge of
**Context:** Verb meaning to have charge over or oversee something.
**Example:** umlebeelte uk-te hi

### kitumpih = to be brought in
**Context:** Passive verb form, likely meaning 'to be brought in' (referring to the vessels).
**Example:** tua nazatte hong kitumpih ciang hita leh

### kilakkhiat = to be taken out
**Context:** Passive verb form, meaning 'to be taken out' (referring to the vessels).
**Example:** a kilakkhiat ciang hita leh

### malzah = by number/by tale
**Context:** Adverbial phrase meaning 'by counting' or 'by tale' (as in counting items).
**Example:** a malzah a sim ding-te kisam hi

### kisam = required/necessary
**Context:** Verb meaning it was required or necessary for them to do something.
**Example:** a sim ding-te kisam hi

### tutphah = offering/sacrifice

### sabuaite = flour/meal

### kituam koih = to be placed/set

### leenggahzute = oils

## Phrases & Usage

- **inn kiimah** = round about the house
  - Usage: Describes the location of lodging relative to a central structure (the temple).

- **zingsang simin kong a hon’ dingin** = for the opening thereof every morning
  - Usage: Describes a specific, recurring duty related to opening something at dawn.

- **a malzah a sim ding-te kisam hi** = it was required that they count them by tale
  - Usage: Indicates a mandatory procedure involving counting items brought in or out.

- **a namtui nate hel dingin** = to compound the spices
  - Usage: Specific instruction for the priests regarding the preparation of fragrant materials.

- **biakna ding moh pheengte** = the set office for the service
  - Usage: Refers to a fixed, established responsibility within the religious service.

- **sun leh zanin na sem-te hi** = they worked day and night
  - Usage: Emphasizes the continuous, non-stop nature of their employment.

- **amau’ khang tawh kizui-in** = according to their generations
  - Usage: Indicates that leadership or status followed the established lineage structure.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X' Y-te hi**
  - A common structure for stating identity or status, often used for listing people or groups. X' is possessive (e.g., 'their' or a name's), Y is the noun/role, and 'uh hi' acts as the plural copula/statement marker.
  - Example: hihte pen lasate... ahi-te hi.

- **bang hang hiam cih leh**
  - A standard introductory clause meaning 'as for why' or 'the reason is that,' often followed by the explanation for a preceding statement. It functions similarly to 'for' or 'because' in English.
  - Example: bang hang hiam cih leh amaute tungah vilna nasep...

- **X in Y' pa ahi hi**
  - A clear genealogical structure indicating paternity: X is the father of Y. The apostrophe denotes possession/relationship.
  - Example: Ner pen Kish' pa, Kish pen Saul' pa ahi hi.

- **A/Tua nazatte hong [Verb-Passive] ciang hita leh**
  - A conditional structure describing an action that must be completed before the next step. 'ciang hita leh' means 'when/after it is done'.
  - Example: tua nazatte hong kitumpih ciang hita leh a kilakkhiat ciang hita leh...
