# 1CH - Part 20

## Vocabulary with Context

### tapate = sons
**Context:** Referring to the male offspring of Tola.
**Example:** Tola’ tapate in: Uzzi, Refaiah, Jeriel...

### beh sung = clan/family line
**Context:** The lineage or ancestral group from which the leaders originated.
**Example:** amau’ beh sung panin innkuan lutangte hi a

### innkuan lutangte = heads of the household/chiefs
**Context:** The leaders or principal figures of the family units.
**Example:** amau’ beh sung panin innkuan lutangte hi a

### galhangte = valiant men/men of might
**Context:** Describing the strength or bravery of Tola's descendants.
**Example:** Tola-te beh in amau’ khangin a galhangte ahi-te hi.

### hun lai takin = in the time of/during the reign of
**Context:** Specifying the time period when the count was taken (David's era).
**Example:** David’ hun lai takin mihing tul sawmnih leh tulnih leh zaguk pha hi.

### tul sawmnih leh tulnih leh zaguk = twenty-two thousand and six hundred
**Context:** A specific numerical count of people.
**Example:** mihing tul sawmnih leh tulnih leh zaguk pha hi.

### nei hi = had/begot (a child)
**Context:** Used to indicate Uzzi fathered Izrahiah.
**Example:** Uzzi’ tapa in Izrahiah ahi hi.

### mi nga = five men
**Context:** The count of Izrahiah's sons who were chiefs.
**Example:** Izrahiah leh a tapate in: Mikhael, Obadiah, Joel, leh Isshiah, mi nga, a vekpi-in ukpite ahi-te hi;

### a vekpi-in = all of them/in total
**Context:** Emphasizing that all five mentioned were chiefs.
**Example:** a vekpi-in ukpite ahi-te hi;

### ukpite = chiefs/leaders
**Context:** Designating the status of Michael, Obadiah, Joel, and Isshiah.
**Example:** a vekpi-in ukpite ahi-te hi;

### ban-ah = besides/in addition to
**Context:** Introducing an additional group of soldiers.
**Example:** amaute’ ban-ah a pate-te innkuan kuanin...

### galsim dingin = for fighting/for war
**Context:** Stating the purpose of the organized military bands.
**Example:** galsim dingin galkap honte mi tul sawmthum leh tulguk om hi;

### galkap honte = bands of soldiers/military companies
**Context:** Referring to organized military units ready for battle.
**Example:** galsim dingin galkap honte mi tul sawmthum leh tulguk om hi;

### bang hang hiam cih leh = because/for the reason that
**Context:** Introducing the explanation for having many soldiers.
**Example:** bang hang hiam cih leh amaute in zi tampi leh tapa tampi nei-te hi;

### kiciamteh = reckoned/counted/registered
**Context:** Referring to the genealogical registration of the Issachar tribe members.
**Example:** khang simna ah a kiciamteh, a galhang a thahat amau beh mi...

### thahat = strong/mighty
**Context:** Describing the quality of the Issachar men, alongside 'galhang' (valiant).
**Example:** a galhang a thahat amau beh mi...

### tul sawmgiat leh tul sagih = fourscore and seven (87,000)
**Context:** The total count for the Issachar tribe's valiant men.
**Example:** tul sawmgiat leh tul sagih ahi hi.

### thum pha-te hi = there were three (in number)
**Context:** Stating the total number of Bela's sons listed.
**Example:** Bela, Bekher, leh Jediael, thum pha-te hi.

### sawmthum-le-li = thirty and four (34)
**Context:** The final number in the count for Bela's descendants.
**Example:** tul sawmnih leh tulnih leh sawmthum-le-li pha-te hi.

### khempeuh = all of them
**Context:** Used to summarize the list of Becher's sons.
**Example:** Hihte khempeuh in Bekher’ tapate ahi-te hi;

### zanih = two hundred
**Context:** Part of the final count for Becher's descendants (2200).
**Example:** tul sawmnih leh zanih ahi hi.

### suante = descendants/offspring
**Context:** Referring to the sons of Jediael.
**Example:** Jediael’ suante gal dona-ah nasem dingin...

### gal dona-ah nasem dingin = to serve in battle/warfare
**Context:** Describing the military function of Jediael's descendants.
**Example:** gal dona-ah nasem dingin a baihsa-in a om...

### a baihsa-in = fit/ready/able
**Context:** Describing the readiness of the soldiers for war.
**Example:** nasem dingin a baihsa-in a om mihing...

### tua ciangin = then/after that
**Context:** Used to transition to the next set of genealogical information (Dan's line).
**Example:** Tua ciangin Shuppim leh Huppim in zong hih minamte sung mah hi a...

### kici tapa khat = a son named
**Context:** Introducing a son by name.
**Example:** Dan in Hushim kici tapa khat nei hi.

### sung pan-a piangte = those born from the line of
**Context:** Indicating the maternal or secondary lineage connection for Naphtali's sons.
**Example:** Naftali’ tapate: Jahziel, Guni, Jezer, leh Shallum hi a, Bilhah sung pan-a piangte ahi-te hi.

### zineu = concubine/secondary wife
**Context:** Describing the status of the Aram woman who bore Asriel.
**Example:** A zineu Aram numei tawh a neih Asriel leh Makhir ahi hi.

### sanggamnu = sister (female sibling)
**Context:** Referring to the sister of Huppim and Shuppim, who married Machir.
**Example:** Ama sanggamnu’ min in Ma-akah ahi hi.

### tanu bek nei hi = had only daughters
**Context:** Describing Zelophehad's progeny.
**Example:** Zelofehad in a tanu bek nei hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **amau’ khangin** = in their might/by their strength
  - Usage: Used to describe the manner in which they were counted or recognized (e.g., as valiant men).

- **khang simna-ah a kiciamtehna tawh kizui-in** = following their registration in the genealogy/census
  - Usage: Indicates that the numbers are based on official records or lineage counts.

- **a pate-te innkuan kuanin** = according to the house of their fathers
  - Usage: A standard phrase in genealogical texts, meaning organized by paternal lineage.

- **a vekpi-in** = in total/all together
  - Usage: Used for summarizing a group count.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X’ tapa in Y ahi hi.**
  - Possessive ('s) followed by 'son' (tapa) and the verb 'is/was' (ahi hi). Structure for stating a father-son relationship.
  - Example: Uzzi’ tapa in Izrahiah ahi hi.

- **X leh Y, Z pha-te hi.**
  - Listing items (X and Y) followed by a total count (Z) and the verb 'were' (pha-te hi). Used for enumeration.
  - Example: Bela, Bekher, leh Jediael, thum pha-te hi.

- **amau’ beh sungah innkuan lutang, a thahat a galhang mihing [Number] ahi hi.**
  - A complex descriptive clause identifying the group: 'in their clan, heads of household, mighty valiant men [Number] they were.'
  - Example: amau’ beh sungah innkuan lutang, a thahat a galhang mihing tul sawmnih leh zanih ahi hi.

- **X in Y kici tapa khat nei hi.**
  - Subject (X) 'had' (nei hi) 'one son named' (kici tapa khat) Y. Structure for introducing a specific son.
  - Example: Dan in Hushim kici tapa khat nei hi.
