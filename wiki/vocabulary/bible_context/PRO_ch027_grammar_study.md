## Verses

### v1
**EN:** Boast not thyself of to morrow; for thou knowest not what a day may bring forth.

**TDB77:** Zing ciang hun-a ding kima-sialh kei in, tu leh tua hun kikal-a piang ding na thei kei hi.

**ZVS:** Zing ciang hun-a ding kima-sialh kei in. Tuhun leh tua hun kikalah a piang ding thu bangmah na thei kei hi.

**Grammar:** negation: kei | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v2
**EN:** Let another man praise thee, and not thine own mouth; a stranger, and not thine own lips.

**TDB77:** Midangte’ hong phat loh buang, nang kiphat tawm kei in. Khualmite’ hong phat loh buang, nangma kam-in kiphat tawm kei in.

**ZVS:** Midangte in hong phat lohbuang nangmah kiphattawm kei in. Khual mite in hong phat lohbuang nangma kamin kiphattawm kei in.

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then) | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v3

### v6

### v7

**ZVS:** A gilvah mipa in khuaizu nangawn duh nawnlo a, a gilkial mipa in a kha an nangawn limsa hi.

### v8

**ZVS:** Inn panin gamlapi-ah a zinkhia mipa a bu panin a lengkhia vasa tawh kibang hi.

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v9
**EN:** Ointment and perfume rejoice the heart: so doth the sweetness of a man's friend by hearty counsel.

**TDB77:** Sathau namtui leh paknamtui in lungnuam sak a, lawmte’ limbawlna in tha hatsak hi.

**ZVS:** Sathau namtui leh paknamtui in lung nuamsak a, lawmte' limbawlna in tha hatsak hi.

### v10
**EN:** Thine own friend, and thy father's friend, forsake not; neither go into thy brother's house in the day of thy calamity: for better is a neighbour that is near than a brother far off.

**TDB77:** Na lawmte daang koih kei in la, na pa’ lawmte dong nangawn lawm bawl in. Na lungkham ciangin na sanggampa va ko kei in, a gamla-a om i sanggampa sangin, i nai-a om i vengpa kimang zaw hi.

**ZVS:** Na lawmte daangkoih kei in la, na pa' lawmte dong nangawn lawm bawl in. Na lungkham ciangin na sanggampa va ko kei in. A gamla-ah a om i sanggampa sangin a nai-ah a om i vengpa kimangzaw hi.

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v11
