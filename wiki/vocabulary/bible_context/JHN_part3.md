# JHN - Part 3

## Vocabulary with Context

### lianzaw = greater/more important
**Context:** Used to describe the one who came after John, implying superiority or precedence.
**Example:** kei sangin a lianzaw ka nungah hong pai hi

### nungah = one who comes after/successor
**Context:** Refers to the person who follows John the Baptist (Jesus).
**Example:** kei sangin a lianzaw ka nungah hong pai hi

### thei = to know (in this context, to recognize or acknowledge)
**Context:** John stating he did not recognize Jesus initially in a personal sense.
**Example:** Kei in tua pa ka thei hi.

### kilat = to be made manifest/revealed
**Context:** The purpose of John's baptism was for Jesus to be revealed to Israel.
**Example:** Israel mite kiangah a kilat nadingin

### baptaiz = to baptize
**Context:** The act of immersing in water, used for John's ministry.
**Example:** keimah in tui sungah baptaiz-in hong pai ka hi hi

### vakhu = dove
**Context:** Used as a simile for the descent of the Holy Spirit.
**Example:** Kha Siangtho vantung pan vakhu bangin hong kumsuka

### kumsuk = to descend/come down
**Context:** Describing the manner in which the Spirit came from heaven.
**Example:** Kha Siangtho vantung pan vakhu bangin hong kumsuka

### tuang = to abide/remain/rest upon
**Context:** Describing the Spirit remaining on Jesus.
**Example:** tua pa tungah a tuang ka mu hi

### sawlpa = the one who sent
**Context:** Referring to God who commissioned John to baptize.
**Example:** tui sungah baptaiz dingin kei hong sawlpa in

### teci = testimony/record
**Context:** John bearing witness about Jesus.
**Example:** ci-in teci ka pang zo hi

### Tuuno = Lamb
**Context:** A title used for Jesus, referencing the Lamb of God.
**Example:** Pasian' Tuuno hong pai hi

### kampauna = first word/saying
**Context:** Referring to John's proclamation when he saw Jesus.
**Example:** Hih lai-ah Pasian' Tuuno hong pai hi,” a ci hi.

### Rabbi = Rabbi (Master/Teacher)
**Context:** The title used by the disciples when addressing Jesus.
**Example:** Rabbi aw, koi lai-ah om na hi hiam?

### khiatna = meaning/interpretation
**Context:** Explaining the meaning of the title Rabbi.
**Example:** Rabbi a khiatna in ‘Sia’ cihnopna ahi hi.

### kihei = to turn around
**Context:** Jesus turning to see his followers.
**Example:** Zeisu kihei a

### giak = to stay/lodge/remain (often overnight)
**Context:** The disciples staying with Jesus for the night.
**Example:** ama kiangah a giak-te hi.

### teen'na = hometown/place of dwelling
**Context:** Referring to the city where Andrew and Peter lived.
**Example:** Andru leh Piter-te’ teen’na khua Bethsaida khuami ahi hi.

### ngaihsun = to intend/plan/consider
**Context:** Jesus planning his journey to Galilee.
**Example:** Galile gamah pai ning ci a

### Messiah = Messiah
**Context:** The title recognized by Andrew.
**Example:** Messiah ka mu zo-te hi

### Khazih = Christ
**Context:** The Tedim translation for Messiah/Christ.
**Example:** Tua pen Khrih a cihnopna ahi hi.

### Kefas = Cephas (Rock)
**Context:** The new name Jesus gave to Simon.
**Example:** Kefas hong kici ding hi

### suangtum = stone/rock
**Context:** The meaning of the name Cephas/Peter.
**Example:** Kefas in Piter cihnopna hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **kei sangin a lianzaw** = greater than me
  - Usage: Used to express precedence or superiority of one person over another.

- **a kilat nadingin** = so that he might be made manifest
  - Usage: Used to state the purpose or reason for an action (teleological clause).

- **Kha Siangtho vantung pan vakhu bangin** = like the Holy Spirit descending from heaven like a dove
  - Usage: A direct simile describing the manner of the Spirit's descent.

- **ci-in teci ka pang zo hi** = I have borne witness saying that...
  - Usage: A concluding statement confirming a testimony or declaration.

- **Bang zong na hi-te hiam?** = What are you seeking?
  - Usage: A direct question used by Jesus to inquire about the disciples' purpose in following him.

- **Hong pai le-uh teh na mu ding-te hi** = Come and you will see
  - Usage: An invitation followed by a promise of revelation or sight.

- **Messiah ka mu zo-te hi** = We have found the Messiah
  - Usage: A declaration of recognition regarding the promised deliverer.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X in Y ci-in Z a gen hi.**
  - Quotation structure: X said Z, saying Y. (e.g., Johan in...ci-in a gen hi.)
  - Example: ci-in Johan in a gen hi.

- **Ahih hangin...**
  - Concessive clause marker: 'Although' or 'However'. Used to introduce a contrasting or qualifying statement.
  - Example: Ahih hangin amah in Israel mite kiangah a kilat nadingin...

- **A tungah [Noun] hong kumsuka, a tuang na muh leh...**
  - Conditional structure describing the sign for recognition: 'If you see [Noun] descending and remaining on him, then...'
  - Example: Mi khat tungah Kha Siangtho hong kumsuka, a tuang na muh leh...

- **X leh Y-te' [Possessive Noun] [Location] ahi hi.**
  - Identifying the origin or association of a person: X and Y's [Possessive Noun] is [Location].
  - Example: Filip in Andru leh Piter-te’ teen’na khua Bethsaida khuami ahi hi.
