# 1CH - Part 43

## Vocabulary with Context

### nasempa = servant/worker
**Context:** Referring to David, God's servant, in the context of his lineage and actions.
**Example:** na nasempa’ hang

### lungsim = heart/mind
**Context:** Used to describe the manner or will of God ('according to your heart').
**Example:** nangma lungsim bangin

### kitheihsakna = making known/revelation
**Context:** The act of God making known or revealing things.
**Example:** na liante khempeuh na kitheihsakna ah

### hiziatna = greatness/magnificence
**Context:** Referring to the great deeds or magnificence performed by God.
**Example:** hih liatna khempeuh na hih hi

### kizui-in = following/in accordance with
**Context:** Describing how the hearing (zakna) aligns with the eyes (bil-un).
**Example:** Ka bil-un ka zakna uh tawh kizui-in

### simloh = other than/besides
**Context:** Used in a negative construction to emphasize uniqueness ('no other God besides you').
**Example:** nang simloh Pasian om lo hi

### hawlkhiatna = driving out/casting out
**Context:** Referring to God driving out other nations for the sake of Israel.
**Example:** minamte hawlkhiatna tawh

### lauhuai = terrible/awesome
**Context:** Describing the name God made for Himself through redemption.
**Example:** a lian a lauhuai min khat na bawlna

### hotkhiat = to redeem/to deliver
**Context:** Used repeatedly for God's act of delivering Israel from Egypt.
**Example:** Egypt panin na hotkhiatna mite

### tawntungin = forever/eternally
**Context:** Used to describe the perpetual nature of Israel belonging to God, or the perpetuation of God's name.
**Example:** a tawntungin nangma mi ahi dingin

### kip hen la = be established/let it be confirmed
**Context:** A plea or command for a promise or word to be permanently established.
**Example:** na gensa thu a tawntungin kip hen la

### kipin kiliansak ding = to be glorified/magnified
**Context:** The purpose for establishing God's name forever.
**Example:** nangma min a tawntungin kipin kiliansak ding a

### suan a khakte = descendants and children/posterity
**Context:** Referring to the future generations of David's line.
**Example:** a suan a khakte tawh kisai-in

### nong lak khin zo hi = you have chosen/taken up
**Context:** God choosing David's servant (David) to build a house (dynasty) for Him.
**Example:** na nasempa tungah nong lak khin zo hi

### kizop tawntung = to continue/to be connected perpetually
**Context:** The desire for the blessing to continue eternally before God.
**Example:** nangma mai-ah a kizop tawntung theih nadingin

### nuaisiah = to smite/to subdue
**Context:** David's military action against the Philistines.
**Example:** David in Filistiate zoin nuaisiah a

### siah puak = to bring tribute/to pay taxes
**Context:** The result of being conquered; becoming servants who pay tribute.
**Example:** siah hong puak uh hi

### ngah kik nuamin = in order to regain/to recover
**Context:** Hadadezer's motivation for marching towards the Euphrates.
**Example:** gamte ngah kik nuamin kuan ahih manin

### zagui atsat sak = to hamstring/to hough (horses)
**Context:** David's action of disabling the captured chariot horses, keeping only a few.
**Example:** leengkai sakol galkap khempeuh a zagui atsat sak a

### kicing a sit = to reserve/to keep sufficient amount
**Context:** David reserving a specific number of chariot horses.
**Example:** leeng za khat-a' dingin a kicing a sit hi

### khuacingte koih = to set up garrisons/to place guards
**Context:** David establishing military presence in Damascus.
**Example:** Damaskas-ah khuacingte koih hi

## Phrases & Usage

- **nangmah tawh a kibang kuamah om lo** = there is none like you
  - Usage: Used to express the uniqueness and incomparability of God (Topa).

- **a lian a lauhuai min khat na bawlna** = making a name for yourself great and terrible
  - Usage: Describes God's action of establishing His reputation through mighty deeds (like redemption).

- **a tawntungin thupha ngah ahi hi** = they receive blessing forever
  - Usage: A concluding statement affirming the eternal nature of the blessings bestowed by God.

- **David a paina peuhpeuhah** = wherever David went
  - Usage: Indicates the consistent success David experienced in all his military campaigns.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X aw, ... na hih hi.**
  - Vocative address (Topa aw) followed by a statement describing the subject's actions or nature, often using the verb 'hi' (to be) at the end.
  - Example: Topa aw, ... na hih hi.

- **nangmah tawh a kibang kuamah om lo a, nang simloh Pasian om lo hi.**
  - Double negative construction emphasizing exclusivity: 'There is no one like you, and there is no other God besides you.' (A=B, not C=A).
  - Example: nangmah tawh a kibang kuamah om lo a, nang simloh Pasian om lo hi.

- **X panin Y tawh a kibang Z kua om ahi hiam?**
  - Rhetorical question structure used for comparison: 'Who in the world (Z) is like (a kibang) the people (Y) whom you redeemed from (X)?'
  - Example: Egypt panin na hotkhiat na mite' mai-ah ... Israelte tawh a kibang mi leitungah minam dang kua om ahi hiam?

- **X in Y zo a, Z suakin siah hong puak uh hi.**
  - Sequential action structure: Subject (X) conquers (Y), and as a result, they (Z) become servants and bring tribute.
  - Example: Moab mite zo a, Moab mite David' nasem suakin siah hong puak uh hi.
