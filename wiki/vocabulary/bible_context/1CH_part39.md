# 1CH - Part 39

## Vocabulary with Context

### meihal = burnt offerings
**Context:** Referring to the type of sacrifice David offered.
**Example:** David in meihal biaknate leh thu-nuama biaknate a piak a man khit ciangin...

### thu-nuama = peace offerings (or fellowship offerings)
**Context:** Paired with 'meihal' (burnt offerings) as sacrifices David made.
**Example:** David in meihal biaknate leh thu-nuama biaknate a piak a man khit ciangin...

### piak a man khit ciangin = when he had finished offering/making
**Context:** A temporal clause indicating the completion of the sacrifices.
**Example:** David in meihal biaknate leh thu-nuama biaknate a piak a man khit ciangin...

### thupha pia = blessed
**Context:** The action David performed after finishing the offerings.
**Example:** amah in Topa min tawh thupha pia a,

### anlum = loaf of bread
**Context:** One of the items distributed to the people.
**Example:** Israel mi numei leh pasal khempeuhte tungah anlum lum khat, sa bak khat...

### leenggahkeu = flagon (of wine)
**Context:** The third item distributed to each person.
**Example:** leh leenggahkeu bek khat ciat hawm hi.

### tham loin = Moreover / In addition
**Context:** Used to introduce an additional action or appointment.
**Example:** Tua tham loin amah in Topa’ thuciamna singkuang mai-ah na semin thu ngen ding...

### Thuciamna Singkuang = Ark of the Covenant
**Context:** The sacred object before which the Levites were appointed to minister.
**Example:** amah in { Topa' } Thuciamna Singkuang mai-ah na semin thu a ngen ding...

### lungdamna ko ding = to give thanks
**Context:** One of the duties assigned to the appointed Levites.
**Example:** thu ngen ding, lungdamna ko ding, leh Israel Pasian, Topa a phat dingin...

### phat dingin = to praise
**Context:** The purpose for which the Levites were appointed to stand before the Ark.
**Example:** leh Israel Pasian, Topa a phat dingin Levi mite pawlkhat seh hi.

### ukpi = chief / leader
**Context:** Describing Asaf's role among the musicians.
**Example:** Asaf in a ukpi hi a, amah zomah a nihnate in...

### harp lasaknate = playing of harps
**Context:** One of the musical activities assigned to the Levites.
**Example:** harp lasaknate leh laiza lasaknate a tum dingte ahi-te hi;

### daltuahte = cymbals
**Context:** The instrument Asaf was specifically designated to play.
**Example:** Asaf in daltuahte a tum ding hi a,

### peengkulte = trumpets
**Context:** The instrument the priests Benaiah and Jahaziel were to play before the Ark.
**Example:** Pasian’ Thuciamna Singkuang mai-ah peengkulte a tum paisuak ding-te ahi hi.

### lungdamkohna la = song of thanksgiving
**Context:** The type of song David instructed Asaf and the Levites to sing first.
**Example:** Topa’ tungah lungdamkohna la a sak ding seh masa hi.

### kitheisak un = make known / proclaim
**Context:** An imperative command to spread the news of God's deeds.
**Example:** ama sepnate mite lakah kitheisak un!

### phatna la = song of praise
**Context:** Another type of song commanded to be sung to God.
**Example:** Phatna la amah sak un la,

### lamdang ama septe = his wondrous works / marvellous deeds
**Context:** Referring to the extraordinary acts of God.
**Example:** a lamdang ama septe khempeuh gen un!

### ompihna = presence
**Context:** Something the people are commanded to seek continually.
**Example:** leh ama hatna zong un la, ama ompihna zong den un!

### thukhennate = judgments / decrees
**Context:** Referring to God's pronouncements or laws.
**Example:** a lamdang ama genkhiat a thukhennate phawk un,

### teeltuamte = chosen ones
**Context:** Referring to the descendants of Abraham and Jacob.
**Example:** Jakob' suante, ama teeltuamte aw!

### thupiaknate = judgments / ordinances
**Context:** Referring to God's laws that extend throughout the world.
**Example:** ama thupiaknate leitung khempeuh-a' ding ahi hi.

### khang tul khat = a thousand generations
**Context:** The duration for which God's command/covenant word is intended.
**Example:** Khang tul khata dingin a thupiakna kammal,

## Phrases & Usage

- **Topa min tawh** = in the name of the Lord
  - Usage: Used when performing an action (like blessing) invoking God's authority.

- **khat ciat hawm hi** = distributed one each to
  - Usage: Used when detailing the equal distribution of items to individuals.

- **lungdamna ko-in** = giving thanks
  - Usage: Used as an introductory action before singing praise.

- **ama min lo un la** = call upon his name / invoke his name
  - Usage: A command related to worship and remembrance.

## Grammar Patterns

- **V + a man khit ciangin**
  - A structure indicating completion of an action before the next clause begins. (V = Verb stem, 'a man' implies completion, 'ciangin' means 'when').
  - Example: a piak a man khit ciangin (when he had finished offering)

- **Noun + khempeuhte tungah + Item + khat ciat hawm hi**
  - Pattern for distributing items equally to a whole group (khempeuhte = all of them).
  - Example: Israel mi numei leh pasal khempeuhte tungah anlum khat... ciat hawm hi.

- **V + ding**
  - The suffix '-ding' is used here to express purpose or future action/intent, often translated as 'in order to' or 'to'.
  - Example: thu ngen ding (to pray), lungdamna ko ding (to give thanks)

- **V + un la, V + un la, V + un!**
  - Repetitive imperative structure using the suffix '-un' (command form) connected by 'la' (and/then), used for a series of commands in worship.
  - Example: Amah la sak un, phatna la amah sak un la, a lamdang ama septe khempeuh gen un!

- **V + tawntung hi**
  - Indicates a continuous or perpetual state/action ('tawntung' means forever/always).
  - Example: Amah in a thuciamna phawk tawntung hi (He remembers His covenant always).
