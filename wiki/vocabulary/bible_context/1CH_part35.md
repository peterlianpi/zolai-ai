## Phrases & Usage

- **a om lai i sanggamte** = our brethren who are still in the land
  - Usage: Used to refer to Israelites residing in the land of Israel.

- **a khut va zan kha hi** = stretched out his hand to hold/steady
  - Usage: Describes the action of reaching out to support something unstable.

- **a tha neih zahun** = with all their might/strength
  - Usage: Indicates performing an action with maximum effort, often in worship.

- **bangci ciahpih theih ding ka hi hiam?** = How shall I be able to bring up... to me?
  - Usage: An expression of fear and uncertainty regarding handling a sacred object.

- **inn khat a lamsak dingin** = in order to build a house for himself
  - Usage: Expresses the purpose of sending materials and skilled workers.

## Grammar Patterns

- **Tua ciangin [Subject] in [Object] kiangah, “[Quotation]” a ci hi.**
  - Standard narrative structure for reporting speech following an event ('Then... said to...').
  - Example: Tua ciangin David in Israel a kikhawm khempeuhte kiangah, “...” a ci hi.

- **Note in [Verb-na] na sak-te a, [Condition] ahih leh, [Action]**
  - A conditional structure used to propose an action contingent on the audience's approval and God's will.
  - Example: Note in kilawm na sak-te a, Topa i Pasian' deihna ahih leh, amaute eite kiangah hong pai khop theih nadingun...

- **[Subject] in [Object] tungah [Emotion] hi; [Reason], [Result]**
  - Describes a sequence of divine reaction, cause, and consequence.
  - Example: Tua ciangin Uzza tungah Topa heh hi. Amah in a khut tawh Thuciamna Singkuang sukha ahih manin Topa in amah vat hi.

- **Tua ahih ciangin [Subject] in [Object] [Location] a [Verb] lo a, [Alternative Action]**
  - Shows a decision made by the subject to *not* do something in one place, and instead do it elsewhere.
  - Example: Tua ahih ciangin David in Thuciamna Singkuang David khuapi a inn-ah ciahpihlo a, Obededom' inn-ah va koih hi.
