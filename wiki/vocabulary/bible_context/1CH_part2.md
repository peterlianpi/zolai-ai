# 1CH - Part 2

## Vocabulary with Context

### mite = people/descendants of (used for tribal/familial groups)
**Context:** Used after proper names (Hiv, Arka, Sin) to denote the people group descended from them.
**Example:** Hiv mite, Arka mite, Sin mite,

### leh = and
**Context:** Used to connect items in a list, similar to 'and' in English.
**Example:** Zemari mite, leh Hamath mite' pian'napa ahi hi.

### pian'napa = descendants/offspring (literally 'the one who was born')
**Context:** Refers to the descendants of the preceding groups.
**Example:** leh Hamath mite' pian'napa ahi hi.

### ahi hi = they are / it is
**Context:** A common copula/verb phrase indicating existence or identity.
**Example:** leh Hamath mite' pian'napa ahi hi.

### tapate = sons (plural)
**Context:** Refers to the male offspring or descendants of a patriarch (Shem).
**Example:** Shem tapate in:

### min pua-in = by name (literally 'name coming out')
**Context:** Indicates that the resulting nations were named after these individuals.
**Example:** amau min pua-in minam piang ciat hi.

### minam = nation/tribe
**Context:** The resulting political/ethnic group formed from the descendants.
**Example:** amau min pua-in minam piang ciat hi.

### ciat = each/separately
**Context:** Emphasizes that each descendant formed their own distinct nation.
**Example:** minam piang ciat hi.

### pen = is/was (emphatic marker, often used for subjects)
**Context:** Used to identify the subject in a statement of identity or relationship.
**Example:** Arpakshad pen Shelah' pa hi a,

### a = and/while (conjunction)
**Context:** Used to link sequential clauses or parallel statements.
**Example:** Arpakshad pen Shelah' pa hi a, Shelah pen Eber' pa ahi hi.

### sung pan = from within/out of
**Context:** Indicates origin from within a lineage.
**Example:** Eber' sung pan a piang tapa nihte:

### khatpa’ = the first one's
**Context:** Refers to the firstborn son.
**Example:** khatpa’ min in Peleg ahi hi

### bang hang hiam cih leh = because (literally 'for what reason, if you ask, then')
**Context:** Introduces the reason or explanation for the name Peleg.
**Example:** (bang hang hiam cih leh ama hunin gam kikhen hi),

### gam kikhen = the earth was divided
**Context:** The specific event that occurred during Peleg's time.
**Example:** ama hunin gam kikhen hi

### sanggampa’ = his older brother's
**Context:** Refers to the other son born to Eber.
**Example:** a sanggampa’ min in Joktan ahi hi.

### suante = descendants/offspring (plural)
**Context:** General term for the progeny of Joktan.
**Example:** Hihte khempeuh, Joktan' suante ahi uh hi.

### tua ciangin = after that/then
**Context:** Indicates chronological sequence.
**Example:** tua ciangin Abraham a kici Abram ahi suk uh hi.

### a kici = who is called
**Context:** Used to state an alternative or current name.
**Example:** Abraham a kici Abram ahi suk uh hi.

### ahi suk uh hi = they became/they were called
**Context:** Indicates a change or establishment of a name.
**Example:** Abram ahi suk uh hi.

### khangsimna = generations/lineage
**Context:** Refers to the recorded line of descent.
**Example:** Amau' khangsimna hih bang ahi hi:

### tacil = firstborn
**Context:** Designates the first son born to Ishmael.
**Example:** Ishmael' tacil Nebaioth ahi hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **pian'napa ahi hi** = are the descendants of
  - Usage: Used after a list of names followed by 'leh' (and) to summarize that the preceding list constitutes the offspring of the final named entity.

- **min pua-in minam piang ciat hi** = nations were formed, each named after them
  - Usage: Describes the result of the lineage—the establishment of distinct nations based on the names of the sons.

- **bang hang hiam cih leh** = because (lit. if you ask the reason)
  - Usage: A parenthetical phrase used to introduce an explanation or etymology for a name.

- **ahi uh hi** = they are
  - Usage: A concluding statement identifying the subject group (e.g., the sons of Abraham).

## Grammar Patterns

- **X mite, Y mite, leh Z mite' pian'napa ahi hi.**
  - Listing of tribal groups (X, Y, Z) followed by the possessive marker ('s) and the term for descendants ('pian'napa') to show their origin.
  - Example: Arvad mite, Zemari mite, leh Hamath mite' pian'napa ahi hi.

- **A in B' pa ahi hi.**
  - Simple statement of paternity: A is the father of B. The possessive marker ('s) is used on the son (B') to link him to the father (A).
  - Example: Arpakshad pen Shelah' pa hi a,

- **A' sung pan a piang tapa nihte: ...**
  - Introduction to listing the sons born from a specific ancestor (A). 'Sung pan' means 'from within'.
  - Example: Eber' sung pan a piang tapa nihte:

- **A a kici B ahi suk uh hi.**
  - Indicates a name change or clarification: A, who is called B, they became/were known as.
  - Example: Abraham a kici Abram ahi suk uh hi.
