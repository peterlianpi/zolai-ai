# 1CH - Part 6

## Vocabulary with Context

### luppih = married/took as wife
**Context:** Hezron took Machir's daughter as his wife.
**Example:** Hezron in a ten’pih Gilead’ pa Makhir’ tanu luppih hi

### nei = had (children/offspring)
**Context:** The woman bore him Segub.
**Example:** tua nu in amah tawh Segub nei hi

### khuapi = city/town
**Context:** Segub had twenty-three cities in the land of Gilead.
**Example:** Gilead gam sungah khuapi sawmnih-le-thum nei hi.

### Ahih hangin = However/But
**Context:** However, Geshur and Aram took cities from them.
**Example:** Ahih hangin Geshur leh Aram in amaute tung panin Havvothjair khua Kenath leh a khuaneute, khua sawmguk la hi.

### suan leh khakte = descendants and relatives (sons and families)
**Context:** All these belonged to the descendants and families of Machir.
**Example:** Hihte khempeuh in Gilead’ pa Makhir’ suan leh khakte ahi-te hi.

### sih khit ciangin = after (he) died
**Context:** After Hezron died...
**Example:** Hezron a sih khit ciangin Kaleb in a pa Hezron’ zi Efrath luppih a

### tacil = firstborn/chief son
**Context:** The sons of Hezron's firstborn Jerahmeel were...
**Example:** Hezron’ tacil Jerahme-el’ tapate in: A tacil gam, Bunah, Oren, Ozem, leh Ahijah ahi-te hi.

### sanggampa = brother (often used for a close male relative or peer in this context, though literally 'older brother')
**Context:** The sons of Jada, Shammai's brother...
**Example:** Tua ciangin Shammai’ sanggampa Jada’ tapate in: Jether leh Jonathan ahi-te hi

### ta nei loin si = died without children
**Context:** Seled died without having children.
**Example:** Seled in ta nei loin si hi.

### sila = servant/slave
**Context:** Sheshan had a servant, an Egyptian.
**Example:** Sheshan in Jarha min nei Izipt sila khat nei hi.

### pia = gave (in marriage)
**Context:** Sheshan gave his daughter to his servant Jarha to wife.
**Example:** Sheshan in a sila Jarha tungah a zi dingin a tanu pia hi

## Phrases & Usage

- **Tua khit ciangin** = After that / Afterward
  - Usage: Used to mark a sequence of events in time.

- **a ten’pih** = whom he married (lit. whom he took as wife)
  - Usage: Used to specify the act of taking a wife, often implying a formal marriage.

- **ta nei loin si** = died without children
  - Usage: A standard phrase in genealogies indicating childless death.

- **a zi dingin a tanu pia** = gave his daughter to be his wife
  - Usage: Used specifically for giving a daughter in marriage, often to a servant or someone of lower status in this context.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X in Y luppih hi**
  - Structure indicating 'X married Y' or 'X took Y as wife'. 'luppih' functions as the main verb for marriage.
  - Example: Hezron in a ten’pih Gilead’ pa Makhir’ tanu luppih hi

- **X’ tapate in: A, B, C ahi-te hi.**
  - A common genealogical structure listing the sons (tapate) of a person (X).
  - Example: Hezron’ tacil Jerahme-el’ tapate in: A tacil gam, Bunah, Oren, Ozem, leh Ahijah ahi-te hi.

- **X in Y tawh Z nei hi.**
  - Structure showing 'X had Z with Y' (i.e., Y is the mother of Z, or Y is the co-parent/spouse).
  - Example: tua nu in amah tawh Segub nei hi
