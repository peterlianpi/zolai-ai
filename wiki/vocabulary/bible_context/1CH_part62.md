# 1CH - Part 62

## Vocabulary with Context

### siangtho = holy
**Context:** Used to describe the 'inn' (house/temple) where things are dedicated.
**Example:** a siangtho inn adingin

### ngun = gold
**Context:** Refers to the precious metal dedicated to God's house.
**Example:** ngun leh kham keima aa mahmah zong om a

### kham = silver
**Context:** Refers to the precious metal dedicated to God's house, often paired with 'ngun' (gold).
**Example:** ngun leh kham keima aa mahmah zong om a

### ngaihsutna = affection/desire/thought
**Context:** The reason for the offering; setting one's heart/affection on something.
**Example:** ka Pasian’ inn ka ngaihsutna hangin

### piakte = I give/offer
**Context:** The act of dedicating possessions to God's house.
**Example:** ka Pasian’ innah ka piakte in

### Ofir = Ophir
**Context:** A geographical location known for its gold.
**Example:** Ofir gam kham ahi kham talen

### kisiangsaksa = refined/purified
**Context:** Describing the quality of the silver.
**Example:** a kisiangsaksa ngun talen

### siamneelte = artificers/craftsmen
**Context:** Those who will perform the work using the dedicated materials.
**Example:** leh siamneelte in a hih ding nasepna

### utna lungsim = willing heart/desire
**Context:** The required attitude for consecrating service to the Lord.
**Example:** Kua in utna lungsim tawh Topa tungah tu ni-in

### kipia-in ki-ap = consecrate/dedicate oneself
**Context:** The act of willingly offering oneself or one's service.
**Example:** amahmah kipia-in ki-ap ding na hi uh hiam?”

### beh lutangte = chiefs of the fathers/heads of families
**Context:** The leaders who initiated the willing offerings.
**Example:** Tua ciangin beh lutangte in utthu-a piakna bawl uh a

### utthu-a piakna = willing offering/act of willingness
**Context:** The action taken by the leaders to offer willingly.
**Example:** beh lutangte in utthu-a piakna bawl uh a

### kumpipa’ nasepna = the king's work
**Context:** The area of work overseen by the rulers under the king.
**Example:** leh kumpipa' nasepna-ah a uk uliante in zong hih uh hi.

### sumngo = brass/bronze
**Context:** Another metal dedicated for the temple service.
**Example:** sumngo talen tul sawm

### sik = iron
**Context:** Another metal dedicated for the temple service.
**Example:** leh sik talen tul za khat

### suangmanpha = precious stones
**Context:** Items given by those who possessed them.
**Example:** Suangmanpha a nei peuhmah in

### kepna sung = treasury/storage place
**Context:** The designated location for storing the dedicated valuables.
**Example:** Gershon mi Jehiel' kepna sung

### cingtakin = perfectly/sincerely/wholeheartedly
**Context:** Describing the manner in which the people offered to the Lord.
**Example:** amaute in lungsim khempeuh tawh Topa tungah cingtakin pia uh hi

### phat = praise
**Context:** David's action in the assembly after the offerings.
**Example:** David in Topa phat hi

### liatna = glory/honor
**Context:** One of the attributes belonging to God.
**Example:** Topa aw, liatna, leh vangliatna

### vangliatna = might/power/majesty
**Context:** An attribute of God's nature.
**Example:** leh vangliatna

### gualzawhna = victory/triumph
**Context:** Something that belongs to God.
**Example:** leh gualzawhna

### hauhna = riches/wealth
**Context:** Something that comes from God.
**Example:** Hauhna leh minphatna nangma kiang pan hong pai a

### thakhauhsakna = to strengthen/to give strength
**Context:** An action God performs through His hand.
**Example:** leh thakhauhsakna ding na khutsungah om hi.

### nuam uh hi = we rejoice/we are pleased
**Context:** The feeling of the people regarding God.
**Example:** Tu-in nang tungah ka nuam uh hi

### mikhualte = strangers/sojourners
**Context:** Describing the status of the people before God, like their ancestors.
**Example:** nangma mai-ah mikhualte leh peemtate ka hi uh hi.

### peemtate = sojourners/temporary residents
**Context:** Used alongside 'mikhualte' to emphasize their temporary status on earth.
**Example:** mikhualte leh peemtate ka hi uh hi.

### nilim = shadow
**Context:** Used metaphorically to describe the brevity of their earthly life.
**Example:** ka hunte uh leitungah nilim bang hi a

### thumanna = uprightness/sincerity
**Context:** The quality God tests and delights in.
**Example:** Nangmah in lungsim entelin thumanna ah na lungkimna ka thei hi

## Phrases & Usage

- **keima aa mahmah** = of mine own proper good/of my own
  - Usage: Emphasizes that the offering was from personal possessions, not just collected resources.

- **kham zat nading nate ah kham** = silver for things of silver
  - Usage: A parallel structure indicating specific allocation of materials for specific purposes (like things made of silver use silver).

- **a tawntungin thupha ngah ta in** = May you be blessed forever
  - Usage: A formal blessing or prayer directed towards God.

- **a lutangin hong kisangsak hi** = is established/made firm according to your pleasure
  - Usage: Describes how God's kingship is established based on His will.

- **a vekpi-in nangma aa mah ahi hi** = all of it is truly yours
  - Usage: A statement of ultimate ownership, acknowledging that everything comes from God.

- **lungsim khempeuh tawh** = with a whole heart/sincerely
  - Usage: Describes the completeness and sincerity of the offering or devotion.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X-a' mahmah**
  - Possessive structure emphasizing 'one's own' or 'belonging entirely to oneself'. (e.g., keima aa mahmah = mine own proper)
  - Example: ngun leh kham keima aa mahmah zong om a

- **X-a' ding**
  - Purpose marker, indicating 'for the purpose of X' or 'for X'. (e.g., nasepna-a' dingin = for the purpose of work)
  - Example: nasepna khempeuh-a dingin

- **Kua in X tawh Y na hi uh hiam?**
  - Rhetorical question structure asking 'Who is it that, with X, you are Y?' Used to prompt self-reflection on willingness.
  - Example: Kua in utna lungsim tawh Topa tungah tu ni-in amahmah kipia-in ki-ap ding na hi uh hiam?”

- **X leh Y zong hih uh hi**
  - Structure indicating that others, in addition to the primary actors, also performed the action. (e.g., 'and the rulers also did it')
  - Example: leh kumpipa' nasepna-ah a uk uliante in zong hih uh hi.

- **Bang hang hiam cih leh X**
  - A common structure meaning 'Why is it that X?' or 'Because X is true'. Used to introduce a reason or explanation.
  - Example: Bang hang hiam cih leh amaute in a lungsim khempeuh uh tawh...
