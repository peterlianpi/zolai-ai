# GEN - Part 4

## Vocabulary with Context

### nisuahna = east/eastern direction
**Context:** Referring to the direction of Assyria (Assiria gam nisuahna lama - towards the east of Assyria).
**Example:** Assiria gam nisuahna lama a luang a thumna gun min in Tigris ahi hi.

### luang = river
**Context:** Used to denote a river, often paired with 'gun' (which seems to mean 'river' or 'stream' in this context, or perhaps 'branch/course').
**Example:** a luang a thumna gun min in Tigris ahi hi.

### thumna = third
**Context:** Ordinal number modifying 'gun' (river/stream).
**Example:** a luang a thumna gun min in Tigris ahi hi.

### Hiddekel = Hiddekel (Tigris)
**Context:** The proper name given to the third river in the Tedim version.
**Example:** Assiria gam nisuahna lamah a luang a thumna gun min Hiddekel ahi hi.

### lina = fourth
**Context:** Ordinal number modifying 'gun' (river/stream).
**Example:** A lina gun in Eufrates gun ahi hi.

### Eufrates = Euphrates
**Context:** Proper name for the fourth river.
**Example:** A lina gun pen Eufratis gun ahi hi.

### kho-a = to cultivate/to tend/to keep (in a garden context)
**Context:** Used with 'kem dingin' (for keeping/guarding) regarding the garden.
**Example:** Eden huan kho-a a kem dingin koih hi.

### kem dingin = for the purpose of keeping/guarding
**Context:** Indicates the purpose for which man was placed in the garden.
**Example:** Eden huan kho-a a kem dingin koih hi.

### koih hi = He placed/set (him)
**Context:** The action of God placing man.
**Example:** Topa Pasian in mipa la-in Eden huan kho-a a kem dingin koih hi.

### thu pia = gave command/spoke
**Context:** God giving instructions to man.
**Example:** Topa Pasian in mipa thu pia a,

### singkung = tree
**Context:** Referring to the trees in the garden.
**Example:** huan sung singkung khempeuh na nethei ding hi;

### nethei ding = will be able to eat
**Context:** Permission granted to eat from the trees.
**Example:** huan sung singkung khempeuh na nethei ding hi;

### pha leh a sia theihna = knowledge of good and evil
**Context:** The specific tree whose fruit was forbidden.
**Example:** ahih hangin a pha leh a sia theihna singkung pen na ne kei ding hi;

### bang hang hiam = why/for what reason
**Context:** A rhetorical question structure used when explaining the consequence of disobedience.
**Example:** bang hang hiam na cih leh tua na nek ni-in na si taktak ding hi,” ci hi.

### si taktak ding = will surely die
**Context:** The consequence of eating the forbidden fruit.
**Example:** tua na nek ni-in na si taktak ding hi,” ci hi.

### guak = alone/lonely
**Context:** Describing the state of man being alone.
**Example:** Mipa amah guak a om ding hoih lo hi;

### hoih lo = not good/not right
**Context:** Describing the state of being alone as unsuitable.
**Example:** Mipa amah guak a om ding hoih lo hi.

### kituak a huh ding = a suitable helper
**Context:** The purpose for creating the helper for man.
**Example:** ama adinga kituak a huh ding ka bawlsak ding hi,”

### ganhing = beast/animal (of the field)
**Context:** Used generally for land animals.
**Example:** gamsung ganhing khempeuh leh huih lak vasate khempeuh lei tawh bawl a,

### huih lak vasate = birds of the air/flying creatures
**Context:** Referring to birds.
**Example:** leh huih lak vasate khempeuh lei tawh bawl a,

### lei tawh bawl = formed from dust/clay
**Context:** Describing the creation process of the animals.
**Example:** gamsung ganhing khempeuh leh huih lak vasate khempeuh lei tawh bawl a,

### paipih hi = He brought (them)
**Context:** God bringing the animals to Adam.
**Example:** mipa' kiangah tuate paipih hi;

### min vawh = named/gave names
**Context:** Adam naming the animals.
**Example:** Mipa in ganhing namte khempeuh... min vawh hi;

### kilawm = suitable/fitting
**Context:** Describing what was suitable for Adam.
**Example:** ahih hangin mipa’ adinga a kilawm, a huh ding kimu lo hi.

### ihmut suak mahmah sak = caused (him) to fall into a deep sleep
**Context:** God causing Adam to sleep deeply.
**Example:** Topa Pasian in mipa ihmut suak mahmah sak a,

### nakguh = rib
**Context:** The specific body part taken from Adam.
**Example:** a nakguhte khat la-in tua mun pen satak tawh dimsak hi;

### satak = flesh
**Context:** Used to describe the material used to fill the gap left by the rib.
**Example:** tua mun pen satak tawh dimsak hi;

### lakkhiat = taken out/removed
**Context:** Referring to the rib being taken from Adam.
**Example:** mipa’ tung panin a lakkhiat nakguh pen numei khatin bawl a,

### Numei = Woman
**Context:** The name given to the created female being.
**Example:** ahih manin Numei kici ding hi,” ci hi.

### nusia-in = leaving behind
**Context:** The action of leaving parents upon marriage.
**Example:** a nu leh a pa nusia-in a zi beel a,

### beela = cleave/adhere/join
**Context:** Used in the context of marriage union.
**Example:** a zi beel a,

### sakhat suak uh = become one flesh
**Context:** Describing the unity of husband and wife.
**Example:** amaute in sakhat suak uh hi.

### guaktangin = nakedly/without shame
**Context:** Describing their state before the fall.
**Example:** Mipa leh a zi guaktangin om uh a,

### maizum lo = not ashamed
**Context:** Their state of innocence.
**Example:** maizum lo uh hi.

### gulpi = serpent
**Context:** The creature that spoke to the woman.
**Example:** Tu-in gulpi pen, Topa Pasian' bawlsa gamlak ganhing dangte khempeuh sangin ngiansiam zaw hi.

### ngiansiam zaw = more subtle/cunning
**Context:** Describing the serpent's nature compared to other animals.
**Example:** gulpi pen... sangin ngiansiam zaw hi.

### hong ci ahi hiam = Did He say?
**Context:** The serpent questioning the woman about God's command.
**Example:** Pasian in, ‘Huan sung sing khatpeuh peuh ne lo ding,’ hong ci ahi hiam?”

### laizang-a om = located in the middle/center
**Context:** Describing the location of the forbidden fruit tree.
**Example:** Huan laizang-a om singgah na ne kei ding uh hi,

### lawng kei ding = shall not touch
**Context:** Part of the command regarding the forbidden fruit.
**Example:** zong na lawng kei ding uh hi,

## Phrases & Usage

- **a pha leh a sia theihna singkung** = the tree of the knowledge of good and evil
  - Usage: Used to refer specifically to the forbidden tree in Eden.

- **ama adinga kituak a huh ding** = a suitable helper for him
  - Usage: Used to describe the intended companion for man (Eve).

- **a nu leh a pa nusia-in** = leaving his father and his mother
  - Usage: Standard phrase describing the act of leaving parental home upon marriage.

- **a zi beel a** = cleave unto his wife
  - Usage: Used to describe the marital bond.

- **gulpi pen... sangin ngiansiam zaw hi** = the serpent was more subtle/cunning than
  - Usage: Used for comparison to highlight the serpent's craftiness.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X min Y ahi hi**
  - Structure used to identify something: 'The name of X is Y.' (e.g., The name of the third river is Tigris).
  - Example: a luang a thumna gun min in Tigris ahi hi.

- **V-ding (infinitive/future purpose)**
  - Used extensively to express purpose or future action, often following 'dingin' (for the purpose of) or as a direct future marker (e.g., 'na ne thei ding hi' - you will be able to eat).
  - Example: a kem dingin koih hi; na nethei ding hi.

- **Ahih hangin, ... ci hi**
  - Structure indicating contrast or condition followed by a statement/command: 'But/However, ... it was said.'
  - Example: ahih hangin a pha leh a sia theihna singkung pen na ne kei ding hi; ... ci hi.

- **X khempeuh sangin Y zaw hi**
  - Comparative structure: 'Y is more [adjective] than all X.'
  - Example: ganhing dangte khempeuh sangin ngiansiam zaw hi.
