# 1CH - Part 15

## Vocabulary with Context

### tapa = son (of)
**Context:** Used repeatedly to denote descent, similar to 'begat' or 'son of' in genealogies.
**Example:** a tapa Zadok

### leh = and
**Context:** Used to connect names in a list or sequence.
**Example:** Libni leh Shimei

### sungah = in the house/office of
**Context:** Used here to specify the location/role of the priest's office ('lam inn sungah' - in the house of the ministry/temple).
**Example:** Solomon in Jerusalem ah a lam inn sungah siampi a sempa

### siampi = priest
**Context:** Refers to the religious official performing priestly duties.
**Example:** siampi a sempa

### sempa = one who serves/does/performs (the action)
**Context:** Derived from 'sem' (to do/make), used here to describe the one performing the office.
**Example:** siampi a sempa

### ahi hi = it is/they are
**Context:** A common concluding phrase in Zolai, often used for identification or affirmation, similar to 'is' or 'are'.
**Example:** ahi hi.

### khut tawh = by the hand of
**Context:** Indicates the agent or means by which an action was performed (Nebukhadnezzar's hand).
**Example:** Nebukhadnezzar’ khut tawh

### sal-in = into captivity/as captives
**Context:** Describes the state of being taken away (captivity).
**Example:** Jerusalem sal-in a mat ciangin

### mat ciangin = when (it was) captured/taken
**Context:** Temporal clause marker indicating the time when the action occurred.
**Example:** a mat ciangin

### salin pai hi = went into captivity
**Context:** Describes the movement of Jehozadak into exile.
**Example:** Jehozadak salin pai hi.

### tapate = sons/descendants (plural)
**Context:** Plural form of 'tapa' (son), used for groups of descendants.
**Example:** Levi’ tapate in

### beh = family/clan/lineage
**Context:** Used to denote tribal or familial divisions.
**Example:** amau’ beh tawh kizui-in

### kizui-in = following/according to
**Context:** Indicates adherence or classification based on the preceding noun (their lineage).
**Example:** amau’ beh tawh kizui-in

## Phrases & Usage

- **a tapa Azariah, (Solomon in Jerusalem ah a lam inn sungah siampi a sempa) ahi hi.** = Azariah the son of Johanan, (he it is who served as priest in the temple/house of ministry in Jerusalem built by Solomon), he is.
  - Usage: Used parenthetically to provide explanatory detail about a specific individual in a genealogy.

- **Topa in Nebukhadnezzar’ khut tawh Judah leh Jerusalem sal-in a mat ciangin** = When the Lord, by the hand of Nebuchadnezzar, captured Judah and Jerusalem into captivity
  - Usage: A complex temporal clause setting the historical context for the subsequent action.

- **Hihte in amau’ beh tawh kizui-in Levi minamte ahi uh hi.** = These were the tribes/peoples of Levi, following their respective families/clans.
  - Usage: A concluding statement summarizing the classification of the listed individuals.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X, a tapa Y, a tapa Z, ... ahi hi.**
  - Standard genealogical structure in Zolai, listing individuals sequentially using 'a tapa' (son of) to show direct descent, concluding with 'ahi hi' (it is/they are).
  - Example: a tapa Zadok, a tapa Ahima-az, ... ahi hi.

- **Noun' + tapate in: List of names + ahi uh hi.**
  - Structure used to list the descendants of a specific patriarch (e.g., Gershom's sons). 'tapate' is the plural form.
  - Example: Gershom’ tapate in: Libni leh Shimei ahi uh hi.

- **Clause + ciangin + Action.**
  - Temporal clause structure using '-ciangin' (when/at the time that) to link a preceding condition or event to the main action.
  - Example: a mat ciangin Jehozadak salin pai hi.
