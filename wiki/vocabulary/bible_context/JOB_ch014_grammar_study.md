## Verses

### v1
**EN:** Man that is born of a woman is of few days, and full of trouble.

**TDB77:** “Mihingte a nuntak hun tom a, lungkhamna tawh kidim hi.

**ZVS:** “Mihingte a nuntak hun tom mahmah a, lungkhamna tawh kidim hi.

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v2
**EN:** He cometh forth like a flower, and is cut down: he fleeth also as a shadow, and continueth not.

**TDB77:** Amaute pakpalh bangin khangin keu pak uh a, niliim bangin pai hanhanin mangthang uh hi.

**ZVS:** Amaute paak bangin khangin keu pak uh a, niliim bangin pai hanhanin, mangthang uh hi.

**Grammar:** plural: uh marker

### v3
**EN:** And dost thou open thine eyes upon such an one, and bringest me into judgment with thee?

**TDB77:** Pasian aw, tua bang lel mi kei hong en a, thukhenna thuak dingin na mai-ah hong sam na hi hiam?

**ZVS:** Pasian aw, tua bang lel mi kei hong en a, thukhenna a thuak dingin na mai-ah hong sam na hi hiam?

**Grammar:** purpose: in order to

### v4
**EN:** Who can bring a clean thing out of an unclean? not one.

**TDB77:** Mihing banga a ninte sung panin a siang bangmah hong piang lo ding hi.

**ZVS:** Mihing banga a ninte sung panin a siang hong pianglo ding hi.

**Grammar:** future: will

### v5
**EN:** Seeing his days are determined, the number of his months are with thee, thou hast appointed his bounds that he cannot pass;

**TDB77:** Ama nuntak tan ding kisehkhol a—a nuntak ding kha phazah kiciangtan khin hi. Nangmah in na ciangtan khin a, kikheel thei lo hi.

**ZVS:** A nuntak tan ding kisehkhol a, a nuntak kha phazah ding kiciangtan khin hi. Nangmah in na ciangtan khin a, kikheel thei nawnlo hi.

**Grammar:** completed: already done | negation: lo | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v6
**EN:** Turn from him, that he may rest, till he shall accomplish, as an hireling, his day.

**TDB77:** Amah en loin tuaci omsak mai in la, a nuntakna haksa mah zomsak hoh in.

**ZVS:** Amah enloin tuaci omsak mai in la, a nuntak haksapi mah zomzomsak hoh in.

### v7
**EN:** For there is hope of a tree, if it be cut down, that it will sprout again, and that the tender branch thereof will not cease.

**TDB77:** “A kiphuksa singkung nangawn in lametna nei a, a bul sel-in nungta kik thei hi.

**ZVS:** “A kiphuk khinsa singkung nangawn in lametna nei buang a, a bul sel kikin nungtakik thei hi.

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then) | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v8
**EN:** Though the root thereof wax old in the earth, and the stock thereof die in the ground;

**TDB77:** Lei sungah a zungte a teek zongin, lei sungah a bulsum a si zongin,

**ZVS:** Lei sungah a zungte teek a, a bulsum a si zongin,

### v9
**EN:** Yet through the scent of water it will bud, and bring forth boughs like a plant.

**TDB77:** guahtui ngahin, singkungno bangin hong sel kik hi.

**ZVS:** guahtui ngahin, singkungno-in hong selkik hi.

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v10
**EN:** But man dieth, and wasteth away: yea, man giveth up the ghost, and where is he?

**TDB77:** Ahi zongin, mi si a, amah maimang pah hi; amah a beina tungta a, koi-ah om nawn ahi hiam?

**ZVS:** Ahi zongin mi si a, amah maimang pah hi. Amah a beina tungta a, koi-ah om nawn ahi hiam?

**Grammar:** punctual: immediately/suddenly

### v11
**EN:** As the waters fail from the sea, and the flood decayeth and drieth up:

**TDB77:** Gun tuite leh tuipi tuite a kan hun om thei ding hi.

**ZVS:** “Gun tuite leh tuipi tuite a kan' hun om thei ding hi.

**Grammar:** future: will

### v12
**EN:** So man lieth down, and riseth not: till the heavens be no more, they shall not awake, nor be raised out of their sleep.

**TDB77:** Ahi zongin vanpi a om lai tengin, misite tho kik ngei lo ding a, a ihmutna panun khanglo ngei lo, tang ngei lo ding uh hi.

**ZVS:** Ahi zongin vanpi a omlaiteng, misite thokik ngeilo ding a, a ihmutna panun khanglo ngeilo, gamtang ngeilo ding uh hi.

**Grammar:** future: will | reciprocal: ki- prefix | plural: uh marker

### v14
**EN:** If a man die, shall he live again? all the days of my appointed time will I wait, till my change come.

**TDB77:** “Hih gimna tawh ka kipelh theihna dingin nong phawklohna ding mun misite' gam sungah kei hong busak in. Mi a si khinsate hingkik theilo ahih manin a hingin hong omsak peuh lecin tua lai-ah ka ut hi. Na hehna a dam ciangin hong phawkkik peuh lecin tua lai-ah ka ngak nuam hi.

**Grammar:** purpose: in order to | causal: because | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v15
**EN:** Thou shalt call, and I will answer thee: thou wilt have a desire to the work of thine hands.

**TDB77:** Tua ciangin kei na hong sam ding a, kong dawng ding hi; nangma bawlsa ahi keimah na hong lunggulh ding hi.

**ZVS:** Tua khit ciangin kei na hong sam ding a, ke'n kong dawng ding hi. Nangma bawlsa ahi keimah na hong lunggulh ding hi.

**Grammar:** sequence: then/therefore | future: will

### v16
**EN:** For now thou numberest my steps: dost thou not watch over my sin?

**TDB77:** Tua ciangin ka kalsuanna khempeuh nong en ding a, ahi zongin ka khialhnate nong sulzuih nawn kei ding hi.

**ZVS:** Tua hun ciangin ka kalsuanna khempeuh nong en ding a, ahi zongin ka khialhnate nong sulzuih nawn kei ding hi.

**Grammar:** sequence: then/therefore | future: will | negation: kei

### v17
**EN:** My transgression is sealed up in a bag, and thou sewest up mine iniquity.

**TDB77:** Ka khialhnate hong maisakin, nong paihkhiatsak ding hi; ka mawhna khempeuh nong phiat khiatsak ding hi. Tu-a bangin a piang thei hi leh ka ut mahmah hi.

**ZVS:** Ka khialhnate hong maisakin, nong paihkhiatsak ding a, ka mawhna khempeuh nong phiatkhiatsak ding hi. Tu-a bangin a piang thei hi leh ka ut mahmah hi.

**Grammar:** intensifier: very/greatly | future: will

### v18
**EN:** And surely the mountain falling cometh to nought, and the rock is removed out of his place.

**TDB77:** “Ahi zongin a tak takin ci lehang, mualte a puk ding hun, suangkeente nangawn a cip ding hun hong tung ding hi.

**ZVS:** “Ahi zongin a taktakin, mualte a puk ding hun suangkeente nangawn a cip ding hun hong tung ding hi.

**Grammar:** future: will

### v19
**EN:** The waters wear the stones: thou washest away the things which grow out of the dust of the earth; and thou destroyest the hope of man.

**TDB77:** Tui in suangte nawt khia-in, guahtui in lei a nawt khiat bangin, nangmah in mihingte’ lametna na beisak hi.

**ZVS:** Tui in suangte nawtkhia-in, guahtui in lei a nawtkhiat bangin nangmah in mihingte' lametna na beisak hi.

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then)

### v20
**EN:** Thou prevailest for ever against him, and he passeth: thou changest his countenance, and sendest him away.

**TDB77:** Nangmah in mi khat zo gawpin amah bei hi; A meel kheksak gawpin na hawl mang hi.

**ZVS:** Nangmah in mi khat zogawpin na hawlkhia a, na sa mahmah ahih manin a si dingin a ha a li hi.

### v21
**EN:** His sons come to honour, and he knoweth it not; and they are brought low, but he perceiveth it not of them.

**TDB77:** A tapate in amah pahtawi dingin hong pai ta leh zong bangmah phawk zo nawn lo a, a tapate mindai ta leh zong bangmah phawk zo nawn lo hi.

**ZVS:** Amah a pahtawi dingin a tapate hong pai ta leh zong amah gimlua ahih manin bangmah phawkzo nawnlo a, a tapate mindai ta leh zong bangmah phawkzo nawnlo hi.

**Grammar:** purpose: in order to | negation: lo

### v22
**EN:** But his flesh upon him shall have pain, and his soul within him shall mourn.

**TDB77:** A pumpi sunga a natnate leh a lungsim gimna bek phawk in,” a ci hi.

**ZVS:** A pumpi sunga a natnate leh a lungsim gimnate bek a phawkzo lai hi,” a ci hi.

**Grammar:** quotative: said/saying
