## Phrases & Usage

- **tungah [person] ahi hi** = [person] was over [thing]
  - Usage: Used to denote administrative oversight or responsibility for a specific resource or group (e.g., over the olive trees, over the camels).

- **tapa [father] ahi** = son of [father]
  - Usage: Standard patronymic structure used for identification (e.g., Adlai' tapa Shafat).

- **kumpipa’ [resource] a kemte** = the keepers/overseers of the king's [resource]
  - Usage: Describes the officials responsible for managing the king's possessions.

- **a sih ciangin** = after [someone] died
  - Usage: Used to mark a chronological transition or succession of office.

- **a khe-in ding to in** = stood up on his feet
  - Usage: A formal action indicating the beginning of a significant speech or address.

- **ka thugen hong za un** = Hear my words
  - Usage: A formal imperative used to command attention at the start of a proclamation.

- **ka tapa dingin amah ka teel khin a** = I have chosen him to be my son
  - Usage: Used to state a divine selection based on a specific relationship (here, God choosing Solomon).

- **gamh-in na nusiat tawntung theih nadingun** = so that you may leave behind as an inheritance forever
  - Usage: Expresses the purpose of an action related to future generations and legacy.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X leh Y tungah Z ahi hi.**
  - Structure indicating dual responsibility or listing two categories under one overseer. (e.g., Oliv leh theiba kungte tungah Baalhanan ahi hi.)
  - Example: Oliv leh theiba kungte tungah Baalhanan ahi hi

- **Ahih hangin [Reason], [Result].**
  - Conjunction 'Ahih hangin' (However/But) introduces a contrasting clause, often citing divine intervention or restriction.
  - Example: Ahih hangin Pasian in kei kiangah, ‘...hong ci hi.

- **Bang hang hiam cih leh [Reason/Justification], [Action/Result].**
  - Used to introduce the rationale or divine basis for a preceding statement, often translated as 'for' or 'because'.
  - Example: bang hang hiam cih leh amah in makai-in Judah teel a...

- **Note in [Condition] theih nadingun [Action] zui-in zong un.**
  - A concluding exhortation structure: 'So that you may [Goal], follow [Commandments] and seek [God]'.
  - Example: note khit na tate uh tungah gamh-in na nusiat tawntung theih nadingun... zui-in zong un.
