## Phrases & Usage

- **kei sangin a lianzaw hi** = He is greater than I
  - Usage: Used to express precedence or superiority in rank/status, often used by John the Baptist regarding Jesus.

- **khat banah khat i ngah sawnsawn hi** = we received grace upon grace
  - Usage: Describes the continuous and abundant reception of grace.

- **hehpihna leh thuman thutak** = grace and truth
  - Usage: A common theological pairing, contrasting with the Law given by Moses.

- **Pasian hong kilangsak hi** = He has declared God
  - Usage: Used to describe the unique revelation of God through the Son.

- **gampa lakah a kiko aw ka hi hi** = I am the voice of one crying in the wilderness
  - Usage: A direct quotation/allusion to Isaiah's prophecy regarding John's role.

- **leitung’ mawhna a puakhia Pasian’ Tuuno** = The Lamb of God, which taketh away the sin of the world
  - Usage: The definitive title John gives to Jesus.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X in ama thu gen a, '...' ci-in ka genpa pen hihpa ahi hi**
  - A complex structure used to identify the speaker (John) and confirm that the person he spoke about (the one greater than him) is the one present.
  - Example: Johan in ama thu gen a, “Keima nungah hong pai hinapi-in keima suah ma-in amah omkhin ahih manin kei sangin amah lianzaw hi,” ci-in ka genpa pen hihpa ahi hi, ci-in a kiko hi.

- **X khut tungah kipia hi; ahih hangin Y khut tungah a hong piang hi.**
  - A contrastive structure using 'ahi hangin' (but/however) to show the difference between the Law (given through Moses) and Grace/Truth (coming through Jesus Christ).
  - Example: Thukhamna Moses khut tungah kipia hi; ahih hangin hehpihna leh thuman thutak in Zeisu Khazih khut tungah a hong piang hi.

- **Pasian kuamah in a mu ngei kei hi. [Subject] bek in Pasian hong kilangsak hi.**
  - A negative statement followed by an affirmative statement emphasizing the uniqueness of the revealer (the only begotten Son). The use of 'ngei' emphasizes 'ever'.
  - Example: Pasian kuamah in a mu ngei kei hi. Pa’ ang sungah a om Tapa khat bek in Pasian hong kilangsak hi.

- **X na hi hiam? ... ci-in a dot-te ciangin**
  - Repetitive interrogative structure used when the questioners (priests/Levites) repeatedly ask John to identify himself, followed by the action of asking.
  - Example: Tuate in zong, “Tua ahih leh, nang kua na hi hiam? Elijah na hi hiam?” ci-in a dot-te ciangin amah in, “Elijah ka hi kei hi,” a ci hi.
