# 1CH - Part 56

## Vocabulary with Context

### sawmnih-le-lina = twenty-four
**Context:** Referring to a specific number of people or items in a list.
**Example:** a sawmnih-le-lina Romamtiezer, leh a tapate leh a sanggamte, sawm-le-nih tung tu hi.

### tapate = sons
**Context:** Used repeatedly to denote descendants, often in genealogical lists.
**Example:** leh a tapate leh a sanggamte

### sanggamte = brethren/relatives
**Context:** Used alongside 'tapate' (sons) to include extended male relatives in the count.
**Example:** leh a tapate leh a sanggamte

### tung = total/amounting to
**Context:** Indicates the final count or total number.
**Example:** sawm-le-nih tung tu hi.

### Kongcingte = Porters/Gatekeepers
**Context:** The subject of the division being described in this section.
**Example:** Kongcingte a kiseh khennate in:

### kiseh khennate = divisions/allotments
**Context:** Refers to the organized distribution or assignment of duties/groups.
**Example:** Kongcingte a kiseh khennate in:

### behte = family/clan (plural)
**Context:** Used to denote a specific lineage group (Korah's family).
**Example:** Korah behte sung panin

### innkuan = household/family unit
**Context:** Used for lineage groups, specifically Asaph's household.
**Example:** Asaf innkuan sung panin

### tacil = firstborn
**Context:** Used to denote the first son in a sequence of numbered sons.
**Example:** Zekhariah a tacil

### nihna = second
**Context:** Used for ordinal numbering.
**Example:** Jediael a nihna

### thumna = third
**Context:** Used for ordinal numbering.
**Example:** Zebadiah a thumna

### lina = fourth
**Context:** Used for ordinal numbering.
**Example:** Jathniel a lina

### ngana = fifth
**Context:** Used for ordinal numbering.
**Example:** Elam a ngana

### gukna = sixth
**Context:** Used for ordinal numbering.
**Example:** Jehohanan a gukna

### sagihna = seventh
**Context:** Used for ordinal numbering.
**Example:** Elieho-enai a sagihna ahi hi.

### giatna = eighth
**Context:** Used for ordinal numbering.
**Example:** Peullethai a giatna ahi hi.

### bang hang hiam cih leh = for/because
**Context:** A common conjunction introducing a reason or explanation.
**Example:** bang hang hiam cih leh Pasian in Obededom thupha pia hi.

### thupha pia hi = blessed (him)
**Context:** Indicates divine favor bestowed upon Obed-edom.
**Example:** Pasian in Obededom thupha pia hi.

### upa pen = the eldest/chief one
**Context:** Refers to the most senior or principal son.
**Example:** Ama tapa upa pen Shemaiah sung panin

### ukte = rulers/leaders
**Context:** Describes the role of the descendants in their father's house.
**Example:** zong a beh sungah ukte ahi tapate suak hi

### siamna a nei mite = mighty men of valour/capable people
**Context:** Describes the quality of the descendants, implying strength and capability.
**Example:** bang hang hiam cih leh amaute in siamna lian a nei mite ahi uh hi.

### nasepna dingin = for the service/work
**Context:** Specifies the purpose of their capability (service in the temple/tabernacle).
**Example:** nasepna dingin siamna a nei mikician ahi uh hi

### mikician = able men/qualified individuals
**Context:** Emphasizes their fitness for the required service.
**Example:** siamna a nei mikician ahi uh hi

### ukpi = chief/head
**Context:** Designates a person appointed as the leader, even if not the firstborn.
**Example:** Shimri a ukpi

### bawl hi = made him/appointed him
**Context:** Indicates the father's action in appointing Shimri as chief.
**Example:** a pa in a ukpi-in amah bawl hi

### Innkuan pasal lutangte = Heads of the household/Chief men
**Context:** Refers to the leaders among the porters.
**Example:** Innkuan pasal lutangte kongcingte ahi uh hi

### ai saan uh = cast lots
**Context:** The action taken to determine assignments.
**Example:** amau’ kongpi ding ai saan uh hi.

### leilu lam-a dingin = for the south side
**Context:** Specifying a cardinal direction for the lot drawn.
**Example:** leilu lam-a dingin ama ai pha hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **Korah behte sung panin** = Of the family of Korah
  - Usage: Used to specify the lineage from which an individual or group originates in genealogical listings.

- **a tacil, a nihna, a thumna, ...** = the firstborn, the second, the third, ...
  - Usage: Standard construction for listing descendants sequentially by birth order.

- **siamna a nei mite** = mighty men of valour/capable ones
  - Usage: A descriptive phrase used to highlight the strength, skill, or qualification of the listed individuals.

- **a neu a lian a kibangin** = as well the small as the great
  - Usage: An idiom indicating that the process (casting lots) applied universally to all members, regardless of status or age.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X a [ordinal number] ahi hi.**
  - This pattern is used extensively to list individuals in order of birth or assignment, where X is the name and the ordinal number indicates their position (e.g., Zekhariah a tacil - Zechariah the firstborn).
  - Example: Jediael a nihna

- **X leh Y, Z tung hi.**
  - A structure used for summation, listing components (X and Y) and stating the total (Z).
  - Example: a tapate leh a sanggamte, sawm-le-nih tung tu hi.

- **bang hang hiam cih leh [reason].**
  - A causal clause structure meaning 'because' or 'for' (introducing the reason for a preceding statement).
  - Example: bang hang hiam cih leh Pasian in Obededom thupha pia hi.

- **X’ tapate in: Y, Z, ... ahi hi.**
  - A standard genealogical structure indicating the sons of a specific father (X) and listing them.
  - Example: Meshelemiah’ tapate in: Zekhariah a tacil, Jediael a nihna, ...
