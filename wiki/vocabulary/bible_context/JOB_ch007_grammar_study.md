## Verses

### v1
**EN:** Is there not an appointed time to man upon earth? are not his days also like the days of an hireling?

**TDB77:** “Leitungah mihing in nasep haksa nei a, ama hun in thacial nasempa’ aa bang hilo ahi hiam?

**ZVS:** “Leitungah mihing in nasep haksa nei a, ama hun pen nasempa-a' bang hilo ahi hiam?

### v2
**EN:** As a servant earnestly desireth the shadow, and as an hireling looketh for the reward of his work:

**TDB77:** Sila nasemte in niliim lunggulhin, a kicial nasempa in a thaman a ngak tawh kibang hilo ahi hiam?

**ZVS:** Sila nasemte in niliim lunggulhin nasempa in a thaman a ngak tawh kibang hilo ahi hiam?

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v3
**EN:** So am I made to possess months of vanity, and wearisome nights are appointed to me.

**TDB77:** A mawkna-ah kha tampi sung ka om a, zan thapai lungkhamna in keima tanh ahi hi.

**ZVS:** A mawkna-in kha tampi sung ka om a, zanthapai-in lungkhamna bek keima tanh ahi hi.

### v4
**EN:** When I lie down, I say, When shall I arise, and the night be gone? and I am full of tossings to and fro unto the dawning of the day.

**TDB77:** Nitak ka lup ciangin, ‘Cik ciangin khuavak in ka tho tam,’ ka ci hi. Ahi zongin zan sau a, zingsang khuavak mateng ka kigawigawi hi.

**ZVS:** Nitak ka lup ciangin, ‘Cik ciangin khua hong vakin zing ka tho tam,’ ka ci hi. Ahi zongin zan sau a, zingsang khuavak mateng ka kigawigawi hi.

**Grammar:** quotative: said/saying | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v5
**EN:** My flesh is clothed with worms and clods of dust; my skin is broken, and become loathsome.

**TDB77:** Ka pumpi khempeuh than dim a, ka vun tung khempeuh meima in tuamcip hi.

**ZVS:** Ka pumpi tung khempeuhah than bekbek kidim a, ka vun tung khempeuh meima in tuamcip hi.

### v6
**EN:** My days are swifter than a weaver's shuttle, and are spent without hope.

**TDB77:** Ka hunte in siamgante’ siam ciangtang sangin manlang zaw a, lametna om loin a mong tung pak hi.

**ZVS:** Ka hunte siamgante' siam ciangtang sangin manlang zaw a, lametna omloin a mong tung pak hi.

### v7
**EN:** O remember that my life is wind: mine eye shall no more see good.

**TDB77:** “Pasian aw, ka nuntakna in husan’ bang bek ahihna hong phawk in; ka mit in hun nuam mu kik ngei nawn lo ding hi.

**ZVS:** “Pasian aw, ka nuntakna husan' bang bek ahihna hong phawk in. Ka mit in hun nuam mukik ngei nawnlo ding hi.

**Grammar:** future: will | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v8
**EN:** The eye of him that hath seen me shall see me no more: thine eyes are upon me, and I am not.

**TDB77:** Kei hong mu ngeipa in kei hong mu nawn lo ding a, na hong et laitak mahin ka mangthang ding hi.

**ZVS:** Kei hong mu ngeipa in kei hong mukik nawnlo ding a, na hong et laitak mahin ka mangthang ding hi.

**Grammar:** future: will

### v9
**EN:** As the cloud is consumed and vanisheth away: so he that goeth down to the grave shall come up no more.

**TDB77:** Meiilum kaite bei-in a man’than’ bangin Leinuai-a paisukpa hong ciahkik ngei nawn lo ding hi.

**ZVS:** Meiilum kaite bei-in a man'than' bangin Leinuai-a paisukpa mangthang ding hi.

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then) | future: will

### v10
**EN:** He shall return no more to his house, neither shall his place know him any more.

**TDB77:** A inn-ah amah hong ciahkik nawn lo ding a, ama omna ngei mun in amah thei nawn lo ding hi.

**ZVS:** Ama inn-ah hong ciahkik nawnlo ding a, ama omna ngei mun in amah thei nawnlo ding hi.

**Grammar:** future: will

### v11
**EN:** Therefore I will not refrain my mouth; I will speak in the anguish of my spirit; I will complain in the bitterness of my soul.

**TDB77:** “Tua ahih manin pau loin ka om kei ding hi; ka lungkham manin kam ka pau ding a, ka gim luat manin ka phun ding hi.

**ZVS:** Tua ahih manin pauloin ka om kei ding hi. Ka lungkham manin kam ka pau ding a, ka gimluat manin ka phun ding hi.

**Grammar:** future: will | negation: kei | causal: because

### v12
**EN:** Am I a sea, or a whale, that thou settest a watch over me?

**TDB77:** Kei hong cing ding na koih nadingin, kei tuipi ganhing hong sa na hi hiam?

**ZVS:** “Kei a hong cing ding na koihna dingin kei, tuipi ganhing hong sa na hi hiam?

**Grammar:** purpose: in order to

### v13
**EN:** When I say, My bed shall comfort me, my couch shall ease my complaint;

**TDB77:** Lum leng nuam ding a, ihmu leng ka phunna phatuam ding hi, ci leng lah,

**ZVS:** Lum leng nuam ding a, ihmu leng phatuam ding hi, ci leng lah,

**Grammar:** future: will

### v14
**EN:** Then thou scarest me with dreams, and terrifiest me through visions:

**TDB77:** a lauhuai mang nong man’sak a, a lauhuai mangmuhna tawh nong lau sak hi.

**ZVS:** a lauhuai mang nong man'sak a, a lauhuai maangmuhna tawh nong lausak hi.

### v15
**EN:** So that my soul chooseth strangling, and death rather than my life.

**TDB77:** Tua ahih manin gui-awk ding ka ut zaw a, hih bang ka thuak sangin ka si nuam zaw hi.

**ZVS:** Tua ahih manin gui-awk ding ka utzaw a, hih bang ka thuak sangin ka si nuamzaw hi.

**Grammar:** causal: because

### v16
**EN:** I loathe it; I would not live alway: let me alone; for my days are vanity.

**TDB77:** Ka lungkham a, ka nungta nuam nawn kei hi. Ka nuntakna in husan’ bang hi lel ahih manin kei guak hong hici om sak hoh in, kimanna om lo hi.

**ZVS:** Ka lungkham a, ka nungta nuam nawn kei hi. Ka nuntakna pen husan' bang hi lel ahih manin hong hici omsak hoh in.

**Grammar:** negation: kei | negation: lo | causal: because | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v17
**EN:** What is man, that thou shouldest magnify him? and that thou shouldest set thine heart upon him?

**TDB77:** Nang in hih zahin na thupi ngaihsut dingin na lungsimah na ngaihsut dingin,

**ZVS:** “Nang in hih zahin na thupi ngaihsut dingin na lungsimah na ngaihsut dingin

**Grammar:** purpose: in order to

### v18
**EN:** And that thou shouldest visit him every morning, and try him every moment?

**TDB77:** zingsang simin daantat dingin ama kiangah pai-in amah na ze-et themthum nadingin mihing in bang hi lel ahi hiam?

**ZVS:** zingsang simin gim a pia dingin ama kiangah pai-in amah na ze-et themthumna dingin mihing bang hi lel ahi hiam?

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then) | purpose: in order to | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v19
**EN:** How long wilt thou not depart from me, nor let me alone till I swallow down my spittle?

**TDB77:** Tom kha sung beek hong pelh lo ding a, ka hu beek hong sang sak man lo ding na hi hiam?

**ZVS:** Tomkha sung beek hong pelhlo ding a, ka hu beek hong sangsak manlo ding na hi hiam?

### v20
**EN:** I have sinned; what shall I do unto thee, O thou preserver of men? why hast thou set me as a mark against thee, so that I am a burden to myself?

**TDB77:** Mihingte a encikpa aw, a khial ka hih leh zong, nang tungah bang hong gamta ka hi hiam? Bang hangin kei hong ngim a, bang hangin na muitum suak ka hi hiam?

**ZVS:** Mihingte a encikpa aw, a khial ka hih leh zong nangma tungah bang gamta khial ka hi hiam? Bang hangin kei hong ngim a, bang hangin nangma muitum suak zenzen ka hi hiam?

### v21
**EN:** And why dost thou not pardon my transgression, and take away mine iniquity? for now shall I sleep in the dust; and thou shalt seek me in the morning, but I shall not be.

**TDB77:** Bang hangin ka khialhna hong maisak lo a, ka mawhna hong paihkhiat sak lo na hi hiam? Tu-in han sungah ka lum ding a, nong zon’ hangin ka na om kei ding hi,” a ci hi.

**ZVS:** Bang hangin ka khialhna hong maisaklo a, ka mawhna hong maisaklo na hi hiam? Tu-in han sungah ka lum ding a, nong zon'zon hangin ka na om nawn kei ding hi,” a ci hi.
