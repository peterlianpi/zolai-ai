## Verses

### v1
**EN:** So the king and Haman came to banquet with Esther the queen.

**TDB77:** Tua ahih ciangin Kumpinu Esther tawh an ne dingin kumpipa leh Haman va pai-te hi.

**ZVS:** Tua ahih ciangin Kumpinu Esther tawh an a ne dingin kumpipa leh Haman va pai-te hi.

**Grammar:** sequence: then/therefore | purpose: in order to | plural: uh marker

### v2
**EN:** And the king said again unto Esther on the second day at the banquet of wine, What is thy petition, queen Esther? and it shall be granted thee: and what is thy request? and it shall be performed, even to the half of the kingdom.

**TDB77:** A ni nih ni, amaute in zu a nek lai takun kumpipa in Esther’ kiangah, “Kumpinu Esther aw, nong nget nop bang ahi hiam? Nong nget peuhpeuh ka kumpi gam phel lang khat dongin hong kipia ding hi,” ci kik leuleu hi.

**ZVS:** A ni nih ni, amaute in zu a nek laitakun kumpipa in Esther kiangah, “Kumpinu Esther aw, nong ngetnop bang ahi hiam? Nong nget khempeuh ka kumpi gam phelkhat dongin hong kipia ding hi,” ci kik leuleu hi.

**Grammar:** future: will | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v3
**EN:** Then Esther the queen answered and said, If I have found favour in thy sight, O king, and if it please the king, let my life be given me at my petition, and my people at my request:

**TDB77:** Tua ciangin Kumpinu Esther in dawngin, “Kumpipa in nong hehpih zawh a, kumpipa in hoih nasak leh, kong nget kong thum nopna thu in keimah leh ka mite sihna tawh nong kipelhsak nading ahi hi.

**ZVS:** Tua ciangin Kumpinu Esther in dawngin, “Kumpipa in nong hehpih-zawh a, kumpipa in hoih na sak leh, kong nget kong thumnop thu pen keimah leh ka mipihte sihna tawh nong kipelhsakna ding ahi hi.

**Grammar:** sequence: then/therefore | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v4
**EN:** For we are sold, I and my people, to be destroyed, to be slain, and to perish. But if we had been sold for bondmen and bondwomen, I had held my tongue, although the enemy could not countervail the king's damage.

**TDB77:** Bang hang hiam cih leh kei leh ka mite suksiat ding, thah ding, beisak mang dingin hong kizuak hi. Kote, numei pasal a kibangin sal dingin hong kizuak hi leh bangmah ka pau kei ding hi; tua bang lel tawh kumpipa nawngkaisak taak lo hi,” a ci hi.

**ZVS:** Bang hang hiam cih leh kei leh ka mite suksiat ding, thah ding, beisakmang dingin hong kizuak hi. Kote, numei pasal a kibangin sal dingin hong kizuak hi leh bangmah ka pau kei ding hi; tua bang lel tawh kumpipa nawngkaisak taaklo hi,” a ci hi.

**Grammar:** quotative: said/saying | purpose: in order to | future: will | negation: kei | negation: lo | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v6
**EN:** And Esther said, The adversary and enemy is this wicked Haman. Then Haman was afraid before the king and the queen.

**TDB77:** Tua ciangin Esther in, “Hong langpang ka galpa! Hih mi gilo Haman!” a ci hi. Tua lai takin kumpipa leh kumpinu’ mai-ah cih nading thei loin Haman om hi.

**ZVS:** Tua ciangin Esther in, “Hong langpang ka galpa! Hih migilo Haman!” a ci hi. Tua laitakin kumpipa leh kumpinu' mai-ah cihna ding theiloin Haman a om hi.

**Grammar:** quotative: said/saying | sequence: then/therefore

### v7
**EN:** And the king arising from the banquet of wine in his wrath went into the palace garden: and Haman stood up to make request for his life to Esther the queen; for he saw that there was evil determined against him by the king.

**TDB77:** Tua ciangin kumpipa hehsa-in an nekna pan ding khia-in kumpi huan sungah pai hi. Ahi zongin Haman ahih leh tua lai mah-ah omin Kumpinu Esther in a nuntakna a suahtaksak nading thuum hi; bang hang hiam cih leh kumpipa in amah thah dingin a ngaihsutna lungsim thei hi.

**ZVS:** Tua ciangin kumpipa a hehsa-in annekna pan dingkhia-in kumpi huan sungah a pai hi. Ahi zongin Haman ahih leh tua lai mahah omin Kumpinu Esther in a nuntakna a suahtaksakna ding a thum hi; bang hang hiam cih leh kumpipa in amah thah dingin a ngaihsutna a lungsim a thei hi.

**Grammar:** sequence: then/therefore | serial verb: -in (and then) | purpose: in order to

### v8
**EN:** Then the king returned out of the palace garden into the place of the banquet of wine; and Haman was fallen upon the bed whereon Esther was. Then said the king, Will he force the queen also before me in the house? As the word went out of the king's mouth, they covered Haman's face.

**TDB77:** Kumpi huan panin a annekna mun uhah kumpipa hong paikik ciangin Esther’ tutna lamah Haman na awn phei-a om mu a, kumpipa in, “Ka inn sung ka muhna nangawn-ah hih mipa in kumpinu hih ding ahi hiam?” ci hi. Kumpipa in tua thu a cih phetin amaute in Haman’ mai puan khuh-te hi.

**ZVS:** Kumpi huan panin a annekna mun uhah kumpipa hong paikik ciangin Esther' tutna lamah Haman na awn phei-in a om mu a, kumpipa in, “Ka innsung ka muhna nangawnah hih mipa in kumpinu hih ding ahi hiam?” ci hi. Kumpipa in tua thu a cih phetin amaute in Haman' mai puan a khuh-te hi.

**Grammar:** quotative: said/saying | plural: uh marker

### v9
**EN:** And Harbona, one of the chamberlains, said before the king, Behold also, the gallows fifty cubits high, which Haman had made for Mordecai, who had spoken good for the king, standeth in the house of Haman. Then the king said, Hang him thereon.

**TDB77:** Tua ciangin kumpipa’ na a sem innsung mangte lakah Harbona in, “Ni dangin kumpipa’ na hoih takin a sem ngei Mordekai thah nadingin Haman mahmah in a phuhsak, pi sawmsagih-le-nga a sang khuam pen Haman’ innah tu mah-in om hi,” a ci hi.

**ZVS:** Tua ciangin kumpipa' na a sem innsungmangte lakah Harbona in, “Nidangin kumpipa' na hoihtakin a sem ngei Mordekai thahna dingin Haman mahmah in a phuhsak, pi sawmsagihnga a sang khuam pen Haman' inn-ah tumah-in om hi,” a ci hi.

**Grammar:** quotative: said/saying | sequence: then/therefore | intensifier: very/greatly | serial verb: -in (and then) | purpose: in order to
