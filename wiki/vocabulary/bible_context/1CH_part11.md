# 1CH - Part 11

## Vocabulary with Context

### tapa = son
**Context:** Used to denote male offspring, often in genealogies.
**Example:** Ezrah' tapa lite: Jether, Mered, Efer, leh Jalon ahi uh hi.

### lite = sons (plural)
**Context:** Plural form of 'tapa' (son).
**Example:** Ezrah' tapa lite: Jether, Mered, Efer, leh Jalon ahi uh hi.

### ahi uh hi = they were
**Context:** A common concluding phrase in lists or statements of identity/being.
**Example:** Jether, Mered, Efer, leh Jalon ahi uh hi.

### tanu = daughter
**Context:** Used to denote female offspring.
**Example:** Mered in Faro' tanu Bithiah ten'pih a, tanu Miriam leh tapa Shammai leh Ishbah nei hi.

### ten'pih = took (as wife/married)
**Context:** Indicates taking a woman in marriage.
**Example:** Mered in Faro' tanu Bithiah ten'pih a, tanu Miriam leh tapa Shammai leh Ishbah nei hi.

### nei hi = had/begot
**Context:** Used for having children.
**Example:** tanu Miriam leh tapa Shammai leh Ishbah nei hi.

### khuapi = city/town
**Context:** Refers to a specific settlement or place of residence.
**Example:** Ishbah pen Eshtemoa khuapi a satpa ahi hi.

### satpa = dweller/resident
**Context:** One who lives in a particular place (khuapi).
**Example:** Ishbah pen Eshtemoa khuapi a satpa ahi hi.

### numei = woman/female
**Context:** General term for woman.
**Example:** Mered in Judah minam numei khat zong ten'pih a...

### minam = tribe/people/family
**Context:** Refers to a lineage or group of people.
**Example:** Judah minam numei khat zong ten'pih a...

### sanggamnu = sister (of a man)
**Context:** Used for a female sibling.
**Example:** Hodiah in Naham’ sanggamnu ten’pih a...

### teeng = dwell/reside
**Context:** Verb indicating habitation.
**Example:** amaute’ suante in Keilah khua-ah a teeng Garm mite...

### suante = descendants/offspring (plural)
**Context:** Plural of 'tapa' or used generally for descendants.
**Example:** amau’ suante in Keilah khua-ah a teeng Garm mite...

### tuanthu lui pekpek = ancient things/old matters
**Context:** Refers to historical or very old records/events.
**Example:** (Hih pen tuanthu lui pekpek ahi hi.)

### kumpipa = king
**Context:** Ruler or monarch.
**Example:** ama na a sem dingin kumpipa tawh tua khuate ah teeng khawm uh hi.

### sem = work/do
**Context:** Used here in the context of performing a duty or service.
**Example:** Ama na a sem dingin kumpipa tawh...

### uk hun mateng = until the reign of
**Context:** Specifies a time period ending with a ruler's time in power.
**Example:** Hihte in David’ uk hun mateng amaute' khuapite ahi hi.

### pung lo uh hi = did not multiply/increase
**Context:** Used in comparison to show a lack of population growth.
**Example:** Judah minamte a pun' bangin Simeon minamte punglo uh hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **a khuapi a satpa** = the dweller of the city of
  - Usage: Used in genealogies to specify the location of residence for an individual or family line.

- **ten'pih a** = having taken (as wife)
  - Usage: Connects the act of marriage to the resulting offspring.

- **tua tawh** = with that/by means of that
  - Usage: Indicates consequence or accompaniment.

- **a na a sem dingin** = in order to do their work
  - Usage: Expresses purpose or reason for an action (dwelling in a place).

- **David’ uk hun mateng** = until the reign of David
  - Usage: Defines a historical duration tied to a king's rule.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X' tapa/suante: Y, Z, leh A ahi uh hi.**
  - Standard structure for listing the sons/descendants of an individual (X). 'ahi uh hi' concludes the list.
  - Example: Shimon' tapate: Amnon, Rinnah, Benhanan, leh Tilon ahi uh hi.

- **A tapa B, a tapa C ahi uh hi.**
  - Chain of descent showing direct lineage: A's son is B, B's son is C.
  - Example: Shaul' tapa Shallum, a tapa Mibsam, a tapa Mishma ahi uh hi.

- **X in Y ten'pih a, Z nei hi.**
  - Structure showing marriage (X took Y) resulting in offspring (Z).
  - Example: Mered in Faro' tanu Bithiah ten'pih a, tanu Miriam leh tapa Shammai leh Ishbah nei hi.

- **Hihte pen X hi a, Y khua-ah a tengte ahi uh hi.**
  - Identification followed by location: 'These are X, and they are the dwellers in place Y.'
  - Example: Hihte pen beelsekte hi a, Netaim khua leh Gederah khua-ah a tengte ahi uh hi.
