# 1CH - Part 32

## Vocabulary with Context

### khuami = of the place/town
**Context:** Used to denote origin or belonging to a specific place (e.g., Ashterath khuami = of Ashterath).
**Example:** Ashterath khuami Uzzia

### tapate = sons of
**Context:** Indicates lineage, 'sons of' (plural form implied by context, though singular 'tapa' might also be used).
**Example:** Hotham’ tapate Shama leh Jeiel

### sanggampa = leader/chief/captain
**Context:** Used to describe Joha's position among the people of Tiz.
**Example:** Tiz khuami a sanggampa Joha

### mi = person/people of
**Context:** Used to denote nationality or origin, similar to 'khuami' but often for larger groups/nations (e.g., Moab mi = Moabite).
**Example:** Moab mi Ithmah

### ahi-te hi = they were
**Context:** A concluding statement identifying the group being listed.
**Example:** Jaasiel ahi-te hi.

### Tu-in = Now/At that time
**Context:** A temporal marker introducing a new section or context.
**Example:** Tu-in Kish’ tapa Saul’ hangin...

### hangin = because of/on account of
**Context:** Indicates the reason or cause for an action or state.
**Example:** Saul’ hangin a ut utin...

### a ut utin = while he yet/still
**Context:** Indicates a continuous state or action in the past.
**Example:** a ut utin David a tat theihloh laitakin

### tat theih loh = could not hide/keep close
**Context:** Describes David's inability to remain concealed.
**Example:** a ut utin David a tat theihloh laitakin

### laitakin = while/at the time when
**Context:** Temporal conjunction indicating simultaneity.
**Example:** a tat theihloh laitakin

### hong paite in = those who came to
**Context:** Describes the action of people arriving at David's location.
**Example:** Ziklag-a ama kiangah mite hong pai-in

### pawlpih-te hi = they joined/followed him
**Context:** Indicates allegiance or joining a group (Tedim2010 version).
**Example:** amah pawlpih-te hi.

### gal sungah = in the war/battle
**Context:** Refers to military conflict.
**Example:** Galsungah amah a huh a thahatte lakah

### huh a thahatte = helpers and smiters/killers
**Context:** Describes those who aided in battle (helpers and fighters).
**Example:** amah a huh a thahatte lakah

### kihelte = those who protected/guarded themselves
**Context:** Implies those who were saved or who stood firm among the fighters.
**Example:** amaute a kihelte ahi-te hi.

### thalthawite = archers/bowmen
**Context:** Describes the type of warriors they were (armed with bows).
**Example:** Amaute pen thal tawite hi-te a

### tak lam khut = right hand
**Context:** Refers to physical dexterity.
**Example:** taklam khut tawh ahi a

### vei lam khut = left hand
**Context:** Refers to physical dexterity.
**Example:** veilam khut tawh ahi zongin

### lawn thei-te hi = could handle/use
**Context:** Indicates skill or ability.
**Example:** thaltang leh suangte lawn thei-te hi.

### behte = brethren/relatives
**Context:** Refers to Saul's kinsmen from Benjamin.
**Example:** Saul’ behte Benjamin mi ahi-te hi.

### ukpi = chief/leader
**Context:** A title for a commander.
**Example:** A ukpi Ahiezer hi a

### gel = group/company (often used as a suffix for 'their group')
**Context:** Used to refer to the specific company or group associated with the preceding name.
**Example:** Amaute gel pen Gibeah khuami...

### nuai-ah galkapte = under them were soldiers/warriors
**Context:** Indicates subordinates or those under the command of the preceding leaders (Tedim2010 specific).
**Example:** Amau gel nuai-ah galkapte:

### mihing sawmthumte lakah a thahatpa = the mighty man among the thirty men
**Context:** A specific military rank or designation.
**Example:** mihing sawmthumte lakah a thahatpa

### makaipa = leader/captain (over a specific number)
**Context:** Indicates leadership over a contingent of thirty.
**Example:** leh sawmthumte a makaipa

### gamlak = wilderness/open country
**Context:** Refers to the area outside settled places, where David was hiding.
**Example:** Gad minam sung panin gamlaka

### munmuan'huai = stronghold/safe place
**Context:** A fortified or secure location.
**Example:** gamlaka munmuan'huai-a

### galhangte = warriors/fighters
**Context:** General term for soldiers.
**Example:** a thahat a siam galhangte

### lum leh teipi zat a siam = skilled in using shield and spear/javelin
**Context:** Describes their military equipment proficiency.
**Example:** lum leh tei zat a siam

### humpinelkai maitang = faces like lions' faces
**Context:** A simile describing their fierce appearance.
**Example:** a maitang-te humpinelkai maitang bangin

### sakhi bangin = like the roes/gazelles
**Context:** A simile describing their swiftness.
**Example:** mualtunga om sakhi bangin

### manlang mi = swift men/fast runners
**Context:** Describes their speed.
**Example:** a manlang mi hong paite

### a nihna = the second
**Context:** Ordinal number used to list leaders.
**Example:** a nihna Obadiah

### a gukna = the sixth
**Context:** Ordinal number used to list leaders.
**Example:** a gukna Attai

## Phrases & Usage

- **Ashterath khuami Uzzia** = Uzzia of Ashterath
  - Usage: Used to identify a person by their place of origin (common in genealogies).

- **a ut utin a tat theih loh lai takin** = while he yet could not keep himself close
  - Usage: Describes a continuous state of hiding or concealment in the past.

- **a maitang-te humpinelkai maitang bangin** = whose faces were like the faces of lions
  - Usage: A simile used for vivid description, emphasizing fierceness.

- **mual tunga om sakhi bangin** = as swift as the roes upon the mountains
  - Usage: A simile used to describe great speed and agility.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X khuami Y**
  - Possessive/Locative structure: Y of X (X is the place, Y is the person).
  - Example: Ashterath khuami Uzzia

- **A tapa B leh C**
  - Genealogical structure: B and C, sons of A.
  - Example: Hotham’ tapate Shama leh Jeiel

- **X ahi-te hi**
  - Copular sentence structure indicating identity or state for a plural subject (They are X).
  - Example: Jaasiel ahi-te hi.

- **a [ordinal number] [Name]**
  - Used for sequential listing of individuals, often leaders or members of a group.
  - Example: a nihna Obadiah, a thumna Eliab
