## Phrases & Usage

- **lungsim khempeuh leh utna lungsim tawh** = with all your heart and with a willing mind
  - Usage: Used to express complete, willing devotion and effort in service to God.

- **bang hang hiam cih leh** = for/because
  - Usage: Introduces the reason or justification for the preceding command or statement.

- **amah na zon’ leh amah na mu ding hi** = if you seek him, you will find him
  - Usage: A conditional promise regarding the relationship between seeking God and finding Him.

- **Thakhauhin hang in la, sem in** = Be strong and do it
  - Usage: A direct encouragement to proceed with courage and action.

- **Topa Pasian, kei’ Pasian nangawn nang tawh hong om hi** = The LORD God, even my God, is with you
  - Usage: A powerful statement of assurance that God's presence accompanies the speaker/listener.

## Grammar Patterns

- **Verb + in (Imperative)**
  - Used frequently to give direct commands or strong exhortations (e.g., thei in, sem in, kingaihsun in).
  - Example: thei in la, na sem in

- **Amah na zon’ leh amah na mu ding hi (Conditional Structure)**
  - A standard conditional structure: If X happens, then Y will result. (Zon' = seek; mu ding hi = will find).
  - Example: Nangmah in amah na zon’ leh nangmah in amah na mu ding hi

- **Noun + khempeuh**
  - The suffix 'khempeuh' functions as 'all' or 'entirety' when attached to a noun, emphasizing totality (e.g., lungsim khempeuh = whole heart; nasepna khempeuh = all the work).
  - Example: na lungsim khempeuh leh utna lungsim tawh

- **Noun + adingin**
  - The suffix '-adingin' means 'for the purpose of' or 'for' a specific noun, often indicating dedication or allocation.
  - Example: Ompihna Anlum-a' dingin sabuai kham a gihna ciat

- **Noun + tawh**
  - The postposition 'tawh' means 'with' or 'by means of', indicating accompaniment or instrument.
  - Example: lungsim khempeuh leh utna lungsim tawh ama na sem in
