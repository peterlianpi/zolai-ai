# 1CH - Part 42

## Vocabulary with Context

### nasempa = servant
**Context:** Used to refer to David, indicating his role as God's servant.
**Example:** ka nasempa David kiangah gen in.

### ci = say/speak
**Context:** Used to introduce direct speech or a message from God.
**Example:** Topa in hih bangin ci hi

### teen = dwell/reside
**Context:** Refers to a place of dwelling or habitation (in TDB77, 'teen' is used; Tedim2010 uses 'ten').
**Example:** Nangmah in teen’ nadingin kei adingin inn na lam kei ding hi.

### lam = build/make
**Context:** Used in the context of building a house or temple.
**Example:** kei adingin inn na lam kei ding hi.

### puanbuk = tent/tabernacle
**Context:** Refers to the temporary dwelling place used before the permanent temple was built.
**Example:** puanbuk khat pan puanbuk khat

### omna = dwelling place/station
**Context:** Used alongside 'puanbuk' to describe moving from one temporary location to another.
**Example:** omna khat pan omna khatah ka pai kawikawi hi.

### kawikawi = move around/wander
**Context:** Describes the continuous movement of the dwelling place.
**Example:** omna khat pan omna khatah ka pai kawikawi hi.

### cing = feed/tend (as a shepherd)
**Context:** Used in the context of commanding someone to care for God's people.
**Example:** keima mite tuu bangin a cing dingin ka sawl thukhente

### thukhen = judge/ruler
**Context:** Refers to the leaders of Israel whom God commanded to care for the people.
**Example:** ka sawl thukhente khatpeuh peuh tungah

### lamsak lo = fail to build/neglect to make
**Context:** A negative phrase indicating the failure to build a permanent house for God.
**Example:** Bang hangin lamsak lo na hi hiam?”

### Vanglianpen = Most High/Almighty
**Context:** A title used for God.
**Example:** Vanglianpen’ Topa in hih bangin ci hi

### ulian = great/honorable
**Context:** Describes the status God intended for Israel.
**Example:** Ka mi Israel tungah ulian a hi dingin lonona

### lonona = to be a leader/to rule
**Context:** Relates to the purpose of God's presence with Israel (to lead them).
**Example:** ulian a hi dingin lonona

### susia = cut off/destroy
**Context:** Refers to God destroying David's enemies.
**Example:** na galte khempeuh ka susia hi

### miliante = great men/mighty ones
**Context:** Refers to famous or powerful people on earth, whose fame David's name will equal.
**Example:** leitung miliante khat min bangin

### kipsak = establish/make firm
**Context:** Used for establishing David's kingdom and throne.
**Example:** ama kumpi tokhom ka kipsak paisuak ding hi.

### khangsak = raise up/cause to stand up
**Context:** Refers to raising up David's successor (son).
**Example:** nangma nungah na tapate khat ka khangsak ding a

### tokhom = throne
**Context:** The seat of royal authority.
**Example:** ama kumpi tokhom ka kipsak paisuak ding hi.

### lak khiat = take away/withdraw
**Context:** Refers to God withdrawing His mercy, as He did from Saul.
**Example:** Saul tung panin ka lak khiat bangin

### maangmuhna = vision
**Context:** Refers to the divine revelation Nathan delivered to David.
**Example:** hih maangmuhna khempeuh tawh kizui-in

### tun’ pih = bring/lead to this point
**Context:** David acknowledging God's faithfulness in bringing him to this stage.
**Example:** nangmah in hih ciang dong kei nong tun’ pih nadingin

### suanlehkhak = posterity/descendants
**Context:** Refers to David's lineage and future generations.
**Example:** nasempa’ suan leh khak tawh kisai thu

### pahtawina = honor/glory
**Context:** David reflecting on the honor God has bestowed upon him.
**Example:** Na nasempa nong pahtawina ah

## Phrases & Usage

- **Pai in la** = Go and
  - Usage: Used as an imperative command to initiate an action, often preceding a message delivery.

- **inn sungah ka teeng kei a** = I have not dwelt in a house
  - Usage: Describes a state of non-residence in a permanent structure, emphasizing God's itinerant presence with Israel.

- **omna khat pan omna khatah ka pai kawikawi hi** = I have gone from one station to another
  - Usage: Idiomatic expression showing continuous movement between temporary locations (tents/tabernacles).

- **min khat kong bawlsak ding hi** = I will make you a name
  - Usage: A promise of establishing great renown or fame for the recipient.

- **na nuntakna bei-in na pate kiang na zuat ciangin** = When your days are expired and you go to be with your fathers
  - Usage: A formal way of saying 'when you die' (referring to David).

- **ka inn sung leh ka kumpi gam sungah** = In my house and in my kingdom
  - Usage: Refers to the spiritual (temple) and temporal (kingdom) spheres where the successor will be established.

- **Topa Pasian aw** = O Lord God
  - Usage: A direct, reverent address to God in prayer or supplication.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X in hih bangin ci hi**
  - Structure used to quote God's direct speech ('Thus says X').
  - Example: Topa in hih bangin ci hi

- **Bang hang hiam cih leh Y**
  - A rhetorical structure meaning 'Why is it that Y?' or 'For what reason Y?'
  - Example: Bang hang hiam cih leh Israelte ka paipih toh a...

- **Nangmah in X a hi dingin Y kong bawlsak ding hi**
  - Future promise structure: 'You (Nangmah) will cause Y to be X.' (God establishing David's name to be like great men).
  - Example: leitung miliante khat min bangin nang adingin min khat kong bawlsak ding hi.

- **X tungah Y na gen khin a, Z na lak khin hi**
  - Compound sentence structure showing past actions completed by God: 'You have already spoken X concerning Y, and you have already taken Z.'
  - Example: nangmah in a hong tung ding hunpi khat sung tangding na nasempa’ suan leh khak tawh kisai thu zong na gen khin a, mai lam a, khang dingte thu kei nong lak khin hi
