# PSA - Part 1

## Vocabulary with Context

### nuamsa = blessed, happy
**Context:** Used to describe the state of the person who avoids the ways of the ungodly.
**Example:** Mi nuamsa i cih in...

### migilote = ungodly people
**Context:** The ungodly; plural form of 'migiloh'.
**Example:** migilote’ thu a zui lo mi

### zui = to follow, to pursue
**Context:** Used in the negative to mean 'not following' or 'not heeding'.
**Example:** thu a zui lo mi

### mimawhte = sinners, wicked ones
**Context:** Refers to those who commit sin or act wickedly (similar to 'sinners').
**Example:** mimawhte’ lampi-a ding lo mi

### lampi = way, path, road
**Context:** Used metaphorically for the course of action or lifestyle.
**Example:** mimawhte’ lampi-a ding lo mi

### thusim lote = scornful ones, mockers
**Context:** Those who mock or scorn; related to 'scornful' in KJV.
**Example:** mi a thusim lote’ phual-a tu lo mi

### phual = seat, place
**Context:** Used metaphorically for the position or company of the scornful.
**Example:** phual-a tu lo mi

### nopsakna = delight, pleasure
**Context:** What the blessed person finds joy in (the law of the Lord).
**Example:** ama nopsakna in Topa’ thu sung bek mah hi

### ngaihsunsun = to meditate upon, to ponder
**Context:** Describes continuous reflection on God's word, day and night.
**Example:** sun leh zanin ama thu a ngaihsunsun mi ahi hi.

### kisuan = to be planted, to be set
**Context:** Describes the tree being established near water sources.
**Example:** luitui luang gei-a kisuan

### gah hun = season for fruit, proper time
**Context:** Refers to the appointed time when fruit is produced.
**Example:** ama gah hunin gah a ### vul = to wither, to dry up
**Context:** Used in the negative ('a teh a vul lo') meaning the leaf will not wither.
**Example:** a teh a vul lo singkung tawh kibang hi.

### pai = to prosper, to succeed, to go well
**Context:** What happens to everything the righteous person does.
**Example:** Ahihna khempeuh ah a ma pai hi.

### huit = wind
**Context:** Used in comparison to the ungodly being driven away like chaff by the wind.
**Example:** huih letkhiat ansi tawh kibang-te hi.

### letkhiat = to drive away, to scatter
**Context:** The action of the wind scattering the chaff (anvai).
**Example:** huih letkhiat ansi tawh kibang-te hi.

### thukhenna = judgment
**Context:** The context where the ungodly will fail to stand.
**Example:** migilote in thukhenna nang zo lo ding a

### kihel = to enter, to join
**Context:** The inability of sinners to join the company of the righteous.
**Example:** midikte’ pawl sungah kihel thei lo ding hi

### kem = to keep, to guard
**Context:** The Lord guards/keeps the way of the righteous.
**Example:** Topa in midikte’ lampi kem a

### siamang = to perish, to be destroyed
**Context:** The fate of the way of the ungodly.
**Example:** migilote’ lampi ahi leh siamang ding hi.

### minamte = nations, peoples
**Context:** Used in Chapter 2 regarding the nations raging against God.
**Example:** Bang thu hangin minamte in siat nading ah kithukhat sim a

### kumpite = kings
**Context:** Rulers of the earth who conspire against the Lord.
**Example:** Leitung kumpite leh gam ukte in

### sathau nilhpa = anointed one (Messiah/Christ)
**Context:** Refers to God's anointed one, whom the kings oppose.
**Example:** Topa leh ama sathau nilhpa langpangin vaihawm-te a

### hennate = bonds, restraints
**Context:** What the rulers wish to break off from themselves.
**Example:** Amau’ hong hennate su-tat khia-in

### su-tat = to break apart, to tear asunder
**Context:** The action desired by the rulers against God's bonds.
**Example:** hong hennate su-tat khia-in

### nuihsan = to laugh at, to mock, to deride
**Context:** The reaction of the one who sits in heaven towards the earthly rulers.
**Example:** Vantunga tu-pa in nuihsan a ### zahko = to hold in derision, to scorn
**Context:** The Lord's action towards those who oppose Him.
**Example:** tua Topa in amaute zahko hi.

### hehluatna = great wrath, intense displeasure
**Context:** The intensity of God's anger when speaking to them.
**Example:** heh luatna tawh amaute linglawng sakin

### linglawng = to trouble, to vex
**Context:** The action taken by the Lord using His great wrath.
**Example:** heh luatna tawh amaute linglawng sakin

### tusak = to set up, to establish
**Context:** God establishing His King on Mount Zion.
**Example:** ka Kumpi ka tusak khin hi

### thukhenna = decree, ordinance
**Context:** What the Lord will declare (related to the King).
**Example:** Topa’ thukhenna kong gen ding hi

### suak = to be begotten, to be brought forth (as a son)
**Context:** Used in the context of the Sonship of Christ: 'tuni-in na Pa ka suak hi' (Today I have begotten you/made you my Son).
**Example:** tuni-in nang kong piang sak khin zo hi.

### luah = inheritance, possession
**Context:** What the nations will be given to the Son as an inheritance.
**Example:** nangma luah dingin minamte ka bawl ding a

### leimong = uttermost parts of the earth, horizon
**Context:** The extent of the possession given to the Son.
**Example:** leimong dongin nangma neihsa ka hisak ding hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **migilote’ thu a zui lo mi** = a person who does not follow the counsel of the ungodly
  - Usage: Describes the first characteristic of the blessed man (avoiding bad counsel).

- **mimawhte’ lampi-a ding lo mi** = a person who does not stand in the way of sinners
  - Usage: Describes the second characteristic of the blessed man (avoiding sinful paths).

- **mi a thusim lote’ phual-a tu lo mi** = a person who does not sit in the seat of the scornful
  - Usage: Describes the third characteristic of the blessed man (avoiding scornful company).

- **sun leh zanin** = day and night
  - Usage: Indicates continuous action (meditating on God's word).

- **luitui luang gei-a kisuan** = planted by the banks of flowing rivers
  - Usage: A metaphor for stability and nourishment, describing the righteous person.

- **huih letkhiat ansi tawh kibang-te hi** = they are like the chaff which the wind drives away
  - Usage: A simile contrasting the fate of the ungodly with the stability of the righteous.

- **thukhenna nang zo lo ding** = will not be able to stand in judgment
  - Usage: Describes the consequence for the ungodly in the final judgment.

- **Topa’ thukhenna kong gen ding hi** = I will declare the decree of the Lord
  - Usage: A declaration of authority regarding God's established plan (referring to the King).

- **nangma luah dingin minamte ka bawl ding a** = I will make the nations your inheritance
  - Usage: A promise from God to the Son regarding His dominion.

## Grammar Patterns

- **Mi [adjective] i cih in, [negative participial phrase 1], [negative participial phrase 2], [negative participial phrase 3];**
  - This structure defines the blessed person ('Mi nuamsa') by listing three things they *do not* do, using the negative participial form ('lo mi'). This mirrors the structure of Psalm 1:1 in English.
  - Example: Mi nuamsa i cih in, migilote’ thu a zui lo mi, mimawhte’ lampi-a ding lo mi, mi a thusim lote’ phual-a tu lo mi;

- **Ahih leh [subject] ahih leh tua bang hi lo a, [comparison phrase].**
  - A contrastive structure introduced by 'Ahih leh' (But/However), setting up a direct comparison between the righteous and the ungodly.
  - Example: Migilote ahih leh tua bang hi lo a, huih letkhiat ansi tawh kibang-te hi.

- **[Subject] in [Object] [Verb] a, [Object] [Verb].**
  - A common narrative structure showing sequential actions, often used when describing the actions of the rulers in Chapter 2.
  - Example: Leitung kumpite leh gam ukte in, Topa leh ama sathau nilhpa langpangin vaihawm-te a,

- **Tua ciangin [circumstance], [subject] in [action], [result/manner].**
  - Indicates a consequence or subsequent action following a preceding event (e.g., God speaking after the rulers conspire).
  - Example: Tua ciangin hehna tawh amau kiangah thu gen ding a, heh luatna tawh amaute linglawng sakin,

- **Nang in ka tapa na hi a, tuni in [result/status].**
  - A declaration of relationship followed by a statement of establishment or current status, typical of divine pronouncements.
  - Example: Nang in ka tapa na hi a, tuni in nang kong piang sak khin zo hi.
