# 1CH - Part 14

## Vocabulary with Context

### mite = people/tribe
**Context:** Used to denote groups of people, like 'Hagri mite' (Hagarites).
**Example:** Hagri mite, Jetur mite, Nafish mite, leh Nodab mite zo uh hi

### zo = to conquer/defeat/subdue
**Context:** In the context of war, meaning they were subdued or defeated by the subject.
**Example:** Hagri mite, Jetur mite, Nafish mite, leh Nodab mite zo uh hi

### a do dingin = in order to fight/for the purpose of fighting
**Context:** Indicates the reason or purpose for seeking help.
**Example:** Amaute a do dingin huhna a ngah uh ciangin

### huhna = help/assistance
**Context:** Refers to aid received, especially in battle.
**Example:** Amaute a do dingin huhna a ngah uh ciangin

### tawh a pawl khempeuhte = all those who allied with them
**Context:** Describes the totality of those allied with the Hagarites.
**Example:** Hagri mite leh amau tawh a pawl khempeuhte amaute' khut sungah kipia hi

### khut sungah kipia = delivered into the hand of
**Context:** Idiomatic expression meaning to be handed over or surrendered to someone's control.
**Example:** amau tawh a pawl khempeuhte amaute' khut sungah kipia hi

### bang hang hiam cih leh = for/because (reason marker)
**Context:** Introduces the reason for the subsequent action (crying to God).
**Example:** bang hang hiam cih leh gal kidona sungah amaute in Pasian' tungah ko uh a

### ko uh = they cried out/called upon
**Context:** Refers to prayer or calling out to God during distress.
**Example:** amaute in Pasian' tungah ko uh a

### muang = to trust/rely on
**Context:** Indicates placing faith or confidence in God.
**Example:** amaute in amah muang ahih manin

### thuumna = prayer/petition
**Context:** The request or plea made to God.
**Example:** amah in amaute' thuumna pia hi

### matsak uh hi = they captured/took possession of
**Context:** Refers to seizing the enemy's possessions (cattle).
**Example:** Amaute in amau' ganhingte matsak uh hi

### kalaoh uh = camels (plural/group)
**Context:** Specific type of animal captured.
**Example:** A kalaoh uh tul sawmnga

### tuu = sheep
**Context:** Specific type of animal captured.
**Example:** tuu tul zanih leh tul sawmnga

### la = asses/donkeys
**Context:** Specific type of animal captured.
**Example:** la tul nih

### kithatin puk hi = fell down slain/were killed in great numbers
**Context:** Describes the heavy casualties suffered by the enemy.
**Example:** mi tampi tak kithatin puk hi

### sal a suah dong uh = until the time of captivity/exile
**Context:** Indicates the duration they occupied the conquered territory.
**Example:** Tua ciangin amaute sal a suah dong uh tua munah teng uh hi

### minam a langte = the half tribe
**Context:** Refers to the half-tribe of Manasseh.
**Example:** Manasseh minam a langte gam sungah teeng uh hi

### tam pha mahmah uh hi = they increased greatly/prospered much
**Context:** Describes their growth and success in the new land.
**Example:** Baalhermon, Senir, leh Hermon Mual tungah tam pha mahmah uh hi

### beh lutangte = heads of the clans/chiefs
**Context:** Refers to the leaders of the families/tribes.
**Example:** Amau’ beh lutangte in: Efer, Ishi, Eliel...

### thahat galhangte = mighty men of valour/strong warriors
**Context:** A descriptive title for the leaders, emphasizing their strength.
**Example:** a thahat galhangte, mi minthangte, a beh lutangte ahi uh hi

### thu palsat uh = transgressed against the word/commandment
**Context:** Indicates disobedience to God's law.
**Example:** amaute in a pate uh Pasian’ thu palsat uh a

### mai-ah a suksiat = destroyed before them
**Context:** God's action of eliminating the previous inhabitants.
**Example:** Pasian in amaute' mai-ah a suksiat tua gam mite' pasian-te bia uh hi

### hansuah = stirred up/incited the spirit of
**Context:** God influencing the mind or will of the king.
**Example:** Assiria kumpipa’ lungsim hansuah a

### pua khia-in = carrying them away/taking them out
**Context:** The action of deportation.
**Example:** amah in amaute... pua khia-in

### gun = river/stream (often used for a specific river location)
**Context:** Refers to the river Gozan where the exiles were taken.
**Example:** Halah, Habor, leh Gozan gun-ah paipihin

### tapate = sons/descendants
**Context:** Used generally for male offspring in genealogies.
**Example:** Levi’ tapate: Gershom, Kohath, leh Merari ahi uh hi

### suan leh khakte = descendants and grandchildren (generations)
**Context:** Used to trace lineage through multiple generations.
**Example:** Eleazar' suanlekhakte: A tapa Finehas, a tapa Abishua

## Phrases & Usage

- **leh amau tawh a pawl khempeuhte** = and all those who allied with them
  - Usage: Used to comprehensively include all associated parties in a surrender or defeat.

- **Pasian' tungah ko uh a** = they cried to God
  - Usage: Standard phrase for appealing to God in distress or battle.

- **a beh lutangte in** = the heads of the clans were
  - Usage: Used in genealogical lists to introduce the principal leaders.

- **a pate uh Pasian’ thu palsat uh** = they transgressed against the God of their fathers
  - Usage: A formal way to describe covenant violation or rebellion against ancestral faith.

- **lungsim hansuah a** = stirred up the spirit of
  - Usage: Describes divine influence on a person's motivation or will, often leading to action.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X mite, Y mite, Z mite zo uh hi.**
  - Listing of tribes/peoples followed by the verb 'zo' (conquer/subdue), indicating the subjects of the action.
  - Example: Hagri mite, Jetur mite, Nafish mite, leh Nodab mite zo uh hi

- **A [noun] uh [number] tul [number]**
  - Structure for enumerating captured items (cattle), specifying the item and the quantity using 'tul' (thousand).
  - Example: A kalaoh uh tul sawmnga, tuu tul zanih leh tul sawmnga

- **a tapa [Name], a tapa [Name], ...**
  - Standard genealogical structure indicating direct descent: 'his son [Name], his son [Name], ...'
  - Example: A tapa Finehas, a tapa Abishua,

- **X ahih manin Y**
  - Causal structure: 'Because X, therefore Y.' Used to link a reason (trusting God) to a result (receiving help).
  - Example: amaute in amah muang ahih manin amah in amaute' thuumna pia hi.
