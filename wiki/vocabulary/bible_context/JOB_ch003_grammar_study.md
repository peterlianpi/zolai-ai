## Verses

### v1
**EN:** After this opened Job his mouth, and cursed his day.

**TDB77:** Hih bang teng khit ciangin Job in thugen hong kipan a, mihing-a a pian’ni samsia hi.

**ZVS:** Hih bang teng khit ciangin Job in thugen hong kipan a, mihingin a pian' ni a samsia hi.

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v2
**EN:** And Job spake, and said,

**TDB77:** Tua ciangin Job in,

**Grammar:** sequence: then/therefore

### v3
**EN:** Let the day perish wherein I was born, and the night in which it was said, There is a man child conceived.

**TDB77:** “Ka suah ni, leh ‘pasal naungeek khat suak hi,’ a kicih zan beimang hen!

**ZVS:** “Ka suah ni, leh ‘Pasal naungek khat kipaii hi,’ a kicih zan beimang hen!

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v4
**EN:** Let that day be darkness; let not God regard it from above, neither let the light shine upon it.

**TDB77:** Tua ni khimzin’ ni suak hen! Van-a Pasian in phawkin nei kei hen la, tua ni-in khuavak taang kei hen.

**ZVS:** Tua ni khimzin' ni suak hen! Vana Pasian in phawkin nei kei hen la, tua ni-in khuavak taang kei hen.

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then)

### v5
**EN:** Let darkness and the shadow of death stain it; let a cloud dwell upon it; let the blackness of the day terrify it.

**TDB77:** Khua niim leh khuamial in khuhcip hen. Tua ni-in meiipi zing hen la, tua ni awk in valh tum hen.

**ZVS:** Khuaniim, khuamial in khuhcip hen. Tua ni-in meiipi ziing hen la, tua ni awk in valhtum hen.

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then)

### v6
**EN:** As for that night, let darkness seize upon it; let it not be joined unto the days of the year, let it not come into the number of the months.

**TDB77:** Tua zan—khuamial sahpi in mancip hen! Kum khat sunga ni om dangte tawh kisim khawm kei hen la, kha khat sunga ni om dangte lakah kiseh khawm kei hen.

**ZVS:** “Tua zan, khuamial sahpi in mancip hen! Kum khat sunga nidangte tawh kisim khawm kei hen la, kha khat sunga nidangte lakah kiseh khawm kei hen.

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v7
**EN:** Lo, let that night be solitary, let no joyful voice come therein.

**TDB77:** Tua zan-in ta piang thei kei hen la, lungdam otna awging tua zanin ging nawn kei hen.

**ZVS:** Tua zanin ta piang kei hen la, lungdam otna awging tua zanin ging nawnkei hen.

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then)

### v8
**EN:** Let them curse it that curse the day, who are ready to raise up their mourning.

**TDB77:** Phuisamna tawh gulpi a khiim thei, a mitphiat-te tawh tuipi ganhing a hencip thei mite in tua zan samsia ta hen.

**ZVS:** Phuisamna tawh awle a khiim thei, a mitphial tawh tuipi ganhing a hencip thei mite in tua zan samsia ta hen.

### v9
**EN:** Let the stars of the twilight thereof be dark; let it look for light, but have none; neither let it see the dawning of the day:

**TDB77:** Tua ni zingsang, aksite mial hen. A vak ngei lo ding khuavak ngak niloh hen la, zingsang phalvak mu ngei kei hen:

**ZVS:** Tua ni zingsang aksite mial hen. A vak ngeilo ding khuavak ngak niloh hen la, zingsang phalvak mu ngeikei hen;

### v10
**EN:** Because it shut not up the doors of my mother's womb, nor hid sorrow from mine eyes.

**TDB77:** bang hang hiam cih leh kei hong kipaaina naugil na khakcip lo a, kei’ muhna panin lungkhamna hong hepkhiat sak lo hi.

**ZVS:** bang hang hiam cih leh kei hong kipaiina naugil na khakciplo a, kei' muhna panin lungkhamna hong hepkhiatsak lo hi.

**Grammar:** negation: lo | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v11
**EN:** Why died I not from the womb? why did I not give up the ghost when I came out of the belly?

**TDB77:** Bang hangin a sisa-in suak lo a, bang hangin ka suah phetin si hoh lo ka hi hiam?

**ZVS:** “Bang hangin a sisa-in suak hoh lo a, bang hangin ka suah phetin si pah hoh lo ka hi hiam?

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then)

### v12
**EN:** Why did the knees prevent me? or why the breasts that I should suck?

**TDB77:** Bang hangin ka nu’ khuk tungah hong kitawi a, a nawi hong kiteepsak se ahi hiam?

**ZVS:** Bang hangin ka nu' khuk tungah hong kitawi se a, ka nu' nawi hong kiteepsak se ahi hiam?

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v13
**EN:** For now should I have lain still and been quiet, I should have slept: then had I been at rest,

**TDB77:** Tua hi zenzen kei leh nuam takin han-ah ka lum hithiat khin ding hi; sihna sungah ihmu-in ka tawlnga khin ding a,

**ZVS:** Tua hi zenzen kei leh hanah nuamtakin ka lum hithiat khin ding hi. Sihna sungah ihmu-in ka tawlnga khin ding a,

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then) | future: will

### v14
**EN:** With kings and counsellors of the earth, which built desolate places for themselves;

**TDB77:** amau’ adingin inn hoih nono a lam leitung kumpite leh a uliante,

**ZVS:** inn hoihnono a lam kumpite leh uliante,

**Grammar:** purpose: in order to

### v15
**EN:** Or with princes that had gold, who filled their houses with silver:

**TDB77:** a inn-te ngun leh kham tawh a kidim a hau uliante tawh han-ah ka tawlnga khin ta ding hi.

**ZVS:** ngun leh kham tawh a kidim a hau a liante tawh nuamtakin hanah ka tawlnga khinta ding hi.

**Grammar:** completed: already done | future: will | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v16
**EN:** Or as an hidden untimely birth I had not been; as infants which never saw light.

**TDB77:** Bang hangin a hun cing nai lo-a suak naungeek bang, khuavak mu man lo-a si naungeekte bangin hong kiselcip hoh lo ahi hiam?

**ZVS:** Bang hangin a hun cing lo-a suakte bang, khuavak a mu manloin a si naungekte bangin hong kiseelcip hoh lo ahi hiam?

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v17
**EN:** There the wicked cease from troubling; and there the weary be at rest.

**TDB77:** Tua lai-ah a migilote in siatna sukha nawn lo ding a, a gimte tawlnga hithiat ding hi.

**ZVS:** Tua lai-ah migilote in hong sukha nawnlo ding a, a gim mite tawlnga hithiat ding hi.

**Grammar:** future: will

### v18
**EN:** There the prisoners rest together; they hear not the voice of the oppressor.

**TDB77:** Tua lai-ah galmat salte in nopna ngah ding a, nasem ukpa’ aw za nawn lo ding-te hi.

**ZVS:** Tua lai-ah galmat salte in nopna ngah ding a, nasem ukpa' awging za nawnlo ding-te hi.

**Grammar:** future: will | plural: uh marker

### v19
**EN:** The small and great are there; and the servant is free from his master.

**TDB77:** Tua lai-ah milian mineu omkhawm a, silate nangawn a topa’ kiangpan suakta hi.

**ZVS:** Tua lai munah milian mineu omkhawm a, silate nangawn a topa' kiang pan suakta hi.

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v20
**EN:** Wherefore is light given to him that is in misery, and life unto the bitter in soul;

**TDB77:** Bang hangin lungkhamna a thuak mipa tungah khuavak kipia a, nuntakna haksa a sa mahmah mipa tungah nuntakna kipia hiam?

**ZVS:** “Bang hangin lungkhamna a thuak mipa, a nuntakna haksa a sa mahmah mipa,

**Grammar:** intensifier: very/greatly | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v21
**EN:** Which long for death, but it cometh not; and dig for it more than for hid treasures;

**TDB77:** sih ding lunggulh ngeungau napi a si thei lo, sum phum zon’ sangin sih ding a lim zon’ zaw,

**ZVS:** sih ding lunggulh ngeungau napi, a si theilo, sum phum zon' sangin sih ding a lim zon'zaw,

### v22
**EN:** Which rejoice exceedingly, and are glad, when they can find the grave?

**TDB77:** han sung a tun’ ciangin a nuam mahmah, hankhuk a tun’ ciangin nuam lua-in a diang gawp mite’ khuavak mu dingin mi-in piang laizang ahi hiam?

**ZVS:** han sung a tun' ciangin a nuam mahmah, hankhuk a tun' ciangin nuam lua-in a dianggawp mite, khuavak a mu dingin mi-in piang laizang ahi hiam?

**Grammar:** intensifier: very/greatly | serial verb: -in (and then) | purpose: in order to

### v23
**EN:** Why is light given to a man whose way is hid, and whom God hath hedged in?

**TDB77:** Bang hangin mai lam thu ding thei lopi-in a mot om ding, a kimkotah Pasian in dai a khaknelh mipa, mi-in piang zenzen ahi hiam?

**ZVS:** Bang hangin mailam thu ding a theilopi-in a mot om ding, a kimkotah Pasian in dai a khaknelh mipa, mi-in piang zenzen ahi hiam?

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then) | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v24
**EN:** For my sighing cometh before I eat, and my roarings are poured out like the waters.

**TDB77:** Bang hang hiam cih leh khuisatna in ka annek suak a, ka tauna in luipi ging bang ahi hi.

**ZVS:** Bang hang hiam cih leh khuisatna, ka annek suak a, ka tauna, luipi ging bang hi.

### v25
**EN:** For the thing which I greatly feared is come upon me, and that which I was afraid of is come unto me.

**TDB77:** Ka kihtaktak ka tungah hong tung a, ka laulau ka tuak ta hi.

**ZVS:** Ka kihtaktak thu ka tungah hong tung ta a, ka laulau thu, ka tuak ta hi.

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v26
**EN:** I was not in safety, neither had I rest, neither was I quiet; yet trouble came.

**TDB77:** Lungnopna ahi a, daitak-a om hithiat theihna ahi zongin ka nei kei hi; lungkhamna in hong nawk niloh a, tawldam hun ka nei kei hi,” a ci hi.

**ZVS:** Lungnopna, daitakin omna, ka nei kei hi. Lungkhamna in hong nawk niloh a, tawldam hun ka nei kei hi,”a ci hi.
