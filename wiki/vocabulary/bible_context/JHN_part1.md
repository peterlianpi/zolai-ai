# JHN - Part 1

## Vocabulary with Context

### kipatcil-in = in the beginning
**Context:** Used to denote the initial state or time, equivalent to 'In the beginning' in John 1:1.
**Example:** A kipatcil-in Thu a om hi.

### omkhawm = was with (dwelt together)
**Context:** Indicates co-existence or being in company with someone/something.
**Example:** Thu in Pasian tawh a omkhawm hi.

### khempeuh = all things
**Context:** Used as a universal quantifier, meaning 'everything' or 'all things'.
**Example:** Thu tawh na khempeuh a kipiangsak hi.

### loin = without
**Context:** Used to negate the existence or involvement of something in an action.
**Example:** Thu loin a kipiangsak khat beek a om kei hi.

### nuntakna = life
**Context:** Refers to the state of being alive, spiritual or physical life.
**Example:** Ama sungah nuntakna a om hi.

### khuavak = light
**Context:** Used metaphorically for spiritual illumination or truth, contrasting with darkness.
**Example:** Tua nuntakna in leitung mite ading khuavak ahi hi.

### khuamial = darkness
**Context:** Used to denote spiritual ignorance or evil, the opposite of 'khuavak' (light).
**Example:** Tua khuavak in khuamial lakah taang a,

### taang = shines/stands firm
**Context:** In this context, it means to shine or to remain steadfast against opposition.
**Example:** Tua khuavak in khuamial lakah taang a,

### zo = comprehend/overcome/grasp
**Context:** Used here to mean that darkness could not overcome or understand the light.
**Example:** khuamial in amah a zo zo kei hi.

### sawl = sent
**Context:** Used for being commissioned or dispatched by a higher authority (God).
**Example:** Johan kici mi khat Pasian in hong sawl hi.

### teci pang = to bear witness
**Context:** A compound phrase meaning to testify or give evidence about something.
**Example:** ama hangin thu um dingin teci pang a,

### uptheihna = to be able to believe/receive
**Context:** Derived from 'up' (believe/receive) plus the potential suffix '-theih'.
**Example:** tua khuavak a uptheihna dingun

### mahmah = that very one/emphatic marker
**Context:** Used for emphasis, similar to 'that very' or 'himself'.
**Example:** Tua Johan mahmah pen khuavak hilo a,

### vaksak = lighteth/illuminates
**Context:** The verb form of 'khuavak' (light), meaning to shine upon or illuminate.
**Example:** piang mi khempeuh a vaksak hi.

### kipiangsak = was made/created
**Context:** The passive form of 'piang' (to be born/happen), meaning 'was created' or 'was brought into being'.
**Example:** Amah tawh leitung kipiangsak hi.

### sang = receive/accept
**Context:** Used for accepting or welcoming someone or their message.
**Example:** ama mite in amah a sang kei uh hi.

### suah theihna = the ability to become/be born
**Context:** The potential/ability ('-theih') to be born or brought forth ('suah').
**Example:** Pasian’ tate suah theih na’ng thu a pia hi.

### si leh sa = blood and flesh (natural descent)
**Context:** A common idiom referring to physical, natural birth or lineage.
**Example:** Tua tate in si leh sa tawh suak ahi kei uh hi;

### hehpihna = grace
**Context:** Refers to divine favor or unmerited kindness.
**Example:** hehpihna leh thuman thutak tawh kidimin

### thuman thutak = truth and reality/fullness of truth
**Context:** A compound phrase emphasizing complete truthfulness and substance.
**Example:** hehpihna leh thuman thutak tawh kidimin

### minthan’na = glory
**Context:** Refers to the splendor, majesty, or inherent honor of God/the Son.
**Example:** a Ta khat bek a piaksa minthan’na bangin

## Phrases & Usage

- **A kipatcil-in** = In the beginning
  - Usage: Used to set the context at the very start of time or existence (cf. Genesis 1:1).

- **Pasian tawh a omkhawm hi** = was with God
  - Usage: Describes the eternal relationship and presence of the Word alongside God.

- **a zo zo kei hi** = did not comprehend/overcome it
  - Usage: Used to express that darkness failed to grasp or extinguish the light.

- **mi khempeuh tungah khuavak hong tun'pih hi** = brought the light to all people
  - Usage: Describes the action of the life/light extending its influence to humanity.

- **a khuapihte a tuipihte** = his own people (literally: his clan and his water-dwellers/neighbors)
  - Usage: An idiomatic way to refer to one's own people or nation (John 1:11).

- **Pasian’ tate suah theihna ding thu a pia hi** = gave the word/power to become sons of God
  - Usage: Describes the granting of authority or potential to believe and be adopted by God.

- **si leh sa tawh suak ahi kei uh hi** = were not born of blood and flesh
  - Usage: Used to contrast natural, physical birth with spiritual rebirth.

## Grammar Patterns

- **Noun + tawh + Verb (omkhawm)**
  - Indicates accompaniment or being 'with' someone/something. (e.g., Pasian tawh a omkhawm hi - was with God).
  - Example: Pasian tawh a omkhawm hi.

- **Noun + loin + Verb (om kei)**
  - The suffix '-loin' functions as a negative preposition meaning 'without'. (e.g., Thu loin a kipiangsak khat beek a om kei hi - Without the Word, not one thing was made).
  - Example: Thu loin a kipiangsak khat beek a om kei hi.

- **Noun + in + Verb (kipiangsak)**
  - The suffix '-in' often marks the agent or instrument in an active sentence, or the subject in certain constructions. Here, it marks the agent of creation (Thu in... a kipiangsak hi).
  - Example: Thu tawh na khempeuh a kipiangsak hi.

- **Verb + theihna dingun**
  - Indicates purpose or goal, meaning 'in order that [subject] might be able to [verb]'. (e.g., uptheihna dingun - in order that they might believe).
  - Example: tua khuavak a uptheihna dingun

- **Noun + kici + Noun + khat**
  - Structure used to introduce a person identified by a name or title. (e.g., Johan kici mi khat - a man named John).
  - Example: Johan kici mi khat Pasian in hong sawl hi.
