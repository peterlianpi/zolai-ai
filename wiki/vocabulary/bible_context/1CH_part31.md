## Vocabulary with Context

### khuami = of/from [place]
**Context:** Used to denote origin or place of belonging, similar to '-ite' in English names (e.g., Hushathite).
**Example:** Hushah khuami Sibbekai

### beh = and/also (connecting names)
**Context:** Used to link two names together, often implying they are associated or listed together.
**Example:** Ahoah-te beh Ilai

### tapa = son of
**Context:** A crucial term indicating direct lineage, equivalent to 'son of' in genealogies.
**Example:** Baanah' tapa Heled

### mite = people/tribe/clan
**Context:** Refers to a group or tribe, often used with a possessive marker ('mite' or 'mite' in some forms).
**Example:** Benjamin mite sung panin

### sung panin = pertaining to/belonging to the side of
**Context:** Indicates affiliation or belonging to a specific group (Benjamin tribe).
**Example:** Benjamin mite sung panin

### mualkuam = brooks/valleys/ravines
**Context:** Describes a geographical feature associated with a place name (Ga-ash).
**Example:** Ga-ash mualkuam mi Hurai

### mi = person of/resident of
**Context:** Used similarly to 'khuami' but sometimes used for specific features like brooks ('mualkuam mi').
**Example:** Ga-ash mualkuam mi Hurai

### sanggampa = brother
**Context:** Indicates a sibling relationship.
**Example:** Nathan’ sanggampa Joel

### galhiam puapa = armourbearer
**Context:** A specific military or service title.
**Example:** Joab’ galhiam puapa Be-eroth khuami Naharai

### leh = and (connecting items in a list, often used instead of 'beh')
**Context:** A conjunction used to connect subsequent items or people in a list.
**Example:** Ira leh Gareb

### makai = captain/leader
**Context:** A title denoting leadership or command.
**Example:** Reuben mite’ makai khat ahi

### khat = one
**Context:** The numeral one, used here with 'makai' (one captain).
**Example:** makai khat ahi

### ahi = is/was (a form of 'to be')
**Context:** A copula verb, often appearing at the end of a clause.
**Example:** makai khat ahi

### tawh = with/alongside
**Context:** Indicates accompaniment or inclusion.
**Example:** amah tawh mihing

### mihing = people/men
**Context:** Refers to a count of individuals.
**Example:** mihing sawmthum

### sawmthum = thirty
**Context:** The number thirty.
**Example:** mihing sawmthum

## Phrases & Usage

- **Hushah khuami** = The Hushathite (person from Hushah)
  - Usage: Used to identify a person by their place of origin, common in biblical lists of warriors/officials.

- **Benjamin mite sung panin** = that pertained to the children of Benjamin
  - Usage: Used to specify tribal affiliation or allegiance.

- **galhiam puapa** = armourbearer of
  - Usage: A descriptive title indicating a specific role held in relation to another person (Joab).

- **makai khat ahi** = was one captain
  - Usage: A declarative statement identifying someone's rank or role.

## Grammar Patterns

- **[Place Name] khuami [Person Name]**
  - Standard structure for identifying a person by their place of origin (e.g., Sibbekai the Hushathite).
  - Example: Hushah khuami Sibbekai

- **[Name A]' tapa [Name B]**
  - Standard genealogical structure indicating 'Name A, son of Name B'.
  - Example: Baanah' tapa Heled

- **[Name A], [Name B]' sanggampa**
  - Structure for indicating a sibling relationship, where the second person is the brother of the first.
  - Example: Nathan’ sanggampa Joel

- **[Group] mi [Name], leh amah tawh mihing [Number]**
  - Structure used to state the leader of a group and the number of men accompanying them.
  - Example: Reuben mi Shiza’ tapa Adina, leh amah tawh mihing sawmthum
