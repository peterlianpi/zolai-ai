# JHN - Part 4

## Vocabulary with Context

### gelhkholhsa = foretold/prophesied
**Context:** Refers to what the prophets wrote about Jesus in the scriptures.
**Example:** Moses in Pasian’ Thukhamna Lai Siangtho sung leh kamsangte in lai sungah a gelhkholhsa Nazareth mi Josef’ tapa Zeisu ka mu zo uh hi

### kamsangte = prophets
**Context:** Used alongside 'Moses in Pasian’ Thukhamna Lai Siangtho' (Moses in the Law of God) to refer to the Old Testament writers.
**Example:** Moses in Pasian’ Thukhamna Lai Siangtho sung leh kamsangte in lai sungah a gelhkholhsa

### hoih khat = anything good
**Context:** Nathanael questions if anything good can come from Nazareth.
**Example:** Nazareth khua sung pan na hoih khat hong piang thei ding hiam?

### kheemna = guile/deceit
**Context:** Describing Nathanael as a true Israelite in whom there is no deceit.
**Example:** En un, ama sungah kheemna om lo Israel mi taktak ahi hi

### Theikung nuai-ah = under the fig tree
**Context:** Jesus tells Nathanael that he saw him while he was under the fig tree, indicating prior knowledge.
**Example:** Filip in nang hong sap ma-in Theikung nuai-ah na om lai takin ke’n kong mu hi

### kumpipa = king
**Context:** Nathanael recognizes Jesus as the King of Israel.
**Example:** Israel mite’ kumpipa lah na hi hi

### kihongin = opened
**Context:** Describing the heavens opening up for Nathanael to see.
**Example:** van kihongin Pasian’ vantungmite in Mihing Tapa tungah kahsuk kahto a, om na mu ding hi

### mo la lawmvakna = wedding feast/celebration
**Context:** The event where Jesus and his disciples were invited in Cana.
**Example:** Galile gam Kana khua-ah mo la lawmvakna om a

### Leenggahtui = wine (specifically grape juice/wine)
**Context:** The substance that ran out at the wedding feast.
**Example:** Leenggahtui a bei ciangin Zeisu’ nu in Zeisu kiangah, “Leenggahtui a nei kei uh hi,” ci-in a ko hi.

### lunghihmawhna = worry/concern/trouble (lit. heart-trouble)
**Context:** Jesus' response to his mother about the lack of wine, questioning why she is bringing this concern to him.
**Example:** Nu aw, bang hangin na lunghihmawhna hong ko zenzen na hi hiam?

### kikum = to discuss/to have business with
**Context:** Jesus asks his mother what concern she has with him ('What is there between me and you?').
**Example:** Numei aw, kei tawh bang kikum i hi hiam?

### seeng = firkin/a measure of capacity
**Context:** Used to describe the size of the stone waterpots.
**Example:** tua munah seeng nih seeng thum bang a ta thei leibeel guk a kikoih hi.

### thambelpi = large jar/pot (specifically waterpot)
**Context:** Refers to the stone waterpots used for Jewish purification rites.
**Example:** tua lai-ah tui tambong guk tambong sagih bang a ta thei thambelpi guk a om hi.

### zuphungpa = master of the feast/ruler of the banquet
**Context:** The person to whom the newly made wine was to be taken.
**Example:** lawmvakna pawi ukpa kiangah paipih un

## Phrases & Usage

- **a gelhkholhsa** = that which was foretold/prophesied
  - Usage: Used to describe the fulfillment of scripture regarding Jesus.

- **na hoih khat hong piang thei ding hiam?** = Can anything good come out of...?
  - Usage: A rhetorical question expressing doubt or skepticism about a source.

- **ama sungah kheemna om lo** = in whom there is no guile/deceit
  - Usage: A commendation of character, often used in scripture to describe an honest person.

- **kei tawh bang kikum i hi hiam?** = What is there between me and you? / What concern do we have?
  - Usage: A formal or slightly distancing way of asking why someone is involving you in their matter (often used when asserting independence or timing).

- **a mantak kong cih-inah** = Because I told you the truth
  - Usage: Used to emphasize the veracity of a preceding statement, leading to a promised revelation.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X in, 'Y' ci-in a dot leh, Z in, 'W' a ci hi.**
  - A common narrative structure for reporting dialogue: X asked 'Y', and Z replied 'W'.
  - Example: Nathanael in, “Nazareth khua sung pan na hoih khat hong piang thei ding hiam?” ci-in a dot leh, Filip in, “Hong pai in la, hong en in,” a ci hi.

- **X in Y tungah Z a om hi.**
  - Indicates location or presence: X was at/in Y.
  - Example: Zeisu’ nu zong tua lai-ah a om hi.

- **X in Y kiangah, 'Z' ci-in a ko hi/a gen hi.**
  - Reporting speech directed to a specific person: X said to Y, 'Z'.
  - Example: Leenggahtui a bei ciangin Zeisu’ nu in Zeisu kiangah, “Leenggahtui a nei kei uh hi,” ci-in a ko hi.

- **X in Y na hih un, ci-in a gen hi.**
  - Reporting a command or instruction: X said, 'Do Y to X'.
  - Example: Ama hong sawl khempeuh na hih un,” ci-in a gen hi.
