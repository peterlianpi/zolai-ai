## Phrases & Usage

- **Topa’ thupiakna a zuih lohna** = His disobedience/failure to follow the Lord's command
  - Usage: Used to describe Saul's sin leading to his downfall.

- **kumpi gam hei hi** = bestowed the kingdom
  - Usage: Used when God transfers kingship or authority to someone else.

- **nangma guh leh nangma sa ka hi-te hi** = We are your bone and your flesh
  - Usage: A strong idiom expressing deep kinship and unity (similar to KJV 'bone of my bone, and flesh of my flesh').

- **a cingpa na hi ding a, ka mi Israelte tungah ulian na hi ding hi** = You shall be the shepherd/ruler of my people Israel, and you shall be a prince/leader over my people Israel
  - Usage: A prophetic declaration of leadership and kingship.

- **Topa mai Hebron ah amaute tawh thuciamna khat bawl** = Made a covenant with them before the Lord in Hebron
  - Usage: Describes the formal establishment of a binding agreement under divine witness.

- **David khuapi a kici Zion munmuanhuai** = The stronghold of Zion, which is called the city of David
  - Usage: Identifying a specific, fortified location taken by David.

- **Vanglianpen Topa in amah tawh om ahih manin** = Because the Most High Lord was with him
  - Usage: Explaining the reason for someone's increasing success or greatness.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X hangin Y si hi**
  - Structure indicating the cause (X) of death (Y si hi). (e.g., Saul died because of his disobedience).
  - Example: Tua ahih ciangin a thuman’lohna hangin Saul si hi

- **leh... leh... ahi hi**
  - A structure used for listing multiple reasons or aspects of a single subject, often translated as 'and... and... it was'.
  - Example: Amah in Topa’ thupiakna a zuih lohna, leh vankah khat thu dongin a lamlah ding a zon’na, leh Topa tung panin lamlahna a zon’ lohna ah Topa tungah a thuman lo ahi hi.

- **X in Y kiangah, 'Z' a ci hi**
  - Direct speech structure: X said to Y, 'Z'.
  - Example: Jebus mite in David kiangah, “Hih lai-ah nang nong pai kei ding hi,” a ci-te hi.

- **X in Y tawh thuciamna khat bawl**
  - Structure for making a covenant: X made a covenant with Y.
  - Example: David in Topa mai Hebron ah amaute tawh thuciamna khat bawl a

- **X a kici Y**
  - Structure used for naming or identifying something: X which is called Y (or: X, the one called Y).
  - Example: Jebus a kici Jerusalem ah

- **X in amah tawh om ahih manin Y lian semsem hi**
  - Causal structure: Because X was with him, Y became increasingly great.
  - Example: Vanglianpen Topa in amah tawh om ahih manin David lian semsem hi.
