# 1CH - Part 54

## Vocabulary with Context

### nasepna = work, service
**Context:** Referring to the service or work assigned to the musicians/singers.
**Example:** David leh nasepna ah a ukpite in...

### ukpite = captains, leaders
**Context:** The leaders or captains who organized the service.
**Example:** David leh nasepna ah a ukpite in zong...

### laiza lasaknate = psalteries (stringed instruments)
**Context:** One of the musical instruments used in the service.
**Example:** zong laiza lasaknate, harp lasaknate, leh daltuahte tawh...

### daltuahte = cymbals
**Context:** One of the musical instruments used for praise.
**Example:** leh daltuahte tawh mai lam thu a genkhol dingin...

### genkhol = prophesy, foretell
**Context:** To speak forth, often in a prophetic sense regarding future things ('mai lam thu').
**Example:** tawh mai lam thu a genkhol dingin...

### tapate = sons (descendants)
**Context:** Used to refer to the male descendants of Asaph, Heman, and Jeduthun.
**Example:** Asaf, Heman, leh Jeduthunte' tapate pawlkhat...

### koih tuam uh hi = set apart, appointed
**Context:** The action of setting aside specific individuals for a particular service.
**Example:** nasepna-a dingin koih tuam uh hi.

### makaihna nuai-ah = under the hand/authority of
**Context:** Indicates being under the direction or authority of someone (like the king or the father/leader).
**Example:** kumpipa’ makaihna nuai-ah mai lam thu a genkhol...

### lungdamkohna = thanksgiving
**Context:** An act of giving thanks, often associated with worship music.
**Example:** Lungdam kohna leh { Topa } phatna-in...

### phatna = praise
**Context:** The act of praising God.
**Example:** leh { Topa } phatna-in laia lasakna tawh...

### guk pha uh hi = there were six
**Context:** Stating the number of sons under Jeduthun's leadership.
**Example:** Mattibiah, guk pha uh hi.

### thuciamna = prophecy, word/promise
**Context:** Referring to the word or promise of God.
**Example:** Pasian thuciamna bangin amah a pahtawi dingin...

### thupiakna = commandment, order
**Context:** The official order or decree from the king.
**Example:** kumpipa’ thupiakna nuai-ah om uh hi.

### sinna a nei a siam mi = those who were skilled/instructed
**Context:** Describing the Levites who were trained in music for the Lord's service.
**Example:** lasakna lamah sinna a nei a siam mi a vekpi-in...

### ai a san uh hi = they cast lots
**Context:** The process of determining assignments by lot.
**Example:** amau' nasep ding uh ai a san uh hi.

### ai masa = the first lot
**Context:** Referring to the first draw in the casting of lots.
**Example:** Asaf' behte-a' dingin ai masa Josef tungah tu hi.

### sanggamte = brethren (kinsmen/peers)
**Context:** Used alongside 'sons' to denote the group assigned in the lot.
**Example:** a sanggamte, leh a tapate, sawmlenih tung tu hi.

### sawm-le-nih = twelve (ten and two)
**Context:** The specific number assigned in each lot group.
**Example:** leh a sanggamte, sawmlenih tung tu hi;

## Phrases & Usage

- **mai lam thu a genkhol** = to prophesy future things
  - Usage: Used specifically for the prophetic function of the musicians, often accompanied by instruments.

- **Topa phatna-in** = in the praise of the Lord
  - Usage: Describes the purpose or manner of the musical offering.

- **a pa uh ukna nuai-ah** = under the leadership/rule of their father
  - Usage: Indicates direct subordination to the patriarch/leader in the service structure.

- **a neu a lian, sia leh a sinte a kibangin** = as well the small as the great, the teacher as the scholar
  - Usage: A comprehensive phrase indicating that the casting of lots applied equally to all ranks and statuses within the group.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X leh Y leh Z tawh**
  - Conjunction structure using 'leh' (and) repeatedly to list multiple items (instruments or people).
  - Example: laiza lasaknate, harp lasaknate, leh daltuahte tawh...

- **X' tapate sung panin:**
  - A common introductory structure indicating the lineage or group being detailed next (e.g., 'Of the sons of X:').
  - Example: Asaf' tapate sung panin:

- **bang hang hiam cih leh**
  - A causal conjunction phrase introducing the reason for the preceding statement.
  - Example: ahi hi; bang hang hiam cih leh Pasian in...

- **X tungah Y pia hi**
  - Structure showing the recipient (X) and the gift (Y) from God.
  - Example: Pasian in Heman tungah tapa sawmleli leh tanu thum pia hi.

- **ai masa/nih/thumna... tungah tu hi**
  - Used specifically in the context of casting lots to assign the sequence of selection.
  - Example: ai masa Josef tungah tu hi.
