# 1CH - Part 37

## Vocabulary with Context

### adingin = for him/it
**Context:** Used to indicate purpose or destination for David.
**Example:** David in ama adingin David khuapi sungah innte lam hi

### bawlin = having made/prepared
**Context:** Participle form indicating the action of making/preparing something.
**Example:** mun khat bawlin puanbuk khat lamsak hi

### lamsak = pitched/erected
**Context:** Used for setting up a tent or tabernacle.
**Example:** puanbuk khat lamsak hi

### Thuciamna Singkuang = Covenant Ark (Ark of the Covenant)
**Context:** The specific religious object being discussed.
**Example:** Pasian thuciamna singkuang’ adingin

### tawntung = forever/continually
**Context:** Adverb describing the continuous nature of the work.
**Example:** ama na a sem tawntung dingin

### teel = chosen
**Context:** Past participle used to describe those selected by God (Topa).
**Example:** Topa in amaute teel ahih manin

### sim loh = except for/other than
**Context:** Used to exclude a specific group (Levites) from an action.
**Example:** Levi mite sim loh kuamah in

### paipih to = to bring up/carry up
**Context:** Compound verb indicating the action of transporting something to a higher place.
**Example:** Topa’ thuciamna singkuang a paipih to dingin

### khawm = gathered
**Context:** Used for assembling people together.
**Example:** Israel khempeuh khawm hi

### suante = descendants/sons
**Context:** Used to refer to the lineage of Aaron.
**Example:** Aaron’ suante leh Levi mi

### beh = clan/family group
**Context:** Used to categorize the Levites by their ancestral lines.
**Example:** Kohath-te beh sung panin

### ukpi = chief/leader
**Context:** Title for the head of a specific clan group.
**Example:** a ukpi Uriel

### behte = his brethren/kinsmen
**Context:** Refers to the other members belonging to the same clan group.
**Example:** a behte za leh sawmnih tawh

### sam = called (summoned)
**Context:** Used when David summoned specific individuals.
**Example:** Uriel, Asaiah, Joel... sam a

### lutangte = heads/chiefs
**Context:** Refers to the leaders among the Levites.
**Example:** Levi mite beh lutangte na hi uh hi

### kisiansuah = sanctify yourselves
**Context:** Reflexive verb meaning to make oneself holy or set apart.
**Example:** nomau mah kisiansuah tawm un

### kisehsa = as it was prescribed/ordained
**Context:** Refers to the established rule or previous command.
**Example:** a kisehsa bang danin kem lo i hih manin

### hong hehin = has afflicted us/brought trouble upon us
**Context:** Used to describe God's judgment or consequence for disobedience.
**Example:** eite tungah Topa i Pasian hong hehin daan hong pia hi

### kamgenna = utterance/word (of God)
**Context:** Refers to the divine command or word spoken by God.
**Example:** Topa’ kamgenna tawh kizui-in

### liangko = shoulders
**Context:** The part of the body used for carrying the Ark.
**Example:** a liangko uh tungah zawlte tawh

## Phrases & Usage

- **mun khat bawlin** = having prepared a place
  - Usage: Used to describe the action of setting aside or constructing a specific location for a sacred purpose.

- **sim loh kuamah in** = no one except for
  - Usage: A construction used to emphasize exclusivity; only the specified group may perform the action.

- **a sem tawntung dingin** = in order that [it] may serve continually
  - Usage: Expresses the purpose of an action intended to be perpetual.

- **beh lutangte na hi uh hi** = you are the heads of the clans
  - Usage: A formal declaration identifying the status or role of the addressed individuals.

- **nomau mah kisiansuah tawm un** = you yourselves should sanctify
  - Usage: An imperative or strong suggestion urging self-consecration.

- **a liangko uh tungah zawlte tawh** = with the staves upon their shoulders
  - Usage: Describes the specific method prescribed for carrying the Ark (using poles resting on the shoulders).

## Grammar Patterns

- **V-in [Subject] [Object] [Verb]**
  - Standard Zolai sentence structure where the subject often follows the verb or is implied, with the verb often ending in '-in' (a form of past tense/completed action marker).
  - Example: David in ama-a' dingin David khuapi sungah innte a lam hi.

- **X-te beh sung panin Y, Z tawh ahi hi.**
  - Structure used for listing lineage members: 'From the clan of X, Y (the chief), along with Z (number) of his brethren, they were.' This is a common way to enumerate groups by clan.
  - Example: Kohath-te beh sung panin a ukpi Uriel, a behte za leh sawmnih tawh ahi hi;

- **Ahih manin [Reason], [Consequence]**
  - Causal structure meaning 'Because/Since [Reason], therefore [Consequence].'
  - Example: Levi mite teel ahih manin kuamah in Pasian’ thuciamna singkuang pua thei lo ding hi

- **V + toh theih nadingun**
  - Purpose clause structure: 'In order that [one] may be able to do V.' (V + toh = with/by doing V; theih = able; nadingun = for the purpose of).
  - Example: na paipih toh theih nadingun
