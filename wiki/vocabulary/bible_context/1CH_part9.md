# 1CH - Part 9

## Vocabulary with Context

### tapa = son
**Context:** Used repeatedly to denote direct male descent in a genealogy.
**Example:** a tapa Joram

### ahi uh hi = they were / they are
**Context:** A concluding phrase indicating a list of individuals are the sons/descendants.
**Example:** Zedekiah, a lina Shallum ahi uh hi.

### tapate = sons (plural)
**Context:** The plural form of 'tapa' used when listing multiple sons.
**Example:** Josiah’ tapate in:

### tacil = first
**Context:** Used to denote the firstborn son in an ordered list.
**Example:** A tacil Johanan

### nihna = second
**Context:** Used to denote the second son in an ordered list.
**Example:** a nihna Jehoiakim

### thumna = third
**Context:** Used to denote the third son in an ordered list.
**Example:** a thumna Zedekiah

### lina = fourth
**Context:** Used to denote the fourth son in an ordered list.
**Example:** a lina Shallum

### suan leh khakte = sons and daughters (offspring)
**Context:** Used to refer to the general offspring of Jehoiakim, though the KJV translation focuses only on sons.
**Example:** Jehoiakim’ suan leh khakte in:

### suanlekhakte = offspring/descendants
**Context:** Alternative term for offspring, used for Jehoiakim.
**Example:** Jehoiakim' suanlekhakte:

### suan = offspring/descendants (general)
**Context:** Used in Tedim2010 for Jehoiakim's offspring, contrasting with 'tapa' (son).
**Example:** Jehoiakim' suanlekhakte

### sal a suak = who went into captivity / exiled
**Context:** Describes Jeconiah's status when his sons are listed.
**Example:** Babilon ah sal a suak Jekoniah’ tapate in:

### leh = and
**Context:** Standard conjunction used to connect names in a list.
**Example:** Meshullam leh Hananiah

### kici tanu = called daughter
**Context:** Used to introduce a female descendant by name.
**Example:** Shelomith kici tanu khat zong nei hi

### zong nei hi = also had / possessed
**Context:** Indicates that Zerubbabel also had a daughter.
**Example:** Shelomith kici tanu khat zong nei hi

### tapa dang = other sons
**Context:** Used to introduce additional sons not previously listed.
**Example:** tua ciangin tapa dang Hashubah...

### nga nei lai hi = had five (in total)
**Context:** Indicates the total count of the preceding list of sons.
**Example:** nga neilai hi.

### tupa = daughters
**Context:** Used alongside 'tapa' (son) to indicate the total number of children.
**Example:** tapa khat leh tupa nga nei a

### vekin guk pha uh hi = totaling six
**Context:** Indicates the total count of Shemaiah's sons.
**Example:** a vekin guk pha uh hi.

### thum pha uh hi = totaling three
**Context:** Indicates the total count of Neariah's sons.
**Example:** thum pha uh hi.

### sagih pha uh hi = totaling seven
**Context:** Indicates the total count of Elio-enai's sons.
**Example:** sagih pha uh hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **a tapa** = his son
  - Usage: Standard possessive/relational marker in genealogies, linking the father to the son.

- **ahi uh hi** = they were
  - Usage: Used at the end of a list of names to confirm that those listed constitute the group being discussed (e.g., the sons).

- **kici tanu khat zong nei hi** = also had one daughter named
  - Usage: Used when a male figure has both sons and at least one daughter, specifying the daughter's name.

- **tapa khat leh tupa nga nei a** = had one son and five daughters
  - Usage: Used to summarize the gender breakdown of offspring before naming the son.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X’ tapate in: Y, Z, ahi uh hi.**
  - Possessive structure showing the sons of X are Y and Z. The apostrophe (') indicates the genitive/possessive case for the father's name.
  - Example: Josiah’ tapate in: A tacil Johanan...

- **A [ordinal number] [Name]**
  - Used for ordered lists of sons, specifying birth order (first, second, third, etc.).
  - Example: A tacil Johanan, a nihna Jehoiakim...

- **[Name]’ tapate in: [List of sons], [count] pha uh hi.**
  - Structure used to conclude a list of descendants by stating the total count of individuals in that generation.
  - Example: Neariah’ tapate in: Elio-enai, Hizkiah, leh Azrikam, thum pha uh hi.
