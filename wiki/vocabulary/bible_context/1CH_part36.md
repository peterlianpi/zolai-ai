# 1CH - Part 36

## Vocabulary with Context

### zi = more (wives/children)
**Context:** Used to indicate an increase in the number of wives David took.
**Example:** David in Jerusalem ah zi nei thuahin tapate leh tanute nei hi.

### thuahin = after that / subsequently
**Context:** Indicates a sequence of events; David took more wives after a previous action or state.
**Example:** David in Jerusalem ah zi nei thuahin tapate leh tanute nei hi.

### a tate' = his children (plural)
**Context:** Refers to the offspring of David.
**Example:** Jerusalem-ah a neih a tate' min:

### khempeuh = all / entire
**Context:** Used to specify the totality of the people of Israel.
**Example:** Israel khempeuh tungah kumpi-in David sathau a kinilhna thu...

### sathau = oil (anointing oil)
**Context:** Refers to the anointing oil used when David was made king.
**Example:** kumpi-in David sathau a kinilhna thu

### kinilhna = anointing (being anointed)
**Context:** The act of being consecrated or made king.
**Example:** David sathau a kinilhna thu

### zong dingin = in order to seek / to look for
**Context:** Indicates the purpose of the Philistines' movement towards David.
**Example:** David a zong dingin Filistia mi khempeuh kuanto uh hi.

### kuankhia = went out / marched out
**Context:** David's action of moving out to confront the enemy.
**Example:** David in zong tua thu za ahih manin amaute a do dingin a kuankhia hi.

### kuam sung = valley (lit. 'hollow/depression area')
**Context:** Geographical location where the Philistines spread out.
**Example:** Refaim kuamsung a luh uh hi.

### Pasian thu dong = inquired of God / asked God's word
**Context:** David seeking divine guidance before battle.
**Example:** Tua ciangin David in Pasian kiangah thu dong a,

### nong pia ding hiam? = Will you deliver (them)? (Interrogative)
**Context:** A question posed to God regarding victory.
**Example:** Keima khutsungah amaute nong pia ding hiam?

### phulkhap = breaking forth / bursting out
**Context:** Used metaphorically to describe the sudden and overwhelming nature of God's help, like bursting water.
**Example:** Tuikhang in a phulkhap bangin Pasian in...

### phukham = broke / shattered / defeated
**Context:** The action God performed against David's enemies.
**Example:** ka galte phukham hi.

### kici hi = is called / is named
**Context:** Used to state the resulting name of a place.
**Example:** tua mun a min Baalperazim kici hi.

### nusia uh = left behind / abandoned
**Context:** The Philistines abandoned their gods.
**Example:** Filistia mite in tua lai-ah a pasian-te uh nusia uh a,

### kihal = were burned
**Context:** The fate of the abandoned gods according to David's command.
**Example:** tuate kihal hi.

### kik = again (repetition)
**Context:** Indicates the Philistines returned to the valley.
**Example:** kuam sung hong luhkik lai uh hi.

### dotkik = inquired again
**Context:** David seeking counsel from God a second time.
**Example:** David in Pasian kiangah thu a dotkik ciangin

### nungah = after / behind
**Context:** Directional instruction from God not to pursue directly.
**Example:** Amaute' nungah na kuanto kei ding hi.

### kalsuan huging = sound of going / marching sound
**Context:** A specific auditory signal David was told to listen for before attacking.
**Example:** Lungli kungte dawnah kalsuan huging na zak ciangin

### vat = smite / strike down (battle term)
**Context:** The action David must take when he hears the sound.
**Example:** Filistia galkapte a vat dingin nangma mai-ah Pasian kuan khin hi.

### vatgawp = smote completely / defeated thoroughly
**Context:** The comprehensive defeat of the Philistine army.
**Example:** Filistia galkapte vatgawp uh hi.

### minthan'na = fame / renown
**Context:** David's growing reputation throughout the land.
**Example:** Tua ciangin David' minthan'na in gam khempeuh zel a,

### kikihtakna = fear / awe
**Context:** The respect/fear David inspired in other nations.
**Example:** minam dang khempeuh tungah amah kihtakna a tungsak hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **Pasian thu dong** = to inquire of God
  - Usage: Used when seeking divine counsel or direction, especially before major actions like battle.

- **Tuikhang in a phulkhap bangin** = like the breaking forth of waters
  - Usage: A simile used to describe a sudden, overwhelming, and decisive victory granted by God.

- **kalsuan huging na zak ciangin** = when you hear a sound of going (marching)
  - Usage: A conditional phrase indicating the precise moment to initiate military action based on a divine signal.

- **nangma mai-ah Pasian kuan khin hi** = God has gone forth before you
  - Usage: A phrase assuring David that God has already taken the lead in the battle.

## Grammar Patterns

- **A leh B nei hi.**
  - Structure for listing multiple items (wives/children) taken/born by the subject (David). 'leh' functions as 'and'.
  - Example: tapate leh tanute nei hi.

- **X in Y tungah Z a ci hi.**
  - Structure for reporting direct speech or a statement made to someone. (X said to Y that Z).
  - Example: Tua ciangin Topa in ama kiangah, 'Kuanto in...'

- **X ahih manin Y hi.**
  - Causal structure: Because X happened, Y is the result. (Since X, therefore Y).
  - Example: David in zong tua thu za ahih manin amaute a do dingin a kuankhia hi.

- **X ciangin Y kici hi.**
  - Naming convention: After X event/statement, the place/thing is called Y.
  - Example: tua mun a min Baalperazim kici hi.

- **Pasian in ama kiangah, '...' a ci hi.**
  - Standard structure for quoting God's direct instruction or response to an individual (David).
  - Example: Pasian in ama kiangah, 'Amaute' nungah na kuanto kei ding hi...'
