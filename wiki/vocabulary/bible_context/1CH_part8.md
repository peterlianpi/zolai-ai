# 1CH - Part 8

## Vocabulary with Context

### khuapi = city
**Context:** Used to denote a specific city name, e.g., Bethlehem khuapi (Bethlehem city).
**Example:** Bethlehem khuapi a sat Salma

### sat = father of / founder of
**Context:** In this genealogical context, it means 'father of' or 'founder of' a place/group, similar to 'the father of' in KJV.
**Example:** Bethlehem khuapi a sat Salma

### leh = and
**Context:** A conjunction used to connect nouns or phrases.
**Example:** Salma, leh Bethgader khuapi

### ahi-te hi = they were
**Context:** A common copula structure indicating past existence or identity for a plural subject.
**Example:** Haref ahi-te hi.

### tapa = son
**Context:** Standard term for 'son'.
**Example:** Shobal in tapa nei hi

### nei = had / possessed
**Context:** Used here with 'tapa' (son) to mean 'had sons'.
**Example:** Shobal in tapa nei hi

### mi a langte = half of the people/group
**Context:** Literally 'people of the other side/half'. Used here to translate 'half of the Manahethites'.
**Example:** Haroeh leh Menuhoth mi a langte pian’na ahi hi.

### pian’na = origin / descent / place where they were born
**Context:** Refers to the origin or lineage of the mentioned groups.
**Example:** Menuhoth mi a langte pian’na ahi hi.

### sungah = in / within
**Context:** Indicates location inside a boundary (Kiriath-jearim khua sungah = within the town of Kiriath-jearim).
**Example:** Kiriath-jearim khua sungah a teengte

### a teengte = those who dwelt / the inhabitants
**Context:** From 'teeng' (to dwell/live). Plural subject marker 'te' and relative marker 'a'.
**Example:** Kiriath-jearim khua sungah a teengte

### hong piang hi = came forth / originated
**Context:** Indicates the emergence or origin of groups from a preceding lineage.
**Example:** Zorah mite leh Estaol mite hong piang hi.

### suante = descendants / offspring
**Context:** Plural form of 'sun' (offspring/child), used for descendants.
**Example:** Salma’ suante in:

### minam = clan / tribe / family group
**Context:** Used to denote specific family or tribal divisions.
**Example:** Rekhab minamte tawh a kiteeng Ken mite

### tawih a kiteeng = intermarried with / joined with
**Context:** Implies a relationship or union between two groups (Kenites and Rechabites).
**Example:** Rekhab minamte tawh a kiteeng Ken mite

### laithei = scribe (literally 'writing tool' or 'writing people')
**Context:** Refers to the scribes mentioned in the KJV.
**Example:** Jabez-ah a teeng laithei minamte

### sung panin = from among / out of
**Context:** Indicates the source or group from which individuals were born or originated.
**Example:** David sung panin Hebron ah a piang tapate

### a ta cil = the first son / the firstborn
**Context:** Indicates birth order (cil = first).
**Example:** a ta cil Ammon

### numei = woman / lady
**Context:** Used to specify the mother's origin or status (e.g., Karmel numei Abigail = Abigail the Carmelitess).
**Example:** Karmel numei Abigail tawh

### kumpipa = king
**Context:** Title for a king (kumpipa Talmai = Talmai the king).
**Example:** Geshur kumpipa Talmai’ tanu

### tanu = daughter
**Context:** Standard term for daughter.
**Example:** Talmai’ tanu

### a zi = his wife
**Context:** Refers to the wife of the subject (e.g., Eglah his wife).
**Example:** a zi Eglah tawh

### kum sagih leh kha guk = seven years and six months
**Context:** A specific duration of time.
**Example:** kum sagih leh kha guk sung a ukna

### uk hi = he reigned
**Context:** Verb indicating the act of ruling or reigning.
**Example:** Hebron ah ama sung panin guk suak hi.

### kua pha-te hi = totaling nine
**Context:** Used at the end of a list of sons to state the total count (kua = nine).
**Example:** Eliada, leh Elifelet, kua pha-te hi.

### zineute’ = concubines'
**Context:** Possessive form referring to the concubines.
**Example:** a zineute’ tapate sim lohin

### sim lohin = not counting / excluding
**Context:** Used to exclude a group from a total count.
**Example:** a zineute’ tapate sim lohin

### zong = also / too
**Context:** An adverb emphasizing inclusion.
**Example:** tanu khat zong nei hi.

### suan leh khakte = descendants and offspring (sons and daughters)
**Context:** Used generally for the lineage following Solomon.
**Example:** Solomon’ suan leh khakte in:

## Phrases & Usage

- **a sat Salma** = Salma the father of
  - Usage: Used in genealogies to establish paternity or founding lineage for a place.

- **mi a langte** = half of the people
  - Usage: Used specifically to denote a division of a group into halves (e.g., half of the Manahethites).

- **a neih a tacil** = the firstborn whom he had
  - Usage: Indicates the first son born to David (or another father) by a specific mother.

- **a nu [Name] ahi** = whose mother was [Name]
  - Usage: Used to specify the mother when listing sons, often following the father's name.

- **a zi [Name] tawh a neih** = whom he had with his wife [Name]
  - Usage: Used to specify a son born through a recognized wife.

## Grammar Patterns

- **[Noun] khuapi a sat [Name]**
  - Structure identifying the founder/father of a city. (e.g., Bethlehem khuapi a sat Salma = Salma, the father of Bethlehem city).
  - Example: Bethlehem khuapi a sat Salma

- **[Name] in tapa nei hi: [Son 1], leh [Son 2] mi a langte pian’na ahi hi.**
  - Describes a father having sons, and then specifying the lineage/origin of those sons, including fractional divisions.
  - Example: Shobal in tapa nei hi: Haroeh leh Menuhoth mi a langte pian’na ahi hi.

- **[Group A] mite tawh a kiteeng [Group B] mite ahi-te hi.**
  - Structure indicating that one group is intermarried or allied with another group.
  - Example: Hihte in Rekhab minamte tawh a kiteeng Ken mite ahi-te hi.

- **David sung panin [Place]-ah a piang tapate in: [Son 1], [Son 2], ...**
  - Standard structure for listing sons born to David in a specific location (Hebron).
  - Example: David sung panin Hebron ah a piang tapate in: Jezre-el mi Ahinoam tawh a neih a ta cil Ammon;

- **[Duration] sung a ukna [Place]-ah ama sung panin [Number] suak hi.**
  - Describes the duration of reign in one location before moving to another.
  - Example: amah in kum sagih leh kha guk sung a ukna Hebron ah ama sung panin guk suak hi.
