# GEN - Part 2

## Vocabulary with Context

### leitung = world/earth
**Context:** Used to refer to the physical world or earth, often in contrast to heaven.
**Example:** leitung khua a vaksak dingin

### khua = darkness/place/area
**Context:** In this context, combined with 'leitung', it means 'the world's darkness' or 'the dark places of the earth'.
**Example:** leitung khua a vaksak dingin

### vaksak = to illuminate/to shine upon
**Context:** Used in the context of light being given to the darkness of the world.
**Example:** leitung khua a vaksak dingin

### vantung = heaven/firmament
**Context:** The expanse above, where the lights (sun, moon) are placed.
**Example:** vantungah khuavak hi uh hen

### khuavak = light/lights
**Context:** Refers to the celestial bodies placed in the heaven to give light.
**Example:** vantungah khuavak hi uh hen

### hen = let it be/may it be (hortative/permissive suffix)
**Context:** Used at the end of a command or request, similar to 'let them be'.
**Example:** khuavak hi uh hen

### ci = say/said
**Context:** The verb used for quoting speech.
**Example:** ci hi

### bangin = thus/in that way/similarly
**Context:** Indicates that the following action occurred according to what was said or commanded.
**Example:** Tua mah bangin piang pah hi.

### piang = to happen/to come into being/to be created
**Context:** Used to describe the immediate result of a command.
**Example:** piang pah hi

### pah = immediately/at once
**Context:** Adverb indicating immediacy.
**Example:** piang pah hi

### lian = great/large
**Context:** Used to describe the size of the lights created by God.
**Example:** khuavak lian nih bawl a

### lianzaw = greater (comparative)
**Context:** Used to compare the two lights.
**Example:** khuavak lianzaw pen sun a uk ding

### pen = the most/the one (superlative/marker)
**Context:** Used here with the comparative 'lianzaw' to emphasize the greater one, or simply marking the subject.
**Example:** khuavak lianzaw pen sun a uk ding

### sun = day
**Context:** The period ruled by the greater light.
**Example:** sun a uk ding

### uk = to rule/to govern
**Context:** The function assigned to the celestial bodies.
**Example:** sun a uk ding

### neuzaw = lesser (comparative)
**Context:** The smaller of the two lights.
**Example:** khuavak neuzaw pen zan a uk dingin

### zan = night
**Context:** The period ruled by the lesser light.
**Example:** zan a uk dingin

### aksite = stars
**Context:** The other celestial objects created by God.
**Example:** amah in aksite zong a bawl hi.

### set = to set/to place
**Context:** Used in the KJV translation context, implying placement in the firmament.
**Example:** And God set them in the firmament...

### divide = to divide/to separate
**Context:** The action performed by the lights regarding light and darkness.
**Example:** to divide the light from the darkness

### darkness = darkness
**Context:** The opposite of light.
**Example:** divide the light from the darkness

### bei = to end/to pass away
**Context:** Used to describe the passing of the night.
**Example:** Nitak hong bei-in

### zingsang = morning
**Context:** The time following the night.
**Example:** zingsang hong tung a

### tung = to arrive/to come
**Context:** Used for the arrival of morning.
**Example:** zingsang hong tung a

### ni lina = fourth day
**Context:** Indicates the sequence of creation days.
**Example:** ni lina ahi hi.

### honpi = abundantly/swarming things
**Context:** Refers to creatures that move or swarm in great numbers.
**Example:** a nungta ganhing honpite piangsak hen la

### vasate = birds/flying creatures
**Context:** Creatures that fly in the heaven.
**Example:** vasate leeng ta hen

### leeng = to fly/to soar
**Context:** The action of the birds.
**Example:** vasate leeng ta hen

### tuipi = sea/great water
**Context:** The body of water where sea creatures are to multiply.
**Example:** Tuipi sungah tampi a pha

### ganhing = animal/beast/creature
**Context:** General term for animals.
**Example:** nungta ganhing honpite

### golpi = great/large (often used for whales/large sea creatures)
**Context:** Describing the large sea creatures.
**Example:** tuipi ganhing golpite

### pha = to bring forth/to produce/to multiply
**Context:** Used for the creatures being brought forth abundantly.
**Example:** tampi a pha

### nuntakna = life
**Context:** Possessing life.
**Example:** nuntakna a nei a gamtang thei ganhing

### gamtang = to move/to crawl/to creep
**Context:** Describing the movement of creatures.
**Example:** a gamtang thei ganhing

### kha = spirit/breath
**Context:** Used here in connection with flying creatures, likely meaning 'having breath/life'.
**Example:** kha a nei vasate

### mu = to see/to perceive
**Context:** Used when God observes that creation is good.
**Example:** ci-in mu hi.

### thupha pia = to bless
**Context:** The act of giving a blessing.
**Example:** Pasian in amaute thupha pia a

### dim = to fill
**Context:** The command to fill the waters.
**Example:** tuipi sung dim un la

### ni nga-na = fifth day
**Context:** The sequence of creation days.
**Example:** ni nga-na ahi hi.

### innsa = domestic animal/livestock
**Context:** Category of land animals created on the sixth day.
**Example:** Innsa, gamsa, leh a bokvak ganhingte

### gamsa = wild beast/animal
**Context:** Category of land animals.
**Example:** Innsa, gamsa, leh a bokvak ganhingte

### bokvak = creeping thing
**Context:** Animals that crawl on the ground.
**Example:** a bokvak ganhingte

### eima = our (exclusive)
**Context:** Used when God speaks of making man in 'our' image.
**Example:** Eima' lim

### lim = image/likeness
**Context:** The form in which man is created.
**Example:** Eima' lim, eimah hong suunin

### hong suun = to resemble/to be like
**Context:** Used in the phrase 'in our likeness'.
**Example:** eimah hong suunin mihing bawl ni

### mihing = human being/man
**Context:** The creation being discussed.
**Example:** mihing bawl ni

### ukna neisak = to give dominion/to cause to rule
**Context:** The command given to humanity over creation.
**Example:** ukna neisak ni

### pasal = male/man
**Context:** One of the genders created.
**Example:** amah in pasal leh numei piangsak hi.

### numei = female/woman
**Context:** The other gender created.
**Example:** pasal leh numei piangsak hi.

### replenish = to fill up/to replenish
**Context:** Part of the blessing given to man (similar to 'multiply and fill').
**Example:** leitung dimin zo un

### zo = to conquer/to subdue/to fill completely
**Context:** Used in the context of subduing the earth.
**Example:** leitung dimin zo un

### En un = Behold/Look
**Context:** An introductory phrase used by God to draw attention to a statement.
**Example:** Pasian in, “En un, ...

### tang = fruit/seed
**Context:** Refers to the fruit/seed-bearing plants given for food.
**Example:** tang a piangsak kungnote

### kungnote = trees
**Context:** The source of the fruit/seed.
**Example:** tang a piangsak kungnote khempeuh

### kong = food/provision
**Context:** What is given to man to eat.
**Example:** kong pia khin ta hi

### an = to eat
**Context:** The purpose of the provision given.
**Example:** tuate an-in na nei ding uh hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **leitung khua a vaksak dingin** = in order to illuminate the darkness of the world
  - Usage: Used to state the purpose of the lights placed in the firmament.

- **ci pha-in tampi pha-in** = Be fruitful and multiply
  - Usage: A standard blessing formula used when commanding creatures or humans to increase in number.

- **a nungta ganhing honpite** = the moving creatures abundantly
  - Usage: Refers to teeming, living creatures, especially those in the water.

- **Eima' lim, eimah hong suunin** = In our image, after our likeness
  - Usage: The key phrase describing the manner in which humanity is created.

- **ukna neisak ni** = let them have dominion
  - Usage: The command given for governance over creation.

- **tang a piangsak kungnote** = trees yielding seed
  - Usage: Describes the type of vegetation given as food.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X a Y-ding**
  - Purpose clause structure. X is the object/goal, Y is the verb, and '-ding' indicates 'for the purpose of Y'. (e.g., sun a uk-ding = for ruling the day).
  - Example: khuavak lianzaw pen sun a uk ding

- **Nitak hong bei-in zingsang hong tung a, ...**
  - Sequential temporal clause structure indicating the passing of time (Night passes, morning arrives).
  - Example: Nitak hong bei-in zingsang hong tung a, ni lina ahi hi.

- **Pasian in, '...' ci hi.**
  - Standard structure for quoting God's speech: Subject (Pasian) + Quotation + Verb of saying (ci hi).
  - Example: Pasian in, “Tuite in a nungta ganhing honpite piangsak hen la, ...” ci hi.

- **X khempeuh amau nam ciatin**
  - Structure emphasizing totality and kind: 'All X, according to their kind'.
  - Example: ganhing namkim leh kha a nei vasate khempeuh amau nam ciatin piangsak hi.

- **X leh Y leh Z khempeuh' tungah ukna neisak ni**
  - Listing items (X, Y, and Z) followed by 'khempeuh' (all of them) and the command 'tungah ukna neisak ni' (give dominion over them).
  - Example: Tuipi ngasate' tung, huih lak vasate' tung, ganhingte' tung, ... tungah ukna neisak ni
