# Job Chapter 23 — Grammar Study

## Grammar Patterns

| Pattern | Count |
|---|---|
| reciprocal: ki- prefix | 5 |
| future: will | 5 |
| negation: kei | 4 |
| purpose: in order to | 2 |
| sequence: then/therefore | 1 |
| serial verb: -in (and then) | 1 |

## Verses

### v1
**EN:** Then Job answered and said,

**TDB77:** Tua ciangin Job in dawng a,

**Grammar:** sequence: then/therefore

### v2
**EN:** Even to day is my complaint bitter: my stroke is heavier than my groaning.

**TDB77:** “Tu lai takin Pasian tungah phun lo, heh lo in ka om thei kei a, thuum loin ka om thei kei hi.

**ZVS:** “Tu laitakin Pasian' tungah a phunlo, a hehloin ka om thei kei a, thumloin ka om thei kei hi.

**Grammar:** negation: kei

### v3
**EN:** Oh that I knew where I might find him! that I might come even to his seat!

**TDB77:** Amah ka zon’na ding dan thei-in, ama kiang ka tun’na ding zia thei hi leng,

**ZVS:** Amah ka zon' dan ding thei-in, ama kiang ka tun'na ding zia a thei hi leng,

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then) | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v4
**EN:** I would order my cause before him, and fill my mouth with arguments.

**TDB77:** ama mai-ah ka thu ka gen ding a, kei ka dikna thu a kicingin ka gen ding hi.

**ZVS:** a mai-ah ka thu ka gen ding a, kei ka dikna thu a kicingin ka gen ding hi.

**Grammar:** future: will | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v5
**EN:** I would know the words which he would answer me, and understand what he would say unto me.

**TDB77:** Ama hong gen ding thute leh kei hong dawn’ nading thute ka thei nuam hi.

**ZVS:** Ama hong gen ding thute leh kei a hong dawn'na ding thute ka thei nuam hi.

### v6
**EN:** Will he plead against me with his great power? No; but he would put strength in me.

**TDB77:** Pasian in a thaneih khempeuh tawh kei hong langpan’ ding ahi hiam? Langpan’ lo ding hi, ka thugenna ngai ding hi.

**ZVS:** Pasian in a thaneih khempeuh tawh keima langkhatah hong pang ding ahi hiam? Hong langpan'lo ding a, ka thugen hong ngai ding hi.

**Grammar:** future: will

### v7
**EN:** There the righteous might dispute with him; so should I be delivered for ever from my judge.

**TDB77:** Kei a thuman ka hi a, Pasian kiangah ka gen thei ding hi; kuama phel zawh nawn loh dingin kei’ dikna genkhia ding hi.

**ZVS:** Kei a thuman ka hi a, Pasian kiangah ka gen ding hi. Kuama phelzawh nawnloh dingin kei' dikna a genkhia ding hi.

**Grammar:** purpose: in order to | future: will | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v8
**EN:** Behold, I go forward, but he is not there; and backward, but I cannot perceive him:

**TDB77:** “Ahi zongin Nisuahna lam ka zong a, Pasian ka mu thei kei hi; Nitumna lam ka zong a, amah ka mu thei tuan kei hi.

**ZVS:** “Ahi zongin Nisuahna lam ka zong a, Pasian ka mu kei hi. Nitumna lam ka zong a, amah ka mu thei tuan kei hi.

**Grammar:** negation: kei

### v9
**EN:** On the left hand, where he doth work, but I cannot behold him: he hideth himself on the right hand, that I cannot see him:

**TDB77:** Leilu lam ka zon’ hangin amah ka mu thei kei a, Leitaw lam ka zon’ hangin zong amah ka mu thei tuan kei hi.

**ZVS:** Leilu lam ka zon' hangin amah ka mu thei kei a, Leitaw lam ka zon' hangin zong amah ka mu thei tuan kei hi.

**Grammar:** negation: kei

### v10
**EN:** But he knoweth the way that I take: when he hath tried me, I shall come forth as gold.

**TDB77:** Ahi zongin Pasian in ka kalsuanna khempeuh thei a, kei hong ze-et khit ciangin kham bangin ka sian’thona a mu ding hi.

**ZVS:** Ahi zongin Pasian in ka kalsuanna khempeuh thei a, kei hong ze-et leh ka sian'thona a mu ding hi.

**Grammar:** future: will

### v11
**EN:** My foot hath held his steps, his way have I kept, and not declined.

**TDB77:** Ama hong lamlahna citakin ka zui a, lam ka pial ngei kei hi.

**ZVS:** Ama hong lamlahna citakin ka zui cinten a, lam ka pial ngei kei hi.

**Grammar:** negation: kei

### v12
**EN:** Neither have I gone back from the commandment of his lips; I have esteemed the words of his mouth more than my necessary food.

**TDB77:** Pasian’ thupiakna bangin ka gamta ngitnget a, keima utna zui loin, ama deihna ka zui hi.

**ZVS:** Pasian' thupiakna bangin ka gamta ngitnget a, keima utna zuiloin ama deihna ka zui hi.

### v13
**EN:** But he is in one mind, and who can turn him? and what his soul desireth, even that he doeth.

**TDB77:** Amah in a lungsim kheel mi hi lo a, kuamah in amah nial zo lo hi. Ama hihnopte kuamah in khaktan zo lo hi.

**ZVS:** “Amah kikheel ngeilo a, kuamah in amah nial zolo hi. Ama hih nopte kuamah in khaktan zolo hi.

**Grammar:** negation: lo

### v14
**EN:** For he performeth the thing that is appointed for me: and many such things are with him.

**TDB77:** Tua ahih manin kei’ adingin a geelsate amah in tungsak ding hi. Amah in hih banga geelna tampi nei hi.

**ZVS:** Tua ahih manin kei-a' dingin a geelsate tungsak ding hi. Amah in hih bangin geelna tampi tak a nei ahi hi.

**Grammar:** purpose: in order to | future: will | causal: because

### v15
**EN:** Therefore am I troubled at his presence: when I consider, I am afraid of him.

**TDB77:** Tua hi a, ama omna-ah kei ka lau mahmah a, ka ngaihsut khak ciangin amah ka kihta hi.

**Grammar:** intensifier: very/greatly | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v16
**EN:** For God maketh my heart soft, and the Almighty troubleth me:

**TDB77:** Vanglian Pasian in ka lungsim hanna hong maimangsak hi.

**ZVS:** Vanglian Pasian in ka lungsim han'na hong mangsak hi.

### v17
**EN:** Because I was not cut off before the darkness, neither hath he covered the darkness from my face.

**TDB77:** Khuamial in ka mit hong mialsak hangin keima kihtak in khuamial hilo a, Pasian ahi hi.

**ZVS:** Khuamial in ka mit a mialsak hangin keima kihtak pen khuamial hilo a, Pasian ahi hi.

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix
