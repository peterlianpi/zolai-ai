# 1CH - Part 38

## Vocabulary with Context

### Lungdamna aw = voice of thanksgiving/joy
**Context:** Used to describe the sound or song that should be made for thanksgiving.
**Example:** Lungdamna aw gin’sak nadingin, la sakna nate...

### gin’sak = to make sound/to sound forth
**Context:** Used in connection with 'Lungdamna aw' (voice of thanksgiving) indicating the action of producing that sound.
**Example:** Lungdamna aw gin’sak nadingin...

### daltuahte = cymbals
**Context:** Listed as one of the musical instruments to be played loudly.
**Example:** harp lasakna leh laiza lasaknate leh daltuahte nakpi takin tumin la a sa dingin...

### nakpi takin = loudly/with great force
**Context:** Adverbial phrase modifying the verb 'tum' (to sound/play), emphasizing the volume.
**Example:** daltuahte nakpi takin tumin la a sa dingin...

### seh = to command/to instruct/to order
**Context:** Used for David giving an instruction to the Levitical leaders.
**Example:** Levi ukpite in a mipihte-te a seh nading David in thu pia hi.

### beh = clan/family/branch (of a tribe)
**Context:** Used to specify the lineage or division within the Levites.
**Example:** tua ciangin a behte sung panin Berekhiah’ tapa Asaf...

### kongcingte = doorkeepers/gatekeepers
**Context:** A specific role assigned to certain Levites, particularly concerning the Ark.
**Example:** leh kongcingte Obededom leh Jeiel seh-te hi.

### sumngo daltuahte = cymbals of brass
**Context:** Specific type of cymbals mentioned in relation to the singers Heman, Asaph, and Ethan.
**Example:** Lasate Heman, Asaf, leh Ethan-te in sumngo daltuahte a tum ding-te ahi-te hi;

### paisuak-aw-in = with a clear sound/with a high pitch (often associated with stringed instruments like the lyre/psaltery)
**Context:** Describes how the psalteries were to be played, likely indicating a specific register or style.
**Example:** Zekhariah, Aziel, ... harp lasaknate a tum ding-te ahi hi;

### un-aw-in = with a deep/low sound (often associated with the Sheminith)
**Context:** Describes the manner of playing the harps, contrasting with 'paisuak-aw-in'.
**Example:** ahih hangin Mattihiah, ... laiza lasaknate a un aw-in makai ding ahi-te hi.

### makai = to lead/leader
**Context:** Used here as a verb/role indicating leadership in the musical performance.
**Example:** laiza lasaknate a un aw-in makai ding ahi-te hi.

### thei = to know/to be skilled/expert
**Context:** Used to explain why Chenaniah was appointed as the song leader—because he was skillful.
**Example:** bang hang hiam cih leh amah in thei hi.

### Thuciamna Singkuang = Ark of the Covenant
**Context:** The sacred chest central to the procession and worship.
**Example:** Berekhiah leh Elkanah-te pen Thuciamna Singkuang-a' dingin kongcingte hi ding-te hi.

### peengkulte = trumpets
**Context:** Instruments blown by the priests before the Ark.
**Example:** Pasian’ thuciamna singkuang mai-ah peengkulte tum ding-te hi.

### saihadial puan tualpi = robe of fine linen
**Context:** The specific garment David wore during the procession.
**Example:** David in saihadial puan tualpi silh a...

### otna tawh = with shouting/exultation
**Context:** Describes the manner of movement during the procession of the Ark.
**Example:** awging tawh kizui-in otna tawh Topa’ thuciamna singkuang paipih to-te a...

### neu-et = to despise/to look down upon
**Context:** Michal's reaction to seeing David dancing joyfully before the Lord.
**Example:** Tua ciangin amah in a lungsim sungah David a neu-et hi.

### lamsak puanbuk = tent of meeting/tabernacle (specifically the one David prepared)
**Context:** The structure where the Ark was placed after being brought to the city.
**Example:** David in ama-a' dingin a lamsak puanbuk sungah koih-te hi.

### meihal biakna = burnt offerings
**Context:** Sacrifices offered before God after the Ark was set in place.
**Example:** Tua ciangin amaute in meihal biaknate leh thunuama biaknate Pasian' mai-ah pia-te hi.

### thu-nuama biaknate = peace offerings
**Context:** Sacrifices offered alongside burnt offerings.
**Example:** meihal biaknate leh thunuama biaknate Pasian' mai-ah pia-te hi.

## Phrases & Usage

- **nakpi takin tumin la a sa dingin** = in order to sing loudly/with great sound
  - Usage: Used to express the purpose or manner of musical performance.

- **a beh sung panin** = from within his clan/family branch
  - Usage: Used to specify the lineage or group from which an individual was selected.

- **a paisuak aw-in** = with a high/clear sound (playing)
  - Usage: Describes the specific technique for playing stringed instruments (psalteries).

- **a un aw-in** = with a deep/low sound (playing)
  - Usage: Describes the specific technique for playing harps (on the Sheminith).

- **bang hang hiam cih leh** = because/for the reason that
  - Usage: Introduces the explanation or justification for an action or appointment.

- **Topa’ thuciamna singkuang a paipih to dingin** = in order to bring up the Ark of the Covenant of the Lord
  - Usage: Expresses the goal of the procession.

## Grammar Patterns

- **Noun + tapa + Name**
  - Standard structure for indicating 'son of' in genealogies (e.g., Joel's son Heman).
  - Example: Joel’ tapa Heman

- **Verb + ding + a/uh + hi**
  - Future/Intentional structure indicating what someone 'will do' or 'is appointed to do'. (e.g., 'a tum ding-te ahi hi' - they are to sound/play).
  - Example: daltuahte a tum ding-te ahi-te hi;

- **Noun + sungah + Noun + neu-et hi**
  - Structure showing an internal emotional reaction (despising/looking down upon) directed towards someone.
  - Example: amah in a lungsim sungah David a neu-et hi.

- **Noun + in + Verb + a/uh + hi**
  - Simple past tense structure indicating the action performed by the subject (e.g., 'Levi mite in... seh-te hi' - the Levites appointed/designated).
  - Example: Levi mite in Joel' tapa Heman seh-te hi.
