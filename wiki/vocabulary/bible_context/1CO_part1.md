# 1CO - Part 1

## Vocabulary with Context

### sawltak = apostle/messenger
**Context:** Referring to Paul being sent by Christ.
**Example:** Khazih Zeisu’ sawltak za-ah hong koih

### sanggampa = brother (in faith/fellowship)
**Context:** Used to refer to Sosthenes alongside Paul.
**Example:** keimah Paul leh i sanggampa Sosthenes in

### mawhna = sin/guilt
**Context:** Used in connection with being set apart/called.
**Example:** Khazih Zeisu hangin mawhna tawh kipelh

### misiangtho = saint/holy person
**Context:** Describing the status of the believers in Corinth.
**Example:** misiangtho dinga hong koih ahi Korin khua ah

### pawlpite = church/assembly/group of believers
**Context:** Referring to the church in Corinth.
**Example:** Pasian’ pawlpite akipan

### kipelh = to be set apart/separated (often used for sanctification)
**Context:** Being set apart for holiness through Christ.
**Example:** mawhna tawh kipelh

### hehpihna = grace/favor
**Context:** A standard greeting element, grace from God.
**Example:** hehpihna thupha leh nopna nomau tungah hong tung ta hen.

### nopna = peace
**Context:** Paired with grace as a blessing.
**Example:** hehpihna thupha leh nopna nomau tungah hong tung ta hen.

### thu tecipan’na = testimony/witnessing/confirmation
**Context:** The confirmation of Christ's word among them.
**Example:** Khazih’ thu tecipan’na nomau lakah a kip ahih ciangin

### kicing = to be complete/abundant/enriched
**Context:** Describing how they were enriched in all things.
**Example:** na khempeuh na kicing-te a

### thu genna = utterance/speech/speaking ability
**Context:** One of the gifts they were enriched in.
**Example:** thu genna leh lungpilna a kipan

### lungpilna = knowledge/understanding
**Context:** Paired with utterance as a gift they possessed.
**Example:** thu genna leh lungpilna a kipan

### kilat ding = to be revealed/manifested (often referring to Christ's return)
**Context:** Waiting for the revelation/coming of the Lord Jesus Christ.
**Example:** i Topa Zeisu Khazih hong kilat ding ngakin na om lai takun

### tawp = end/consummation
**Context:** Until the end, when Christ confirms them.
**Example:** a tawp dongin no hong kipsak ding hi.

### kipsak = to keep/sustain/confirm
**Context:** Christ will sustain them until the end.
**Example:** no hong kipsak ding hi.

### mawhsak nading = blamelessness/faultlessness
**Context:** The state they will be in on the Day of the Lord.
**Example:** mawhsak nading tawh na kipelh nadingun

### ciamtehna = testimony/assurance/pledge
**Context:** Asking for unity in testimony/understanding.
**Example:** kiciamtehna kikhatin

### kikhenthang = division/schism
**Context:** A plea against divisions among them.
**Example:** kikhenthang loin

### kikhatsak = to be united/made one
**Context:** The goal of their unity in mind and judgment.
**Example:** kicing takin na om nadingun

### kitawng = contentions/disputes/quarrels
**Context:** The issue reported by Chloe's household.
**Example:** note lakah a kitawng om hi ci-in

### singlamteh = cross (literally 'wood-frame')
**Context:** Referring to Christ's crucifixion.
**Example:** singlamteh tungah a si hiam?

### kibaptaiz = to be baptized (in the name of)
**Context:** Questioning if they were baptized in Paul's name.
**Example:** Paul’ min tawh kibaptaiz na hi-te hiam?

### tuiphum = to baptize (literally 'to dip in water')
**Context:** Paul expressing joy that he baptized few people.
**Example:** note khat beek hong tuiphumlo ka hih manin

## Phrases & Usage

- **Pasian’ deihna hangin** = Through the will of God
  - Usage: Indicates the divine source or reason for an action (e.g., being called as an apostle).

- **mawhna tawh kipelh** = Set apart from sin/sanctified
  - Usage: Describes the state of believers who are consecrated to God.

- **mun khempeuh ah** = In every place
  - Usage: Used to denote universality or scope (all locations).

- **lungsim kikhatin** = With one mind/thought
  - Usage: Used when urging spiritual unity in thought and purpose.

- **a tawp dongin** = Until the end
  - Usage: Specifies the duration until a final event (like Christ's return).

- **Khazih a kikhen hiam?** = Is Christ divided?
  - Usage: Rhetorical question used to challenge factionalism.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X'sui/hangin/tawh**
  - Indicates agency, cause, or means ('by means of X' or 'because of X').
  - Example: Pasian’ deihna hangin (by the will of God); Khazih Zeisu hangin (through Jesus Christ)

- **A hong koih / hong ko**
  - Use of the prefix 'hong-' indicating action directed towards the speaker/audience (benefactive/directional marker). 'koih' means to place/appoint.
  - Example: sawltak za-ah hong koih (appointed us as apostles)

- **X kiangah thu a ngen mi**
  - A relative clause structure meaning 'those who call upon X'.
  - Example: Topa Zeisu Khazih kiangah thu a ngen mi khempeuhte (all those who call upon the name of the Lord Jesus Christ)

- **Note tungah X hong tungsak ta hen.**
  - A common benediction structure: 'May X come upon you (note tungah)'.
  - Example: hehpihna thupha leh nopna nomau tungah hong tung ta hen (Grace and peace be unto you).

- **Note in, '...' na ci tek-te ka cihna ahi hi.**
  - Reporting speech structure: 'When I say this, I mean that some of you say, '...''.
  - Example: Note in, “Kei Paul’ mi ka hi...” na ci tek-te ka cihna ahi hi.
