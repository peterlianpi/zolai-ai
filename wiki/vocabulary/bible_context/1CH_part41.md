# 1CH - Part 41

## Vocabulary with Context

### awng = roar/sound out
**Context:** Used for the sea making a loud sound (roaring).
**Example:** Tuipi leh a sunga om khempeuh awng hen la

### diang = rejoice/be glad
**Context:** Used for the fields rejoicing.
**Example:** gamlak leh a sunga om khempeuh diang hen!

### lungdamin = with gladness/joyfully
**Context:** Adverbial form of 'lungdam' (gladness), describing how the fields should rejoice.
**Example:** gamlak leh a sunga om khempeuh lungdamin diang ta-te hen!

### singte = trees (plural)
**Context:** Referring to the trees of the wood/forest.
**Example:** Tua ciangin gamlak singte in lungdamin Topa mai-ah la sa ding-te hi

### mai-ah = before/in the presence of
**Context:** Indicates location relative to the Lord (Topa mai-ah = in the presence of the Lord).
**Example:** Topa mai-ah la sa ding-te hi

### thukhen = judge/discern matters
**Context:** Referring to the Lord coming to judge the world.
**Example:** leitung thukhen dingin amah hong pai hi.

### lungdamna = thanksgiving/gladness
**Context:** The act of giving thanks to the Lord.
**Example:** Topa tungah lungdamna ko un

### kip = eternal/everlasting (often used with 'tawntung')
**Context:** Describing the nature of His love (a itna kip a tawntungin).
**Example:** bang hang hiam cih leh a itna kip a tawntungin a kho hi!

### khahkhia = save/deliver (from trouble)
**Context:** A plea to be saved or delivered.
**Example:** Kote hong khahkhia in

### kihotkhiatna = salvation (noun form)
**Context:** Referring to God as the God of salvation.
**Example:** ka kihotkhiatna Pasian aw

### minamte = nations/peoples (plural)
**Context:** From among the nations/heathen.
**Example:** minamte lak panin hong kaikhawmin

### minthan = be glorified/be made famous
**Context:** The purpose of deliverance is to glorify His name.
**Example:** nangmah phatna ah ka minthan’ nadingun

### thupha = blessed (verb/state)
**Context:** A declaration that the Lord should be blessed.
**Example:** Israel Pasian, Topa in thupha ngah ta hen

### tawntung = forever/eternally
**Context:** Used to denote duration from beginning to end.
**Example:** a tawntung pan a tawntung dongin!

### thuciamna singkuang = Ark of the Covenant
**Context:** The specific religious artifact.
**Example:** Topa thuciamna singkuang mai-ah nusia a

### nusia = left behind/stationed
**Context:** David stationed certain people before the Ark.
**Example:** Asaf leh a Levi mipihte Topa’ thuciamna singkuang mai-ah nusia a

### sawmguk-le-giat = sixty-eight (a specific number)
**Context:** The count of Obededom's family members stationed.
**Example:** Obededom leh a behte sawmguk-le-giatte zong nusia hi

### cing = guard/porter/gatekeeper
**Context:** The role assigned to Jeduthun's sons.
**Example:** kongpi cing dingte ahi-te hi.

### meihal biakna = burnt offering
**Context:** The type of sacrifice offered on the altar.
**Example:** meihal biaknate a pia tawntung dingin

### kilo khia = chosen/selected (literally: made to appear/stand out)
**Context:** Referring to those chosen to give thanks.
**Example:** a minin a kilo khia a om laite om hi.

### peengkulte = trumpets
**Context:** Musical instruments used by Heman and Jeduthun.
**Example:** Heman leh Jeduthun in la aw dingin peengkulte leh daltuahte nei-te a

### daltuahte = cymbals
**Context:** Musical instruments used alongside trumpets.
**Example:** peengkulte leh daltuahte nei-te a

### taamtaak = cedar (adjective form)
**Context:** Describing the house David lived in (house of cedars).
**Example:** keimah in taamtaak inn hoih sungah ka teeng a

### puanbuk = tent/tabernacle (specifically the tent covering the Ark)
**Context:** The temporary dwelling place of the Ark of the Covenant.
**Example:** ahi hangin puanbuk nuai-ah { Topa' } Thuciamna Singkuang om lai hi

### ompih = be with/accompany
**Context:** Nathan assuring David that God is with him.
**Example:** Pasian in nang hong ompih ahih manin

## Phrases & Usage

- **a tawntung pan a tawntung dongin** = from everlasting to everlasting
  - Usage: Used to express eternal duration, often in blessings or declarations about God's nature.

- **Topa mai-ah** = in the presence of the Lord
  - Usage: Indicates performing an action directly before God or in His sanctuary.

- **lungdamna kong koh theihna ding uh** = so that we may offer thanksgiving
  - Usage: Expresses the purpose or reason for an action (purpose clause).

- **a itna kip a tawntungin a kho hi** = His love endures forever
  - Usage: A common theological statement affirming the permanence of God's love.

## Grammar Patterns

- **X hen la, Y hen!**
  - Imperative structure using the suffix '-hen' (let it be so/command) followed by 'la' (and/then), used for parallel commands or wishes directed towards inanimate objects or forces (e.g., Let the sea roar, and let the fields rejoice).
  - Example: Tuipi leh a sunga om khempeuh awng hen la, gamlak leh a sunga om khempeuh diang hen!

- **bang hang hiam cih leh**
  - A rhetorical structure meaning 'Why? Because...' or 'For the reason that...', introducing the justification for the preceding statement.
  - Example: la sa ding-te hi, bang hang hiam cih leh leitung thukhen dingin amah hong pai hi.

- **X tungah lungdamna ko un**
  - Imperative structure: 'Give thanks to X' (X tungah = upon X; lungdamna = thanks; ko un = offer/put).
  - Example: Topa tungah lungdamna ko un

- **Ahih hangin tua zan mahin Y hong tung a**
  - Contrastive structure: 'However, that very night Y came to him.' Used to introduce a divine intervention or counterpoint to a previous situation.
  - Example: Ahih hangin tua zan mahin Nathan’ kiangah Topa’ thu hong tung a
