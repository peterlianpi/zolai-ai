## Verses

### v1
**EN:** Dead flies cause the ointment of the apothecary to send forth a stinking savour: so doth a little folly him that is in reputation for wisdom and honour.

**TDB77:** A sisa thote in paknamtui bung sunga om paknamtui uih sak thei a, haitatna themno khat in pilna lianpen susia thei hi.

**ZVS:** A sisa thote in paknamtui bung sungah a om paknamtui uihsak thei a, haitatna themno khat in pilna lianpen susia thei hi.

### v2
**EN:** A wise man's heart is at his right hand; but a fool's heart at his left.

**TDB77:** Mipilte a man-a a gamtat ding a ngeina hi a, mihaite a sia-a gamtat ding a ngeina ahi hi.

**ZVS:** Mipilte a manin a gamtat ding a ngeina hi a, mihaite a sia-in a gamtat ding a ngeina ahi hi.

### v3
**EN:** Yea also, when he that is a fool walketh by the way, his wisdom faileth him, and he saith to every one that he is a fool.

**TDB77:** Mihaite, lampi-ah a pai kawm nangawnin haitat uh a, a haina uh mi khempeuh theisak uh hi.

**ZVS:** Mihaite lampi-ah a paikawm nangawnin hai tat uh a, a haina uh mi khempeuh theisak uh hi.

**Grammar:** plural: uh marker

### v4
**EN:** If the spirit of the ruler rise up against thee, leave not thy place; for yielding pacifieth great offences.

**TDB77:** Nang tungah gam-ukpa a heh leh, na nasepna za taisan pah kei in. Dai tak-a om hithiatna in khialhna lianpi maisak thei hi.

**ZVS:** Nang tungah gam ukpa a heh leh na nasep za taisan pah kei in. Dai tak-a om hithiatna in khialhna lianpi maisak thei hi.

### v5
**EN:** There is an evil which I have seen under the sun, as an error which proceedeth from the ruler:

**TDB77:** Hih leitungah a thuman lo, gam-ukte’ pian’sak ka muhte in:

**ZVS:** Hih leitungah a thumanlo gam ukte' pian'sak ka muhte:

### v6
**EN:** Folly is set in great dignity, and the rich sit in low place.

**TDB77:** Mihaite, za lianpi kipia a, mihaute kiniam koih suk hi.

**ZVS:** Mihaite, zalianpi kipia a, mihaute kiniamkoih suk hi.

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v7
**EN:** I have seen servants upon horses, and princes walking as servants upon the earth.

**TDB77:** Sakol tungah silate tuang a, miliante sila bangin a khe-un pai uh hi.

**ZVS:** Sakol tungah silate tuang a, miliante, sila bangin a khe-un pai uh hi.

**Grammar:** plural: uh marker

### v8
**EN:** He that diggeth a pit shall fall into it; and whoso breaketh an hedge, a serpent shall bite him.

**TDB77:** Gum a tote, gum sung mah-ah kia ding a, mi’ phung a phelsakte, gul in tu ding hi.

**ZVS:** Gum a tote gum sung mahah kia ding a, mi' phung a phelsakte gul in tu ding hi.

**Grammar:** future: will | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v9
**EN:** Whoso removeth stones shall be hurt therewith; and he that cleaveth wood shall be endangered thereby.

**TDB77:** Suang tokhiate, suang mah in liam sak ding a, sing-ekte, sing ehna mah-ah liam ding hi.

**ZVS:** Suang a tokhiate suang mah in liamsak ding a, sing a ekte sing ehna mahah liam ding hi.

**Grammar:** future: will

### v10
**EN:** If the iron be blunt, and he do not whet the edge, then must he put to more strength: but wisdom is profitable to direct.

**TDB77:** I hei mawl napi-in i tat kei leh, nakpi takin sat kul a, ngaihsut masak ding pil huaizaw hi.

**ZVS:** I hei mawlnapi-in i tat kei leh nakpi takin sat kul a, ngaihsut masak ding pilhuai zaw hi.

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then)

### v11
**EN:** Surely the serpent will bite without enchantment; and a babbler is no better.

**TDB77:** Gul khiim thei napi-in i khiim kei leh, a kiman’na om tuan lo hi; gul in hong tu veve ding hi.

**ZVS:** Gul khimthei napi i khim kei leh a kiman'na om tuanlo hi; gul in hong tu veve ding hi.

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then) | future: will | negation: lo | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v12
**EN:** The words of a wise man's mouth are gracious; but the lips of a fool will swallow up himself.

**TDB77:** Mipilte’ kampauna in pahtawina ngahsak a, mihaite’ kampauna in amau mah siasak hi.

**ZVS:** Mipilte' kampauna in pahtawina ngahsak a, mihaite' kampauna in amau mah siasak hi.

### v13
**EN:** The beginning of the words of his mouth is foolishness: and the end of his talk is mischievous madness.

**TDB77:** Thugen a kipatun bang tum lo tawh kipan pah malam uh a, gitloh hai vai teng tawh thugen khup lel uh hi.

**ZVS:** Thugen a kipat ciangun bangtumlo teng tawh kipan pah ma-lam uh a, gitloh vai teng tawh a thugen a khum lel uh hi.

**Grammar:** reciprocal: ki- prefix | plural: uh marker

### v14
**EN:** A fool also is full of words: a man cannot tell what shall be; and what shall be after him, who can tell him?

**TDB77:** Mihaite in thu gengenin ciakciak uh hi. Mai lam-a thupiang ding kuamah in thei lo a, i sih khit-a thupiang ding hong gen thei om lo hi.

**ZVS:** Mihaite in thu gengenin ciakciak uh hi. Mailam-a thupiang ding kuamah in theilo a, a sih khit-a thupiang ding a hong gen thei omlo hi.

**Grammar:** negation: lo | plural: uh marker

### v15
**EN:** The labour of the foolish wearieth every one of them, because he knoweth not how to go to the city.

**TDB77:** A inn zuat nading lampi a thei lo mihai luate bek, a innlam zon’na in gim ding uh hi.

**ZVS:** A inn zuatna ding lampi a theilo mihai luate bek, a inn lam zon'na-in gim ding uh hi.

**Grammar:** future: will | plural: uh marker

### v16
**EN:** Woe to thee, O land, when thy king is a child, and thy princes eat in the morning!

**TDB77:** A kumpipa mite’ cih bang bangin gamta lel a, zan khuavakin zu ne makai a nei, gam khatpeuh in lungkhamna thuak hi.

**ZVS:** A kumpipa mi' cih bangbangin gamta lel a, zankhuavak-in zune makai-a a nei, gam khat peuhpeuh in lungkhamna thuak hi.

### v17
**EN:** Blessed art thou, O land, when thy king is the son of nobles, and thy princes eat in due season, for strength, and not for drunkenness!

**TDB77:** A kumpipa in lungsim khentatna nei thei a, a makaite in annek tuidawn a hun thei a, amau ki-uk thei-in zu a kham lo makai a nei, gam khat peuhpeuh, a hampha gam ahi hi.

**ZVS:** A kumpipa in lungsim khentatna nei thei a, a makaite in annek tuidawn a hun thei a, amau ki-uk thei-in zu a khamlo makai a nei, gam khat peuhpeuh a hampha gam ahi hi.

**Grammar:** serial verb: -in (and then)

### v18
**EN:** By much slothfulness the building decayeth; and through idleness of the hands the house droppeth through.

**TDB77:** Thadah lua-a a inn vang nangawn a hu nuam lopa, a inn keh ding a, a innpi nangawn in puklawh ding hi.

**ZVS:** Thadah lua-in a inn vang nangawn a hu nuamlo mipa, a inn keh ding a, a inn nangawn in puklawh ding hi.

**Grammar:** future: will

### v19
**EN:** A feast is made for laughter, and wine maketh merry: but money answereth all things.

**TDB77:** An in lung damsak a, zu in lung nuamsak a, sum in ahih leh na khempeuh a kicingsak hi.

**ZVS:** An in lung damsak a, zu in lung nuamsak hi. Sum in ahih leh na khempeuh a kicingsak hi.

**Grammar:** causative: -sak suffix | reciprocal: ki- prefix

### v20
**EN:** Curse not the king, no not in thy thought; and curse not the rich in thy bedchamber: for a bird of the air shall carry the voice, and that which hath wings shall tell the matter.

**TDB77:** Na lungsim nangawn-ah, kumpipa gensia kei in la, kuama zak lohna mun na lupna tung nangawn-ah, mihaute samsia kei in. Thopi in na thugen teng na puak kha ding hi.

**ZVS:** Na lungsim nangawnah kumpipa gensia kei in. Kuama zaklohna mun na lupna tung nangawnah mihaute samsia kei in. Thopi in na thugen teng na puakkha ding hi.

**Grammar:** future: will
