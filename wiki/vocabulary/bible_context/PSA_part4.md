# PSA - Part 4

## Vocabulary with Context

### belte = those who trust/rely on
**Context:** Used in the context of people putting their trust in God.
**Example:** nang hong belte khempeuh

### lungdam sak in la = rejoice and be glad
**Context:** An imperative command to express joy.
**Example:** nang hong belte khempeuh lungdam sak in la

### nuamna la sasak = shout for joy
**Context:** Expressing loud, continuous joy.
**Example:** nuamna la sasak tawntung in

### gualzo-in = victoriously/triumphantly
**Context:** Describing the manner in which others can be glad through the subject's name.
**Example:** gualzo-in a lungdam theih nadingin

### hu = lead/guide/protect (in this context, 'be joyful in thee' or 'find joy in you')
**Context:** Used in the sense of finding joy or being led to joy in God.
**Example:** amaute na hu hi

### thuman mi = righteous person/man of truth
**Context:** Referring to someone who is upright or truthful.
**Example:** thuman mi thupha na pia hi

### lum tawh dal bangin = as with a shield of fat/thick fat (metaphor for strong protection)
**Context:** A metaphor for strong, enveloping favor or protection.
**Example:** lum tawh dal bangin deihsakna tawh na dal hi

### heh laitak = wrath/anger (intense)
**Context:** A plea not to be abandoned in anger.
**Example:** na heh laitak hong tai kei in la

### lungso sa-in = with compassion/pity
**Context:** Asking for instruction or rebuke to be given gently.
**Example:** lungso sa-in hong thuhilh kei in

### thanem = weak/feeble
**Context:** Describing the speaker's physical or spiritual state.
**Example:** thanem ka hih manin

### dikna = justice/righteousness
**Context:** A plea for God to act justly on the speaker's behalf.
**Example:** kei tungah dikna hong zang in

### guh ka tang = my refuge and my strength/my hiding place and my strength
**Context:** A common biblical metaphor for God as a source of safety and power.
**Example:** ka guh ka tang na ahih manin

### hin’gim = soul is vexed/my life is troubled
**Context:** Expressing deep distress or affliction of the soul.
**Example:** Ka hin’gim mahmah hi

### zekai = delay/tarry/linger (often negatively, as in waiting too long)
**Context:** Questioning how long God will delay in answering or acting.
**Example:** bang tanvei nang hong zekai lai ding na hi hiam?

### kihei in = turn back/return (in favor or attention)
**Context:** A plea for God to turn His attention back to the speaker.
**Example:** Topa aw, hong kihei in la

### itna kip neipa = Lord of steadfast love/Lord of unfailing love
**Context:** An appellation for God emphasizing His covenant love.
**Example:** Itna kip neipa na hih manin

### Leinuai = the grave/Sheol (literally 'under the earth')
**Context:** Referring to the realm of the dead.
**Example:** Leinuai panin kua in nang hong phat thei ding ahi hiam?

### tautauna = groaning/wailing
**Context:** Describing the sound of deep suffering.
**Example:** Ka tautauna-in ka gim hi

### khitui = tears (literally 'water of the eye')
**Context:** Used to describe weeping profusely.
**Example:** ka khitui in ka lupphah tum a

### mit mial = eyes grow dim/fail
**Context:** Describing physical deterioration due to grief.
**Example:** Dahna hangin ka mit mial a

### mitzung zaw semsem = eyes grow weaker/more worn out
**Context:** Emphasizing the effect of enemies' actions on the speaker's sight/strength.
**Example:** ka galte hangin ka mitzung zaw semsem hi

### siatna bawlte = workers of iniquity/evil-doers
**Context:** Referring to enemies or wicked people.
**Example:** Siatna bawlte khempeuh aw

### kihemkhia un = depart/move away
**Context:** A command for the wicked to leave the speaker's presence.
**Example:** ka kiang panin kihemkhia un

### thumna = prayer (often referring to the act of crying out)
**Context:** Referring to the speaker's earnest appeal to God.
**Example:** Topa in ka thumna za zo a

### zahhuai = ashamed/put to shame
**Context:** Predicting the fate of the enemies.
**Example:** Ka galte khempeuh zahhuai in genthei mahmah ding-te hi

### nungkik = return (in defeat)
**Context:** Describing the enemies turning back in disgrace.
**Example:** Amaute in nungkik-in

### maizumna thuak = suffer sudden shame/be suddenly confounded
**Context:** The consequence for the enemies.
**Example:** maizumna thuak pah ding-te hi

### hong delhte = those who pursue/persecute me
**Context:** Referring to the speaker's pursuers.
**Example:** Kei hong delhte khempeuh

### humpinelkai = lion (a powerful predator)
**Context:** Used as a simile for a fierce enemy who might destroy the speaker.
**Example:** humpinelkai bangin hong kaikhia-in

### balgawp = tear to pieces/destroy completely
**Context:** Describing the violent action of the pursuer if God doesn't save him.
**Example:** hong balgawp ding-te hi

### khialh = stray/err/sin
**Context:** Confessing wrongdoing or error.
**Example:** ka khut a khialh leh

## Phrases & Usage

- **tawntung in** = forever/continually
  - Usage: Used to emphasize the continuous nature of an action (e.g., shouting for joy).

- **bang tanvei... na hi hiam?** = How long... will you be?
  - Usage: A rhetorical question expressing impatience or distress over a perceived delay by God.

- **ka kiang panin kihemkhia un** = Depart from me/Move away from my side
  - Usage: A strong command used when addressing wicked people or enemies.

## Grammar Patterns

- **Ahih hangin [Clause 1], [Clause 2]; [Clause 3] in [Clause 4].**
  - A structure indicating cause/reason ('Because of this...') followed by resulting actions or commands, often seen in parallelism.
  - Example: Ahih hangin nang hong belte khempeuh lungdam sak in la, nuamna la sasak tawntung in;

- **Topa aw, [Imperative/Plea] in [Action/State].**
  - Direct address to God ('O Lord,') followed by a petition or request, often using the benefactive prefix 'hong-' (for me).
  - Example: Topa aw, thanem ka hih manin kei tungah dikna hong zang in;

- **Bang hang hiam cih leh [Reason/Justification], [Consequence/Question].**
  - A structure meaning 'Because [reason], [what happens/question]?' often used to argue for God's intervention.
  - Example: Bang hang hiam cih leh sihna-ah nangmah phawk theihna om lo ahih manin, Leinuai panin kua in nang hong phat thei ding ahi hiam?

- **ka [Body Part] [Adjective/Verb] hi/a.**
  - Describing the speaker's physical or emotional state using body parts as the subject.
  - Example: Ka tautauna-in ka gim hi.
