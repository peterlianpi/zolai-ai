## Sentence Patterns

- `X in Y ci hi.` = X said that Y.
  - e.g. `Naupangno a genthei mahmah khat in, maikum teh leitung a bei ding bel laukei ing ci hi.`
- `A hihhangin X ah Y cihte a tun teh Z hi.` = However, when X reaches Y, Z happens.
  - e.g. `A hihhangin nasepna phual hong tunteh, mi dangte tawh na a sepkhopna ah A nu ii pumgup danin kuamah in amah na gup bawllo hi.`
- `X lah omlo, Y lah neilo, Z lah bawllo nahih manin W ciin...` = Because you have no X, no Y, and no Z, ...
  - e.g. `Experience lah omlo, knowledge lah neilo, exercise lah bawllo nahih manin "piangkei cei" ciin ama ut mahmah hun a tun teh kizangh thei tuanlo hi.`
- `X cih sanloin Y kisam hi.` = Without saying X, Y is needed.
  - e.g. `Na khatpeuhpeuh neulua thupilo cih sanloin i sep nop pen lian i sep khak masiah a kisem thei bangbang, i sep khak bangbang sep nop ding, sep siam ding kisam hi.`
- `A zai theithei muh zawh ding, ngaihsut theihna kisam hi.` = The ability to see broadly and think widely is needed.
  - e.g. `Ngimna tupna zongh a neu a niam sangin a lian a sang mah tumhzaw aa a zai theithei muh zawh ding, ngaihsut theihna kisam hi.`
- `Ei le ei sung bek tawh X thei khinlo ahih manin Y kisam hi.` = Because X cannot be managed by ourselves alone, Y is needed.
  - e.g. `Ei le ei sung bek tawh vai kihawm thei khinlo ahih manin midangte tawh kizopna, nasepna ah hoih nading ei le ei sung ah kihilhna, kimakaih siamna kisam hi.`
