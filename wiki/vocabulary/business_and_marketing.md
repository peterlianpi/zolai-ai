# Business, Marketing & Strategy

## Concept / Rule
Zolai possesses a rich vocabulary for traditional agriculture and basic trade, but the modern domains of Marketing, Business Strategy, and Economics require the adaptation of older terms to new, broader concepts.

### Key Business and Strategic Vocabulary
1. **Market/Commerce:** 
   - `Sumdawnna` (Money + doing/trading) - Business/Commerce
   - `Khuapi/Pila` (City/Bazaar) - Market (Physical or Abstract)
2. **Strategy/Plan:**
   - `Khenna/Ngimna` (Intention/Aim) - Strategy/Goal
   - `Vaihawmna` (Arranging affairs) - Management/Planning
3. **Advertising/Marketing:**
   - `Tangko` (To announce loudly) - Advertise/Promote
   - `Pulak` (To reveal/make known) - Marketing
4. **Profit/Investment:**
   - `A Pung / A Gah` (The increase/fruit) - Profit/Return
   - `Sum Kholh` (To save money) or `Sum Tuah` (To put money in) - Investment
5. **Customer/Client:**
   - `Leite` (The buyers) or `A Deite` (Those who want it) - Customer
   - `Kileh-ngeite` (Regular returners) - Client/Regular

## Decision / Application
The AI must use these adapted compound terms when discussing abstract business and strategic concepts. It should rely on foundational roots like `Sum` (Money), `Vai` (Affairs), and `Thu` (Words/Information) to construct complex business ideas.

## Reason
Using traditional roots (like `Sumdawnna` for Business) anchors new economic concepts in familiar Zolai logic, ensuring that even non-technical speakers grasp the underlying meaning. It honors the agrarian roots of the language while adapting to globalized commerce.

## Pattern / Code Snippet
When discussing strategy and marketing, the AI should use:
*   `Sumdawnna Ngimna` - Business Strategy
*   `Sumdawnna Tangko` - Business Advertisement/Marketing
*   `A Gah Ngah` - To turn a profit (To receive the fruit)

## Mistake / Anti-pattern
Do not translate "Marketing" as `Sia` (Teacher) or `Siamsinna` (Learning), confusing the promotion of a product with education. Always use roots related to revealing/announcing (`Tangko`, `Pulak`).
