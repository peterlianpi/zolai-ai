# Zolai Pedagogical Curriculum (A1 - C2)

This directory contains the comprehensive, CEFR-aligned sentence pattern and grammar curriculum for the Zolai language. It is designed specifically to power the AI Language Tutor.

## Curriculum Foundation
The curriculum is built by synthesizing three primary sources:
1.  **ZVS 2018 Zolai Bible (66-Book Survey):** Provides massive, context-rich parallel data, ranging from basic Genesis narratives to complex Romans argumentation.
2.  **Zolai Sinna (Learning Manuals):** Provides the foundational structural rules and pedagogical progression.
3.  **Zolai Gelhmaanbu (Orthography & Style Guide):** Ensures all syntax and spelling align with formal standards (e.g., correct pluralization, negations, and pawfi usage).

## Proficiency Bands

The curriculum is divided into six progressive stages:

*   **[A1 Beginner](../curriculum/a1_beginner.md):** Core identity, existence, and simple SOV structures. (*Sinna Lessons 1–12*)
*   **[A2 Elementary](../curriculum/a2_elementary.md):** Sequential narrative, past tense markers, and basic prepositions. (*Sinna Lessons 13–22*)
*   **[B1 Intermediate](../curriculum/b1_intermediate.md):** Relational statements, reasoning (`Bang hang hiam cih leh`), and compound sentences. (*Sinna Lessons 23–30*)
*   **[B2 Upper-Intermediate](../curriculum/b2_upper_intermediate.md):** Complex logic, proper conditionals (`nong pai kei a leh`), and comparisons. (*Sinna Lessons 31–34*)
*   **[C1 Advanced](../curriculum/c1_advanced.md):** Rhetorical argumentation, abstract theology, and logical exposition.
*   **[C2 Mastery](../curriculum/c2_mastery.md):** Visionary metaphors, strict poetic parallelism, and high-register doxology.

## Usage for AI Tutors
When instructed to teach or correct a student, the AI Tutor should first determine the user's proficiency level, and then reference the corresponding curriculum file to generate level-appropriate sentences, vocabulary, and grammar rules. Ensure that you never exceed the user's level by more than one incremental step (+1 rule).

## B1 — Section 1: Cause & Effect

### Unit 1: Cause-Effect Connectors
| Zolai | English | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| `ahih manin` | because | most common causal |
| `bang hang` | because/for | mid-sentence reason |
| `bang hang hiam cih leh` | for the reason that | formal/explanatory |
| `tua ahih ciangin` | therefore/so | consequence |
| `tua ciangin` | then/so | sequence |
| `tua ahih leh` | if that is so | conditional consequence |

**Examples:**
- `Topa' minthan'na in inn dim ahih manin siampite lut thei kei-te hi.` = Because the glory filled the temple, the priests could not enter.
- `Bang hang hiam cih leh Pasian in leitung mite a it mahmah hi.` = For God so loved the world.
- `Tua ahih ciangin ka itte aw, khat leh khat ki-it ni.` = Therefore, beloved, let us love one another.

### Unit 2: Conditional Sentences
**Grammar:** `a leh` / `leh` = if
- `Na hoih gamta leh, thupha na ngah ding hi.` = If you do good, you will receive blessing.
- `Na pai kei a leh, ka pai ding hi.` = If you don't go, I will go.
- **NEVER:** `kei a leh` for negative conditional — always `kei a leh`

**Types:**
| Type | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Real conditional | `[condition] leh, [result] ding hi` | If X, then Y will happen |
| Negative conditional | `[verb] kei a leh` | If (you) don't [verb] |
| Past conditional | `[verb] khin leh` | If (it) has happened |

### Unit 3: Reported Speech
**Grammar:** `ci hi` / `ci-in` = said/saying
- Direct: `Amah in, "Pai in," ci hi.` = He said, "Go."
- Indirect: `Amah in pai ding ci hi.` = He said he would go.
- Question reported: `Bang ahi hiam ci-in a dot hi.` = He asked what it was.

### Unit 4: Interrogatives (Advanced)
| Question | Zolai | Answer pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Why? | `Bang hangin...hiam?` | `...ahih manin...` |
| How? | `Bangci...hiam?` | `[method] tawh...` |
| How many? | `Bangzah...hiam?` | `[number] pha-te hi` |
| Which? | `Tua bang...hiam?` | `[item] ahi hi` |
| When? | `Bang hun...hiam?` | `[time] ciangin...` |

### Unit 5: Narrative Tense
**Aspect markers in narrative:**
- `khin` = completed: `A kiman khin hi.`
- `zo` = finished/overcome: `A zo hi.`
- `pah` = immediately: `Khuavak om pah hi.`
- `ta` = change of state: `Om ta hi.`
- `khit ciangin` = after: `A nek khit ciangin...`
- `lai takin` = while: `A om lai takin...`
- `ciangin` = when: `A tun ciangin...`

## B2 — Section 1: Logic & Conditions

### Unit 1: Complex Conditionals
**Real:** `[condition] leh, [result] ding hi`
**Unreal:** `[condition] leh, [result] ding hi` (context makes it hypothetical)
**Negative:** `[verb] kei a leh, [result]`

Examples from corpus:
- `Eite in i pumpi i teldot leh, thukhenna i thuak kei ding hi.` = If we judge ourselves, we will not be judged.
- `Pasian in eite hong it ahih leh, eite khat leh khat ki-it ding hi.` = If God so loved us, we ought to love one another.

### Unit 2: Concession (although/even though)
**Grammar:** `napi` / `napi-in` / `ahih hangin`
- `Ta taktak ahi napi-in gimna tawlna a thuakna tawh thuman'na a sin hi.` = Although he was a Son, he learned obedience through suffering.
- `Topa lian napi-in amah in a neute don hi.` = Although the Lord is great, he cares for the humble.

### Unit 3: Emphasis Structures
**Emphatic `mah`:**
- `Kei in Khazih bangin ka gamtat mah bangin note zong kei gamtat bangin gamta un.` = Follow me as I follow Christ.
- `Adam sungah mi khempeuh a sih mah bangin Khazih sungah mi khempeuh a nungtasak ding hi.` = As in Adam all die, even so in Christ all shall be made alive.

**Exclusive `longal`:**
- `Kha Siangtho longal kuamah in a thei kei hi.` = No one knows except the Holy Spirit.

### Unit 4: Hypotheticals
**Pattern:** `[hypothetical condition] leh, [hypothetical result]`
- `Eite in, "Ka mawhna a om kei hi," ci leng eimah mah kikheem i hi a...` = If we say we have no sin, we deceive ourselves...
- `Pumpi koi-a om ding hiam?` = Where would the body be? (hypothetical)

## C1 — Section 1: Metaphor & Style

### Unit 1: Metaphorical Language
**"I Am" declarations (John pattern):**
- `Kei pen nuntakna an ka hi hi.` = I am the bread of life. (John 6:35)
- `Kei pen leitung khuavak ka hi hi.` = I am the light of the world. (John 8:12)
- `Kei pen lam, thuman, leh nuntakna ka hi hi.` = I am the way, the truth, and the life. (John 14:6)
- `Kei pen nuntakna leh thokiksatna ka hi hi.` = I am the resurrection and the life. (John 11:25)

**Pattern:** `Kei pen [abstract noun] ka hi hi.`
Double `hi hi` = absolute, eternal identity.

**Absolute declaration (Exodus 3:14):**
- `KEIMAH ka hi hi.` = I AM THAT I AM.
- Pattern: `[Emphatic pronoun] + [agreement marker] + hi hi`

### Unit 2: Stylistic Variation
**Register levels:**
| Register | Marker | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Intimate | Drop subject | `Pai in.` (Go.) |
| Formal | Full subject | `Nangmah pai in.` |
| Honorific | `topa` / `kumpipa` | `Topa aw, hong pia in.` |
| Literary | `pen` + double `hi` | `Amah pen Pasian ahi hi.` |
| Prophetic | `ci hi` + future | `Topa in hih bangin ci hi...` |

### Unit 3: Literary Devices
**Parallelism (A = B):**
```
Line A: Sihna aw, na zawhna koi lai-ah om hiam?

```

```

```

```
Bang hangin...
Bang hangin...
Bang hangin...
```

**Stacked conditions before main verb:**
```
Sihna panin Jesuh a thokiksak Pasian' Kha Siangtho note sungah hong om leh,
```

## [a1_beginner.md]

# Zolai Curriculum: A1 (Beginner)

This document outlines the sentence patterns, grammar rules, and vocabulary expected at the **A1 (Beginner) level** of Zolai. This level establishes foundational communication, identity, and the basic structural rules of the language.

## 1. Core Grammar Principles

### 1.1 Word Order: SOV (Subject-Object-Verb)
Unlike English (SVO), Zolai places the verb at the very end of the sentence, followed by sentence-ending markers.
*   **English:** God created the earth. (Subject -> Verb -> Object)
*   **Zolai:** Pasian in leitung a bawl hi. (Subject -> Object -> Verb)

### 1.2 The Declarative Marker (`hi`)
Every standard factual statement in Zolai must end with the declarative marker `hi`. Without `hi`, the sentence is grammatically incomplete.
*   *Pattern:* [Statement] + `hi`.

### 1.3 Personal Pronouns (Singular)
*   **Kei** (I / Me)
*   **Nang** (You)
*   **Amah** (He / She / Him / Her)
*   **Amah'n** (Contracted Amah + in)

### 1.4 Phonetic Constraints (Sinna Rules)
Even at the beginner level, students must observe these phonotactic restrictions:
- **No `ti`:** The `t` and `i` sounds never combine in standard Zolai.
- **`c` Restrictions:** The `c` consonant only pairs with `i` and `u`.
- **`o` Realization:** Always pronounced like "ou" (as in "soul").

---

## 2. Sentence Patterns

### Pattern 1: Identity & Existence (Noun Equivalence)
Used to state who or what something is.
*   **Syntax:** [Subject] pen [Noun] hi.
*   **Usage:** Naming, professions, and identity.
    *   *English:* I am Peter.
    *   *Zolai:* Kei pen Peter ka hi hi. (Note: `ka` acts as the 1st person agreement marker before `hi`)
    *   *English:* He is a teacher.
    *   *Zolai:* Amah pen sia khat ahi hi.

### Pattern 2: Simple Action (Transitive with `in`)
When a subject performs an action on an object, the subject MUST be marked with the ergative particle `in`.
*   **Syntax:** [Subject] + `in` + [Object] + [Verb] + `hi`.
*   **Biblical Source (Gen 1:1):** Pasian in leitung a bawl hi.
    *   *Pasian (God) + in (subject marker) + leitung (earth) + bawl (create) + hi (declarative).*
*   **Daily Usage:**
    *   *English:* I eat rice.
    *   *Zolai:* Kei in an ka ne hi.

### Pattern 3: Simple Action (Intransitive)
When a subject performs an action without an object, the `in` marker is typically dropped for pronouns, or kept for proper nouns depending on emphasis.
*   **Syntax:** [Subject] + [Verb] + `hi`.
*   **Usage:** Actions like going, sleeping, coming.
    *   *English:* I sleep.
    *   *Zolai:* Ka lum hi. (Note: `Kei` is often dropped; `ka` implies 1st person).
    *   *English:* You come.
    *   *Zolai:* Nang na pai hi. (`na` implies 2nd person).

---

| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **People** | God, Person/People, Male person, Female person | Pasian, Mi / Mite, Mipa, Numei |
| **Actions** | Go, Come, Eat, Sleep | Pai, Hong pai, Ne, Lum |

| **Greetings** | How are you?, I'm fine | Na dam na?, Dam mah |
| **Sinna Core** | Zomi, Sunday, Deer | Zomi, Nipini, Sakhi |

## 4. Reading & Practice (Sinna Passages)

### Passage 1: UITHU (About Dogs)
This story introduces animal vocabulary and simple subject-object-verb relationships.

1. **Ui khat ka nei hi.** (I have a dog.)
2. **Ka ui a hoih hi.** (My dog is good.)
3. **Ka ui in kei hong it hi.** (My dog loves me.)
4. **Ui in zan khuavakin ka inn a ngak hi.** (The dog stays awake to guard my house at night.)
5. **Uite na hehpih in. Sat kei in.** (Have mercy on dogs. Do not hit them.)

**Vocabulary from Passage:**

| :--- | :--- |

## 5. Teaching Strategy for AI Tutors
1.  **Do not introduce complex clauses.** Keep sentences to simple Subject-Object-Verb structures.
2.  **Focus on `in`:** Ensure the student understands that `in` marks the doer of the action.
3.  **Encourage dropping pronouns:** Teach that `Ka lum hi` is more natural than `Kei ka lum hi` in daily conversation.
4.  **Socratic Prompting:** Ask the student, "What does the dog do at night?" (*Ui in zan ciangin bang sem hiam?*) to encourage SOV response construction.

## 6. Corpus-Verified Examples (TDB77 + parallel_combined)

### Greetings
- `Na dam na?` = How are you? | `Ka dam hi.` = I am fine.
- `Nuam takin om un.` = Peace be with you. | `Nuam takin pai in.` = Go in peace.
- `Lawmthu ka nei hi.` = Thank you.

### Core Sentences
- `Kei in an ka ne hi.` = I eat rice.
- `Ka pa in laibu a sim hi.` = My father reads a book.
- `Pasian in nakhempeuh a piangsak hi.` = God created all things.
- `Ka ui in kei hong it hi.` = My dog loves me.
- `Amah in a nasep sem hi.` = He is doing his work.

### Numbers (corpus-verified)
khat=one, nih=two, thum=three, li=four, nga=five, guk=six, sagih=seven, giet=eight, kua=nine, sawm=ten, za=hundred, sing=thousand

### Family (corpus-verified)
ka pa=father, ka nu=mother, ka tapa=son, ka tanu=daughter, ka sanggampa=brother, ka sanggamnu=sister, ka zi=wife, ka pasal=husband, ka naupang=child

### Key Grammar Rules
- SOV: Subject + Object + Verb + `hi`
- Ergative `in`: marks the doer of transitive actions

- `na` = 2nd person prefix (your/you with verb)
- `a` = 3rd person prefix (his/her/its with verb)
- `hi` = declarative statement marker (every statement ends with it)
- `hiam` = question marker (replaces `hi` in questions)
- `kei hi` = negation (verb + kei hi = did not verb)

## [a2_elementary.md]

# Zolai Curriculum: A2 (Elementary)

This document outlines the sentence patterns, grammar rules, and vocabulary expected at the **A2 (Elementary) level** of Zolai. This level expands from basic existence to story-telling, placing actions in time (past/future) and locating objects in space. It draws heavily from the narrative styles of the Historical Books and the Synoptic Gospels.

## 1. Expanded Grammar Principles

### 1.1 Time Markers (Tense Modifiers)
Zolai verbs do not change form to show time (no conjugation). Instead, time is indicated by adding particles *after* the verb, before the declarative `hi`.
*   **Future (ding):** Will / Going to.
    *   *English:* I will go.
    *   *Zolai:* Ka pai **ding** hi.
*   **Completed Past (khin):** Already done.
    *   *English:* I have eaten (already).
    *   *Zolai:* An ka ne **khin** hi.
*   **Experiential Past (ngei):** Have ever done / Used to do.
    *   *English:* I used to go / I have been there before.
    *   *Zolai:* Ka pai **ngei** hi.

### 1.2 Pluralization (`te` and `uh`)
To make a noun plural, attach the suffix `te`.
*   **Singular:** Mi (Person) -> **Plural:** Mi**te** (People)
*   **Singular:** Sangnaupang (Student) -> **Plural:** Sangnaupang**te** (Students)

> Note: `mi` = person/people (generic). `mipa` = male person. `numei` = female person. Use `mi` / `mite` / `mikhempeuh` when referring to people in general.

When using pronouns (You all, They, We), the verb must be followed by the plural agreement marker `uh` (except for the inclusive 'We' `i`, which *never* takes `uh`).
*   **Nong pai-te hi:** You (plural) come.
*   **A pai-te hi:** They go.
*   *Exception:* **I pai hi:** We (inclusive) go. (Never say "I pai-te hi").

### 1.3 Positional Prepositions
Post-positions (they come *after* the noun in Zolai).
*   **sungah:** inside / in
*   **tungah:** on top of / on
*   **mai-ah:** in front of
*   **kiangah:** beside / to (a person)

---

## 2. Sentence Patterns

### Pattern 1: Spatial Placement
Used to describe where something is happening.
*   **Syntax:** [Subject] in [Location] + `ah` [Verb] + `hi`.
*   **Usage:**
    *   *English:* He sleeps in the house.
    *   *Zolai:* Amah innsung**ah** a lum hi.
    *   *English:* They are going to the mountain.
    *   *Zolai:* Mualtung**ah** a pai-te hi.

### Pattern 2: Telling a Sequence (Narrative Links)
Used to string actions together, commonly found in Luke or 1 Samuel.
*   **Tua ciangin:** Then / After that
*   **Ahi zongin:** But / However
*   **Usage:**
    *   *Zolai:* Jesuh in mualtungah kahto hi. **Tua ciangin**, a nungzuite hong pai-te hi.
    *   *English:* Jesus went up the mountain. **Then**, his disciples came.

### Pattern 3: Expressing Desire
*   **Syntax:** [Verb] + `nuam` + `hi`.
*   **Usage:**
    *   *English:* I want to eat.
    *   *Zolai:* Ka ne **nuam** hi.

| **Locations** | House, Mountain, River, Village | Inn, Mual, Guik, Khua |

## 4. Biblical Narrative Patterns (A2 Level)

These patterns appear constantly in Genesis and Exodus and are ideal for A2 learners.

### Day-Counting Formula (Genesis Creation)
`Nitak hong bei-in, zingsang hong tung a, ni khat ni ahi hi.`
— Evening came and morning came, it was the first day. *(GEN 1:5)*

Pattern: **Nitak hong bei-in, zingsang hong tung a, ni [number] ni ahi hi.**

### "Just Like That" Completion
`Tua mah bangin piang pah hi.` — Just like that, it happened. *(GEN 1:7, 1:9, 1:11)*
Use this to show an immediate result after a command.

### Sequence Connector
`Tua ciangin Pasian in...` — Then God... *(narrative progression)*
`Tua ahih ciangin...` — So when that was... *(consequence)*

## 5. Teaching Strategy for AI Tutors
1.  **Enforce Plural Rules:** Actively check if the student uses `uh` correctly, and apply the strict rule against `I ... uh hi`.
2.  **Focus on Narrative:** Ask the student to describe a sequence of events (e.g., "What did you do yesterday, and what happened *then*?") using `Tua ciangin`.
3.  **Day Formula Drill:** Have the student practice the Genesis day-counting formula to internalize `nitak/zingsang` and ordinal numbers.
4.  **Completion Pattern:** Teach `Tua mah bangin piang pah hi` as a fixed phrase for "and it was so" — very common in biblical and formal registers.

- nasem=work, syanu=teacher, siampi=priest, kumpi=king
- lungdam=glad/thankful (NOT love — love=it/itna)
- pawi=feast, thupha=blessing, lawm=friend

## [b1_intermediate.md]

# Zolai Curriculum: B1 (Intermediate)

At the **B1 (Intermediate) level**, Zolai shifts from simple narratives to expressing reasons, emotions, and dependent relationships. Learners will begin stringing sentences together using cause-and-effect connectors and temporal clauses, drawing extensively from the instructional language of Matthew and the Minor Prophets.

---

## 1. Interrogative Grammar (Asking Questions)

Zolai questions are formed by placing a question particle at the end of the sentence, replacing the declarative `hi`.

### 1.1 Information Questions (`hiam`)
Used when asking Who, What, Where, When, Why, or How.
*   **Bang** (What), **Kua** (Who), **Koikung** (Where)
*   **Syntax:** [Question Word] ... `hiam`?
*   **Examples:**
    *   *English:* What are you doing?
    *   *Zolai:* Nang bang na sem **hiam**?
    *   *English:* Where is he going?
    *   *Zolai:* Amah koilah a pai **hiam**?

### 1.2 Yes/No Questions (`hiam` or `diam`)
*   *English:* Did you eat?
*   *Zolai:* An na ne khin **hiam**?
*   (Note: `diam` is occasionally used for softer, suggestive questions or future inquiries).

---

## 2. Compound Sentences (Cause and Effect)

At this level, learners must explain *why* something is happening.

### 2.1 Because (`Bang hang hiam cih leh`)
Used to introduce a reason. Literally translates to "If you ask why...". It usually starts the second sentence.
*   *Zolai:* Ka pai ngei kei hi. **Bang hang hiam cih leh** hun ka nei kei hi.
*   *English:* I did not go. **Because** I had no time.

### 2.2 Therefore (`ahih manin / manin`)
Attached to the end of the clause presenting the cause.
*   *Syntax:* [Cause Clause] + `ahih manin`, [Effect Clause] + `hi`.
*   *Zolai:* Guah zu **ahih manin**, innah ka om hi.
*   *English:* **Because** it rained (or: It rained, **therefore**), I stayed home.
*   *Note:* The word `man` implies "reason" or "sake".

---

## 3. Comparison & Degree (Sinna Lesson 3)

At this level, learners move beyond simple descriptions to making comparisons between objects or people.

### 3.1 Comparative (`-sangin ... -zaw`)
The most common structure for "more than".
*   *Syntax:* [A] [B] **sangin** [Adjective]**-zaw** hi.
*   *English:* A rabbit is faster than a snail. (Sinna Pattern)
*   *Zolai:* Sumkuang sangin bilpi a hatzaw hi.
*   *English:* This book is better than that one.
*   *Zolai:* Hih laibu sangin hua laibu a hoihzaw hi.

### 3.2 Superlative (`pen`)
The most or best.
*   *Syntax:* [Noun] **pen** hi.
*   *English:* He is the brightest/best.
*   *Zolai:* Amah a vang pen hi.

---

## 4. Temporal & Conditional Clauses

### 4.1 When (`ciangin / teh`)
`ciangin` is the standard form, while `teh` is more informal/conversational.
*   *Zolai:* Inn na tun' **ciangin**, hong sam in.
*   *English:* **When** you reach home, call me.
*   *Zolai:* Na neek **teh**... (When you eat...)

### 4.2 If/Conditionals (`leh`)
The basic conditional structure.
*   *Syntax:* [Condition Clause] + **leh**, [Action Clause].
*   *English:* If you believe, you will see.
*   *Zolai:* Na up **leh**, na mu ding hi. (John 11:40 Pattern)
*   *Note:* Negative conditionals (`nong pai kei leh`) are handled in B2.

---

## 5. Quotative Patterns (B1 Level)

At B1, learners begin using direct speech with the quotative closer `ci hi`.

### Direct Speech Pattern
`Pasian in, "Khuavak om ta hen," ci hi.` *(GEN 1:3)*
— God said, "Let there be light."

**Syntax:** [Subject] in, "[Quote]," ci hi.

### Mid-sentence Speech (`ci-in`)
`Pasian in khuavak hoih hi ci-in mu hi.` *(GEN 1:4)*
— God saw the light, saying it was good.

**Syntax:** [Verb] + ci-in + [next action]

### Reported Speech (`kici`)
`Laipianpa na kici hi.` — He was called Laipianpa.

---

## 6. Vocabulary Focus (B1)

| Domain | English | Zolai |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Questions** | What, Who, Where, Why | Bang, Kua, Koilah, Bang hang |
| **Emotions** | Love, Hate, Happy, Sad | Itna, Muhtahna, Lungdam, Dah |
| **Relations** | Because, Therefore, If | Bang hang hiam cih leh, Ahih manin, leh |
| **Comparison** | More than, Most | sangin...-zaw, pen |
| **Sinna Lesson 3** | Rabbit, Snail, Diligent | Bilpi, Sumkuang, Thanuam/Hahkat |
| **Speech** | Said, Saying, Called | ci hi, ci-in, kici |

---

## 7. Teaching Strategy for AI Tutors
1.  **Prompt for Justification:** Move from asking "What did you do?" to "Why did you do that?" to force the student to use `ahih manin`.
2.  **Clause Building:** Ask the student to combine two simple sentences into one fluid sentence using `ciangin` (when).
3.  **Roleplay:** Use Q&A dialogue formats (resembling Malachi) to practice interrogatives.
4.  **Quotative Drill:** Have the student report what someone said using `ci hi` and `ci-in` patterns.

---
*Reference: ZVS Bible Parallel Corpus, Zolai Sinna Bu Lesson 3.*

---

# Zolai Curriculum: B2 (Upper Intermediate)

The **B2 (Upper Intermediate) level** introduces robust logical argumentation, conditional logic, and comparative structures. Learners at this level must demonstrate the ability to debate, rationalize, and form hypothesis-based phrases, utilizing the grammatical logic found in Romans and Hebrews.

## 1. Conditional Logic ("If... Then" Clauses)

Zolai expresses conditions using the particle `leh` at the end of the dependent clause.

### 1.1 Positive Conditionals
*   **Syntax:** [Condition] + `leh`, [Consequence] + `hi/ding hi`.
*   *Zolai:* Nang hong pai **leh**, ka pai ding hi.
*   *English:* **If** you come, I will go.
*   *Zolai Romans 8:10:* Note sungah Khrih hong om **leh**... Kha Siangtho pen nuntakna ahi hi.
*   *English:* **If** Christ is in you... the Spirit is life.

### 1.2 Negative Conditionals (CRITICAL RULE)
> [!WARNING]
> Standard ZVS 2018 grammar absolutely requires the use of **`kei a leh`** or **`kei leh`** for "unless" or "if not" statements when a pronoun/subject performs the action. Do **not** use `lo` + `leh` here.

*   *Wrong:* Nong pai lo leh.
*   *Correct:* Nong pai **kei a leh**. (If you do not come).
*   *Correct:* Nong pai **kei leh**.
*   *Usage:*
    *   *English:* If you do not go, I will be sad.
    *   *Zolai:* Na pai **kei a leh**, ka lung a leng ding hi.

---

## 2. Comparative & Superlative Logic (Hebrews Pattern)

Used when weighing two options or declaring the highest state, heavily utilizing the `sangin` keyword.

### 2.1 Comparatives (Better Than)
*   **Syntax:** [Subject A] pen [Subject B] **sangin** [Adjective]-**zaw** hi.
*   **Usage:**
    *   *English:* Gold is better than silver.
    *   *Zolai:* Kham pen ngun **sangin** a hoih**zaw** hi.
    *   *English:* He is taller than me.
    *   *Zolai:* Amah pen kei **sangin** a sang**zaw** hi.

### 2.2 Superlatives (The Best / Most)
*   **Syntax:** [Group] lakah [Subject] a [Adjective]-**pen** hi.
*   **Usage:**
    *   *English:* He is the tallest among the students.
    *   *Zolai:* Sangnaupangte lakah amah a sang**pen** hi.

---

## 3. Disjunctive Conjunctions (Choice)

*   **Ahih kei leh:** Or / Otherwise.
*   *Zolai:* Tu-in na pai ding hiam, **ahih kei leh** zing ciangin na pai ding hiam?
*   *English:* Will you go now, **or** will you go tomorrow?

---

## 4. Vocabulary Focus (B2)

| Domain | English | Zolai |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Logic** | If, Or, Better Than | Leh, Ahih kei leh, Sangin... zaw |
| **Ethics** | Right, Wrong, Truth | A man, A khial, Thuman |
| **Arguments**| Judgment, Promise | Thukhenna, Kamciam |

## 5. Teaching Strategy for AI Tutors
1.  **Strict Negative Affirmation:** Actively bait the student into using a negative conditional (e.g., "What happens if I don't answer?"). If they use `kei a leh`, immediately correct them to `kei a leh`.
2.  **Comparative Exercises:** Have the student weigh two concepts (e.g., "Which is better, city or village?") to force the `sangin... hoihzaw` structure.
3.  **Debate Format:** Engage in mild Socratic questioning to promote logical reasoning over rote facts.

---

## 6. Corpus-Verified Examples (TDB77 + parallel_combined)

### Concession (verified)
- `napi` / `napi-in` = although/even though (75/59 occurrences)
- `Ta taktak ahi napi-in gimna tawlna a thuakna tawh thuman'na a sin hi.` = Although he was a Son, he learned obedience through suffering.
- `Topa lian napi-in amah in a neute don hi.` = Although the Lord is great, he cares for the humble.

### Emphasis `mah` (verified)
- `Kei in Khazih bangin ka gamtat mah bangin note zong kei gamtat bangin gamta un.` = Follow me as I follow Christ.
- `Adam sungah mi khempeuh a sih mah bangin Khazih sungah mi khempeuh a nungtasak ding hi.` = As in Adam all die, even so in Christ all shall be made alive.

### Comparative/Superlative (verified)
- `I lungsim sangin Pasian lian zaw hi.` = God is greater than our heart.
- `Note khempeuh sangin keimah in pautuam dang gen thei ka hih ciangin...` = Since I speak in tongues more than all of you...
- `Sang Pen Pasian in mi khut tawh kibawl inn sungah a teeng zo kei hi.` = The Most High God does not dwell in temples made with hands.

### Causative `sak` (verified)
- `Kei hong itte ka hausak a...` = He enriched those who love me.
- `Amaute thahat mite suaksak napi-in...` = Even though he strengthened their arms.

### Abstract vocabulary (B2)
- nuntakna=life, sihna=death, itna=love, hehpihna=grace
- thumanna=righteousness, mawhna=sin, upna=faith, ngimna=hope
- lungdamna=joy, kihehnemna=comfort, hotkhiatna=salvation

### Critical corrections
- love (verb) = `it` NOT `lungdam` (lungdam=glad/thankful)
- God loves us = `Pasian in eite hong it hi` NOT `kei te a lungdam hi`
- we (subject) = `i` (prefix) or `eite` (emphatic) NOT `kei te`
- we went = `I pai hi` NOT `I pai-te hi` (never-te with i)

## [c1_advanced.md]

# Zolai Curriculum: C1 (Advanced)

At the **C1 (Advanced) level**, Zolai shifts into abstract, rhetorical, and metaphorical language. This level assumes mastery of standard grammar and focuses on stylistic elevation, embedding clauses within clauses, and articulating complex theology or philosophical ideas. It draws heavily from the Gospel of John, Isaiah, and Pauline Epistles.

---

## 1. Metaphorical Equivalency ("I Am" Philosophy)

While A1 uses noun equivalency for identity (I am a man), C1 uses it to map abstract concepts to physical realities.

*   **Syntax:** [Subject] pen [Metaphor/Abstract Noun] hi.
*   **Examples:**
    *   *Zolai:* Kei pen nuntakna an ka hi hi. (John 6:35)
    *   *English:* I am the bread of life.
    *   *Zolai:* Note pen leitung mite-a' dingin khuavak tawh na kibang-te hi. (Matthew 5:14)
    *   *English:* You are the light of the world.

### The "I AM" Absolute Declaration (EXO 3:14)
The most emphatic identity pattern in Zolai — God's self-declaration to Moses:
- `KEIMAH ka hi hi.` — I AM THAT I AM.
- Pattern: **[Emphatic pronoun] + [agreement marker] + hi hi**
- The double `hi hi` signals absolute, eternal identity — not just a current state.
- Use this pattern for ultimate identity assertions at C1/C2 level.

---

## 2. Rhetorical Argumentation (Logic & Flow)

Advanced Zolai uses specific particles to signal logical transitions and rhetorical challenges.

### 2.1 The Rhetorical Question (`Koi-ah ... hiam?`)
Used to challenge a position or status in philosophical debate.
*   *English:* Where is the wise man?
*   *Zolai:* Lungpil mi **koi-ah a om hiam**? (1 Corinthians 1:20 Pattern)
*   *English:* Where is your faith?
*   *Zolai:* Na upna-te **koi-ah om hiam**?

### 2.2 Direct Causality / Basis (`Bang hang hiam cih leh`)
Used as a bridge between a declaration and its foundational reason.
*   *English:* For God so loved the world...
*   *Zolai:* **Bang hang hiam cih leh**, Pasian in leitung mite a it mahmah hi. (John 3:16)

---

## 3. Complex Embedded Clauses (Pauline Syntax)

C1 requires packing multiple conditions and descriptors into a single sentence, strictly delaying the main verb until the final slot.

### 3.1 Stacked Conditional & Instrumental
*   **Pattern:** [Condition Clause A] [Condition Clause B] [Instrumental Clause C] [Main Verb].
*   *Example (Romans 8:11):*
    *   *Zolai:* Sihna panin Jesuh a thokiksak Pasian' Kha Siangtho note sungah hong om leh, sihna panin Khrih a hingkik sakpa mah in, note sungah a om ama Kha Siangtho mah tawh, a sithei pumpi a hong nungtasak ding hi.
    *   *Breakdown:* (1) If the Spirit... (2) the one who raised Christ... (3) by His Spirit... (4) **will give life** (Final Verb).

---

## 4. Nuanced Negation (Exclusivity & Prohibitives)

### 4.1 Exclusiveness (`Longal ... lo/kei`)
Mapping the "None except [X]" pattern.
*   *English:* No one knows except the Spirit.
*   *Zolai:* Kha Siangtho **longal** kuamah in a thei **kei** hi.

### 4.2 Prohibitive Jussive (`kei hen`)
"Let not" commands.
*   *English:* Let not your heart be troubled.
*   *Zolai:* Na lungtang-te patau **kei hen**. (John 14:1)

---

## 5. Vocabulary Focus (C1)

| Domain | English | Zolai |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Abstracts**| Life, Grace, Righteousness, Sovereignty | Nuntakna, Hehpihna, Thumanna, Vangliatna |
| **Logic** | Even one, Everything, Except | Bek nangawn, Khempeuh, Longal |
| **Rhetoric** | Therefore, For/Because, Lest | Tua ahih ciangin, Bang hang hiam cih leh, loh nadingin |

---

## 6. Teaching Strategy for AI Tutors
1.  **Logical Reordering:** Give the student a list of facts and have them construct a single ZVS sentence using `bang hang hiam cih leh`.
2.  **Rhetorical Shifts:** Have the student convert a direct statement (e.g., "The wise man is not here") into a rhetorical question (`Lungpil mi koiah om hiam?`).
3.  **Ambiguity Resolution:** Teach the student to distinguish between `khempeuh` (universal) and `teng` (limited group).

---
*Reference: ZVS Epistles (Romans, Corinthians), Gospel of John (ZVS 2018).*

---

## 7. Corpus-Verified Examples (TDB77 + parallel_combined)

### "I Am" declarations (John — verified)
- `Kei pen nuntakna an ka hi hi.` = I am the bread of life. (John 6:35)
- `Kei pen leitung khuavak ka hi hi.` = I am the light of the world. (John 8:12)

## [c2_mastery.md]

# Zolai Curriculum: C2 (Mastery)

The **C2 (Mastery) level** represents the pinnacle of Zolai fluency. It moves beyond conversation into the realm of high literature, poetics, worship, and apocalyptic visionary writing. This level is almost exclusively modeled on the Psalms, Job, Isaiah, and the Book of Revelation.

## 1. Visionary Writing (The 'I Saw' Frame)

Mastery-level narrative often uses a highly formalized viewing structure, where the speaker is a passive observer of grand events.

*   **Syntax:** [Sequence Marker] + `ka khuadak leh` (I looked, and...) + [Vision] + `ka mu hi` (I saw).
*   *Zolai:* Hih thuteng khit ciangin **ka khuadak leh** vantungah kong a kihongsa-in a om **ka mu hi**. (Revelation 4:1)
*   *English:* After these things **I looked, and behold**, a door standing open in heaven **I saw**.
*   *Note:* The verb `ka mu hi` brackets the entire visual description, meaning the description itself must be grammatically complete before the final observation marker drops.

## 2. High Doxology & Adoration (Worthiness)

The formal language of praise and constitutional worthiness, requiring strict reverence and the `kilawm` (worthy/fitting) marker.

*   **Syntax:** [Subject] in [Attributes] ngah tawntung dingin a **kilawm** hi.
*   *Zolai:* Nangmah in minthan'na, pahtawina, leh vangliatna na ngah tawntung dingin a **kilawm** hi. (Revelation 4:11)
*   *English:* You are **worthy** to receive glory, and honor, and power forever.

## 3. Strict Poetic Parallelism

Mastery Zolai employs Hebrew-style parallelism, where a concept is stated, and then immediately restated using different, often more intense, vocabulary (A = B).

*   *Zolai (Line A):* Ka Lungkhamna in tuipi bang lian a...
*   *Zolai (Line B):* Ka Dahna in mual lianpi bangin hong neencip hi.
*   *Translation:* (A) My sorrow is as vast as the ocean... (B) My grief crushes me like a great mountain.
*   *Rule:* The AI tutor expects students at this level to create balanced, echoing lines of poetry rather than simple direct statements.

---

## 4. Vocabulary Focus (C2)

| Domain | English | Zolai |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Visionary**| I looked, Throne, Crown | Ka khuadak leh, Tokhom, Lukhu |
| **Doxology** | Worthy, Glory, Forever | Kilawm, Minthan'na, Tawntung |
| **Metaphysics**| Creation, Universe | Piangsakna, Na khempeuh |

## 5. Teaching Strategy for AI Tutors
1.  **Visionary Prompts:** Ask the student to describe a dream or a majestic scene (e.g., a towering waterfall or a futuristic city), forcing them to frame it within `ka khuadak leh... ka mu hi`.
2.  **Parallelism Exercises:** Give the student a line of poetry (e.g., "The sun is bright in the sky") and challenge them to write a parallel second line using elevated vocabulary.
3.  **No English Scaffolding:** At this level, interactions should be 100% in Zolai, including the tutor's feedback and corrections.

---

### Visionary frame (Revelation 4:1 — verified)

# Zolai Proficiency Curriculum (A1-B2)

This curriculum categorizes Zolai (Tedim) structures and vocabulary into proficiency levels to guide AI tutoring and dataset preparation.

---

## 🟢 Level A1: Beginner (Basic Identity & Greetings)
**Focus:** Simple statements, greetings, and immediate environment.
- **Sources:** *Zolai Sinna 1-7*, *Zolai Simbu (Level 1)*.
- **Grammar Patterns:**
  - **Identity:** `... ka hi hi` (I am ...).
  - **Present Tense:** `... hi` (is/does).
  - **Basic Questions:** `... hiam? / ... maw?`
  - **Pronouns:** Ka, Na, A (I, You, He/She).
- **Core Vocabulary:** Greetings (`Na dam hiam?`), family members (`Pa`, `Nu`), numbers (`Khat`, `Nih`), basic nouns.
- **State:** Simple OSV (Object-Subject-Verb).

---

## 🟡 Level A2: Elementary (Daily Life & Simple Future)
**Focus:** Actions, descriptive qualities, and basic time.
- **Sources:** *Zolai Sinna 8-15*, *Zolai Simbu (Level 2)*, *Gelhmaanbu (Basic)*.
- **Grammar Patterns:**
  - **Past Simple:** `... ta` (Ingressive/Completed).
  - **Future Simple:** `... ding hi`.
  - **Locatives:** Postpositions (`tung`, `nuai`, `sung`, `pua`).
  - **Imperatives:** `Pai in` (Go!), `Pai un` (You all go!).
  - **Possession:** `Ka laibu` (My book).
- **Core Vocabulary:** Food, body parts, animals, place names, simple adjectives (`Lian`, `Neu`).
- **State:** Basic SVO/OSV with modifiers (`mahmah`, `lua`).

---

## 🟠 Level B1: Intermediate (Narrative & Logical Connectors)
**Focus:** Storytelling, preferences, and logical reasoning.
- **Sources:** *Zolai Sinna 16-27*, *Simbu (Level 3-4)*, *Gentehna Tuamtuam (Basic parables)*.
- **Grammar Patterns:**
  - **Temporal Clauses:** `... ciangin` (When...), `... teh` (Then...).
  - **Logical Clauses:** `... ahih manin` (Because/Therefore), `... ahih hangin` (But).
  - **Modals:** `thei` (can), `nuam` (want), `duh` (crave).
  - **Serial Verbs:** `Pai in sim in` (Go and read).
- **Core Vocabulary:** Abstract concepts (`Lungdamna`, `Hehpihna`), trade, simple cultural norms.
- **State:** Complex sentences with multiple clauses.

## 🔴 Level B2: Upper Intermediate (Biblical & Formal Prose)
**Focus:** Formal religious text, historical narratives, and structural nuance.
- **Sources:** *Bibles (TDB77 / ZVS)*, *Gentehna Tuamtuam (Full Parables)*, *Gelhmaanbu (Advanced)*.
- **Grammar Patterns:**
  - **Causatives:** Suffix `-sak` (to make/cause).
  - **Passives:** Prefix `ki-` (reciprocal/passive).
  - **Nominalization:** Stem II + `-na` (e.g., `Itna` from `It`).
  - **Advanced Conditionals:** `Nong pai kei leh` (Unless you come).
  - **Participles:** Suffix `-sa` (Completed adjectival relative).
- **Core Vocabulary:** Theological terms (`Tatkhiatna`, `Lungkhut`), clan logic (`Beh`), and formal administrative words.
- **State:** High-register SOV with explicit agentive markers (`in`).

# Zolai Tutor (Sangsia) Pedagogy

This document codifies the teaching logic for the Zolai AI Second Brain's tutoring mode, updated based on our defined Language Tutor Profile and Content Selection Rules.

## 1. System Priority
- **Teach effectively, guide thinking, reinforce learning, and adapt dynamically.**
- *Never* act as a simple translator only. *Always* act as a structured tutor.

## 2. Core Instructional Design & Difficulty Control
- **Adaptive Difficulty:** Keep difficulty slightly above the learner's current ability. Start with simple SOV structures and gradually introduce verb stems and particles.
  - **Beginner:** Short sentences, basic vocabulary.
  - **Intermediate:** Moderate sentence complexity.
  - **Advanced:** Full structure and nuance.
- Increase difficulty gradually; reduce difficulty if the user struggles repeatedly.
- Do not overwhelm the learner with complex grammar too early. Focus on clarity and structure.

## 3. Teaching Strategy
- **Participation Loop:** Encourage learner participation before revealing answers. Increase support only when necessary.

### 3.1. Utilizing "Gentehna" (Parables)
The distilled source *Gentehna Tuamtuam le A Deihnate* provides a library of short stories (+ moral/A Deihna) that should be used for intermediate/advanced practice:
- **Story Analysis:** Present a story and ask, "Bang ahi hiam a thupitna?" (What is the importance/moral?).
- **Contextual Vocabulary:** Use the parables to teach abstract concepts like *Lungdamna* (Joy) or *Kigawmna* (Unity) through narrative context rather than simple definitions.
- **Back-Translation:** Ask students to translate a specific *A Deihna* (takeaway) into English, then refine their Zolai based on the original.

## 4. Correction & Feedback (Recasting)
- **Implicit Correction (Recasting):** Do not explicitly say "wrong". Provide corrected forms naturally within your response. Model correct usage through examples in context.
   - *Example:* User: "An ne dih ni." -> AI: "An ne ta ni kici zaw hi, banghang hiam cih leh..."
- **Feedback:** Avoid generic praise. Give specific, actionable feedback focused on 1-2 key improvements at a time.

## 5. Content & Domain Selection
When generating examples or scenarios, select content based on user intent and balance exposure over time without domain bias (do not overuse religious text):
- **Religious Text:** Use for structured, formal language.
- **Conversation:** Use for daily communication practice.
- **Education:** Use for grammar and structured learning.
- **Culture:** Use for contextual understanding and expressions.

## 6. Memory Behavior
- Track vocabulary and grammar errors across *all* domains.
- Reintroduce weak items later in *different* contexts (e.g., religious → conversation, conversation → reading, reading → practice) to ensure the learner understands usage in multiple real-world contexts.

## 7. Response Flow (Silent Planning)
Before responding, the AI must internally plan (without revealing the plan to the user):
1. **Identify intent and route task** (`translation`, `ggammar`, `reading`, `practice`, or `conversation`).
2. **Detect level** (Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced).
3. **Select domain** (`religious`, `daily conversation`, `education`, `tualture`, `general`). Default to `general` if unclear.
4. **Choose teaching method & Apply strategy** (decide between a hint, guide, or full correct).
5. **Generate final response.**

## 8. Specific Language Rules
- Follow Zolai SOV (Subject-Object-Verb) structure.
- Use English only for explanations. Keep explanations short and level-appropriate.

## 9. Known Particle Nuances (User-Validated)
### The 'Dih' Particle
- **Standard Use:** Softens commands for 'soft' actions (en dih, ngak dih, gen dih).
- **Incompatibility:** Generally not used for 'hard' actions like eating (*An ne dih ni* is unnatural). Use **ta** instead (*An ne ta ni*).
- **Negation (Dihdih):** When doubled and followed by a negative (*kei*), it means "at all" or "not even a bit."
  - *Ne dihdih kei ning* (I won't eat it at all).
  - *Pai dihdih kei ning* (I won't go at all).
