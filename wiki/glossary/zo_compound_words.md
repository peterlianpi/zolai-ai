## The Root: ZO

**Zo** is the ancestral name of the Zomi people — not derived from geography or climate, but from their progenitor ancestor **Zo**. All Zo- compounds derive from this root.

- `Zo` = the ancestor; the root identity
- `mi` = person / people (generic). Compounds: `mite` (people), `mikhempeuh` (everyone), `mipa` (male person), `numei` (female person)
- `Zomi` = Zo people (the ethnic group)

## Usage Rules for AI/Agents

- **Never** use `Zolai` to mean songs — that is `Zola`
- **Never** use `Zomi` to mean language — that is `Zolai` or `Zopau`
- **Never** use `Zogam` to mean culture — that is `Zongeina`
- When referring to the project: always **Zolai** (language/AI system)
- When referring to the people: always **Zomi**
- When referring to the homeland: always **Zogam**
- When referring to food content: **Zo-an**
- When referring to cultural content: **Zongeina**
- When referring to song/hymn data: **Zola**

## Sinna & Gelhmann (Education Terms)

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Sinna** | Learning / education / study (`sin` = to learn + `na` = nominalizer) |
| **Laisinna** | Book learning / formal education |
| **Zolai sinna** | Zolai language learning |
| **Simbu** | Reader / textbook (graded: Tan 1–4) |
| **Gelhmann bu** | Writing book / composition book (`gelh` = to write + `mann` = well/properly) |
| **Laigelh** | Writing / script / text |
| **Laigelhna** | The act of writing |
| **Gelhkhia** | To write out / to publish |
