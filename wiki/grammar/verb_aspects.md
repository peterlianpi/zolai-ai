# Verb Aspects (Temporal Particle System)

In Zolai (Tedim), verb tense is primarily indicated by post-verbal particles rather than suffixation or prefixation on the verb itself (though stem changes do occur).

## Present Continuous
- **Particle:** `laitak`
- **Example:** `A pai laitak` (He is going/walking at this moment).

## Immediate Past
- **Particle:** `ta`
- **Example:** `A pai ta` (He has gone/went [just now]).

## Remote Past
- **Particle:** `khin`
- **Example:** `A pai khin` (He has already gone [some time ago]).

## Future
- **Particle:** `ding`
- **Example:** `A pai ding` (He will go).

## Perfective/Statative
- **Particle:** `hi`
- **Example:** `A pai hi` (He goes / He went [general/fact]).

## Plural Suffix Omission Rule (The "uh" Redundancy Rule)
In Zolai (Tedim), the plural suffix `uh` is omitted when the first-person plural inclusive prefix `i` is used.
- **Correct:** `I pai hi` (We [inclusive] go).
- **Incorrect:** `I pai uhhi`.
- **Note:** For plural exclusive `ko`, `uh` is required: `Ko pai uh hi`.
