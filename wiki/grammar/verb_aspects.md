# Verb Aspects (Temporal Particle System)

In Zolai (Tedim), verb tense and aspect are primarily indicated by **post-verbal particles** rather than through conjugation (though stem changes occur in certain environments).

---

## 1. Primary Aspect Markers

| Aspect | Particle | Function | Example | Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Simple/Statative** | `hi` | General fact or state | `A pai hi.` | He goes / He went. |
| **Progressive** | `laitak` | Ongoing action (now) | `A pai laitak.` | He is going. |
| **Immediate Past** | `ta` | Just happened | `A pai ta.` | He has gone (just now). |
| **Completed Past** | `khin` | Finished some time ago | `A pai khin hi.` | He has already gone. |
| **Resultative** | `zo` | Action finished/achieved | `A pai zo hi.` | He has finished going. |
| **Future** | `ding` | Intention or future fact | `A pai ding hi.` | He will go. |
| **Experiential** | `ngei` | "Ever" or "Used to" | `A pai ngei hi.` | He has gone before. |

---

## 2. Negation and Aspect

Zolai uses specific particles to negate different temporal states.

- **`kei` (Simple Negation)**: Used for future, habitual, or potential negation.
  - `Ka pai kei ding hi.` (I will not go.)
  - `A thei kei hi.` (He does not know.)
- **`lo` (Simple Negation)**: Used for past or emphatic refusal.
  - `A pai lo hi.` (He did not go.)
- **`nai lo` (Not yet)**: Negates a completed aspect that is expected to happen.
  - `A pai nai lo hi.` (He has not gone yet.)
- **`nawn lo` (No longer)**: Negates an ongoing state or habit.
  - `A pai nawn lo hi.` (He no longer goes.)

---

## 3. Directional Influence on Aspect

Directional particles often imply an completed or resultative aspect:
- `khia` (out): `Pulaakkhia` (to have published/released)
- `to` (up): `Kahto` (to have ascended)
- `suk` (down): `Paisuk` (to have descended)

---

## 4. The "uh" Redundancy Rule (Critical)

In Zolai (Tedim), the plural suffix `uh` is omitted when the **first-person plural inclusive** prefix `i` is used. This is a strict ZVS standard.

- ✅ **Correct:** `I pai hi.` (We [all] go.)
- ❌ **Incorrect:** `I pai uh hi.`
- **Exception:** For the plural exclusive `ko`, the `uh` is required: `Ko pai uh hi.` (We [but not you] go.)

---

## 5. Potential Aspect (`theih`)

To express ability or permission, use `theih`:
- `Ka pai theih hi.` (I can go.)
- `Ka pai thei kei hi.` (I cannot go.)
