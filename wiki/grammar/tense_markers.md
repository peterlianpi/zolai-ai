# Tense, Aspect & Mood Markers

Zolai (Tedim) uses sentence-final particles and verb suffixes to indicate tense, aspect, and mood. This document provides the complete inventory extracted from the ZVS Bible, Sinna exercises, and Khanggui grammar notes.

## 1. Tense Markers

| Tense | Marker | Example | Translation |
|-------|--------|---------|-------------|
| **Present** | **hi** | `A pai hi.` | He goes. / He is going. |
| **Present Continuous** | **laitak** | `A pai laitak hi.` | He is going (right now). |
| **Past Simple** | **ta** | `A pai ta hi.` | He went. / He has gone. |
| **Present Perfect** | **khinzo** | `A pai khinzo hi.` | He has already gone. |
| **Past Perfect** | **khinsa** | `A pai khinsa hi.` | He had gone. |
| **Future** | **ding** | `A pai ding hi.` | He will go. |
| **Future Perfect** | **khinzo ding** | `A pai khinzo ding hi.` | He will have gone. |

## 2. Aspect Markers

| Aspect | Marker | Meaning | Example |
|--------|--------|---------|---------|
| **Completive** | **zo** | Action fully finished | `A pai zo hi.` — He has finished going. |
| **Experiential** | **khin** | Has done at least once | `Ka mu khin hi.` — I have seen [it before]. |
| **Inceptive** | **ta** | Change of state / new situation | `A hoih ta hi.` — It has become good (now). |
| **Habitual** | **den** | Keeps doing repeatedly | `Nasem den hi.` — He keeps working. |
| **Progressive** | **lai** | In the middle of | `A ne lai hi.` — He is eating. |
| **Immediate** | **laitak** | Right at this moment | `Ka pai laitak hi.` — I am going right now. |
| **Perfective** | **khinsa** | Already completed before reference | `A pai khinsa hi.` — He had already gone. |
| **Resumptive** | **kik** | Do again | `A pai kik hi.` — He went again. |
| **Attempted** | **sawm** | Try to do | `A pai sawm hi.` — He tried to go. |
| **Immediate action** | **pah** | Do immediately | `A pai pah hi.` — He went immediately. |
| **Intensive** | **mahmah** | Very much | `A hoih mahmah hi.` — It is very good. |

## 3. Mood Markers

### A. Declarative (Default)
- `A pai hi.` — He goes. (Simple declaration, ends with `hi`)

### B. Interrogative
- `A pai hiam?` — Does he go? (Yes/no question)
- `Koi ah pai hiam?` — Where does he go? (Content question)

### C. Imperative
| Form | Marker | Example | Translation |
|------|--------|---------|-------------|
| Direct command | **un / in** | `Pai un.` | Go! (plural) |
| Polite suggestion | **dih** | `Hong pai pak dih.` | Please come. |
| Hortative ("let us") | **ni** | `Mihing bawl ni.` | Let us make man. *(GEN 1:26)* |
| Benedictive ("let it be") | **hen** | `Khuavak om hen.` | Let there be light. *(GEN 1:3)* |
| Prohibitive | **kei in** | `Pai kei in.` | Don't go. |

### D. Optative (wish/hope)
- `Pasian thupha hong pia ta hen.` — May God's blessing come.
- `Zomite hanlung ciam khangto diam.` — May Zomi hearts grow forever. *(Sinna — ABC)*

## 4. Negation Patterns

| Pattern | Context | Example | Translation |
|---------|---------|---------|-------------|
| **V + lo** | General present/past | `Ka ne lo hi.` | I don't eat. |
| **V + kei** | Future/intent | `Ka pai kei ding hi.` | I won't go. |
| **V + kei + in** | Imperative negative | `Pai kei in.` | Don't go. |
| **V + keng** | 1st person contraction (kei+in) | `Ka pai keng.` | I won't go. |
| **V + ngei lo** | Experiential ("never") | `Ka mu ngei lo hi.` | I have never seen. |
| **V + thei lo** | Ability ("cannot") | `Ka gen thei lo hi.` | I cannot say. |
| **V + nuam lo** | Desire ("don't want") | `Ka pai nuam lo hi.` | I don't want to go. |
| **kuamah...lo** | Universal ("nobody does") | `Kuamah a theilo hi.` | Nobody knows. |

### ⚠️ Critical Negation Rule
- **Future/intent:** Use `kei`, NOT `lo`
  - ✅ `Ka pai kei ding hi.` — I will not go.
  - ❌ `Ka pai lo ding.` — **Unnatural in pure Tedim**
- **Negative conditional:** Use `kei...leh`, NEVER `kei a leh`
  - ✅ `Nong pai kei a leh...` — If you don't come...
  - ❌ `Na pai kei a leh...` — **FORBIDDEN in ZVS**

## 5. Directional & Positional Particles

| Particle | Direction | Example | Translation |
|----------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| **hong** | Toward speaker | `Hong pai in.` | Come (toward me). |
| **va** | Away from speaker | `Va pai in.` | Go (away from me). |
| **suk** | Downward | `A tui lutsuk hi.` | He dived down. |
| **toh** | Upward | `Kahto in...` | Going up... |
| **khia** | Outward | `Paikhia hi.` | He went out. |
| **lut** | Inward | `Inn sungah a lut hi.` | He entered the house. |

## 6. Evidentiality & Source Markers

| Marker | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **kiza hi** | It is heard/reported | `A pai kiza hi.` — It is heard that he went. *(News register)* |
| **kigen hi** | It is said | `A hoih kigen hi.` — It is said to be good. |
| **kiummawh hi** | It is believed/suspected | `Hiding in kiummawh hi.` — It is suspected to be so. |
| **ci hi** | [Someone] says | `Topa in, "..." ci hi.` — The Lord says, "..." |

---

## ZVS 2018 Standard Notes
The **2018 Zokam Standard Version (ZVS)** prioritizes:
- `ta` for completed actions in narrative contexts
- `hi` for universal truths or ongoing present states
- `ding` strictly for future/unrealized events
- This distinction preserves the "Lung-Kha" (Heart-Spirit) emotional weight in translations
