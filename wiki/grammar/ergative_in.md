# Ergative Marker: The Particle "in"

In Zolai (Tedim), the particle **"in"** is the ergative marker. It is required to identify the subject (agent) of a **transitive verb** (a verb that takes an object).

## Core Rule
Whenever a subject performs an action on an object, the subject must be marked with **"in"**.

### Standard Transitive Pattern: [Subject] + **in** + [Object] + [Verb]

| Type | Zolai Sentence | English Translation |
|------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Transitive** | **Amah in** laibu sim hi. | **He** reads the book. |
| **Intransitive** | Amah pai hi. | He goes. (No "in" needed) |

## Pronoun Merging (Contractions)
In spoken and modern written Zolai (ZVS), the ergative "in" often merges with pronouns to create a single, powerful agentive form.

| Root Pronoun | + "in" (Ergative) | Resulting Form | Example |
|--------------|------------------|----------------|---------|
| **Kei** (I) | Kei + in | **Ken** | **Ken** ka sim hi. (I read it.) |
| **Nang** (You) | Nang + in | **Nan** | **Nan** na sim hi. (You read it.) |
| **Ama** (He/She) | Ama + in | **Aman** | **Aman** a sim hi. (He/She reads it.) |
| **Eite** (We) | Eite + in | **Eiten** | **Eiten** i sim hi. (We read it.) |

## 2018 Standard Compliance (ZVS)
The **2018 Zokam Standard Version (ZVS)** strictly enforces these markers to prevent ambiguity between the subject and the object, especially in complex theological texts.

- **Incorrect:** "Amah laibu sim hi." (Ambiguous; lacks agency marker)
- **Correct:** "**Amah in** laibu sim hi." (Clear agentive action)

## Semantic Nuance
- **Emphasis:** Using the merged form (**Ken**, **Nan**) puts stronger emphasis on the agent's responsibility for the action.
- **Formal vs. Informal:** In very formal or biblical contexts, the split form (**Kei in**, **Nang in**) is sometimes used for rhythmic or traditional weight, but the merged forms are the standard for the AI Second Brain's active generation.
