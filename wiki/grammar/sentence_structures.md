# Zolai Sentence Structures — A Complete Reference

This document catalogs every major sentence pattern in the Zolai (Tedim) language, from basic SOV to advanced embedded clauses. All examples are drawn from authentic sources: Bible (ZVS 2018), Zolai Sinna, Gentehna Tuamtuam, and Zolai Khanggui.

## 1. Basic SOV (Subject – Object – Verb)

Zolai is strictly **SOV** (Subject–Object–Verb). The verb always comes last.

| Pattern | Example | Translation | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| S + V | `Pasian om hi.` | God exists. | GEN 1:1 |
| S + O + V | `Ka pa sangah pai hi.` | My father goes to school. | Sinna |
| S + O + V (past) | `Pasian in leitung a piangsak hi.` | God created the earth. | GEN 1:1 |
| S + IO + DO + V | `Pasian in amaute thupha a pia hi.` | God gave them blessings. | GEN 1:28 |

## 2. Tense Constructions

### A. Present (hi)
- `Zomi ka hi hi.` — I am a Zomi. *(Sinna 1)*
- `A pai hi.` — He/She goes.

### B. Past (ta / khin / zo)
- `A pai ta hi.` — He went. (Change of state)
- `A pai khin hi.` — He has gone. (Experiential)
- `A pai zo hi.` — He has finished going. (Completive)

### C. Future (ding)
- `A pai ding hi.` — He will go.
- `Ka pai kei ding hi.` — I will not go. (Future negation uses `kei`, NOT `lo`)

### D. Progressive (lai / laitak / den)
- `A pai lai hi.` — He is going (progressive).
- `A pai laitak hi.` — He is going right now (immediate progressive).
- `Nasep den hi.` — He keeps working (habitual).

---

## 3. Negation Patterns

| Pattern | Example | Translation | Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **V + lo** | `Ka ne lo hi.` | I don't eat. | General present negation |
| **V + kei** | `Ka pai kei ding hi.` | I won't go. | Future/intent negation |
| **V + kei + in** | `Pai kei in.` | Don't go. | Imperative negative |
| **V + keng** | `Ka pai keng.` | I won't go. | Contraction: kei + in |
| **V + ngei lo** | `Ka mu ngei lo hi.` | I have never seen. | Experiential negation |
| **longal...kei** | `Kuamah in a muh kei.` | No one saw it. | Universal negation |

## 4. Interrogative Constructions

### A. Content Questions (wh-questions)
| Question Word | Example | Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bang** (what) | `Bang na duh hiam?` | What do you want? |
| **Koi** (where) | `Koi ah na pai hiam?` | Where are you going? |
| **Kua** (who) | `Kua na hi hiam?` | Who are you? |
| **Bang hang** (why) | `Bang hang hiam?` | Why? |
| **Bang ci** (how) | `Bang ci hiam?` | How is it? |
| **Bang zah** (how much) | `Bang zah hiam?` | How much? |
| **Bang hun** (when) | `Bang hun ah na pai ding?` | When will you go? |

### B. Yes/No Questions (hiam)
- `Na pai ding hiam?` — Will you go?
- `Hoih hiam?` — Is it good?

### C. Rhetorical Questions (Bible)
- `Bang hang hiam cih leh...` — Why? Because... *(GEN 2:3, 2:17)*
- `Kei bang ci hiam?` — What am I?

---

## 5. Conditional Constructions

### A. Simple Conditional (leh)
- `Na pai leh, ka lungdam ding.` — If you go, I'll be happy.
- `Ahih leh, bang ci ding hiam?` — If so, what should we do?

### B. Temporal Conditional (ciangin)
- `A pai ciangin, ka zui ding.` — When he goes, I will follow.
- `Ahih ciangin...` — When it was so... *(narrative connector in Genesis)*

### C. Negative Conditional (kei a leh) ⚠️
> **CRITICAL ZVS RULE:** Negative conditionals use `kei a leh`, NEVER `kei a leh`.
- ✅ `Nong pai kei a leh...` — If you don't come... (**correct ZVS form**)
- ❌ `Nong pai kei a leh...` — **FORBIDDEN** (Hakha/Falam form)
- ❌ `Na pai kei leh...` — **WRONG** (missing the `a` particle before `leh`)

### D. Concessive (hangin / napi)
- `Hak hangin, hanciam hi.` — Although it's difficult, [he] perseveres.
- `A hatloh napi, hanciam hi.` — Despite being weak, [he] perseveres.

| Form | Pattern | Example | Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Polite (hen)** | V + hen | `Khuavak om hen.` | Let there be light. *(GEN 1:3)* |

## 9. Relative Clauses

Zolai relative clauses precede the noun they modify, using the structure `V + [noun]`:

- `Topa in a piangsak ganhingte` — The creatures that the Lord created.
- `Nong gen thu` — The word that you spoke.
- `A kipat cilin Pasian in a piangsak vantung leh leitung` — In the beginning, the heaven and earth that God created. *(GEN 1:1)*

---

## 10. Direct & Indirect Speech

### A. Direct Speech (ci / ci hi)
The quotative particle `ci` introduces or closes direct speech:
- `Pasian in, "Khuavak om hen," ci hi.` — God said, "Let there be light." *(GEN 1:3)*

### B. Indirect Speech (ci-in)
- `Pasian in tua khuavak hoih hi, ci-in a mu hi.` — God saw the light, saying it was good. *(GEN 1:4)*

### C. Reported Identity (kici)
- `Tua mi pen "Laipianpa" na kici hi.` — That person was called "Laipianpa." *(Sinna)*

## 14. Passive Voice (ki- prefix)

Zolai forms passives using the reflexive/reciprocal prefix `ki-`:
- `Kibawl` — Be made (from `bawl` = make)
- `Kikhensak` — Be separated (from `khen` = divide)
- `Kikoih` — Be placed (from `koih` = put)
- `Kiza` — Be known/heard (from `za` = hear)
- Example: `Thupha lapha kiza hi.` — Blessings are heard. *(Sinna 1)*

## 15. Poetic & Literary Structures (from Psalms, Gentehna)

### A. Parallelism
The dominant literary device in Psalms and proverbs, where the second line echoes or contrasts the first:
- `Zogam a ding Pasian, Zomi a ding Pasian` — God for Zogam, God for Zomi. *(Zogam Lapi)*

### B. Call-and-Response
- `Khapi aw hong kia, hong kia / Na koi lai na tat, na tat` *(Sinna — children's song)*

### C. Moral Framing (Gentehna Pattern)
Every parable in *Gentehna Tuamtuam* follows: **Narrative → A Deihna (Moral/Desired Meaning)**

## 16. Register Variation: Formal vs. Informal

Zolai has distinct formal and informal registers, particularly in subject-verb agreement and pronoun dropping.

### Eating / Action Sentences

| Register | Example | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Formal** | `Kei in an ka ne hi.` | Full subject + ergative `in` + object + 1st person `ka` + verb + `hi` |
| **Informal** | `An ka ne hi.` | Subject (`Kei`) dropped; natural in conversation |
| **Casual** | `Ka ne hi.` | Object also dropped when context is clear |

> **Rule:** In informal/conversational speech, the explicit subject pronoun (`Kei`, `na'ng`, `Amah`) is commonly dropped when the 1st/2nd/3rd person agreement marker (`ka`, `na`, `a`) makes the subject clear from context.
>
> **For training data:** Tag informal forms with `"register": "informal"` and formal/biblical forms with `"register": "formal"`.

## 17. Narrative Connectors (Biblical / Story Register)

These are the backbone of Zolai storytelling, extracted from Genesis and Exodus.

| Connector | Meaning | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| `Tua ciangin` | Then | `Tua ciangin Pasian in, "Eima' lim...mihing bawl ni," ci hi.` |
| `Tua ahih ciangin` | So when that was / Therefore | `Tua ahih ciangin Pasian in ama' lim bangin mihing piangsakin...` |
| `Tua mah bangin` | Just like that | `Tua mah bangin piang pah hi.` *(repeated in GEN 1:7, 1:9, 1:11)* |
| `Tu-in` | Now / At this point | `Tu-in gulpi pen...ngiansiam zaw hi.` |
| `Tua hun sungin` | In those days | `Tua hun sungin Kain in lei pan-a piang gah piakna...` |
| `A zing ciangin` | In the morning | `A zing ciangin amah a paikhiat ciangin...` |
| `Nitak hong bei-in, zingsang hong tung a` | Evening came, morning came | Day-counting formula in Genesis creation account |
| `A kipat cil-in` | In the beginning | `A kipat cil-in Pasian in vantung leh leitung a piangsak hi.` |

## 18. Quotative Patterns (Direct Speech)

Zolai direct speech always closes with `ci hi`. Mid-sentence speech uses `ci-in`.

| Pattern | Example | Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| `"[Quote]," ci hi` | `"Khuavak om ta hen," ci hi.` | [He] said, "Let there be light." |
| `ci-in [verb]` | `Hoih hi, ci-in a mu hi.` | Seeing it, [He] said it was good. |
| `ci aa [verb]` | `Tua bang ci aa paikhia hi.` | Having said so, he left. |
| `kici` | `Laipianpa na kici hi.` | He was called Laipianpa. |
| `ci-in gen` | `"Pai in," ci-in gen hi.` | He said, "Go." |

## 19. Directional Verb Particles

Zolai verbs combine with directional particles to specify movement precisely.

| Particle | Direction | Example | Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| `hong` | Toward speaker | `Hong pai in.` | Come (here). |
| `va` | Away from speaker | `Va pai in.` | Go (away). |
| `khia` | Outward / Exit | `Paikhia hi.` | He went out. |
| `lut` | Inward / Enter | `Inn sungah a lut hi.` | He entered the house. |
| `kik` | Back / Return | `Paikik hi.` | He went back. |
| `suk` | Downward | `Paisuk hi.` | He went down. |
| `pih` | Bring / Send | `Paipih hi.` | He was sent / brought. |
| `toh` | Upward | `Kahto hi.` | He went up. |

## 20. Blessing and Prohibition Patterns

### Blessing (thupha pia)
- `Pasian in amaute thupha pia a, "Ci pha-in tampi pha un," ci hi.` *(GEN 1:22)*
  — God blessed them, saying, "Be fruitful and multiply."
- `Pasian in ni sagih ni thupha pia-in siangthosak hi.` *(GEN 2:3)*
  — God blessed the seventh day and sanctified it.

### Prohibition (kei ding hi)
- `Na ne kei ding hi.` *(GEN 2:17)* — You shall not eat.
- `Na lawng kei ding-te hi.` *(GEN 3:3)* — You shall not touch it.
- Pattern: **Verb + kei ding hi** = shall not / must not

---

## 21. Identity Assertion: "I AM" Pattern

The most emphatic identity statement in Zolai uses full pronoun + `ka hi hi`:

- `KEIMAH ka hi hi.` *(EXO 3:14)* — I AM THAT I AM. (God's self-declaration)
- `Kei Zomi ka hi hi.` — I am Zomi.
- `Amah pen ka Pa ahi hi.` — He is my Father.
- `Keimah in na thei hi.` — You yourself know it.

Pattern: **[Emphatic pronoun] + [agreement marker] + hi hi**
