# Dialectal Nuance: Tedim, Sizang, and Zokam

While **Zokam (Tedim)** is the literary and standardized form used in the **2018 ZVS**, the Zomi language family contains several distinct dialects with subtle but important differences in phonology, vocabulary, and grammar.

## Key Dialects in the Corpus

| Dialect | Description | Relationship to Standard |
|---------|-------------|--------------------------|
| **Tedim** | The most widely spoken and the basis for the literary standard. | Primary source for modern ZVS. |
| **Sizang** | Known for its distinct "Sizang" tone and specific vocabulary (e.g., using "Ka" more frequently). | Influences historical texts (1932/1977). |
| **Falam (FCL)** | A closely related Chin language with overlapping vocabulary but distinct grammar. | Used for cross-linguistic reference. |
| **Hakha (HCL)** | Another major Chin language, more distinct from Tedim but shares cultural "Lung-Kha" roots. | Used for comparative theological mapping. |

## Notable Differences

### 1. Pronoun and Particle Usage
- **Standard (ZVS):** Uses "in" for ergative marking and "hi" for present tense.
- **Sizang Influence:** Sometimes omits certain final particles or uses alternative future markers like "suak" in specific contexts.

### 2. Phonetic Shifts in Spelling
- **Archaic Tedim:** Often uses 's' where modern Zokam might use 'sh' in certain transliterations (e.g., *Izipt* vs. *Egypt*).
- **Burmese Influence:** Older dialects (1932) reflect phonetic borrowings from Burmese that the 2018 Standard has moved away from in favor of more native or international phonetic patterns.

### 3. "Lung" (Heart) Idioms
The way "Lung" is paired with verbs varies slightly across dialects, though the core "Lung-Kha" architecture remains universal among the Zomi people.

- **Tedim:** *Lungdam* (Happy).
- **Comparative:** Some dialects may use different modifiers to express the same "Heart-state," which the AI Second Brain must map correctly to maintain tone.
