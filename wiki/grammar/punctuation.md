# Punctuation and Orthography (ZVS 2018 & Khanggui)

## 1. Apostrophe (Pawfi / Tanglak)
The apostrophe is a critical marker in Zolai (Tedim) Standard Format, serving omission, possession, and disambiguation roles.

### A. Possessive Contraction
Contracted from the full possessive particle `ii`.
- **Pasian' thu** (God's word) instead of *Pasian ii thu*.
- **Ama' ta** (His child) instead of *Amah ii ta*.

### B. Agentive/Ergative Omission
Used when the agentive particle `in` is attached to a pronoun or noun ending in a vowel, omitting the 'i'.
- **Kote'n** (We [exclusive] + agentive) instead of *Kote in*.
- **Amah'n** (He/She + agentive) instead of *Amah in*.

### C. Word Distinction (Homonym Disambiguation)
Distinguishes words with similar spellings but different roots or meanings.
- **thuman'na** (faithfulness/action) vs. **thumanna** (truth/abstract).

## 2. Spacing and Compound Words

Consistent with *Zolai Khanggui (AD 1899–2013)* lines 483–500, Zolai follows the **"One Concept = One Word"** (`noun khat word khat`) rule.

### A. Single Concept Compounds
Join words that represent a single, unified idea:
- **Vantun** (Heaven) — *van* (sky) + *tung* (above)
- **Leitung** (Earth) — *lei* (ground) + *tung* (above)
- **Tuipi** (Sea) — *tui* (water) + *pi* (big)
- **Lai Siangtho** (Holy Bible) — Standardized specific capitalization.
- **Innkuan** (Family) — *inn* (house) + *kuan* (gather/group)
- **Mihing** (Human) — *mi* (person) + *hing* (alive)

### B. Logical Joining
- **Plurality:** Suffixes `-te` and `-teng` are joined: `mite`, `naupangteng`.
- **Nominalization:** Suffix `-na` is joined to the preceding verb: `itna`, `theihna`.
- **Reciprocal:** Prefix `ki-` is joined: `kipawl`, `kituak`.

---

## 3. Double Vowels & Tones
- **Double Vowels:** Used for long tones or distinct vowel lengths (e.g., `vaan` for sky, `van` for goods).
- **Phonetic Realization:** The letter `o` is realized as /oʊ/. Doubling `o` is rare; usually, `u` is used for the /u/ sound.

---

## 4. Diminutives
- Names and objects often take the suffix `-no` for endearment or smallness.
- Examples: **Minno**, **Tuangno**, **Aksi** (star — bird-die/seed-small).

---

## 5. Punctuation Marks (Laimal Zatzia)
1. **Tawpna (.)**: Period for ends of sentences.
2. **Husanna (,)**: Comma for lists. **Note:** ZVS forbids a comma before `le` (and).
   - ✅ `Thangpi, Lunsen le Lian Pau`
3. **Ngakna (:)**: Colon for lists or direct quotes.
4. **Thekna (-)**: Hyphen for ranges or specific complex compounds.
