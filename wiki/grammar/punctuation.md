# Punctuation and Orthography (Zolai Standard Format 2018)

## Apostrophe (')
The apostrophe is a critical marker in Zolai (Tedim) Standard Format.

### 1. Possessive Contraction
Contracted from the full possessive particle `ii`.
- Example: `Pasian' thu` (God's word) instead of `Pasian ii thu`.

### 2. Agentive/Ergative Omission
Used when the agentive particle `in` is attached to a pronoun or noun ending in a vowel, omitting the 'i'.
- Example: `Kote'n` (We [exclusive] + agentive) instead of `Kote in`.
- Example: `Amah'n` (He/She + agentive) instead of `Amah in`.

### 3. Word Distinction (Homonym Disambiguation)
Used to distinguish between words that are spelled similarly but have different meanings or underlying structures.
- Example: `thuman'na` (obedience/faithfulness in action) vs `thumanna` (truth/truthfulness).

## Spacing and Double Vowels
- **Double Vowels:** Used for long tones (e.g., `vaan` for sky, `van` for goods).
- **Compound Words:** Generally written together if they form a single concept, but hyphenated or separated based on the 2018 Standard.

## Diminutives
- Names often take the suffix `-no` to indicate smallness or endearment (*Minno*).
- Example: `Tuangno`, `Nuuno`.
