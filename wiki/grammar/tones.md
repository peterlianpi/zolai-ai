# Tones and Phonology (Awsaang)

Tedim Zolai is a tonal language. The Tedim Standard Tone System maps 14 practical tonemes based on pitch and length.

## Tone Categories (Pitch)
1. **P1 (High)**
2. **P2 (Mid-High)**
3. **P3 (Mid-Low)**
4. **P4 (Low)**

## Length Categories
- **Short (S)**
- **Long (L)** (Indicated by double vowels in Standard Format)

## Practical Toneme Matrix
The combination of pitch and length determines the final toneme (e.g., P1S, P1L, P2S, P2L, etc.).

### Homograph Examples
- `van` (P3S - goods/things)
- `vaan` (P3L - sky/heaven)

## Orthographic Tone Markers
In the Zolai Standard Format (2018), double vowels are primarily used for marking long tones, while specialized symbols (e.g., `,`, `'`, `.`) are used in some contexts to denote pitch, though standard literature often relies on context and double vowel length.
