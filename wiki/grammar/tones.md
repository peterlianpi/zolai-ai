# Tones and Phonology (Awsaang)

Tedim Zolai is a **tonal language**. The ZVS (Zokam Standard Version) and ZCLS standards recognize a practical system of pitch and length.

---

## 1. Tone Categories (Pitch)

The pitch of a word in Zolai changes its meaning. There are 4 primary pitch levels:

1. **P1 (High)**: A rising or high-level pitch (similar to an English question).
2. **P2 (Mid-High)**: A steady pitch above the baseline.
3. **P3 (Mid-Low)**: The baseline or normal speaking pitch.
4. **P4 (Low)**: A low or falling pitch.

---

## 2. Length Categories (Vowel Duration)

Vowel length is critical and is marked orthographically in the **Zolai Standard Format (ZVS)**:

- **Short (S)**: Single vowel (e.g., `an` - food).
- **Long (L)**: Double vowel (e.g., `aan` - to lean).

---

## 3. Tonal Minimal Pairs

The combination of pitch and length (Tonemes) creates distinct meanings for identical consonant-vowel combinations.

| Word | Tone/Length | Meaning | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **van** | P3 Short | Thing / Goods / Object | `Hih van bang hi hiam?` |
| **vaan** | P3 Long | Sky / Heaven | `Vantung ah Pasian om hi.` |
| **sa** | P3 Short | Meat / Flesh | `Bawng sa ka ne hi.` |
| **saa** | P1 Long | Hot / To feel hot | `Tui a saa mahmah hi.` |
| **gu** | P4 Short | Bone | `Ka gu a na hi.` |
| **guu** | P4 Long | To steal | `Van a guu hi.` |

---

## 4. Orthographic Tone Markers (Symbols)

While double vowels mark length, Zolai also uses punctuation-like markers for specific tonal or grammatical shifts:

- **Double Vowels (`aa`, `ee`, `ii`, `oo`, `uu`)**: Marks long vowels (P1L, P2L, etc.).
- **Apostrophe (`'`)**: Often marks a glottal stop or a clipped tone at the end of a syllable (e.g., `pa'` in `ka pa'`).
- **Comma (`,`)**: Used in some dictionaries to mark rising pitch.
- **Full Stop (`.`)**: Used in some dictionaries to mark falling pitch.

---

## 5. Phonetic Restrictions (ZVS Rules)

Regardless of tone, Zolai follows strict phonetic rules:
- **No `ti` clusters**: The sounds `t` and `i` never combine. Use `te` or `tu` equivalents.
- **`c` Restrictions**: The `c` consonant only pairs with `i` and `u`. Words like `ca`, `ce`, `co`, `caw` are strictly forbidden.
- **`o` Realization**: The letter `o` is always pronounced as a diphthong `/oĘŠ/` (like "go").

---

## 6. Tone Sandhi (Tone Shifting)

In Zolai, the tone of a word can shift depending on the following word, especially with particles:
- **Declarative `hi`**: Often pulls the preceding verb into a neutral or mid-tone.
- **Interrogative `hiam`**: Forces a rising tone on the final syllable.
