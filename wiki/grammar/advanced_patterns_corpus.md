## 1. Relative Clauses

Zolai has no relative pronoun (who/which/that). Instead, a participial phrase precedes the noun.

**Pattern:** `a [verb] mi` = the one who [verb] / those who [verb]

| Zolai | English | Count |
|---|---|---|
| `a om mi` | the one who is / those who are | 18 |
| `a gamta mi` | the one who does | 8 |
| `a ngah mi` | the one who receives | 8 |
| `a nei mi` | the one who has | 7 |
| `a teeng mi` | the one who dwells | 6 |
| `a mu mi` | the one who sees | 3 |
| `a siangtho mi` | the one who is holy | 3 |
| `mi khat` | a certain person / someone | 77 |

**Examples (corpus-verified):**
- `Kei hong itte ka hausak a...` = He enriched those who love me
- `A om khempeuh in...` = All who are present...
- `A pai mi in...` = The one who went...
- `Topa a it mi in a u a nau a it ding hi.` = He who loves God must love his brother.

**For things:** `a [verb] thu` = the thing that [verb]
- `A bawlsa thu` = the thing he made
- `A gen thu` = what he said

## 2. Serial Verbs (Verb Chaining)

Zolai chains verbs using `-in` or `a` between them. The first verb is subordinate, the second is main.

**Pattern:** `[verb1]-in [verb2] hi` = [verb1] and then [verb2]

| Serial form | Count | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `pai-in` | 499 | went and... |
| `kizui-in` | 244 | following and... |
| `pia-in` | 159 | giving and... |
| `khia-in` | 126 | going out and... |
| `kisai-in` | 119 | concerning and... |
| `zui-in` | 108 | following and... |
| `nei-in` | 81 | having and... |
| `nusia-in` | 71 | leaving and... |

**Examples:**
- `Filistiate hong pai-in Refaim kuam sung luh uh hi.` = The Philistines came and spread in the valley.
- `David kiangah Gad hong pai-in ama tungah gen hi.` = Gad came to David and said to him.
- `Kaleb in Efrath ten'pihin, tua nu in amah tawh Hur nei hi.` = Caleb took Ephrath, and she bore him Hur.

**Rule:** `-in` suffix on first verb = "and then" / sequential action.

## 3. Topic Marker: `tawh kisai` / `kisai-in`

**Meaning:** concerning / regarding / about / as for

- `Topa' min tawh kisai-in Solomon' minthan'na a za hi.` = She heard of Solomon's fame concerning the Lord's name.
- `Judah gam leh Jerusalem khua tawh kisai Isaiah muhna thu.` = The word Isaiah saw concerning Judah and Jerusalem.
- `Hih thu tawh kisai-in...` = Concerning this matter...

---

## 4. Vocative: `aw`

**Meaning:** O! / Hey! (direct address)

- `Sihna aw, na zawhna koi lai-ah om hiam?` = O death, where is your sting?
- `Ka ta aw` = O my son
- `Ka itte aw` = O my beloved (1 John pattern)
- `Topa aw` = O Lord
- `Korin mite aw` = O Corinthians

**Pattern:** `[addressee] aw,` = O [addressee],

---

## 5. Reciprocal: `khat leh khat` / `ki-` prefix

**`khat leh khat`** = one another / each other
- `Khat leh khat ki-it ni.` = Let us love one another.
- `Khat leh khat a siangtho tawh hopih un.` = Greet one another with a holy kiss.
- `Eite khat leh khat ki-it leh...` = If we love one another...

**`ki-` prefix** = reciprocal/reflexive action
- `kikhawm` = gather together
- `kimu` = meet each other
- `kiteeng` = marry / live together
- `kikhen` = separate from each other
- `kiho` = talk with each other
- `ki-it` = love one another

## 6. Distributive: `khempeuh` / `khatpeuh`

| Zolai | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `khempeuh` | all / every / each | `mi khempeuh` = all people |
| `khatpeuh` | every single one | `khua khatpeuh` = every village |
| `a om khempeuh` | all who are | `a om khempeuh in` = all who are present |
| `na khempeuh` | all yours | `na nasep khempeuh` = all your work |

**Examples:**
- `A innkuan khempeuh si hi.` = All his household died.
- `Mi khempeuh in Topa pahtawi uh hi.` = All people praised the Lord.
- `Khua khatpeuh peuh sungah` = in every single village

## 7. Intensifier: `mahmah`

**Meaning:** very / greatly / exceedingly

- `Lian mahmah hi.` = Very great.
- `Hoih mahmah hi.` = Very good.
- `Heh mahmah hi.` = Very angry.
- `Huaiham mahmah hi.` = Very zealous.
- `Tangvalte' khialhna lian mahmah hi.` = The sin of the young men was very great.

**Pattern:** `[adjective/verb] mahmah hi` = very [adj/verb]

**Other intensifiers:**
- `takpi` = truly / really: `nakpi takin` = very severely
- `taktak` = truly/really (emphatic): `Ta taktak ahi napi-in` = Although truly a Son
- `zenzen` = absolutely/completely: `Tua bang i ci zenzen kei ding hi` = May it absolutely never be

---

## 8. Approximative: `bang`

**Meaning:** about / approximately

- `Ni sawm bang ciangin` = After about ten days
- `Kum thum bang` = About three years
- `Mi za bang` = About a hundred people

**Pattern:** `[number] bang` = about [number]

---

## 9. Possession Patterns

**Apostrophe `'`** marks possession:
- `Pasian' inn` = God's house
- `David' tapa` = David's son
- `ama' zi` = his wife
- `amau' omna` = their dwelling

**Possessive pronouns:**
| Person | Possessive | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1sg | `ka` | `ka pa` = my father |
| 2sg | `na` | `na inn` = your house |
| 3sg | `ama` / `ama'` | `ama' tapa` = his son |
| 1pl | `i` / `eite` | `i Pasian` = our God |
| 2pl | `na` / `note` | `na upna uh` = your faith |
| 3pl | `amau` / `amau'` | `amau' gam` = their land |

## 10. Common Verb Compounds

| Zolai | Meaning | Components |
|---|---|---|
| `paikhia` | go out / depart | pai (go) + khia (out) |
| `paisan` | go up / ascend | pai (go) + san (up) |
| `paitawn` | go down / descend | pai (go) + tawn (down) |
| `paikik` | go back / return | pai (go) + kik (back) |
| `kuankhia` | go out | kuan (exit) + khia (out) |
| `kuansan` | go up | kuan + san |
| `suakkhia` | come out / emerge | suak (emerge) + khia |
| `ciahkik` | return / come back | ciah (arrive) + kik (back) |
| `hotkhia` | save / rescue | hot (rescue) + khia (out) |
| `damsak` | heal / make well | dam (well) + sak (causative) |
| `nungtasak` | give life / make live | nungta (live) + sak |
| `thahsak` | kill / cause to die | thah (kill) + sak |

## 11. Sentence Patterns Summary

### Simple statement
`[S] in [O] [V] hi.` = Subject does Object.
`Ka pa in laibu a sim hi.` = My father reads a book.

### Identity
`[S] pen [N] ahi hi.` = Subject is Noun.
`Amah pen siampi khat ahi hi.` = He is a priest.

### Existence
`[S] [place] ah om hi.` = Subject exists at place.
`Pasian vantungah om hi.` = God is in heaven.

### Relative clause
`a [V] mi in [main clause]` = The one who V does...
`A it mi in a u a nau a it ding hi.` = He who loves must love his brother.

### Serial verb
`[V1]-in [V2] hi.` = Did V1 and then V2.
`Hong pai-in gen hi.` = Came and said.

### Purpose
`[V1] nadingin [V2] hi.` = V2 in order to V1.
`Hotkhiat nadingin Tapa hong khal hi.` = Sent the Son in order to save.

### Conditional
`[condition] leh, [result] ding hi.` = If condition, result will happen.
`Na it leh, na thupha ngah ding hi.` = If you love, you will receive blessing.
