# Zolai Poetry, Songs & Literary Forms

This document catalogs the poetic and musical traditions of the Zomi people, extracted from *Zolai Sinna Bu*, *Zolai Khanggui*, and the Biblical corpus (particularly Psalms, Proverbs, and Song of Solomon).

## 1. Zogam Lapi (National Song)

The *Zogam Lapi* is the most iconic Zomi anthem, composed by **Thawn Kham**. It is structured as a call-and-response hymn with 4 stanzas and a chorus. It was taught to 5,000+ children during the 2009 Zolai Sinna program and has become the de facto Zomi national song.

### Chorus (Cho)
```
Zogam, Zomi, Zogam ei hong pia Pa Pasian thang hen
Zogam, Zomi, Zogam ei hong pia Pa Pasian thang hen
```
*(Source: Sinna, line 735–736)*

### Stanza I
```
Beh le Phung, Pawl kideina pau kilamdang zong
Tunsung khat pan piang laizom sanggamte liansak un
Zogam kicinna, Zomite zawhna
Jesu in Topa ci'n phat un
```

### Stanza II
```
Zogam a ding Pasian, Zomi a ding Pasian
Lungsim khutte laamti in, Pasian liansak un
```

### Stanza III
```
Thungen in hanciam, nasem Zogam tate aw
Zomite I belh I suan in Pasian bek hi
Zogam kicinna, Zomi te zawhna
Jesu in Topa ci'n phat un
```

### Literary Features
- **Parallelism:** Lines echo the same structure (`Zogam a ding Pasian, Zomi a ding Pasian`)
- **Collective identity:** Use of `I` (we, inclusive) and `ei` (us)
- **Call to action:** Imperative forms (`liansak un`, `phat un`)

## 2. Khapi Aw Hong Kia (Children's Song)

A traditional Zomi children's song preserved in *Zolai Sinna* (lines 1232–1241), teaching daily rhythm and domestic life through playful narrative.

```
Khapi aw hong kia, hong kia
Na koi lai na tat, na tat
Ka khukbuk ka tat, ka tat
Zingsang an in bang na huan a?
Khuangbai anteh ka huan a,
A tui ka hawp ka hawp a,
A ha ka baak, ka baak a,
Ka gil a tui kiu keu
Buh tei kawi in ka sat kha
```

### Literary Features
- **Call-and-response:** Opening `Khapi aw hong kia, hong kia` (Rooster, come out!)
- **Reduplication for rhythm:** `ka tat, ka tat` / `ka hawp, ka hawp` / `ka baak, ka baak`
- **Domestic imagery:** Morning routine — rising, cooking, drinking, eating
- **Onomatopoeia:** `kiu keu` mimics stomach sounds

## 4. Animal Fables (Sinna Oral Tradition)

The *Zolai Sinna* preserves classical Zomi fables, each teaching moral lessons through animal characters:

| Title | Characters | Moral (A Deihna) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Uiphuk le Naupangte** | Frog + Children | Don't judge what you don't understand (`Saipi in keel mawlpih leh keel in thuak zo lo ding hi`) |
| **Bilpi le Sumkuang** | Hare + Tortoise | Perseverance beats speed (`Thanuamte in thadahte zo tawtung-te hi`) |
| **Keivom le Tuuno** | Wolf + Lamb | The powerful always find excuses to oppress (`Mawhna hong zong nuamte in bangbang ihi zongin paulap tuamtuam nei nuam teitei-te hi`) |
| **Va-ak Utong Kineihna** | Crow + Peacock | Be content with who you are (`I pianzia bangbangin lungkimlo a midangte pianzia banga I kibawl leh eima pianzia ngeina zong kitaanlawh thei hi`) |
| **Kauphe le Miksi** | Grasshopper + Ant | Prepare for the future (`Phaibi ciangin ka nek ding an ka khol hi`) |
| **Naupang le Uiphuk** | Children + Frog | Respect others' lives |

## 5. Gentehna (Parable) Literary Structure

Every parable in *Gentehna Tuamtuam le A Deihnate* (51 total, by Pa Lian Than Tuang, 2019) follows a strict three-part structure:

1. **A Thugil** (Theme) — One-line topic declaration
2. **Narrative** — The story itself, told in simple Zolai
3. **A Deihna** (The Desired Meaning / Moral) — The lesson extracted

### Thematic Categories from the 51 Parables
| Category | Stories | Key Themes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hanciamna** (Perseverance) | Kau le A Bu, Seng Tawh Tui Tawi, Sumkuang le Bilpi | Never give up; struggle builds strength |
| **Lungsim Limlangh** (Heart Clarity) | Tuuno le Keivom, Humpi le Bawng, Satan Kap | Beware deception; see through manipulation |
| **Itna** (Love) | Thangho le Liando, Vaphual, Nute' Itna | Sacrificial love; parental devotion |
| **Kingaihsutna** (Self-reflection) | Sai le A Khihna, Tangvalpa le A Ngaihsutna | Break mental chains; choose what you hear |
| **Cihtakna** (Faithfulness) | Sa le Be, Pawisiimte le Zu, Vaphual | Integrity in relationships |
| **Hun Manphatna** (Time value) | Sum le Hun, Tukpeng Kimawlna | Every second counts |
| **Thungetna** (Prayer/Faith) | Tuikia Khat, Mohluum, Teci Panna | God's provision comes in unexpected ways |

## 6. Biblical Poetic Forms (Psalms & Proverbs)

### A. Hebrew Parallelism in Zolai
The ZVS Bible preserves the parallelism structure of Hebrew poetry:

**Synonymous Parallelism** (saying the same thing twice):
- `Topa pen ka honpa ahi hi; kuamah ka kihta kei ding hi.` — The Lord is my shepherd; I shall not want. *(Psalm 23:1)*

**Antithetic Parallelism** (contrasting ideas):
- `Mihoihpa in a hoihna thaman ngah ding aa, migilopa in a gitlohna thaman thuak ding hi.` — The righteous will receive their reward, the wicked will suffer their punishment. *(Ezekiel 18:20 — via Gentehna)*

**Climactic Parallelism** (building intensity):
- `Zogam a ding Pasian, Zomi a ding Pasian / Lungsim khutte laamto in, Pasian liansak un` *(Zogam Lapi)*

### B. Proverbial Wisdom Patterns
- `A neupen siamna nengno khat na ngawn vallo hi.` — Even the smallest skill is not worthless. *(Gentehna #2)*
- `Thanuamte in thadahte a zo tawtung-te hi.` — The diligent always overcome the lazy. *(Gentehna #10)*
- `Bangbang ihi zongin hong gensia veve ding-te hi.` — No matter what you do, they will always criticize. *(Gentehna #20)*

## 7. Khanggui Lapau (Poetry Section)

The *Zolai Khanggui* (line 302+) contains a dedicated `Lapau` (Poetry) section preserving traditional Zomi verse forms. These poems use:
- **Couplet structure** with internal rhyme
- **Repetition of key moral phrases**
- **Nature metaphors** (mountains, rivers, birds)
- **Historical references** to Ciimnuai and the Zomi diaspora

### Kammalhoih (Beautiful/Wise Words)
A collection of proverbs and maxims from the *Khanggui* (line 298+):
- Themes: `itin` (loving) vs `itlo` (unloving)
- `Thuhoih ciaptehhuai` — Words worth recording
- `Ngaihsutna zatui` — Seeds of thought
