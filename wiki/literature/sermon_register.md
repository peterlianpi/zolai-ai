# Zolai Sermon Register (Biakna / Hanthotna)

This document outlines the formal linguistic patterns and "Lung-Kha" metaphors used in Zolai sermons and religious exhortations (*Hanthotna*).

## 1. Formal Address & Honorifics

In sermons, the address is communal and respectful.

- **Communal Address:** 
  - *Sanggam unau itte aw* (Dear beloved brothers and sisters)
  - *Pawlpi mite aw* (Church members / congregation)
  - *Ka it sanggamte aw* (My beloved siblings)
- **Honorifics:**
  - *Rev. Dr.* / *Rev.* (Reverend)
  - *Sia* (Teacher/Pastor) - Used as a title or suffix (e.g., *Sia Thangpi*).

## 2. Key Sermon Phrases

| Zolai Phrase | English Meaning | Usage |
|--------------|-----------------|-------|
| **Pasian kammal** | God's Word | Referring to the Bible or a specific message. |
| **Hanthotna** | Exhortation / Encouragement | The core purpose of the sermon. |
| **Thupha i paih hi** | We cast/pour blessings | Formal concluding blessing. |
| **Hehnepna** | Consolation / Comfort | Used in funerals or times of distress. |
| **Teci panna** | Testimony | Sharing God's work in one's life. |

## 3. "Lung-Kha" (Heart-Spirit) Metaphors in Sermons

Religious Zolai deepens the "Lung" architecture with spiritual metaphors.

- **Lungsim kitamzante:** (Broken hearts) - Those in need of God's presence.
- **Lungsim zangawpte:** (Humble/Crushed spirits) - Those whom God saves.
- **Kha Siangtho kidim:** (Filled with the Holy Spirit) - A state of spiritual empowerment.
- **Lungdamna kicing:** (Complete joy) - A common prayer for the congregation.

## 4. Structural Patterns

Sermons typically follow this flow:
1. **Thumasa (Intro):** Greeting and establishing the scriptural basis (*Lai Siangtho sungpan...*).
2. **Hanthotna (Body):** The message, often structured with numbered points or parables (*Gentehna*).
3. **Thupitna (Conclusion):** Final blessing (*Pasian in thupha hong pia hen*) and prayer.

## AI Generation Logic
- **Tone:** Humble yet authoritative (*Hanthotna* style).
- **Lexicon:** Use *Topa* (Lord) and *Pasian* (God) with appropriate possessive markers (*i Pasian* - our God).
- **Grammar:** Maintain formal *hi* (present) and *tahen* (optative/blessing) endings.
