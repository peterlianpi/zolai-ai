# ZomiDaily Register Study (Sawltak Style)

## Register Overview
The **ZomiDaily (ZD)** style, historically known as the **Sawltak (Messenger)** style, is the primary modern news register for the Zolai-speaking community. It provides a bridge between formal ZVS 2018 biblical language and everyday contemporary speech.

## Key Stylistic Markers

### 1. Attribution & Reporting (Evidence-based)
- **cih kiza hi** (It is heard/reported that): The standard phrase for relaying news from a secondary source.
- **hiding in ki ummawh hi** (It is suspected to be): Used for developing news where facts are not fully established.
- **pulakkhiakhin** (Has announced/declared): Used for official statements from figures like the President or Hluttaw officials.

### 2. Digital Metadata (News Cycle)
- **ZD Sawltak #zomidaily**: The standard modern sign-off for news snippets.
- **hong ki Update kikding hi**: Indicates an active, ongoing news cycle (Will be updated again).
- **ZD Sawtlak**: A variations often seen in older or dialectal posts.

### 3. Emotional Tone (Public Reaction)
- **ki patau simsim**: (Somewhat anxious) - A common descriptor for public reaction to safety or political events.
- **thakhatthu in**: (Suddenly) - Used to describe breaking news or unexpected events (e.g., accidents, bomb blasts).

### 4. Headlines & Title Formatting
- **Topic + Location + ZOMI DAILY**: (e.g., "Trump in IS te ni 30 sung abeisak nading in Executive Order suaikai – ZOMI DAILY")
- **Author attribution**: ~ Author Name – ZOMI DAILY (e.g., "~ Thang Khan Lian – ZOMI DAILY")

## Linguistic Rules (Sanity Checks)
- **Register Smoothness**: Uses **hiam?** or **hia?** for questions to maintain a professional yet approachable melodic flow.
- **Orthography**: Generally follows **ZVS 2018** but allows for modern loanwords related to technology (e.g., "Attachment nakhak", "25 MB").
