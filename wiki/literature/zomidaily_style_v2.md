# ZomiDaily Register (Sawltak Style)

## Overview
The **ZomiDaily (ZD)** style is the standard modern news register for the Zolai-speaking community. It differs from formal biblical prose by its use of "Messenger" (*Sawltak*) markers and evidentiary language.

## Key Stylistic Markers

### 1. Evidence-Based Reporting (Verification)
- **cih kiza hi** (it is heard/reported): Used to relay news from secondary sources.
- **hiding in ki ummawh hi** (it is suspected/thought to be): Used for unfolding situations where facts are not fully established.
- **pulakkhiakhin** (has announced/declared): Used for official statements from government or religious officials.

### 2. Sign-offs and Metadata
- **ZD Sawltak #zomidaily**: The modern signature used at the end of snippets.
- **hong ki Update kikding hi**: Standard digital news phrase indicating follow-up updates (will be updated again).
- **ZD Sawtlak**: A variations often seen in older or dialectal reporting.

### 3. Emotional Tone (Public Reaction)
- **ki patau simsim** (somewhat anxious/worried): Frequently used to describe the community's reaction to security or political news.
- **thakhatthu in** (suddenly/unexpectedly): Used to introduce breaking news or accidents.

### 4. Header & Attribution
- **Topic + Location + ZOMI DAILY**: (e.g., "Trump in IS te ni 30 sung abeisak nading in Executive Order suaikai – ZOMI DAILY")
- **Author Attribution**: Uses the format `~ Author Name – ZOMI DAILY`.

## Tone Rules
- **Melodic flow**: Uses **hia?** or **hiam?** for questions to maintain a professional yet warm "smooth" register.
- **Directness**: Avoids the overly repetitive "a ci hi" of biblical text in favor of more descriptive action verbs.
